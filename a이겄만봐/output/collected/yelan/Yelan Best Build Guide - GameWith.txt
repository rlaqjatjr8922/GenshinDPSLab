Yelan is a 5-star Hydro character that wields a Bow in Genshin Impact. Her gameplay focuses on being an off-field sub-DPS and support unit, utilizing her Elemental Skill for rapid movement and energy generation, and her Elemental Burst to deal consistent Hydro damage through coordinated attacks that scale with her max HP. She excels in quick-swap teams and enables Hydro-related elemental reactions efficiently.

This is her most important ability, and since it has a high Energy cost, Yelan is usually built around ensuring she can use it every rotation by giving her enough Energy Recharge. Her Elemental Skill also lets her dash in a chosen direction, marking enemies she passes through and damaging them afterward.
Because of the DMG% buff she provides, Yelan is a staple in many teams that need Off-Field Hydro. Her Burst requires the active character to use Normal Attacks to trigger Hydro application, so she works best with On-Field DPS units that weave Normal Attacks into their rotation or combo.
Her place in the meta has remained strong thanks to Hydro’s versatility and the consistent value of her DMG% buff and Off-Field application.

Roles & Playstyle Of Yelan and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Hydro Applicator and DMG% Buffer
Yelan applies consistent, Off-Field single-target Hydro damage to the enemy that the active character is currently targeting with their Normal Attacks. She also provides a substantial DMG% buff to the active character while her Burst is still in use.
Pros and Cons of Support Yelan
Pros
- Easy to use.
- Scales with Max HP only, allowing for easier building since she doesn't care about attack.
- Elemental Skill can be used for exploration.
- DMG% Buff is equivalent to an extra elemental DMG bonus Goblet.
Cons
- High Energy cost on her Burst, leading to similarly high Energy Recharge stat requirement.
- Burst is purely single-target.
- Burst is only trigged with Normal Attacks, which lowers Yelan's usability for DPSes that don't use Normal Attacks often.

Energy Recharge Is Her Most Important Stat
Because most of Yelan's staying power as a support is found in her Elemental Burst, it's necessary to prioritize reaching her ideal Energy Recharge stat above other stats.
Being able to use her Burst will be more beneficial for your On-Field DPS in the long run, especially if they need the Off-Field Hydro to trigger important reactions. Once you can comfortably use her Burst every rotation, you will want to move to striking a balance between her CRIT ratios and her Max HP so her Elemental Burst still contributes a decent margin to the team DPS.

Team Rotation of Yelan and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Yelan
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Yelan
1.Start with the supports with longer ability durations if there are any. If the On-Field DPS needs to use an ability beforehand as part of their kit mechanics (such as
Arlecchino
), then remember to start with them instead.
2. Swap to Yelan, then use Elemental Skill (Hold).
3. Use Yelan's Elemental Burst.
4. Swap to supports with shorter ability durations if there are any.
5. Switch to your On-Field DPS then perform your preferred damage combo.
6. Repeat from Step 1.
Teams with Yelan generally want to use her after the supports with longer ability duration and before both the On-Field DPS and supports with shorter durations.
This is so that the On-Field DPS maximizes the uptime of her Burst's Hydro application and DMG% buff.
Basics Of Using Yelan
Yelan is usually played as an Off-Field Hydro Support whose Elemental Burst attacks in tandem with the active character's Normal Attacks, applying Hydro to a single target.
She fills a similar niche as
Xingqiu
and this comes with the advantage of being able to pair with multiple On-Field DPSes who benefit from consistent Off-Field Hydro application.
Elemental Burst Is Her Most Important Ability
Yelan's Burst creates a dice-like object called Exquisite Throw that hovers near the active character.
When the On-Field or active character uses a Normal Attack, the Exquisite Throw releases a stream of Hydro damage at the enemy that the character is attacking, and this occurs every second provided the active character keeps using their Normal Attacks.
Elemental Skill Can Be Used for Exploration
Yelan's Elemental Skill will first turn her invisible while also causing her to rapidly dash in a specific direction.
While dashing, she will mark enemies in her path, dealing damage to them after exiting her invisible state when her Skill ends. Yelan's Skill can also be tapped or held, both causing a burst of movement. When the Tap version is used, Yelan will briefly dash forward. When the Hold version is used, you can use your directional input to control her dashing, allowing you to mark more enemies and to cover more distance than the Tap version.
Her Skill can be useful in overworld exploration since it travels faster than normal character dashes and doesn't cost stamina.
Yelan Provides a Ramping DMG% Buff
Her passive, Adapt With Ease, lets her Burst provide an external source of DMG% for the On-Field character.
When Yelan's Elemental Burst is active, the On-Field character receives a 1% DMG% buff, which increases at a rate of 3.5% each second.
This buff is hard-capped to 50%, which is an overall substantial boost to damage output. Note that this buff is similar to having one more Elemental damage Goblet equipped on your character, since that is also considered DMG%.
Yelan Scales with HP%
All of Yelan's abilities and therefor her personal damage is based off of her max HP.
Because of this, she will appreciate having HP% stats on her Artifacts, and has the bonus of being able to use low-base Attack weapons since she doesn't really care about her Attack stat. She also pairs well with other Hydro characters since Hydro Resonance increases the Max HP of everyone in the party.

How To Use Yelan's Skills & Passives
How To Play Yelan
▼Off-field Hydro support with ramping DMG% buff.
▼Elemental Skill is a dash and marks enemies.
▼Off-Field Hydro from her Burst is her main appeal.
▼Passives increase her utility and personal damage.

Off-Field Hydro Support With Ramping DMG% Buff
Basic Rotation Of Yelan
1.Start with the supports with longer ability durations if there are any.
2. Swap to Yelan, then use Elemental Skill (Hold).
3. Use Yelan's Elemental Burst.
4. Switch to your On-Field DPS then perform your preferred damage combo.
5. Repeat from Step 1.
Yelan's rotation as a support or sub-DPS follows the same combo trend shared by other Burst-oriented supports.
You want to use her Elemental Skill first even when her Burst is fully charged in order to catch the Energy Particles made by her Skill. Since the Energy is immediately consumed when the Burst is activated, the particles aren't wasted and instead help charge it for the next rotation.
Elemental Skill Is a Dash and Marks Enemies
Elemental Skill:
Lingering Lifeline
- Fires a Lifeline that causes her to become invisible while dashing in a direction.
- Marks and entangles enemies along her path, dealing damage to them after she exits the Lifeline and becomes visible.
└
(Tap Version):
Dashes forward, doesn't require any directional input.
└
(Hold Version):
Dashes in the direction of your movement input.
Yelan's Skill is a burst of movement in a specific direction, with the distance she travels determined by whether you Hold or Tap the Skill.
Her Elemental Skill is her personal source of Energy, so it's important to ensure you catch the particles generated by her Skill. With C1, Yelan will gain one extra use of her Skill, which allows for her to run more offensive stats instead of Energy Recharge.
Off-Field Hydro From Her Burst Is Her Main Appeal
Elemental Burst:
Depth-Clarion Dice
- First activation deals Hydro damage.
- Creates a dice or "Exquisite Throw" that hovers beside the active character.
└ The dice releases a stream of Hydro DMG based on Yelan's Max HP.
└ The dice attacks in tandem with the active character's Normal Attack. This style of attack from an ability is called a 'coordinated attack'.
- The Exquisite Throw's attacks can also be triggered by a Lifeline explosion (or her Skill ending).
Yelan's Elemental Burst is her most important ability as a primarily supportive-type character
Her Burst is what allows her to apply Off-Field Hydro for the On-Field DPS to trigger reactions with, is the bulk of her personal DPS, and is what gives the active character the DMG% from her passive: Adapt With Ease. Supplying enough Energy Recharge for Yelan to use this ability consistently every rotation should be your primary goal while mapping out her weapon and Artifact build.
Passives Increase Her Utility and Personal Damage
Passive Description
Turn Control
When the party has 1/2/3/4 Elemental Types, Yelan's Max HP is increased by 6%/12%/18%/30%.
Yelan's Ascension Passives focus on boosting her damage while also adding supportive value.
Her first passive increases her Max HP based on the variety of elements in the party, which in turn raises her personal DPS since many of her abilities scale with HP.
Yelan is often run in high-reaction teams that use multiple elements, making it easy to maximize this passive. With Hydro Resonance also providing a Max HP boost, having another Hydro teammate will generally yield a similar HP increase as well.
Passive Description
Adapt With Ease
So long as an Exquisite Throw is in play, your own active character deals 1% more DMG. This increases by a further 3.5% DMG every second. The maximum increase to DMG dealt is 50%.
The second Ascension Passive makes it so that the active character receives a ramping DMG% buff when her Elemental Burst is still in use. As stated before,
the buff value provided by the DMG% increase is equivalent to having another Elemental DMG% Bonus goblet equipped on the active character, since they are both the same DMG%.
Skill Can Be Used in Exploration
Yelan's Elemental Skill when held can cover a bit of distance, and can be used for exploration in the overworld since it doesn't consume stamina.
You would generally alternate the usage with a regular dash, then use her Elemental Skill to recharge the stamina. If you have her C1, which grants her an additional Skill usage, the exploration utility of her Skill further increases.
On-Field DPS Yelan Is Best at C6
Constellation Summary
C6: Winner Takes All
- After using Depth-Clarion Dice, Yelan enters the Mastermind state:
Mastermind State:
- Normal Attacks become special Breakthrough Barbs.
- These deal 156% of a normal Breakthrough Barb’s DMG.
- DMG is treated as Charged Attack DMG.
- State lasts 20s or until 5 arrows are fired.
Yelan's Normal Attack has a special mechanic called Breakthrough.
After five seconds out of combat, she enters the Breakthrough state, which makes her next Charged Aimed Shot charge faster. This shot becomes a Breakthrough Barb and deals AoE Hydro damage based on her Max HP. At C6, after using her Elemental Burst, all of her Normal Attacks become Breakthrough Barbs with amplified damage.
This Constellation is very strong and enables Yelan to function as a true On-Field DPS.
Should You Pull for Yelan's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Prioritize Yelan's Constellations
Signature VS Constellations
C1 or C2 >
Aqua Simulacra
Aqua Simulacra Will Underperform Without Constellations
For Yelan specifically, although Aqua Simulacra is a significant boost to her personal damage, using it without her first two constellations will make it harder to reliably charge her Burst to full due to Aqua Simulacra not providing any means of gaining energy. Yelan's burst is the bulk of her Hydro application, so it's better to ensure you can always use it whenever possible. C1 adds another skill proc, which provides energy, and C2 adds another water arrow to her burst, increasing the amount of Hydro she applies.
Unless you already have C1 on Yelan to offset the lack of energy from Aqua Simulacra, it is recommended to pull for her constellations first.
If you are interested in going all the way, C6 will be Yelan's best constellation overall in terms of damage while also transforming her into an on-field damage character.

Aqua Simulacra
> C1 or C2

Pulling for
Aqua Simulacra
with the intention of using it as a universal Crit bow (instead of giving it to Yelan) is a viable choice, since the passive only requires you to hover closer to enemies, and the damage increases persists even when the equipping character is not on-field.
As such, it becomes a great option for almost all bow characters that could benefit from extra Crit, some damage increase or a bit of HP%.
In contrast, bow characters who thrive with more Energy Recharge, such as Yelan or
Faruzan
, would prefer to have
Favonius Warbow

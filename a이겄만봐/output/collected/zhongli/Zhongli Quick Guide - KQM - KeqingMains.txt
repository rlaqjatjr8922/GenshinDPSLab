Character Overview
Playstyles
Shielder (Shieldbot)
By far the most recommended playstyle for using Zhongli. A shield-focused build wants to maximize his HP to maintain the shield. Zhongli is the best pure shielder in the game due to his shield’s strength and uptime.
This Zhongli guide does not cover the following playstyles in-depth. Please refer to the
Zhongli Extended Guide
for more information.
Burst Support
In most situations, it is not recommended to use Zhongli’s Burst because of its long animation and low damage output. However, at C2 or with very high investment, Zhongli can become a viable Burst support in addition to his general shielder playstyle.
Physical DPS (Pikeli)
Physical DPS Zhongli possesses underwhelming personal damage. Pikeli can be viable if Zhongli acts as a driver for strong teammates or has a high-investment build against a single enemy.
Hybrid DPS (Hybridli)
Hybrid DPS Zhongli is a variant of Physical DPS Zhongli that trades some of his Physical damage for some AoE Geo damage. While being able to offer more AoE damage than Pikeli, it still possesses underwhelming personal damage and requires rather high investments. Hybridli is a build only recommended for players who want to make use of the entirety of his kit.
Geo Construct DPS (Microwave)
Zhongli can use his Stone Stele resonance to deal AoE damage in conjunction with Geo Traveler’s Geo Constructs. While Traveler is the most common and recommended teammate, other Geo characters like Albedo and Ningguang place Geo Constructs that resonate with Zhongli’s Stele.
This playstyle also requires you to know how to position Geo Constructs accurately and struggles against highly mobile enemies or bosses who can destroy the constructs.
Talents
Level and Talent Priority
Elemental Skill
In Zhongli’s recommended playstyle as a pure shielder, he only needs to level his Elemental Skill. Leveling him to 90 maximizes his effective shield HP, but this is a lower priority after reaching 80/90.
If you want to play Burst Support, then you should level his Burst equally to his Skill.
If you want to play Physical DPS, then you should prioritize his Normal Attack Talent.

Stone Stele
When created, deals AoE Geo DMG. Additionally, it will intermittently resonate with other nearby Geo constructs, dealing Geo DMG to surrounding opponents. The Stone Stele is considered a Geo construct that can both be climbed and used to block attacks.
Only one Stele created by Zhongli himself may initially exist at any one time.
Jade Shield
Possesses 150% DMG Absorption against all Elemental and Physical DMG. Characters protected by the Jade Shield will decrease the Elemental RES and Physical RES of opponents in a small AoE by 20%. This effect cannot be stacked.
Zhongli’s most significant Talent is his Elemental Skill. When the Skill is tapped or held, Zhongli places a Stone Stele in front of him, which deals regular ticks of AoE Geo damage. The AoE effect resonates with other Geo Constructs in range, which allows for more instances of damage.
When the Skill is held, Zhongli deals Geo damage, generates a Jade Shield, and produces a Stone Stele if there isn’t one already (up to two at C1). This shield shreds the Elemental and Physical RES of enemies close to the on-field character. This is usually referred to as Universal RES Shred.
The Stone Stele’s particle generation is unreliable since each tick only has a 50% chance to generate a particle, and it is difficult to keep enemies within its AoE in practice. Furthermore, some enemies (notably bosses) easily destroy the Stele.

Rainbow
Any artifacts that provide the most HP stats for Zhongli work well enough since he doesn’t rely on set effects in his kit.
2pc HP% + 2pc HP%
Provides the strongest shield for Zhongli in terms of effective HP.
4pc Tenacity of the Millelith
While Zhongli may seem like an ideal user of this set, his Stele’s limited range prevents him from using this set effectively in a lot of circumstances. However, if your team’s DPS unit can snapshot the 4pc ATK% buff, then it can be viable since Zhongli still procs the effect by using his Hold Skill.
4pc Archaic Petra
Zhongli has to personally pick up the desired Elemental Shard to trigger 4pc Archaic Petra’s set bonus for that Element. This can be difficult to accommodate in practice without increasing rotation time. Despite this, the buff does apply to every teammate when you can activate it.
4pc Deepwood Memories
Zhongli can use this set in Dendro teams since his Hold Skill and Stele can trigger the 4pc set effect. The 8s uptime on the buff is more forgiving; however, maintaining 100% uptime can be difficult given the Stele’s limited range.
4pc Instructor
This is a niche set that only benefits specific teams. It often requires altered rotations that make it not worth the hassle in most cases.
4pc Silken Moon’s Serenade
This is a niche 4pc set that is a minor upgrade over 4pc Instructor in teams with at least 2 Nod-Krai characters.
4pc Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
4pc Scroll can be a solid support set if no one else on the team can hold it. Usually, 4pc Archaic Petra or 4pc TotM are still better options.
Weapons
Favonius Lance
Favonius Lance increases Zhongli’s utility by providing the team with particles which lower ER requirements. The passive does not proc off-field and you need to build some CRIT Rate to trigger it more consistently.
Black Tassel
This 3-star Polearm provides the highest HP% secondary stat among Polearms and is easy to obtain. It is Zhongli’s best option for maximum shield health.

Shielder
Zhongli — Flex — Flex — Flex
Zhongli is extremely versatile as the best shielder in the game. The Universal RES Shred from his shield allows him to be useful in a variety of teams. Some DPS characters really need a shield for resistance to interruption like Hu Tao, Lyney, Xiao, Yoimiya, and Wanderer.
Compared to a 4pc Viridescent Venerer user, Zhongli is about 10% worse in terms of damage gains for relevant Elemental DPS characters, but his shield may make him more valuable if you lack an appropriate healer and need the survivability.

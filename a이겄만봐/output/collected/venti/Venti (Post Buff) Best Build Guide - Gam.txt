Venti is a 5-star Anemo character that wields a bow in Genshin Impact. Venti functions as an off-field, sub-DPS that utilizes his Elemental Burst, "Wind's Grand Ode" to gather enemies and trigger elemental reactions which damage enemies caught within the stormeye. Outside of battle, Venti can also use his Elemental Skill, "Skyward Sonnet" to create upward air currents that Travelers can use to reach higher places. Lore-wise, Venti is a traveling bard with an aloof nature towards all things big and small. His love of wine and casual attitude discredits his identity as the Anemo Archon.

What Kind of Character is Venti?
In battle,
Venti is a 5-Star Anemo character that can played as an
On-field
DPS or
Off-field
Sub-DPS or Support.
His Burst is a strong crowd-control ability which can gather multiple enemies, except certain heavy enemies and bosses. It can also absorb an element among Pyro, Hydro, Electro and Cryo, similar to Sucrose and Kazuha's Burst, and do Swirl reactions with the absorbed element. His Normal Attacks gets infused with Anemo as long as the Burst is active, allowing him to function as an On-field DPS.
Venti is now able to compete with existing Anemo DPS units, that is with the addition of the Hexenzirkel Talents. However, likewise with similar Anemo DPS characters such as Xiao and Wanderer, his best support as an On-Field DPS is Faruzan so if you often use either of them, then you don't have to pull or build Venti for that purpose.
If you do lack characters who can debuff enemies or provide crowd control, Venti is a considerable choice as he can reliably use Viridescent Venerer for the Elemental RES shred while also being able to output decent amounts of damage through Swirl reactions.

Roles & Playstyle Of Venti and His Strengths and Weaknesses
Anemo Off-Field or On-Field DPS
Venti was originally designed to be used as an Anemo Off-Field Sub-DPS that can suck in a large quantity of lightweight enemies using his Elemental Burst as a form of Crown Control. This playstyle is still viable for him, but he can also be used as an On-Field DPS if you his Anemo-infused Normal Attacks.
Pros and Cons of Sub-DPS or DPS Venti
Pros
- Easy to play.
- Elemental Burst is one of the strongest crowd-control abilities.
- When paired with other Hexenzirkel members, he can deal decent enough damage as an On-Field DPS.
Cons
- Elemental Burst suction effect only works on a limited number of enemies, not including bosses.
- On-Field Venti's DPS will still lag behind the modern DPSes like
Mavuika
.
- Although Venti's Burst duration can be extended, it also increases the cooldown, so you'll need to be aware of how much time you spend On-Fielding him.

Basic Rotation For Off-Field Sub-DPS Venti
1.Start with the supports with longer ability durations if there are any. If any Sub-DPS or DPS needs to deploy their abilities first as part of their mechanic, such as

His Elemental Burst summons an Anemo Vortex that pulls in enemies. While it's active, his Normal Attacks are infused with Anemo, with each Normal Attack hitting extending the Burst for a few seconds, but also increasing the cooldown of the Burst.
Elemental Burst Creates a Vortex
Venti's most important ability is his Elemental Burst,
since it summons a Stormeye that pulls in enemies that are not immune to Crowd Control towards the center, infusing with an Element that it comes into contact with at the same time.

Witch's Homework

Hexerei
Elemental Skill Can Summon an Updraft
Venti's Elemental creates a Wind Domain in front of him,
dealing Anemo damage and slightly lifting enemies within range. You can hold his Elemental Skill to create an updraft that can be used to enter a gliding state if you jump after entering it, which makes it a helpful tool for exploration purposes.
Energy Recharge Passive
Because Venti relies on his Elemental Burst regardless of playstyle, he will want to have enough Energy Recharge to use it on demand.
Thankfully, he has an Energy-related passive that restores a fixed amount of Energy if his Burst absorbs an Element. He also ascends with Energy Recharge, thus making his ideal build path easier to achieve.
Buffs Increase His DPS Viability
Venti, alongside several other Mondstadt characters, received both direct and indirect buffs after the introduction of the Hexenzirkel faction or resonance. His buffs make it so that he becomes viable as an On-Field DPS, which is only viable with other Hexenzirkel members.

Who Is Venti?
Mondstadt's Infamous Drunk Bard

Actually Barbatos, The Anemo Archon

He Is The Original Anemo Archon

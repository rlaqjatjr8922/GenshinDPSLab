Yaoyao Build
Yaoyao Guide and Best Builds
Yaoyao is a 4-star polearm user that specializes in spreading
Dendro
and team healing. Yaoyao is a pure support character
with limited abilities to DPS on her own. Her toolkit involve summoning a
companion who throws out Radishes, for both damage and healing. She fits well
into Hyperbloom and Quicken teams
with her turret-style game play.

1.
Yaoyao Support Build
Yaoyao offers very little DPS output, but does perform well as a strong 
healer. Her ability to spread
Dendro
off-field is alright, but 
focuses on single-target rather than a large AoE. However, her skill does have 
a small explosion radius so enemies that are grouped together can all be hit with a 
single shot of Dendro. As for her healing, her companion will throw radishes to 
any character under 70% HP before attacking the enemy. Yaoyao is played as an 
off-field support with a very high Energy Recharge and HP focus.

Yaoyao scales on HP for healing, ATK for her
Dendro
damage 
and Elemental Mastery for her reaction DPS. This unfortunately does not create a whole lot of 
synergy when it comes to her gear choices and you will need to pick which stat you 
want her to focus on.
Our weapon recommendations are as follows.
For healing, we recommend
Rightful Reward
— a HP based polearm that grants energy every time 
you are healed and can be crafted at the forge.
For scaling her Dendro capabilities, we recommend
Moonpiercer
, which offers a strong ATK 
buff that can still be applied while off-field.
Lastly, a decent alternative option is the
Favonius Lance
which
is filled with tons of Energy Recharge and can help with energy
generation for your team.
Yaoyao's top choice for Artifacts is the
Deepwood Memories
set, 
with the biggest bonus to the set allowing her companions to shred Dendro 
resistances. This can also occur when she is off-field, triggered through her 
companion. An alternative 5-star healing set can be the
Maiden Beloved
set; this boost to her healing abilities and can occur 
off-field. Finally, the 5-star set
Tenacity of the Millelith
will 
work as a team buffing set, but is generally outperformed by the other two 
5-star sets due to Yaoyao not generating any shields. For an early leveling 
set, you can also use the 4-star
Instructor
set to get her started 
if you are new to the game.
2.
Combos and How to Play Yaoyao
Yaoyao plays like a turret build, with her
Raphanus Sky Cluster
bringing out her companion to toss
Dendro
damage and heals to your characters. The healing 
effect will prioritize any character below 70% with healing, unfortunately the majority of 
her companion's targeting abilities are random. If you match this skill up 
with an active character that focuses on close range damage, you will benefit 
from both the Dendro damage and the healing effects at the same time. Keep that 
in mind when you choose your on-field character.
Yaoyao's Elemental Burst ability,
Moonjade Descent
, allows
her to summon multiple companions (Yueguis), as long as she stays on the field
for 5 seconds. This can summon up to three ‘turrets' at once, which
significantly boosts her damage and healing. Bear in mind, she needs to stay
on-field to complete the summons before you swap her off; swap out too early
and the skill will stop. This ability will also grant you 15% movement speed
and Dendro resistance, which can help in a Bloom if you have Dendro cores detonating all around you. Usually, you will
just dodge and dash around until the effect is over and your team is healed to
max. Most teams focus on her Elemental Skill as a Dendro spreader and her
Elemental Burst as a large group heal.

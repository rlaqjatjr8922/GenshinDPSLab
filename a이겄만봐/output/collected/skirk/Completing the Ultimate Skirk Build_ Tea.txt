Skirk’s Abilities Detailed
While Skirk’s normal attack talent doesn’t contribute much
, she delivers considerable damage through alternative skills:
Skirk gains 45 Serpent’s Subtlety points from using her Skill, regardless of whether you press or hold it. Pressing activates a special state while holding enables high-speed movement; during the special state, Skirk executes Cryo-infused normal attacks and can sprint.
Her Elemental Burst activates with 50 Serpent’s Subtlety, dealing heavy damage, but it’s typically better to utilize her second Burst, Havoc: Extinction, which boosts her normal attack damage in the special state without any cost.
If you do use her normal Elemental Burst, its damage increases for every Serpent’s Subtlety over 50, with a max of 62.
For every 2.5 seconds that her team triggers Frozen, Superconduct, Cryo Swirl, or Cryo Crystallize reactions, Skirk can summon a Void Rift. Up to three can be on the battlefield, and they can be absorbed via her Cryo-infused Charged Attack, Havoc: Extinction Burst, or the hold version of her Elemental Skill.
Each absorbed Void Rift grants eight Serpent’s Subtlety and further boosts the damage of her Cryo-infused normal attacks when using Havoc: Extinction Burst.
You’ll mainly trigger the Frozen reaction in her team because her allies can enhance her normal attack DMG by up to 170% when applying Hydro or Cryo damage, making it necessary to build around a Hydro/Cryo team.

Skirk Talent Focus
Like every other Genshin Impact character, Skirk can upgrade three talents: normal attack, Elemental Skill, and Elemental Burst. However, as noted earlier,
her normal attack is not particularly valuable
since the multipliers for her Cryo-infused attacks are inherent to her Elemental Skill.
Focus on upgrading her Elemental Skill first, as it generates the majority of her damage, followed by her Burst.

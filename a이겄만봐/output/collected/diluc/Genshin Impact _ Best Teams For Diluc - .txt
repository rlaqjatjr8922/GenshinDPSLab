Popular Teams For Diluc

▼Melt Plunge Diluc
★★★★★
- Diluc is paired with strong DMG% buffers like
Citlali
and
Xianyun
in a Melt team to help boost his damage output.
- Diluc will frequently use Plunging Attack thanks to Xianyun's ability allowing him to easily achieve this just by jumping, and this allows him to trigger Melt on most of his attacks consistently.
▼Overload Diluc
★★★★・
- Diluc can be played alongside
Chevreuse
's and an Off-Field Electro Sub-DPS like
Fischl
in a pure Overload team.
-
Chevreuse
's Elemental RES Shred in a team consisting of only Pyro and Electro characters is essential for this team's DPS output.
- This team is slightly ahead of Diluc's Vaporize team in terms of DPS potential when you have
Gest of the Mighty Wolf
,
Fischl
and
Durin
with their
Hexerei
passives enabled.
▼Vaporize Plunge Diluc
★★★・・
- Diluc's simple gameplay allows him to have decent synergy with many Off-Field Hydro supports who can help him trigger Vaporize.
The DMG increase from Vaporize is less than Melt for Pyro attacks, so this team is less effective compared to Diluc's ideal Melt team.
▼Mono Pyro Diluc
★★・・・
- A Mono-Pyro team typically consists of mostly Pyro and Anemo or Geo supports that provide Elemental RES shred to further enhance the raw Pyro damage.
- Diluc's base multipliers are very underwhelming, so it's not surprising that this comp underperforms against Diluc's other possible team setups.

Rotation Of Melt Plunge Diluc Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start the rotation with Bennett's Elemental Skill (tap).
2
Switch to Xianyun. Use her Skill (x3) into a plunge. Deploy her Burst.
3
Use Bennett's Elemental Burst.
4
Swap to Citlali. Use her Skill into her Burst. Normal Attack (x1) after.
5
Spam Plunging Attacks as Diluc by jumping. You can use his Skill into a Normal Attack combo after towards the end, but it's optional.
6
Repeat from step 1

Sub
Details
Citlali
Rosaria
Escoffier
Ganyu
- Citlali offers the most consistent Cryo application, but Rosaria or Escoffier can be decent alternatives.
- Rosaria is better for multi-target content, while Escoffier can sustain with her Burst's healing.
Bennett
Iansan
Dehya
- Bennett is both an ATK% buffer and a sustain character that also activates Pyro Resonance, which gives more ATK%.
- You can replace him with either Iansan or Dehya, albeit to weaker results.
- Iansan's ATK% buff is just as potent as Bennett's, but her healing is tied to Nightsoul Burst, which Diluc can't use.
- Dehya's damage mitigation and transfer is a viable sustain option on top of her also enabling Pyro Resonance. However, she has no other useful ATK% buff in her kit apart from what she can provide through her artifact set.
Xianyun
Xilonen
Sucrose
Kazuha
- Xianyun enables Diluc to spam Plunging Attacks, so replacing her turns this team into a regular Melt team instead. It should still be a playable comp, but the damage output will be lower due to losing the ability to quickly spam Plunging Attacks in a short amount of time, which is what increases the DPS.
- Xilonen will be your best alternative due to her Elemental RES Shred. Note that if your Citlali already has the
Cinder City
set equipped, it's best to give her or Xilonen a different set like
Instructor
since the Cinder City 4-piece effect cannot be stacked.
- Other Anemo supports each bring their own benefits while providing RES Shred through the
Viridescent Venerer
set.

Substitute Characters
Character
Sub
Details
Fischl
Ororon
Yae Miko
Kuki Shinobu
- Fischl is the most optimal Off-Field Electro applicator, especially if she has her
Hexerei
passive active.
- Otherwise, any other Off-Field Electro Sub-DPS who can apply the element consistently is going to be an okay alternative.
- You lose the bonus of the Hexerei: Secret Rite effect without Fischl, so Diluc won't be able to access the CRIT DMG buff from
Gest of the Mighty Wolf

Durin
Mavuika
Iansan
- Durin enables Fischl's Hexerei passive and vice versa. Without either, you won't be able to gain the CRIT DMG buff from
Gest of the Mighty Wolf
.
- Durin's best alternatives are Pyro or Electro characters that can contribute team buffs, deal personal damage, or provide extra sustain if Chevreuse's healing isn't enough for you.
- Natlan characters like Mavuika and Iansan can also supply a DMG% buff by equipping the
Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
set.
Chevreuse
Durin
- Chevreuse's Elemental RES shred and teamwide ATK% buff are what allows most Overload teams (that include her) to be competitive.
- Durin is the closest possible alternative since he also provides RES shred, but he loses out in terms of buffing power to Chevreuse due to her teamwide ATK buff and especially if you have her C6 (which gives Pyro and Electro DMG% bonus to all party members).
Overload teams that include Chevreuse will need to ensure that all its members are either Pyro or Electro, since Chevreuse's RES Shred is disabled if the team includes characters with other elements.

This team performs slightly worse than the Melt version because the Vaporize reaction triggered by Pyro attacks has a lower damage multiplier (1.5x) compared to Melt (2.0x).

No.

2

Mono-element teams are typically composed of 2 to 3 characters of the same element, who are then complemented by a RES Shred support to decrease the resistance of enemies to that element, which is important since the team has no other damage amplification buffs from reactions.
Rotation Of Mono Pyro Diluc Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Bennett's Skill (tap)
2
Use Sucrose's Skill. Burst is optional.
3
Swap to Durin. Deploy his Skill (x2) to enter his Confirmation of Purity mode, then Burst.
4
Switch to Diluc. Perform his common Skill combo.
└
Burst→ Normal Attack (x2) → Skill → Normal Attack (x2)→ Skill → Normal Attack (x2) → Skill → Normal Attack (x2)
5
Repeat from step 1.
The only part of this rotation that needs to be strictly followed is to use Xiangling's Burst while Bennett's Burst is on the floor. This is due to Xiangling's Burst snapshotting Bennett's Buff, meaning even if Bennett's Burst expires, her Burst will retain the ATK buff from it.
Substitute Characters
Character
Sub
Details
Durin
Xiangling
Emilie
- Since Diluc's numbers and scalings are very low, a strong Pyro Sub-DPS is needed to make up for the loss of damage amplification from reactions.
- You can also make use of a Burning Sub-DPS like Emilie.
Sucrose
Venti
Kazuha
Xianyun
- Sucrose enables Durin's
Hexerei
passive while also adding more RES Shred through the
Viridescent Venerer
, but she can be replaced by other Anemo supports that can trigger the Viridescent 4-piece effect.
- Venti can maintain Durin's Hexerei passive and provides better grouping. However, Venti's Hexerei DMG% buff only applies to characters that trigger the Swirl reaction themselves, which doesn't apply to Diluc.
- Kazuha disables Durin's Hexerei passive but does provide a DMG% buff.
-  Xianyun allows you to use Diluc's Plunging playstyle, but it's less effective in this team since he isn't triggering any amplifying reaction like Melt or Vaporize.
Bennett
-
- A true Mono-Pyro team will usually include Bennett by virtue of his generous ATK% buff and his sustain from Burst. There's no real alternative to him in a Mono-Pyro comp, as any substitute would result in a significant loss of both sustain and DPS.
Mono-Pyro teams will always feel weaker against comps that have access to reactions. However, this team should still achieve average results at best in easier endgame content (like Imaginarium Theatre), making it a viable Diluc team regardless.

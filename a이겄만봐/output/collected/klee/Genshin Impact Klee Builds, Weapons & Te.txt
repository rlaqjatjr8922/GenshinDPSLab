Klee Gameplay
Klee
is a 5-star Pyro Catalyst wielder who functions as an on-field Main DPS with exceptional area-of-effect damage capabilities. Klee's gameplay centers around her Charged Attack mechanic, which is enhanced by her Explosive Spark passive. When her Normal Attacks or Elemental Skill deal damage, she has a 50% chance to obtain an Explosive Spark, which makes her next Charged Attack cost zero stamina and deal 50% increased damage. Her Elemental Skill, Jumpy Dumpty, throws a bouncing bomb that splits into mines on the third bounce, providing excellent area denial and energy generation with its two charges. Her Elemental Burst, Sparks 'n' Splash, continuously summons explosions that attack nearby enemies, but unfortunately deactivates when she leaves the field.
Her primary strengths include exceptional area-of-effect damage that works even better with enemy grouping, high Pyro application that enables various elemental reactions, the ability to stunlock smaller enemies with her Charged Attacks, and excellent team energy generation through her A4 passive which grants 2 Elemental Energy to all party members when her Charged Attacks CRIT. However, she requires significant mechanical skill due to her long attack animations that must be animation-canceled for optimal DPS, has relatively short attack range for a Catalyst user, is extremely squishy and easily interrupted during combat, and her Elemental Burst deactivates when switching characters, limiting team rotation flexibility.
Upcoming Hexerei Buffs (Version 6.2)
In the Version Luna III,
Klee
will receive significant buffs through the Hexerei Resonance mechanic after completing Witch's Homework: Of Leaves That Spark. Her gameplay will be dramatically improved when paired with at least one other Hexerei character (Durin, Venti, Albedo, Mona, Fischl, Sucrose, or Razor).
The core buff introduces a "Boom Boost" mechanic: when Klee's Normal Attacks, Charged Attacks, Elemental Skill, or Elemental Burst hit enemies, she gains one stack of Boom Boost, with each stack lasting 20 seconds independently. With 1/2/3 stacks of Boom Boost, her Charged Attacks enhanced by Explosive Spark deal 115%/130%/150% of their normal damage.
Additionally, her A1 Passive (Pounding Surprise) receives a major enhancement: during her Elemental Burst, Klee's Normal Attack combo count no longer resets. When she performs her third Normal Attack, she can consume one Explosive Spark to automatically fire a free Charged Attack without breaking her combo sequence. This dramatically reduces her clunkiness and stamina consumption issues.
Her constellations also receive significant upgrades in Version Luna III. C2 now reduces enemy DEF by 23% when dealing damage with her Elemental Skill (previously required enemies to be hit by mines specifically). C4 gains a new effect where if Klee is the active character when her Burst ends, the explosion deals 100% increased damage. C6 now grants Klee a 25% Pyro DMG Bonus when using her Elemental Burst, and with another Magic character in the team, she gains a 50% chance of not consuming Explosive Spark when used, plus a 40% chance for her first or second Normal Attack to trigger an additional Charged Attack blast.

Normal Attack - Kaboom!
Level her Normal Attack talent first as it directly scales both her Normal Attacks and Charged Attacks, which are her primary damage sources. Klee's optimal damage rotation relies heavily on her Charged Attacks enhanced by Explosive Spark, making this talent essential for maximizing DPS.
Elemental Skill - Jumpy Dumpty
Level her Elemental Skill second as it provides substantial burst damage with two charges and generates significant energy particles. The skill's bouncing bomb mechanic deals Pyro damage three times before splitting into eight mines, offering excellent area control. The mines can be grouped together by Anemo characters like Sucrose, Kazuha, or Jean for concentrated explosive damage. This skill is crucial for maintaining Klee's energy economy and generating Explosive Spark procs.
Elemental Burst - Sparks 'n' Splash
Level her Elemental Burst last unless running a Burst DPS build at C2 or higher. While the Burst deals respectable damage, it requires Klee to remain on-field and deactivates upon switching characters, making it less versatile than her other abilities. The 60 energy cost and field-time requirement limit its priority compared to her primary damage sources. However, it can be valuable for animation canceling and provides continuous Pyro application against single targets or enemies at different elevations.

Klee Constellations
Klee performs adequately at C0 as a Main DPS, but her constellations provide significant improvements to her damage output and energy generation capabilities. C2 is particularly valuable as it transforms her into a viable Sub-DPS option.
C1 Performance
Attacks and Skills have a certain chance to summon sparks that bombard opponents, dealing DMG equal to 120% of Sparks 'n' Splash's DMG.
C1 provides a moderate DPS increase by occasionally triggering additional explosions during Klee's attacks. The 20% damage boost to her Elemental Burst enhances her overall damage output but doesn't fundamentally change her playstyle. This constellation is nice to have but not essential. Most players can skip this constellation unless they plan to continue pulling for higher constellations.
C2 Performance
Being hit by Jumpy Dumpty's mines decreases opponents' DEF by 23% for 10s.
C2 is Klee's most valuable constellation and represents an excellent stopping point. The 23% DEF reduction is a massive team-wide damage amplifier that benefits all party members, not just Klee. This constellation transforms Klee into a viable support character for other Pyro DPS units, as she can quickly apply the DEF shred with her dual-charge Elemental Skill. With the Version 6.2 buff, C2 becomes even better as the DEF reduction triggers from any Elemental Skill damage, not just mines.
C4 Performance
If Klee leaves the field during the duration of Sparks 'n' Splash, her departure triggers an explosion that deals 555% of her ATK as AoE Pyro DMG.
C4 allows Klee to function better in quickswap teams by dealing significant burst damage when switching out. This constellation enables Sub-DPS playstyles where Klee uses her Elemental Skill charges and Burst before switching to other damage dealers. With the Version 6.2 buff adding a 100% damage increase when Klee is active as the Burst ends, this constellation becomes more flexible, allowing players to choose between quickswap and on-field strategies.
C6 Performance
While under the effects of Sparks 'n' Splash, Klee will regenerate 3 Energy for all members of the party (excluding Klee) every 3s. When Sparks 'n' Splash is used, all party members will gain a 10% Pyro DMG Bonus for 25s.
C6 transforms Klee into an excellent battery and support character while maintaining her Main DPS capabilities. The energy regeneration helps maintain team rotations, while the Pyro DMG bonus benefits Pyro-heavy teams. However, this constellation is generally not worth the investment for most players, as the energy generation and damage bonus are modest compared to the cost. With Version 6.2 buffs, C6 gains significant value by adding a 25% Pyro DMG bonus for Klee herself when using Burst, plus the 50% chance to not consume Explosive Spark and 40% chance for free Charged Attacks with another Magic character in the team.
Conclusion
This comprehensive guide demonstrates Klee's position as a high-skill-ceiling Main DPS character in Genshin Impact. While she faces strong competition from newer Pyro DPS characters, her unique playstyle, excellent area-of-effect damage, and team energy generation capabilities make her a rewarding choice for dedicated players who master her animation cancels and mechanical requirements.
With the upcoming Version 6.2 Hexerei buffs, Klee is poised to receive significant quality-of-life improvements and damage enhancements that address many of her historical weaknesses. The Boom Boost mechanic and automatic Charged Attack generation will dramatically reduce her clunkiness, making her more accessible to players while increasing her ceiling for veterans. These updates ensure that the adorable Spark Knight will remain a viable and fun option in the evolving meta.

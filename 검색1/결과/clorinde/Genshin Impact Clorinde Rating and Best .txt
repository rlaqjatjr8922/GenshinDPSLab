How Strong is Clorinde in Genshin Impact Meta?
So,
is Clorinde worth the primogems?
The short answer is that she’s a solid pick, but not game-breaking. Clorinde is a mid-to-high tier DPS who performs great in
Aggravate
and
Quickbloom
teams. Her playstyle revolves around converting her Normal Attacks into Electro-infused gunshots, allowing her to drive off-field damage dealers like
Fischl
,
Beidou
, or
Yelan
. She also has high self-sustain thanks to her
Bond of Life
mechanic, making her one of the tankier DPS units in the game.
Clorinde Strengths:
Fast, stylish Normal Attacks
Self-sustaining through Bond of Life (BoL)
High Electro application for reactions
Great synergy with Quicken teams (Aggravate, Hyperbloom)
Strong Constellations, especially C2 and C6
Clorinde Weaknesses:
No innate interruption resistance (unless C2)
Damage falls behind top-tier DPS like Neuvillette, Arlecchino
Relatively high Energy Recharge needs if running solo Electro
Requires careful rotation management for max damage
For a solid Electro main DPS, Clorinde is a good pick in your team, but if you’re purely chasing meta dominance, you should consider going with
Neuvillette
,
Furina
, or
Arlecchino
first.

Genshin Impact Best Clorinde Builds
If you don’t build Clorinde right, she will end up feeling underwhelming compared to top-tier DPS units. So, here’s how to build Clorinde so she can hit hard, stay alive, and not look team’s weakness instead of strength.
Talent Priority: What Should You Level First?
If you want Clorinde to be at her best, her Elemental Skill is the first thing you should max out. Why? Because it’s what makes her unique—it converts her Normal Attacks into Electro-infused shots, letting her deal consistent damage while applying Electro reactions like Aggravate and Quickbloom.
Level Up First: Elemental Skill (Hunter’s Vigil)
– This is Clorinde’s bread and butter. It lets her use ranged Electro gunshots while in her
Night Vigil state
.
Second Priority: Elemental Burst (Last Lightfall)
– It’s not the biggest source of her damage, but it adds some AoE Electro damage and buffs her
Bond of Life
.
Skip Normal Attacks: Oath of Hunting Shadows
– You don’t need to level this since her Skill transforms her Normal Attacks into Electro shots anyway.
So, the rule of thumb for Clorinde Talent build is:
Skill > Burst > Normal Attack
.
Clorinde Best Artifacts
Artifacts can make or break a character in Genshin, and for Clorinde, you’ve got a few options depending on how you want her to play.
Fragment of Harmonic Whimsy (4-piece):
This set was practically designed for Clorinde. It increases her damage whenever she gains or loses
Bond of Life
, which happens constantly during combat. With this set, you get an
18%
attack bonus for
6 seconds
which makes her hit even harder.
Thundering Fury (4-piece):
If you’re running Clorinde in a team focused on
Aggravate
reactions (
Electro + Dendro
), then Thundering Fury is a fantastic choice. It reduces her
Elemental Skill
cooldown by
1 second
, meaning you can spam her lunging Electro attacks more regularly. It’s especially useful if you love quick, flashy combos and reaction-based damage.
Gladiator’s Finale (4-piece):
When you face trouble farming
Whimsy
or
Thundering Fury
, Gladiator’s Finale is a decent fallback.
It levels up Normal Attack damage by 35%
, which works well with Clorinde since her Skill turns her Normal Attacks into Electro shots. It’s not as strong as the other two sets, but still reliable.
Main Stats & Substats
No matter which artifact set you choose, these are the stats you should be looking for:
Sands:
ATK% (or Elemental Mastery in a Quickbloom team)
Goblet:
Electro DMG Bonus
Circlet:
CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG (whichever balances your ratio better)
Substats Priority:
CRIT DMG > CRIT Rate > ATK% > Elemental Mastery > Energy Recharge
Since Clorinde scales best with raw damage and CRIT stats, you need to prioritize high CRIT DMG and CRIT Rate while keeping enough ATK% to boost her overall DPS. In case of building reaction-heavy teams with her like Aggravate and Quickbloom, some Elemental Mastery can also be useful.

Clorinde Best Weapons
Absolution (Her Signature Weapon):
If you manage to get Absolution, this sword is Clorinde’s main weapon. It increases CRIT DMG by
%20
, ATK%, and increases her Bond of Life effects, which turns her to hit harder and survive longer.
Haran Geppaku Futsu (Strong 5-Star Alternative):
If you missed Absolution but still want a powerful 5-star option, Haran Geppaku Futsu will do the job. It levels up CRIT Rate and increases Elemental DMG by
12%
, another great pick along with signature weapon.
Mistsplitter Reforged (High CRIT Damage):
Mistsplitter is one of the best overall DPS swords in the game, and Clorinde can use it well. It grants an Electro DMG Bonus, which increases her damage further. However, it requires frequent Elemental application to maintain its passive, so it works best in Aggravate teams.
The Black Sword (Best Battle Pass ):
If you don’t have a 5-star weapon, The Black Sword from the Battle Pass is a strong 4-star alternative. It provides a
27.6%
CRIT Rate and regenerates
60%
of ATK as HP on Normal Attacks.
Finale of the Deep (Best Free-to-Play):
It’s a free weapon to use for Clorinde which boosts ATK% and synergizes well with her Bond of Life mechanic.
Genshin Impact Best Clorinde Teams
Even though she’s an independent fighter, Clorinde still needs the right teammates to maximize her damage output. Her best teams revolve around Electro reactions, particularly Aggravate and Quickbloom, but she can also be a part of Overloaded and Electro-Charged setups. Let’s break down the best Clorinde teams and why they work so well.
Aggravate Clorinde (Her Best Overall Team)
Clorinde (Main DPS) + Nahida (Dendro Support) + Fischl (Off-field Electro) + Kazuha/Sucrose (Anemo Support)
Aggravate is one of the best reaction-based playstyles for Clorinde because it uses her Electro DMG, and she applies Electro like crazy with her gunshots. Nahida is good for keeping enemies under Quicken, Fischl brings extra off-field Electro damage, and Kazuha/Sucrose can group enemies while reducing their resistance. If you love a fast-paced, reaction-heavy playstyle with massive Electro damage, this is the best team to create.
Alternatives:
Swap
Kazuha/Sucrose
for
Zhongli
to gain shields instead of swirling enemies into a blender.
Replace
Fischl
with
Beidou
for better AoE damage in multi-target fights.
Quickbloom Clorinde (Electro + Dendro + Hydro Strength)
Clorinde (Main DPS) + Nahida (Dendro) + Furina/Yelan/Xingqiu (Hydro) + Fischl/Beidou (Electro Support)
This team is similar to Aggravate but adds Hydro into the mix that allows Hyperbloom reactions. Since Clorinde is constantly applying Electro, she can trigger a ton of Hyperbloom reactions from the Dendro Cores produced by Nahida and Furina/Yelan/Xingqiu.
Bonus Perk:
Furina gives Clorinde a free Healing Bonus, which massively buffs her Bond of Life self-sustain.
Alternatives:
Replace
Furina
with
Yelan or Xingqiu
for a more damage-focused Hydro application.
Swap
Fischl
for
Beidou
if you’re fighting multiple enemies and want Beidou’s chain lightning effect.
Overload Clorinde (Explosion Focused)
Clorinde (Main DPS) + Chevreuse (Pyro Buffer) + Xiangling/Bennett (Pyro Support) + Fischl (Electro Support)
For a playstyle with continuous explosions and high-energy stuff, this team composition is the right pick. Chevreuse buffs Electro and Pyro DMG, making Clorinde hit even harder. Xiangling provides off-field Pyro damage to trigger Overload, while Fischl maintains Electro uptime. However, Overload leads to knockback, so enemies might get yeeted across the battlefield. It’s chaotic but ridiculously fun.
Alternatives:
Replace
Fischl
with
Beidou
for better AoE Electro damage.
Swap
Xiangling
for
Bennett
to have more healing and a big ATK buff instead of pure damage.
Electro-Charged Clorinde
Clorinde (Main DPS) + Furina/Yelan/Xingqiu (Hydro) + Beidou (Electro) + Sucrose/Kazuha (Anemo)
This Clorinde’s team focuses on Electro-Charged reactions, where Hydro tries to wet enemies, and Clorinde zaps them non-stop. Beidou delivers extra Electro damage, while Sucrose/Kazuha increases Clorinde’s damage and keeps enemies grouped. This setup is very interesting in multi-target fights but not as effective against single bosses since Electro-Charged reactions work best when hitting multiple enemies.
Alternatives:
Swap
Beidou

Fischl

Replace
Sucrose

Zhongli

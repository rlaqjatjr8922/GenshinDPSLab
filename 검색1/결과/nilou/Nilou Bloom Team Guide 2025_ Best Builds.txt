Nilou Bloom Fundamentals: 2025 Meta Overview
How Bloom Actually Works (And Why It Matters)
Here’s the thing about Nilou’s ‘Court of Dancing Petals’ passive – it’s basically magic when you stick to Hydro and Dendro characters only. Those regular Dendro Cores? They transform into ‘Bountiful Cores’ that hit harder, explode instantly, and cover way more ground.
You can have up to 5 cores hanging around at once. Create a sixth? The oldest one goes boom immediately. Standard cores are more patient – they’ll wait 6 seconds before exploding and dealing AoE Dendro damage based on whoever triggered them (their EM and level matter here).
Now, 2025 changes everything. Nilou’s sitting pretty in S-Tier, and honestly? It’s all thanks to Lauma fixing her biggest weakness – single-target damage. Version 6.0 brings ‘Lunar-Bloom’ reactions that generate both Dendro Cores AND ‘Verdant Dew’ stacks. But here’s the kicker: Lauma’s Ascension 1 Passive makes Bloom reactions actually CRIT. That’s a flat 15% average damage boost right there.

Lauma Integration: The Game-Changer We Needed
What Makes Lauma Special
Lauma’s a 5-star Dendro Catalyst user from Nod-Krai, and her stats at Level 90 are solid: 10,654 HP, 255 ATK, 669 DEF, plus 115.2 Elemental Mastery. Her Elemental Burst is where things get spicy – it grants ‘Pale Hymn’ stacks (18 on cast, maxing at 36) that add flat damage to Bloom reactions based on her EM.
Her Hold Elemental Skill? That’s your big damage moment. It consumes Verdant Dew stacks for ‘Lunar-Bloom damage’ that scales with EM and CRIT stats, ignores DEF completely, and won’t hurt your own team. Pretty neat, right?
The real magic happens when you combine her with Nilou. You get ‘Bountiful Lunar Cores’ – basically the best of both worlds. This combo finally solves Nilou’s single-target problem, making her viable in every Spiral Abyss chamber. Lauma typically replaces your secondary support, handling both Dendro application and primary buffing duties.
Optimal Nilou Bloom Team Compositions
The Dream Team Setup
Your 2025 meta composition looks like this: Nilou (Sub-DPS), Lauma (Support/Sub-DPS), Nahida (Sub-DPS/Enabler), and Kokomi (Healer/Driver). This Double Hydro, Double Dendro setup is chef’s kiss – you get Lauma’s CRIT-enabling passive, Nahida’s incredible Dendro application and EM sharing, plus Kokomi keeping everyone alive while driving reactions on-field.
Don’t have everyone? No worries. Swap Kokomi for Baizhu if you need extra survivability, or replace Nahida with Collei if you missed her banner. There’s even a ‘Lunar Bloom’ focused team using Lauma, Aino (that new 4-star Hydro Claymore), Nilou, and Baizhu that triggers Lauma’s ‘Ascendant Gleam’ passive for +10% CRIT Rate and +20% CRIT DMG on Lunar-Bloom.

Nilou Build Optimization Guide
Artifact Game Plan
Nilou’s all about that HP life. Your best bet is 2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith (+20% HP) with 2-piece Vourukasha’s Glow (+20% HP) for a solid 40% HP bonus. Every main stat should be HP%: Sands, Goblet, Circlet – the whole shebang.
That magic number of 74,444 HP? It’s the mathematical ceiling for her passive benefit. The formula is 30,000 + (400/9) × 1000, giving you 9% Bountiful Core damage increase per 1,000 Max HP above 30,000. Yeah, it’s specific, but that’s where the damage lives.
Substat priority is straightforward: HP% > Energy Recharge > Flat HP > Elemental Mastery. For Energy Recharge, you’re looking at 150-170% if you want to burst every rotation, or 100-130% if you’re okay with every-other-rotation patterns.
Weapon Recommendations & Comparisons
The 5-Star Champion
Key of Khaj-Nisut is Nilou’s Best-in-Slot, no contest. With 542 base ATK and 66.2% HP bonus, plus a passive that increases HP by 20% and shares EM with the team based on Nilou’s Max HP – it’s over 20% more damage than alternatives.
Budget-Friendly Options
Iron Sting works great for 4-star options (510 base ATK, 165 EM), boosting both personal damage and Bountiful Core damage when you’re triggering reactions. Sapwood Blade gives you Energy Recharge if you’re struggling with burst uptime. For F2P players, Iron Sting is your craftable MVP.
Talent Priority & Constellation Guide
Where to Spend Your Books
Elemental Skill first, then Elemental Burst. Skip Normal Attacks entirely – you won’t need them. The Elemental Skill is where Nilou’s core mechanics live: Bountiful Core transformation and team buffs.
Constellation-wise, C2 ‘The Starry Skies Their Flowers Rain’ is your sweet spot. It drops opponent Hydro and Dendro RES by 35% for 10 seconds – that’s your biggest power spike and honestly, a great place to stop pulling.
Team Rotations & Combat Strategies
Making It All Work Together
Start with Nahida’s Elemental Skill and Burst for Dendro application and EM sharing. Then hit Nilou’s three Elemental Skill inputs (EEE) to activate Golden Chalice’s Bounty through Whirling Steps stance. Follow up with Lauma’s Elemental Skill and Burst for those buff effects and Pale Hymn stacks. Finish with Kokomi’s Elemental Skill and Burst, then go ham with Normal Attacks for reaction driving and healing.
Watch for Lauma’s Verdant Dew stacks – when she hits 3, swap back for that Hold Elemental Skill burst damage. Against single targets, prioritize Lauma’s damage windows while keeping Nilou’s aura active.
Spiral Abyss Performance Analysis
Floor 12 is where Nilou Bloom teams really shine. Enemy grouping and HP thresholds play perfectly into AoE reaction damage, and Lauma’s single-target capabilities cover those awkward isolated enemies that used to give us trouble.
Multi-wave chambers need careful energy management – make sure you’ve got burst availability for each wave. Lauma’s energy requirements are pretty steep (175-190% ER at C0), so you might need Favonius weapons and careful energy funneling.
The 2025 improvements really show in single-target chambers. Lauma’s Lunar-Bloom damage plus CRIT-enabled Bountiful Cores actually give competitive clear times now. Pro tip: manipulate enemy positioning to maximize Bountiful Core AoE overlap – the damage per explosion gets ridiculous.

Frequently Asked Questions
How does Lauma’s Lunar-Bloom work with Nilou’s Bountiful Cores?
When Lauma’s on the team, all Bloom reactions become Lunar-Bloom, creating both Dendro Cores and Verdant Dew stacks. With Nilou’s passive active, you get ‘Bountiful Lunar Cores’ that benefit from both mechanics. Lauma’s Nascent Gleam passive enables 15% CRIT rate and 100% CRIT damage, while her Burst adds flat damage scaling with EM.
What’s the HP target for Nilou in 2025?
74,444 HP maxes out her A4 passive, giving 9% Bountiful Core damage per 1,000 Max HP above 30,000 (capped at 400% bonus). You’ll need HP% artifacts across the board and preferably Key of Khaj-Nisut.
Is Lauma actually better than Nahida for Nilou teams?
In pure Bloom contexts? Often, yeah. The CRIT-enabling passive and damage buffs are massive. But optimal teams use both – Lauma as primary support, Nahida for Dendro application. Lauma’s sitting at SS-Tier support in Version 6.0.
What are the Energy Recharge requirements?
Nilou needs 150-170% ER for every-rotation bursting (100-130% for every-other-rotation). Lauma’s more demanding: 175-190% ER at C0 with Double Dendro for consistent Burst uptime, dropping to 130-140% at C4.
How do Version 6.0 artifact sets affect teams?
Silken Moon’s Serenade becomes Lauma’s best supportive set: 20% ER (2-piece) and team-wide EM plus Lunar Reaction damage bonuses (4-piece). EM bonus varies between 60 (Nascent Gleam) or 120 (Ascendant Gleam).
Investment priority for new players?
Start with Nilou’s weapon and HP-focused artifacts targeting 74,444 HP. Then invest in Lauma’s weapon and EM/ER artifacts (1000 EM, 160-200% ER targets). For constellations, Nilou’s C2 gives the biggest team damage boost, while Lauma’s C1 adds healing. Prioritize Elemental Skills over Bursts for both characters.

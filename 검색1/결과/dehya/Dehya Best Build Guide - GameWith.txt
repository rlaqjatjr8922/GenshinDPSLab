Dehya is a 5-star Pyro character that wields a Claymore in Genshin Impact. Her gameplay focuses on being a strong on-field DPS and shield provider, excelling in sustained melee combat with self-healing capabilities. Her Elemental Skill, "Fiery Might," unleashes a sweeping Pyro attack that deals AoE damage and generates a shield based on her max HP. Her Elemental Burst, "Sweeping Sands," performs a series of powerful slashes that deal massive Pyro damage and enhance her defense, allowing her to withstand heavy attacks while dealing consistent damage. Lore-wise, Dehya is a fierce mercenary from the desert lands of Sumeru, known for her loyalty and fiery spirit that never wavers even in the harshest battles.

What Kind of Character is Dehya?
Dehya is a 5-Star Off-Field Pyro support whose most important ability, her Elemental Skill, creates a Fiery Sanctum field.
This field increases the active character's resistance to interruption, applies Pyro to enemies inside whenever the character takes damage, and mitigates incoming damage before redirecting it to Dehya.
Dehya isn't usually the first choice in most meta teams.
She is usable in some, such as
Kinich's
Burning teams, but she is rarely the best-in-slot support. Instead, she is often considered a side-grade or alternative to other supports that can directly boost team DPS. Over time, however, her interruption resistance and Off-Field Pyro application have gained more value, making her slightly more viable than she was at release.

Roles & Playstyle of Dehya and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Support Dehya
Dehya is best suited as an Off-Field Support who deploys her Fiery Sanctum field to help the On-Field DPS mitigate damage, resist any attacks that could interrupt them, and apply Pyro to enemies.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field Support Dehya
Pros
- Easy to play.
- Only really needs to deploy her Elemental Skill to provide value to a team.
- Interruption Resistance provided by the field is equal to a shield.
- Can equip
Tenacity of the Millelith
so she can also contribute buffs.
Cons
- Personal damage is underwhelming, so her Burst has little use.
- Elemental Skill has uptime issues.
- Damage mitigation isn't quite as good as a sole sustain, so you need to pair her with a healer still.
- Pyro application of the field is very slow and can be unreliable.

Team Rotation of Dehya and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Dehya
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Dehya
1. Start with the other supports. If you have a DPS that needs to use an ability at the start of the rotation, like
Arlecchino
, then start the rotation with them instead.
2. Switch to Dehya then use his Elemental Skill once, then recast it to activate her passives.
3. Swap into your main On-Field DPS to perform their combo. If you need to refresh as part of the rotation, do so.
4. Return to Step 1.
Dehya's team rotation pre-C6 is going to be extremely straight-forward since she rarely, if ever, uses her Elemental Burst.
The most important part of it is remembering to use her Elemental Skill twice to activate her Unstinting Succor passive
, so the interruption resistance and damage mitigation are a little stronger at the start.
C6 Dehya Combo
Use Elemental Skill twice > Swap to her teammates to deploy their abilities > Use Elemental Burst, time your punches to get around 15 or 16 total.
If Dehya is C6 and you're interested in using her as an On-Field DPS, you can follow the combo above as a general rule. You can use a mix of Normal Attacks and Elemental Skill inputs to achieve the desired punch amount.
Basics Of Using Dehya
Dehya is often played as an Off-Field Support whose Elemental Skill deploys Fiery Sanctum field that gives increased interruption resistance.
While the field is active, the damage taken by the character standing inside it is slightly mitigated, with Dehya receiving a portion of that damage over a certain period of time.
Dehya's Field Acts Similar to a Shield
The Flaming Sanctum that Dehya creates is similar in function to a shield.
The active character cannot be interrupted while inside the field, and any damage they take is reduced and instead redirected to Dehya. She automatically triggers a self-heal after she receives a certain amount of damage. Additionally, the field releases a Pyro coordinated attack to enemies inside when the active character takes damage.
Elemental Burst Has a Unique Mechanic
Dehya's Elemental Burst causes her to release a fury of blows in a specific direction. This requires you to input your Normal Attack according to a sound cue that plays at a set interval. Timing it correctly results in Dehya releasing her punches or blows faster, contributing to an increase in damage.
Her Elemental Burst can also be cancelled early if you jump in her Burst mode.
Elemental Skill, Burst and Abilities Guide for Dehya
How To Use Dehya's Skills & Passives
How To Play Dehya
▼Damage mitigation and increased resistance to interruption.
▼Elemental Skill is her most important ability.
▼Elemental Burst has a timing mechanic.
▼Passives increase her viability as a sustain character.
▼Can be played in a Burgeon Team.
▼On-Field DPS playstyle is better at C6.
Damage Mitigation and Increased Resistance to Interruption
Basic Rotation Of Dehya
1. Start with other supports, then use their Elemental Skills or Bursts.
2. Swap to Dehya then use her Elemental Skill twice to trigger one of her passives.
3. Switch to your On-Field DPS to do their combo. Refresh key abilities if its part of the team's regular rotation.
4. Return to step 1.
Off-Field Dehya will usually only need to deploy her Fiery Sanctum through her Skill in a rotation.
This is because her Elemental Burst at C0 doesn't deal enough damage to justify the time used.
Elemental Skill Is Her Most Important Ability
Dehya's main supportive value comes from her Elemental Skill.
On its first cast, Dehya creates a field that provides three effects for the active character: increased interruption resistance, damage mitigation with a portion redirected to Dehya, and Off-Field Pyro application to enemies whenever the character is hit. The redirected damage is known as "Redmane’s Blood" and drains Dehya’s HP over time (DoT).
Her Skill can be recast
, and doing so is usually recommended to trigger her passive, Unstinting Succor, which reduces the damage Dehya receives from Redmane's Blood. Because the damage mitigation stops after Dehya receives 200% of her Max HP in damage, activating this passive ensures that the mitigation is in-effect for a little longer.
Elemental Burst Has a Timing Mechanic
Dehya’s Elemental Burst contributes a large portion of her personal damage but requires her to remain on-field.
When activated, she enters a special stance and unleashes a barrage of punches in the chosen direction. By default, she performs 6 punches, but rapidly pressing the Attack or Skill button can increase this to 10. Once the sequence ends, she automatically recasts her Elemental Skill.
While in this stance, Dehya’s punches are considered Burst attacks rather than Normal Attacks.
As a result, she cannot trigger effects tied to Normal Attacks, such as
Yelan
’s Burst. Jumping will also cancel the stance early, so avoid doing so if you want to complete the full sequence.
Passives Increase Her Viability as a Sustain Character
Passives Descriptions
Unstinting Succor
After Indomitable Flame (Skill cast once):
- For 9s, grants all party members Gold-Forged Form which further boosts interruption resistance inside Fiery Sanctum. Can activate once every 18s.
After Ranging Flame / Leonine Bite (Elemental Burst / Skill cast twice):
- For 6s, Dehya takes 60% less damage from Redmane’s Blood (the deferred damage she absorbs from allies via Fiery Sanctum). Can trigger once every 2s.
▲ This helps increase the uptime of her damage mitigation effect since it stops after hitting the cap (which is damage equal to 200% of Dehya's max HP).
Stalwart and True
When her HP is less than 40%, Dehya will recover 20% of her Max HP and will restore 6% of her Max HP every 2s for the next 10s. This effect can be triggered once every 20s.
Dehya’s Ascension Passives strengthen her Elemental Skill’s two core functions: the interruption resistance and damage mitigation.
Her first passive increases interruption resistance when she first creates a Fiery Sanctum, and if the field is renewed, either by recasting her Skill or after using her Burst, she briefly takes reduced damage from the accumulated mitigation (Redmane’s Blood).
Her second passive heals her after she absorbs a set amount of damage, allowing her to act as a pseudo-sustain unit by steadily mitigating and recovering HP
. However, heavy damage can still overwhelm her, so it is best to pair her with a dedicated healer to ensure survivability.
Can Be Played in a Burgeon Team
Since Dehya's Elemental Skill applies AoE Pyro to enemies within the area, she can act as a convincing Burgeon trigger if built with Elemental Mastery as a focus.
Due to how infrequent her Off-Field Pyro hits, its best to supplement her damage with other DPSes or by triggering other reactions in tandem, such as Hyperbloom or Electrocharged.
On-Field DPS Playstyle Is Better at C6
C6: The Burning Claws Cleaving
The CRIT Rate of Leonine Bite is increased by 10%. Additionally, after a Flame-Mane's Fist attack hits an opponent and deals CRIT Hits during a single Blazing Lioness state, it will cause the CRIT DMG of Leonine Bite to increase by 15% for the rest of Blazing Lioness's duration and extend that duration by 0.5s.
This effect can be triggered every 0.2s. The duration can be extended for a maximum of 2s and CRIT DMG can be increased by a maximum of 60% this way.
Dehya is normally built to function in a supportive role, since her personal damage at C0 is underwhelming.
If you happen to have her C6, her Elemental Burst becomes a decent source of DPS due to the increased CRIT stats she receives with this Constellation, which opens up a use-case for Dehya as an On-Field Pyro DPS.

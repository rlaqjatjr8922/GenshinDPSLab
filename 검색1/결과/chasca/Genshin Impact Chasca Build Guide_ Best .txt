6.5: Chasca Build and Kit Overview
The best Chasca build is pretty simple. | Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chasca’s entire playstyle is all about her Elemental Skill, Spirit Reins, Shadow Hunt. When she uses it, she enters Nightsoul’s Blessing, mounts her flying weapon, and gets enhanced attacks. The most important attack is the held version of her Skill state, which loads up Shadowhunt Shells.
Since she’s an Anemo character, these shells are usually Anemo, but with her talents, they can convert into Pyro, Hydro, Electro, or Cryo depending on the elements of the other three teammates in your party.
What this means is that Chasca’s damage profile is split across multiple elements, and that’s exactly why she feels so different from most carries. She’s not your typical element hypercarry like most DPS characters. The point of her gameplay is to:
fire as many converted shells as possible,
trigger elemental reactions,
and stack buffs that improve her mixed damage output.
Chasca Talent Upgrade Priority
Invest your Mora and books right! | Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chasca’s talents are refreshingly straightforward to level. The best talent level priority is as follows:
Elemental Skill > Elemental Burst > Normal Attack
Her Skill is where almost all of her real damage comes from, so that should be your first priority by a mile. Her Burst is still worth leveling because it hits hard and benefits from her team setup, but it won’t carry her damage profile the way her Skill does.
Her Normal Attack can safely stay last since you’ll rarely use her standard bow string outside of niche situations.
You should also strongly consider taking her to Level 90. Chasca benefits well from the stat scaling, and many of her teams rely heavily on reactions and mixed elemental damage.
Best Artifact Sets for Chasca in
Genshin Impact
6.5
A lot of different options to choose. | Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chasca’s artifact choices are easier than her kit makes them look. There is a clear best set, and then there are a few backup options depending on your roster.
Obsidian Codex (4-piece)
This is Chasca’s best-in-slot artifact set and the one you should farm if you’re serious about building her properly. It gives her:
15% DMG Bonus
40% CRIT Rate
while she’s actively consuming Nightsoul points
These set effects and buffs match her playstyle perfectly, and it makes gearing her much easier than most DPS units. If you’re using Chasca as your main carry, this is the set you want.
Other Good Artifact Sets
Marechaussee Hunter (4-piece)
This is a strong alternative if you’re running Furina. It’s still worse than Obsidian Codex in most cases, but it performs well enough to be worth using if you already have a cracked set lying around.
Viridescent Venerer (4-piece)
This works better in teams where Chasca is helping boost the damage of strong off-field units rather than maximizing her own personal output. It’s usable, but it’s generally more of a sidegrade for specific team ideas than a true main recommendation.
Shimenawa’s Reminiscence (4-piece)
Usable, but awkward. It can buff her Charged Attack damage nicely, though it comes at the cost of making her Burst less practical. Fine as a stopgap, not ideal long term.
Wanderer’s Troupe / Desert Pavilion / 2-piece mixed sets
These are all workable placeholders if your account is still catching up, but they’re clearly behind Obsidian Codex once your build matures.
Best Artifact Stat Priority for Chasca
For most players, Chasca’s best stat setup is:
Main stats:
Sands:
ATK%
Goblet:
ATK%
Circlet:
CRIT DMG or CRIT Rate
Substat priority:
CRIT DMG / CRIT Rate > ATK% > Elemental Mastery > Energy Recharge
The big thing to remember here is that ATK% Goblet is usually her most consistent option.
That sounds wrong at first glance, but Chasca’s damage is spread across multiple elements depending on shell conversion and team RNG. Since she isn’t always dealing the same elemental damage every shot, a standard elemental Goblet often loses consistency unless you’re running a very specific setup.
As for Energy Recharge, Chasca usually does not need to force Burst every rotation. In most builds, she’s perfectly happy using it when available instead of warping her whole setup around ER.

Best Chasca Teams in
Genshin Impact
6.5
It’s all about the reactions! | Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chasca is one of those characters where “best team” depends a lot on what characters you have and what kind of team you want. The good news is that she has multiple team options that are good, and several of them are genuinely strong.
I’m sure you won’t be surprised to hear that Bennett and Furina are absurdly good with her. And you’ll be using them for a lot of the team options.
Chasca + Bennett + Furina + Iansan
This is one of her strongest all-around teams right now. It works because Bennett gives Chasca a massive ATK buff that she uses well. Furina provides off-field Hydro, personal damage, and a huge universal buff. And to round it out, Iansan adds even more ATK scaling and works especially well with Chasca because she can keep moving while firing.
This team gives Chasca access to the vaporize, electrocharged, and overload reactions, premium buffs, and smooth gameplay. If you have all four characters, this is one of the first teams worth trying.
Chasca + Bennett + Furina + Ororon / Fischl / Ineffa
A great alternative team is just by replacing Iansan with a different electro character. These teams let her trigger a ton of different reactions like Vaporize, Electro-Charged, Overloaded, Swirl, and, in the case of Ineffa, Lunar-Charged
Best Electro options:
Ineffa
– one of the strongest new options thanks to Lunar-Charged access, shield utility, and EM support
Iansan
– excellent if you want more buffing
Ororon
– strong AoE and reaction synergy
Fischl
– reliable damage and easy value
Chasca + Bennett + Furina + Mavuika / Xiangling / Pyro Traveler
If you want to focus more heavily on Vaporize, these team options are the way to do it.
Bennett is still almost mandatory for comfort and damage. Furina is still her best Hydro slot user, Mavuika is one of Chasca’s best Pyro partners right now, thanks to off-field application and strong front-loaded damage. And of course, Xiangling is the classic fallback if you want raw Pyro damage and application.
Pyro Traveler is a surprisingly decent budget alternative.
Chasca + Bennett + Citlali / Rosaria / Escoffier + Mavuika / Pyro Traveler
Melt Chasca is very real, and in the right setup, very strong.

If you enjoy reaction-heavy gameplay and want something different from the Furina shell, Melt Chasca is absolutely worth trying.
Chasca + Furina + Citlali / Escoffier + Bennett
This team can slide between Freeze, Melt, and Vaporize depending on the enemy and aura state, which makes it surprisingly flexible.
It’s especially comfortable if you like:
safer gameplay
smoother sustain
less awkward field time management
This archetype is also one of Chasca’s more “premium feeling” setups because almost every slot is doing something useful at all times.

There are some cool constellations to get. | Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chasca is already strong at C0, but she has some very notable constellation spikes.
Best Early Constellation: C2
If you’re considering vertical investment, C2 is her best stopping point for value.
It gives her better consistency in terms of passive uptime and stronger performance in even her weaker team options. And most importantly, AoE damage during her Skill. That AoE is huge because it fixes one of Chasca’s biggest baseline weaknesses.
So if you like Chasca and want her to feel much smoother in multi-target content, C2 is the big one.
Is C1 Good?
Yes, but more as a quality-of-life and consistency upgrade than a big power spike. It improves shell conversion and makes more team variants comfortable, but C2 is where she really starts to feel upgraded.
Is C6 Worth It?
If you’re the kind of player asking whether C6 is “worth it,” the honest answer is probably that you already know what you’re in for. C6 Chasca is extremely strong. It improves her burst windows, gives her more frontloaded damage, and makes her shell pattern much faster and deadlier. It’s a major power spike if you’re planning on going all in.

4 Things You Need to Know Before Playing Flins
Flins deals damage through
Lunar-Charged
— a special reaction unique to him that can crit and scale with Crit Rate/DMG. Everything in his kit feeds into this one mechanic, so before you build him, here are
4 things you need to understand
.
1️⃣ A consistent Hydro applier is essential
When Hydro and Electro coexist on an enemy, they normally trigger
Electro-Charged
— but with Flins in the party, this is automatically converted into
Lunar-Charged
. This means your team must include a character capable of maintaining a
steady Hydro aura
on enemies at all times, ensuring Flins can deal damage reliably.
No Hydro uptime = No Lunar-Charged = Massive DPS loss
2️⃣ Electro DMG Bonus ≠ Lunar-Charged DMG Bonus
Lunar-Charged is an independent
reaction damage
type and is
not
buffed by Electro DMG Bonus, Elemental Mastery, or Aggravate buffs. The only stats that actually boost Lunar-Charged DMG are:
ATK
CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG
Lunar-Charged-specific DMG Bonus
(from certain weapon or Artifact set effects)
⚠️ Electro DMG Bonus does
not
increase Lunar-Charged DMG — don't build in the wrong direction!
3️⃣ Flins must reach Moonsign
Flins has a unique stacking mechanic called
Moonsign: Ascendant Gleam
— think of it as his combat empowerment state. At full stacks, both his
Lunar-Charged DMG
and
CRIT Rate
are significantly enhanced, making it a hard prerequisite for his damage rotation.
How to reach Ascendant Gleam:
Before Flins takes the field, have your teammates use their Skills in sequence to trigger Hydro/Electro reactions. By the time Flins swaps in last, he should be at full stacks. Both
team composition and swap order
should be designed around building up
Ascendant Gleam
before Flins goes on-field.
4️⃣ Flexible team building — one rule only
Flins has no strict team requirements. As long as your team can:
Maintain
consistent Hydro application
Help
maintain Ascendant Gleam
...everything else is open.
RES shred, ATK buffs, Shields, and Healing
can all be freely slotted in around these two priorities.
💡 The bottom line:
play Flins by keeping three things in mind —
enter the field at Ascendant Gleam
,
trigger Lunar-Charged consistently
, and
stack CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG as high as possible
.
Flins Kit Breakdown
Talent Priority: Elemental Burst (Q) > Elemental Skill (E) > Normal Attack (A)
Elemental Burst (Q) — Prioritize leveling to 9 or above.
The vast majority of Flins' Moonlit Electro direct damage comes from her Burst, making it the cornerstone of her entire damage rotation. Every Talent level yields a direct and meaningful increase to her damage output, giving it the highest return on investment of any Talent. Level it to 10 whenever resources allow.
Elemental Skill (E) — Level to 6 or above.
The Skill itself contributes a relatively modest share of total damage, but it serves as the entry point for triggering the Moonlit Electro cycle while generating Energy for the Burst. Leveling it primarily improves rotation consistency rather than raw damage — Level 6 is sufficient.
Normal Attack (A) — Leave at Level 1.
Normal Attacks account for a negligible portion of Flins' overall damage output. In practice, they serve primarily as filler between rotations and as a means of Energy generation, rather than a meaningful damage source. Prioritize resources for Q and E — Normal Attack Talent does not need to be leveled.
In summary: the Burst is Flins' primary damage source, as the majority of her Moonlit Electro direct damage is tied to it — prioritize leveling it to 9 or above. The Skill contributes less and is secondary. Normal Attack can remain at Level 1.

FAQ about Flins
What God Is Flins Based On?
Flins is based on an alleged Slavic deity — potentially a god of death — first mentioned in the 1492 book Cronecken der Sassen by German writer Konrad Bothe, who described Flins as being worshipped by people in the Harz Mountains and Lusatia (present-day Germany and Poland).
Who Exactly Is Flins?
Flins is a Lightkeeper of Nod-Krai and the lone guardian of the lighthouse and graveyard at the Final Night Cemetery. Though appearing enigmatic and cold, he is a cultured and courtly gentleman, and one of the last of the Snowland Fae still residing in Nod-Krai.
What Is Flins' Real Name?
His full name is Kyryll Chudomirovich Flins — and this is how he prefers to introduce himself, as a Ratnik of Nod-Krai.
Is Flins A Bad Guy?
No. Flins is a calm and well-mannered man who treats others with politeness and elegance. He fights for the people of Nod-Krai and honors those who have fallen, even having been awarded a medal for his conduct.
Why Are The Animals Scared Of Flins?
This specific detail isn't directly documented in the available sources. However, it is likely tied to his nature as a Fae and his connection to death and graveyards. Flins is not fully integrated into human customs due to being a Fae, and his role as a guardian of a cemetery — combined with his eerie, enigmatic appearance — may naturally unsettle wildlife around him.
Where Is Flins' Grave?
Flins doesn't actually have a personal grave — he has a dark sense of humor, teasing the Traveler and Paimon during their first meeting by inviting them to visit "his grave," which is in fact the Final Night Cemetery he keeps watch over. It was a joke at their expense.

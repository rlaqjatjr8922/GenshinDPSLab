▼Premium Lunar-Charged Team
★★★★★
-  Uses 2 Nod-Krai 5-stars whose niche is Lunar-Charged (Flins and
Ineffa
).
- Due to the unique damage calculation of Lunar-Charged, EM Buffs and Elemental Shred work better than Elemental Damage Bonus buffs. This is why Sucrose is a very good Support for Lunar-Charged.
-
Aino
and
Columbina
are the preferred Hydro applicator options.
▼F2P Lunar-Charged Team
★★★・・
- Lunar-Charged team options for Flins when you don't have Ineffa.
-  Even when F2P, Lunar-Charged is still a good team for Flins since Aino is given out for free and can enable Level 2 Moonsign effects.
- Any Anemo character can be used as VV holder while the Sub-DPS can be another Hydro or Electro.
▼Hyperbloom Team
★★・・・
- A Dendro Sub-DPS can be added in Flins' teams to have Hyperbloom happening alongside Lunar-Charged.
- But since it won't be focusing much on building around Lunar-Charged, this team's damage is lower than dedicated Lunar-Charged teams.
- A possible team option if you don't have Ineffa and Columbina, but have Lauma instead.
Flins is designed to be an On-Field Electro DPS that focuses around the Lunar-Charged reaction.
While Lunar-Charged deals Electro damage, it is not affected by Electro Damage Bonuses and only uses ATK and CRIT Stats
. This makes teams with Flins better overall when an Elemental RES shredder, like an Anemo character equipped with the
Viridescent Venerer
set, is included in the lineup since Enemy Resistances can affect the damage taken from Lunar-Charged.
Moonsign Ascendant is Important for Flins' Teams
Moonsign is a new mechanic introduced in Nod-Krai. Each Nod-Krai character increases it by 1 level and at the 2nd level, various buffs for Lunar Reactions will be activated. Aside from the buffs given, Flins' Active Skills are able to deal more Lunar-Charged Damage and his Burst gains more attacks dealt.

Rotation Of Premium Lunar-Charged Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Ineffa and use her Skill. Burst is optional.
2
Switch to Columbina. Skill into Burst.
3
Switch to Sucrose and use her Skill or Normal Attack (x1) into Burst.
└
If you're using
Wandering Evenstar
, do Normal Attack into Burst instead so you can use her Skill at the end of the rotation.
4
Switch to Flins. Use his Skill twice and then his Special Burst whenever it's up.
└
His ideal combo will be:
Skill (x2) -> Normal Attack (x3), dash -> Normal Attack (x4) -> Special Burst -> Normal Attack (x4) -> Skill -> Special Burst -> Normal Attack (x3)
5
Swap back into Sucrose to re-use her Skill.
6
Repeat from Step 1.
Flins wants to use his Normal Attacks in between his Special Burst usages in order to gain Energy to use at least 2 Special Bursts each rotation (at C0), but his combo has some room for you to dodge in between Special Bursts (instead of using Normal Attacks) if you need to, making his combo pretty flexible overall.

Does Flins Need Ineffa?
Flins needs
Ineffa
for his team to be competitive with some of the top meta teams. But while Flins' total team DPS is greatly improved with Ineffa due to her EM Buffs and personal damage,
he can still produce decent results without her provided you include Aino on his team, at the cost of significantly less damage potential overall.
Though it's still recommended to pull for Ineffa for a Flins team whenever possible.
You still need another Nod-Krai character to activate the Ascendant Gleam Moonsign Level in order for his damage to be viable into endgame content, so Aino (ideally C6) or Columbina become mandatory without Ineffa.
Note!
No Ineffa? Options to Consider:
- Pull for Ineffa on her rerun since she's still his best teammate overall.
If you aren't in a rush to get Flins, it may be better to prioritize securing Ineffa for him first.
- Obtain Flins' C1, since it provides about the same damage increase as Ineffa contributing her own DPS, but doesn't compensate for her buffs and shield. Aino or Columbina is still required even with his C1 if you don't have Ineffa.
- Inlude
Columbina
on his team, even if you don't have Ineffa. It'll still result in decent DPS that should be enough to at least clear Stygian Onslaught's Menacing difficulty at the very least.
Does Flins Need Columbina?
Columbina significantly increases the total team DPS of Flins' premium Lunar-Charged team because not only does she buff both Flins' and Ineffa's Lunar Reaction DMG, but she also contributes her own personal DPS. However, her pull priority for a Flins team is lower than Ineffa, since Ineffa was likely designed to be Flins' best teammate overall.
While Columbina greatly raises Flins' DPS potential, he can still clear specific endgame content with Aino, especially if you already secured Ineffa beforehand.
Pull Priority: Ineffa or Columbina?
Note!
Who should I pull first for Flins?
The answer will come down to your available primogem count and future pulling plans:
- It's best to pull for Ineffa first if you can't get both Ineffa and Columbina. Ineffa might take longer to rerun again in comparison to Columbina, who is likely to return within a few updates or months like other 'Archon' characters.
- If you don't have Ineffa, Columbina OR Flins, then it's also recommended to prioritize Ineffa and/or Flins before Columbina.
Point!
If you have personal reasons for not wanting to pull for Ineffa now, then it's fine to get Columbina only and replace Ineffa with
Fischl
or
Kuki Shinobu
instead. You can also further invest into Flins' or Columbina's Constellations or Signature Weapons to slightly compensate for the loss of Ineffa's personal damage.
Is Flins Worth Pulling Without His Best Supports?
Assuming Free-to-Play standards, Flins without Ineffa and Columbina performs around the same level as older DPSes, such as
Arlecchino
or
Clorinde
. But him being worth your pulls when your account is missing one or both of his best supports will still depend on your account state and personal in-game goals.

Stygian Onslaught

but results may still vary depending on your character builds, rotation mastery and whether or not Flins is good against the featured bosses.

For F2P,
Aino is a newly released free character that could be obtained by progressing in the Nod-Krai Archon Quest while Lan Yan and Fischl were both free in previous events
. Lan Yan mainly functions as the shielder and shredder while Fischl helps deal damage and generate Particles for Flins. Aino is the 2nd Nod-Krai character for activating Ascendant Moonsign and applies Hydro for Lunar-Charged proc.
Rotation Of F2P Lunar-Charged Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Fischl and use her Burst or Skill to start applying Electro.
2
Switch to Aino and use her Burst then Skill.
3
Switch to Lan Yan and tap her Skill twice to create a shield and swirl Electro.
4
Switch to Flins. Use his Skill first, followed by a Normal Attack (x1), followed by another Skill then trigger his mini-Burst. Once his Skill is back up, use it again, followed by another proc of his mini-Burst. You can fill the downtime between Cooldowns and actions with a Normal Attack (x1).
5
Repeat from Step 1 once buffs expire.

If you have Lauma but no Ineffa, you can opt into a Hyperbloom team as an alternative. The damage output should be fairly decent, but the team variations without Lauma are likely to lag behind. Furthermore, Lauma sees more value in a dedicated Lunar-Bloom team, with Flins also being better in a team centered around Lunar-Charged.
This team is sub-optimal for either, but can be worth the consideration if you happen to have both Lauma and Flins.
Note that because Lauma with Flins activates Ascendant Gleam, Lauma will want to build for personal damage instead since her passive, Light for the Frosty Night, no longer allows Hyperblooms to CRIT at this Moonsign level and buffs Lunar-Bloom damage instead, which she provides through her Hold Skill.
Rotation Of Hyperbloom Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Lauma's Burst into Skill (Hold). For the very first rotation, use her Skill (Tap) version instead.
2
Switch to Aino and use her Skill then Burst.
3
Swap to Yelan then use her Skill into Burst.
4
Switch to Flins. Use his Skill first, followed by a Normal Attack (x1), followed by another Skill then trigger his mini-Burst. Once his Skill is back up, use it again, followed by another proc of his mini-Burst. You can fill the downtime between Cooldowns and actions with a Normal Attack (x1).
5
Swap back to Lauma to use her Skill (Hold) when you have 3 Verdant Dew stacks.
6
Repeat from Step 1 once buffs expire.

Yelan is among the best fourth teammate option for a Freeze team consisting of Skirk, Escoffier and Furina. Her and Furina mutually benefit off of each other's presence on the team via increased Hydro Energy Particle generation and the activation of the Hydro Resonance, which buffs both of their personal damage outputs.
Rotation Of Freeze Yelan Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Furina's Skill into Burst.
2
Swap to Escoffier then use Skill followed by Burst.
3
Use Yelan's Skill, into Burst, then use one Normal Attack to apply more Hydro.
4
Switch to Skirk. Use her Elemental Skill (Tap), then press her Elemental Burst. Do Normal Attack (x5) into a dash cancel until her special state ends. Use a Charged Attack in between to absorb Void Rifts to prolong her special state.
5
Repeat from step 1
This team's rotation follows the familiar support first then On-Field DPS last scheme. Use the abilities of the supports with the longest durations first so the On-Field DPS can fully maximize the ability with the shortest duration by the time you swap into them.

This is the best Lunar-Charged team in terms of DPS potential and overall ability synergy. Flins' combos are likely to consist of Normal Attacks, which allows him to make full use of Yelan's Hydro application via her Elemental Burst. Ineffa and Sucrose are strong sources of Elemental Mastery to further increase the damage dealt by Flins, Ineffa herself and Lunar-Charged.
The biggest downside of this team is that Ineffa is the sole sustain unit, making other options less comfortable in practice.
Rotation Of Lunar-Charged Yelan Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Ineffa's Elemental Burst, then her Skill.
2
Use Yelan's Skill into Burst.
3
Use Sucrose's Skill. Burst is optional.
4
Swap to Flins to do his basic damage combo.
5
Repeat from step 1
Flins has a special mechanic wherein he has an Elemental Burst that costs less Energy (and it has no closeup animation as a result), which is a mechanic he shares with
Varesa
. Because of this, their rotations and combos are likely to be very similar.
The general rotation of this team should be to deploy all of the support abilities first (from longest duration to shortest duration), then swap into the On-Field DPS to deal the most damage.
Flins will take most of the field time, and is likely to be using his Elemental Skill enough times to fuel his discounted Burst (or mini-burst) several times until his support abilities expire.

Yelan and Xingqiu are an established pair that applies consistent Hydro to an enemy while contributing a decent chunk of single-target damage. This single-target DPS potential can be further enhanced by the addition of Hyperbloom, which is a homing Dendro-damage reaction created by applying Electro to Dendro Cores (Dendro plus Hydro).
Rotation Of Hyperbloom Yelan Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Nahida. Use Burst into Skill.
2
Swap into Xingqiu, then use his Skill into Burst.
3
Use Yelan's Skill, followed by her Burst.
4
Switch to Shinobu, then use her Skill. Burst is optional.
5
Swap back into Nahida, then use Normal Attacks to apply Dendro and to constantly trigger both Xingqiu and Yelan's Bursts to apply Hydro at the same time, which creates Dendro cores.
6
Repeat from step 1
Nahida acts as the On-Field character that uses her Normal Attacks to quickly generate Dendro cores. Because of her lack of a shield or interruption resistance on this team, it may be hard to pilot at first since Nahida is likely to get knocked around or interrupted. Though the team at its core is very simple, it can require slightly more skill than teams with a shielder, since you'd need to accurately time your dodges.

The core of a Hyperbloom team consists of a Dendro and Hydro character to create Dendro cores, and then an Electro character to apply Electro on the cores to trigger the reaction. In Yelan's case, you also want to use an On-Field character that can freely use Normal Attacks. She's also best paired with Xingqiu in some setups for more Dendro core generation and better single-target damage.

Although Yelan's DMG% buff from her Elemental Burst doesn't affect Bloom or Lunar-Bloom damage, she can still be used as a Hydro applicator in a Nilou Bloom team with or without Lauma (though Lauma teams will obviously deal more damage). Yelan is a good source of single-target damage in a comp that's otherwise entirely AoE-oriented.
Rotation Of Bloom Yelan Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Lauma's Elemental Skill (Hold). If this is the very first rotation, use the Tap version instead.
2
Swap to Nilou then use Skill (x3).
3
Use Baizhu's Elemental Skill into Burst.
4
Switch to Yelan then do an Elemental Skill (Tap or Hold) then use her Elemental Burst. You can use Normal Attack (x1) to quickly apply even more Hydro.
5
Swap to Lauma. Use her Elemental Burst into an Elemental Skill (Hold). You can wave in a Normal Attack in between each action. After using her Skill, you will be On-Fielding Lauma who'll drive the Bloom reactions with her Normal Attacks.
6
(Optional) While Yelan's Burst and Nilou's Skill are still up, you can quickly use Baizhu's Skill, then swap to Lauma and do more Normal Attacks.
7
Repeat from Step 1.
This Bloom team variation uses Lauma as the On-Field Driver who'll utilize her Normal Attacks to enable Yelan's Elemental Burst to apply Hydro and thus create more Lunar-Blooms. The single-target DPS potential of this team is fairly decent, but due to the Hydro application having less AoE, it may have a bit of trouble into multi-target content.

Character Overview
Pros
Decent single-target damage and range
Fluid field time that allows teammates to refresh abilities
Not reliant on reactions, allowing flexible teammate options
Richest and most elegant character in the game
Cons
Low Base ATK and multipliers compared to other on-field DPS units
Lack of good teammates that favor short rotations
Weaker performance in multi-target scenarios
Lack of supportive utility aside from her Geo Element, situational A4 Passive, and any supportive weapon passive
Playstyles
On-Field and Quickswap DPS
Ningguang is an on-field damage dealer with flexible field time, which allows you to switch characters whenever necessary to accommodate other party members. She casts her Elemental Skill and Burst when they’re available, then fills any gaps in rotations with her Normal and Charged Attacks.
It’s worth noting that her personal damage is lower than other popular damage dealers but still sufficient when played properly.
Support
Ningguang does not have much supporting capability in her base kit. Her A4 Passive gives a Geo DMG Bonus to characters that dash through her Jade Screen, and she generates 3.33 Geo Particles per Skill cast — note, for C2, that this particle generation has a 6s cooldown. Being Geo allows her to hold 4pc Archaic Petra, and being a Catalyst user allows her to use weapons such as TTDS and Starcaller’s Watch to further buff teammates. Generally, this playstyle is only viable when paired with Navia.
Talents
Talent Priority
Burst > Normal Attack ≥ Skill
All of Ningguang’s Combat Talents contribute meaningfully to her damage output, so it’s worthwhile to level up all three. Before C6 or in quickswap teams, prioritize Ningguang’s Elemental Burst over her Normal Attack Talent. At C6, prioritize her Elemental Burst and Normal Attack Talents equally. Her Elemental Skill, while the weakest part of her kit, can do meaningful damage in multi-target scenarios.

Ningguang’s Elemental Skill is her primary source of AoE damage, and her C2 enables her to cast it twice per rotation. The screen is created a fixed distance ahead of Ningguang and can block some ranged attacks.
This ability is one of her key contributors in a supportive role. It generates 3.33 Geo Particles when hitting an enemy and provides a Geo DMG Bonus to characters that dash through it. Her particle generation has a 6s cooldown, so casting two Elemental Skills in a row (with C2 or Sacrificial Fragments) would only generate one set of particles.
The Jade Screen will immediately shatter if obstructed when cast, which often happens against large bosses. There are methods that allow the Jade Screen to hit a large boss and generate Geo Particles without shattering. For more information on these methods, read the Talents section in the

4pc Nighttime Whispers in the Echoing Woods
4pc Nighttime Whispers in the Echoing Woods (Whispers) is Ningguang’s strongest option while shielded. She can easily maintain its passive through Crystallize shards, and even with reduced uptime, it’s still her best set.
4pc Marechaussee Hunter
4pc Marechaussee Hunter is a strong option when paired with Furina. Its 4pc effect does not stack while off-field, meaning Ningguang’s Elemental Skill cast would often have 0–1 stacks.
4pc Unfinished Reverie / 4pc Thundersoother / 4pc Lavawalker
4pc Unfinished Reverie is stronger than 4pc Thundersoother and 4pc Lavawalker. These artifact sets are good if you can consistently maintain a respective Burning, Electro, or Pyro aura on enemies.
4pc Emblem of Severed Fate
4pc Emblem of Severed Fate is good in quickswap teams if Ningguang needs the Energy to meet her ER requirements.
2pc Geo DMG Bonus + 2pc ATK%
This 2pc combination is decent across most team compositions and scenarios. 4pc Husk of Opulent Dreams performs similarly when Ningguang is paired with Bennett.
Other 2pc Mixed Sets
2pc Set Bonuses: Geo DMG Bonus, ATK%, Burst DMG Bonus, Energy Recharge, Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
The 2pc sets with the best substats generally deal the most damage. However, 2pc Noblesse is less desirable outside of quickswap teams, and artifact sets that provide Energy are less desirable when Ningguang’s ER requirements are met.
4pc Archaic Petra
4pc Archaic Petra can be Ningguang’s best artifact set in some teams, as she can usually pick up Crystallize shards effortlessly while on-field.

5★ Cashflow Supervision, Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds, Kagura’s Verity
Ningguang’s most consistent 5-star options. These weapons offer a high Base ATK to make up for Ningguang’s low Base ATK, as well as solid CRIT stats.
When paired with Furina, Cashflow Supervision
is Ningguang’s best weapon. Lost Prayer’s passive is inconsistent in a team where Ningguang has short field time. Kagura’s Verity performs well if Ningguang casts her Elemental Skill multiple times.
5★ Tome of the Eternal Flow
A good 5-star option when Ningguang is paired with Furina but outclassed by Cashflow Supervision. Due to its low Base ATK, it can perform worse than other options when you don’t have access to enough ATK stats.
5★ Memory of Dust, Skyward Atlas
Solid 5-star options that can outperform other weapons in teams that lack ATK buffs. Memory of Dust’s relative strength is conditional on high shield uptime.
5★ Surf’s Up, Tulaytullah’s Remembrance
Decent 5-star options purely because of their stats. Their passives have relatively little impact on Ningguang’s total damage output due to her poor Normal Attack scalings.
4★ Solar Pearl
The most consistent 4-star option. To utilize its passive, Normal Attack once before casting Ningguang’s other skills.
4★ The Widsith
A strong 4-star option, but its randomized buffs can lead to inconsistent damage.
4★ Flowing Purity
A good 4-star option if the Bond of Life can be cleared. Bennett will not heal the active character to clear the Bond of Life if their HP is above 70%. Pairing Bennett with Furina or using Xilonen would help trigger the passive consistently.
4★ Eye of Perception
A good 4-star option in multi-target scenarios.
4★ Oathsworn Eye
A good free-to-play option, but its passive makes it less desirable in teams where Ningguang has a lower ER requirement.
4★ Dodoco Tales
A fine 4-star option in teams where Ningguang receives more field time. To utilize its passive, execute one Charged Attack combo (e.g., N2C) before casting her other skills.

Teambuilding
Geo Resonance: Enduring Rock
Increases shield strength by 15%. Additionally, characters protected by a shield will have the following special characteristics: DMG dealt increased by 15%, dealing DMG to enemies will decrease their Geo RES by 20% for 15s.
Ningguang’s damage benefits significantly from Geo Resonance, which requires having at least two Geo characters in the party. As the damage buffs from Geo Resonance are conditional on shield uptime, this also forces her to make use of either a shielder or the Crystallize reaction.
Ningguang is relatively flexible with team compositions and can adapt her field time to accommodate many different buffers and off-field damage dealers. The longer her field time, the more her Normal Attack Talent would contribute to her damage.

Geo
Geo units enable Geo Resonance and often reduce Ningguang’s Energy requirements.
Xilonen
Xilonen is one of the best partners for Ningguang as she shreds Geo RES, provides a Geo DMG Bonus via 4pc Scroll, and can funnel Geo Particles. Ningguang is often paired with teammates that also contribute their personal damage, and Xilonen is likely to buff them as well.
Chiori
,
Albedo
Chiori and Albedo offer consistent Energy generation and good personal damage, with low field time requirements.
Chiori’s damage is higher than Albedo’s since Ningguang’s construct allows her to summon a second Tamoto doll. Albedo, on the other hand, has a longer-lasting Elemental Skill and fits easier on shorter rotations with Ningguang.
Kachina
Kachina has lower damage than Chiori and Albedo, but she makes up for it by being able to hold 4pc Scroll.
Her long 20s Elemental Skill cooldown limits her viability with Ningguang in some teams; however, at C2+, she can bypass this cooldown at the expense of lower Geo Particle generation.
Zhongli
Zhongli provides immense survivability through his shield, which also applies 20% Universal RES Shred to nearby enemies and guarantees uptime on the damage buffs from Geo Resonance.
Zhongli’s Stone Steles can also resonate with a nearby Jade Screen to deal some additional damage; however, carelessly positioned steles can obstruct Ningguang’s projectiles and Jade Screen creation.
Gorou
(C6)
At C6, Gorou offers excellent damage buffs to Ningguang and is especially potent when paired with Chiori or Albedo, who benefit from his DEF buffs. At lower Constellations, Gorou is not worthwhile for Ningguang teams.
Navia
,
Noelle
Navia and Noelle are both on-field Geo damage dealers with flexible field times that can fit Ningguang into their compositions. Ningguang can provide them with Geo Particles (especially at C2+) and can hold TTDS or deal solid damage. Although not reliable, her Jade Screen can also provide them with a Geo DMG Bonus.
Geo Traveler
Geo Traveler is
not recommended
with Ningguang. Their constructs often obstruct Ningguang’s projectiles and her Jade Screen placement, leading to inconsistent performance.
Pyro
Bennett
Bennett provides healing and immense ATK buffs from his Burst and 4pc Noblesse, which compensate for Ningguang’s glaringly low Base ATK. However, when played as the only Pyro character on a team, he requires either more field time or more Energy Recharge to fulfill his Energy needs.
Xiangling
Xiangling offers great AoE damage to compensate for Ningguang’s largely single-target damage. However, she has extremely high Energy needs that are only partially alleviated by pairing her with Bennett.
Mavuika
Mavuika is a strong AoE damage dealer that needs a Natlan character, like Xilonen, to charge her Elemental Burst. Performing Mavuika’s Charged Attack combo is likely to lead to better results than swapping to Ningguang to perform her own combos.
Emilie
While Emilie is not a Pyro unit, she can still maintain a Pyro aura when paired with Bennett if he refreshes Pyro mid-rotation. She deals great single-target damage and can fit into short rotations with Ningguang.
Hydro
Furina
Furina is a strong buffer and single-target damage dealer with the drawback of needing a healer to stack her DMG% buff. Ningguang is generally run with Bennett or Xilonen, making this condition a non-issue.
Yelan
Yelan provides good single-target off-field damage and a ramping DMG% buff. She pairs really well in a Double Hydro core with Furina or Xingqiu, reducing the reliance on Ningguang’s personal damage. She also fits into 18s rotations but she often wants to cast 2 Elemental Skills, increasing her rotations to 21s+.
Xingqiu
Xingqiu offers good single-target damage and some damage reduction that can help Ningguang’s survivability in teams relying on Crystallize shards.
Electro
Fischl
Fischl offers consistent particle generation and sizable single-target damage from off-field. Ningguang can consistently trigger Fischl’s A4 Passive to deal additional damage, and her flexible field time allows Fischl to maintain good Oz uptime.
Yae Miko
Yae offers good single-target damage through her Elemental Skill and Burst. She has high Energy needs and field time requirements, but Ningguang’s synergy with Fischl and flexible field time help alleviate these.
Beidou
Beidou provides solid damage when facing 2 enemies, compensating for Ningguang’s lower damage in multi-target scenarios. Her high Energy requirements often force her to be paired with Fischl, limiting her flexibility in team comps.
Kujou Sara
Sara offers a Flat ATK buff on a shorter cooldown than Bennett, which synergizes with Ningguang in teams with 12s rotations.

12s Rotation Teams
These teams execute 12s rotations. They place the most emphasis on Ningguang’s personal damage, so they benefit significantly from her Constellations.
This is not a comprehensive list of teams. The inclusion or exclusion of any given team does not necessarily reflect its power level.
Ningguang — Fischl — Furina / Kujou Sara — Xilonen
Fischl’s cooldowns allow Ningguang to perform two consecutive 12–13s rotations, making a 25s rotation. While Furina does not typically fit into 12s rotations, she can cast her abilities every other rotation as her Elemental Skill has a 30s duration. Sara buffs Ningguang with her Elemental Skill and buffs Fischl every other rotation with her Elemental Burst. Xilonen provides RES Shred and DMG% buffs to all party members while helping Ningguang charge her Elemental Burst. Simply casting characters’ abilities when they’re available is also a viable strategy

Ningguang — Kujou Sara / Fischl — Albedo — Zhongli
This team does not have a set rotation. Cast Albedo and Zhongli’s Elemental Skills at the start of the first rotation. For subsequent rotations, only swap to them to use their Elemental Skills if they have expired. Zhongli can be occasionally swapped to if an extra Favonius proc is needed. Sara uses her abilities when they’re available, while Fischl resets Oz when he expires.

20s+ Rotation Teams
These teams execute longer rotations to accommodate party members with longer cooldowns. Ningguang’s teammates tend to deal significant damage in these compositions, which makes her supportive roles more viable.
This is not a comprehensive list of teams. The inclusion or exclusion of any given team does not necessarily reflect its power level.
Ningguang — Yelan — Furina — Xilonen
Furina provides a significant DMG% buff to the team, Yelan provides a ramping DMG% buff to Ningguang and Xilonen provides RES Shred and a DMG% buff via 4pc Scroll. Due to an oversaturation of DMG% buffs with a lack of ATK buffs, Ningguang might prefer an ATK% Goblet. 4pc Archaic Petra is viable as Ningguang can keep a high uptime on its effect, and both Yelan and Furina contribute a good portion of the team’s damage.

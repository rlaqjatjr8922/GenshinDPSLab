Charlotte is a 4-star Cryo character that wields a Catalyst in Genshin Impact. She excels as an off-field Cryo support character who can provide great amounts of healing. Lore-wise, Charlotte is always looking for the next scoops to share with her readers as a reporter of The Steambird, Fontaine's famous news publication.

What Kind Of Character Is Charlotte?
Charlotte can inflict periodic Cryo to enemies and also act as the healer with Elemental Burst. At 0 Constellation, she can only heal with Elemental Burst so building Energy Recharge is important to this character.
Rate Charlotte!
Roles & Playstyle Of Charlotte and Her Strengths and Weaknesses
Cryo Sustain and Applicator
Charlotte's best use-case is as a healer for a team that can take advantage of her decent element application, which will almost always be in a Freeze-oriented team where another Cryo teammate can lower her Energy requirements.
Pros and Cons of Cryo Healer Charlotte
Pros
- Healing affects everyone in the party, not just the active character.
- Cryo application with Burst is decent.
Cons
- Personal damage from Skill and Burst are underwhelming.
- High energy cost.
- Provides no buffs for her teammates apart from what the effects of her equipped artifacts or weapon.

Focus On Her ER Requirements
A lot of Charlotte's best teams have her act as the main or secondary sustain, or as a Cryo applicator. Both require consistent and reliable usage of her Elemental Burst.
It's for this reason that she should generally be built to meet her Energy requirements beforehand, since this will let her best fulfill her role in a majority of team setups.

Team Rotation of Charlotte and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Charlotte
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Charlotte
1. Activate the abilities of the supports with longer ability durations than Charlotte's first.
2. Swap to Charlotte, then use her Skill (tap).
3. Deploy her Elemental Burst for the healing and Cryo application.
4. Use the abilities of the supports with shorter durations, then the On-Field DPS to perform their damage combo.
5. Repeat from Step 1 after all other support abilities expire or if you're done with the main DPS combo.
Charlotte plays like any other Off-Field Burst-dependent Support, using her Skill to generate Energy particles before quickly using her Burst. There are some rare cases where you can use her short-Hold Skill if enemies are spread apart, but her fully held Skill is rarely used due to the long cooldown.
Basics of Using Charlotte (Beginner Guide)
Cryo Healer with Decent Elemental Application
Charlotte is typically played as a sustain character for a team that can make use of the amount of Cryo she inflicts on enemies.
Both her elemental application and healing are tied to her Elemental Burst. This leads to Charlotte being a Burst-reliant character, which naturally makes her want a surplus of Energy Recharge on her artifacts.
Her Burst Is Her Most Important Ability
Charlotte's value as an Off-Field Support is concentrated in her Elemental Burst
since it provides teamwide healing and rapidly applies Cryo to nearby enemies within a certain area.
As stated before, her Burst being on the higher end of Elemental Energy Cost (80), coupled with her Off-Field nature makes Energy Recharge a priority stat for nearly all of Charlotte's use cases.
Note!
Charlotte's 4th Constellation restores 2 Energy whenever her Burst damages an enemy already marked by her Skill.
Skill Uses Charlotte's Camera to Deal Damage
Her Skill can be tapped or held to apply the 'Snappy Silhouette' mark on nearby enemies using her Kamera,  Monsieur Verite.
The Tap version applies the mark to a maximum of 5 targets in front of her, dealing Cryo damage at intervals.
Enter Composition Mode Through Her Hold Skill
The Hold version makes her go into Composition Mode where she can directly select the enemies to be marked.
Depending on how long you hold her Skill, this can either simply apply the same 'Snappy Silhouette' mark or a stronger 'Focused Impression' mark that deals more damage at the cost of increasing her Skill cooldown.
Teammate Nationality Can Give Her Increased Stats
One of Charlotte's passives, called 'Diversified Investigation', gives her more Healing Bonus for every other
Fontaine
character present in the party. Conversely, every non-Fontaine character will instead increase her Cryo DMG Bonus.
Monsieur Verite Has In-Game Camera Features
Charlotte can use her Skill to take actual pictures or screenshots within the game when she's not engaged in combat. To activate it, you need to equip the
Special Analysis Zoom Lens
and have Charlotte as the On-Field character. This lets her Skill to snap photos even while moving regardless if you're in Combat (or in Domains).

How To Use Charlotte's Skills & Passives
How To Play Charlotte
▼Cryo support that can heal the entire team.
▼Burst is her source of healing.
▼Mark from skill deals Cryo damage.
▼Passives are there to help her own abilities.
▼Charged Attack applies Pneuma.

Cryo Support That Can Heal the Entire Team
Basic Rotation Of Charlotte
1. Begin the rotation with the other supports first, ideally starting with the supports that have longer ability durations than Charlotte.
2. Swap to Charlotte. Use her Skill (tap), then her Burst.
3. Swap to the other supports that have shorter ability durations if there are any.
4. Deploy the On-Field DPS to deal the majority of the damage.
5. Repeat from Step 1 after finishing the On-Field DPS combo.
Charlotte's gameplay isn't very complex, especially since a lot of her power budget as a healer is focused on her Burst. Utilizing her Tap Skill to generate Energy before deploying her Burst should suffice in most cases.
Burst Is Her Source of Healing
Elemental Burst Summary
Icon
Elemental Burst Summary
Still Photo: Comprehensive Confirmation
Charlotte creates a Newsflash Field that deals AoE Cryo DMG and restores HP to nearby party members based on Charlotte's ATK.
Newsflash Field:
- Deals AoE Cryo DMG when created.
- Summons Monsieur Verite within the field.
- Monsieur Verite periodically deals Cryo DMG to opponents within the AoE.
- Continuously restores HP to the active character(s) inside the field based on Charlotte's ATK.
Charlotte's healing is tied to her 80 Energy cost Elemental Burst.
This is on the higher end of Energy costs and rightfully encourages you to apply various ways of meeting that cost, which includes a higher Energy Recharge stat on Charlotte and/or having other Cryo party members on the team that can help generate more particles.

Passives Are There to Help Her Own Abilities
Passive Descriptions
Icon
Ascension 1 Passive Description
Moment of Impact
When opponents marked by "Focused Impression" are defeated, Framing: Freezing Point Composition's CD will be decreased by 2s. This CD decrease can be triggered 4 times every 12s.
Icon
Ascension 2 Passive Description
Diversified Investigation
When the party contains 1/2/3 Fontainians other than herself, Charlotte gains a 5%/10%/15% Healing Bonus. When the party contains 1/2/3 non-Fontainians, Charlotte gains a 5%/10%/15% Cryo DMG Bonus.
While they still help increase the combat effectiveness of her kit, Charlotte's Ascension Passives don't really benefit others apart from herself. This makes her fall behind other supports whose passives increase their offensive utility through buffs or enemy debuffs.
Charged Attack Applies Pneuma
Normal Attack: Arkhe
Icon
Arkhe Description
Cool-Color Capture:
Arkhe: Pneuma
At certain intervals, upon using a Charged Attack, Charlotte will cause a Spiritbreath Thorn to descend and pierce opponents, dealing Pneuma-aligned AoE Cryo DMG.
Like all other Fontaine characters, one of Charlotte's abilities applies an Arkhe.
This is a special Fontaine feature or system that creates a blade-like construct that, when used against an opposing energy force, creates an explosion called an Annihilation reaction. It's primarily used for disabling or enabling overworld mechanisms (like puzzles) and enemies. It also deals a modest amount of damage, but not on the same level as
Nightsoul
mechanics.
There are two types of Arkhe: Pneuma, which is a light-colored energy, and Ousia, the dark-colored variant. Charlotte's Arkhe is Pneuma and is triggered whenever she uses her Charged Attack.
Equip a Quest Item to Take Pictures
If you equip and activate the
Special Analysis Zoom Lens
that you get from a World Quest in Fontaine, Charlotte can make use of her Kamera to take screenshots. You can do this while moving, in combat or in Domains. This special skill of hers should feel a little smoother to use than the in-game camera.
Should You Pull for Charlotte's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Aim for C4 if You Are Able To
Constellation Priority
C4 > C6 > C1
Charlotte's C4 Helps with Energy
Because Charlotte's teamwide healing is tied to her burst, you'll want her to have a means of gaining energy reliably to be able to use it as often as you can, which gives Charlotte's C4 significant value. Meanwhile, her C1 and C6 will work in tandem to upgrade her healing output.
Getting her to this constellation level is good and makes using her more comfortable.
Charlotte is a decent healer still even without any constellations.

Framing: Freezing Point Composition  (Elemental Skill)
Effect
Using the keen instincts of an ace reporter, Charlotte uses her photography skills to produce different effects based on whether the ability is Tapped or Held.
Press
Effect
Takes a snapshot using Monsieur Verite, dealing AoE Cryo DMG to opponents in front of her and applying Snappy Silhouette to a maximum of 5 opponents. During this effect's duration, it will deal Cryo DMG to affected opponents at intervals.
Hold
Effect
Popping Monsieur Verite's viewfinder open, Charlotte enters Composition Mode, and during this time, the viewfinder will expand as you hold until you reach a Finisher Frame state. In this state, Charlotte can move and change direction freely.
When the hold state ends, Monsieur Verite will deal Cryo DMG to all opponents locked on within the viewfinder, and apply the same Snappy Silhouette as Tap Mode does to them. If you unleash this ability only after reaching Finisher Frame, you will instead apply Focused Impression, which lasts longer and deals more DMG, although the Skill CD will be longer.

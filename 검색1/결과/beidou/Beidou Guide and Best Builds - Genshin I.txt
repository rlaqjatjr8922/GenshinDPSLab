Beidou Build
Beidou Guide and Best Builds
Beidou is a 4-Star Electro Claymore character, who excels in 2-target
fights while being able to provide Electro RES shred and a strong shield with
Constellations. Her skill is a parry, which can be great for a lot of mechanics
in a pinch. Her burst works really well for off-field DPS, which snapshots on
cast, meaning that you want to stack as many ATK % buffs into it. Her only
drawback is her Energy requirements are high, meaning she needs a lot of
Energy Recharge and an Electro Battery.
This guide will focus on Beidou's best
weapons, builds, artifacts, teambuilding and more!

Beidou's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Great multi-target damage.
Good supportive off-field skills especially with Constellations.
Great Parry skill with good damage if timed correctly.
Easy to get F2P weapons.
Weaknesses
Low single-target damage.
Forced to run an Electro battery together with her for Energy.
Still needs a lot of Energy Recharge even with a battery character.
1.
Beidou Build Guide, Best Artifacts, Weapons and Talent Priority
Beidou is mainly an Off-field DPS, either for raw damage or triggering Aggravate.
The builds are straightforward, as we build around her Burst. This means we want good
amounts of Energy Recharge, ATK %,  CRIT Rate and CRIT DMG, with EM also being a prio for
Aggravate.

Beidou's weapon choices are pretty broad with a F2P weapon,
a Battle Pass pass weapon, and an event weapon in her list. While
Wolf's Gravestone
is technically best on her you can make a case to
use any of the other weapons, especially
Serpent Spine
as there
could be other Claymore users who you will actively use on the field who
benefit more from Wolfs Gravestone. If you do use Serpent Spine, do remember
to pre stack it. The stacks have no duration, but will drop if you take
damage, which is perfect for Beidou as she will either be off-field or shielded,
meaning that she takes no damage. If you use
"Ultimate Overlord's Mega Magic Sword"
,
make sure to hunt down every Melusine world quests as well, to get full benefit
of the weapon's secondary effect.
Her stat scalings are pretty straightforward. Her burst costs 80 energy,
which means Energy Recharge is very important, so you can cast her burst as
often as possible. The amount you need will vary based on your team and
how often you use her skill. At worst, you will need about 162% Energy Recharge
and at best you will need about 120%. The latter is covered by the
Emblem of Severed Fate
and the rest can be covered through a few
rolls of Energy Recharge or the sands having it as the main stat. I would
recommend getting closer to 176%, as your artifact set will convert some of
this into Burst Damage, but also because having lower Energy Recharge makes
you reliant on perfectly executing her skill every single time.
Note, these Energy Recharge numbers are based on having
Fischl
in the party and having Oz on the field. You will need to aim for slightly more
Energy Recharge if you do not use Fischl.
Past Energy Recharge, the priority is the same as most DPS, CRIT Rate/DMG and
then ATK %. Some things to note with Beidou, is that any ATK % buff applied to
her will snapshot onto her burst. This can make ATK % be worth more or less
depending on your team setup.
3.
Combos and How to Play Beidou
Beidou is very easy to play. You play around her Burst and her Skill most
of the time, otherwise you just keep swapping around to continuously generate
Energy so she can Burst on cooldown. Whenever you attack while her burst is up,
it will trigger a chain lightning on the target. This will chain twice, 4 times
with
Bane of Evil
(C2). This chain can jump forth and back
between 2 targets if they are close enough. This makes it a very strong burst
for 2-target situations, but the damage will drop off a cliff if it is pure
single-target.
Beidou's Elemental Skill,
Tidecaller
, functions as a Parry.
Not many characters have this kind of skill, which can be a very valuable
defensive tool if used correctly. Every attack against you will increase the
base damage the skill does when you release the parry, capping at 2 attacks.
The
Retribution
(A1) will make it so you cap the damage if
you parry around the same time the attack comes in. The tooltip says at the
precise timing, but there is a small leeway when you activate the parry.
Outside of this, if the shield breaks, the parry stops and instantly fires.
4.
Beidou's Best Team Comps
Beidou's main team compositions typically has her as an off-Field DPS, where
you use another character as the on-field trigger with her burst. You will typically
see a second Electro in nearly all her builds, as Beidou pretty much requires it to
get enough Energy for her Burst.

Aggravate teams work by combining
Electro
and
Dendro
to create
Quicken
. Quicken is an Aura, where applying Dendro or
Electro to the target will create a new reaction, being
Spread
and
Aggravate
. This
applies a damage buff to the attack that applied Dendro/Electro. Aggravate is
created from applying Electro, and alleviates some of the issues between
Keqing
and Beidou. With this solved, Beidou can now support
Keqing with interruption RES. Past that, the rest of your team is some form of
support with a Dendro enabler so you have Quicken on your targets.
Kazuha
is usable here if you don't need healing, but want to be
able to bunch up a lot of targets.

cooldown.

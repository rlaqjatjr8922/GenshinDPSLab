Skirk’s kit focuses on her Seven-Phase Flash state, which buffs her Normal Attacks, grants Cryo infusion, and is the bulk of her DPS. Per her kit, she performs best when paired with Hydro and Cryo teammates, and Freeze teams are her strongest and most reliable playstyle,
especially for endgame modes
.
So let’s get into this Skirk guide and cover everything you need to know about getting the best Skirk build. This includes her best weapons, artifacts, teams, and gameplay tips.
Skirk Build Overview and Playstyle
Image Credit: HoYoverse
Skirk is a 5-star Cryo Sword character and is an on-field damage dealer. Her abilities are mainly focused on her Elemental Skill, which she can use to enter a special combat stance that gives her enhanced attacks and special buffs.

When using her Elemental Skill (Havoc: Warp), Skirk gains Serpent’s Subtlety and enters Seven-Phase Flash mode. In this state:
Her Normal, Charged, and Plunging attacks are converted into Cryo damage.
Her attacks gain larger AoE and improved interruption resistance.
She consumes Serpent’s Subtlety over time to maintain the stance.
Her Elemental Burst changes into a special version called
Havoc: Extinction
, boosting her Normal Attack damage.
This is the core part of her gameplay. You’ll be activating her Skill and doing continuous attack strings to maximize damage before the state ends.
Alternatively, Skirk can also be used as a quickswap Burst DPS. In this playstyle, she enters the field briefly to use her Elemental Skill (Hold version) and Elemental Burst. You then swap out to allow another DPS character to take the field.
While both styles are viable, the on-field DPS build is the most common and consistent, particularly in Freeze teams.
Skirk Talent Priority
Image Credit: HoYoverse
When leveling Skirk’s abilities, you’ll want to prioritize the talents that contribute the most to her damage.
Recommended priority:
Elemental Skill
Elemental Burst
Normal Attack
You’ll want to focus on her Elemental Skill first, since it’s how she enters the Seven-Phase Flash state. And since most of her rotation is based on her Elemental Skill, this is where most of her damage is dealt. Upgrading the Skill increases the damage of her enhanced attacks and overall DPS.
Her Elemental Burst should be leveled next. While it does not deal damage while she remains in Skill mode, it still has some pretty high scaling burst damage in certain rotations and quickswap teams.
And lastly, leveling her Normal Attack talent is less important because the attacks used during Seven-Phase Flash scale with her Elemental Skill instead.
Best Weapons for Skirk
Image Credit: HoYoverse
As is the case with almost all DPS characters, you want Skirk to have weapons with CRIT stats, ATK, or Normal Attack damage. As a sword user, she has several strong options to choose from.
Best Overall Weapon: Azurelight
Azurelight is Skirk’s signature sword and is naturally the best option for her.
Azurelight has high Base ATK and CRIT Rate stats while also having additional ATK and CRIT DMG through its passive. Since Skirk rarely relies on Elemental Energy, she can easily maintain the weapon’s passive bonuses.
Compared to other options, Azurelight can outperform alternatives by roughly 18–22%, making it her best-in-slot weapon.
Strong 5-Star Alternatives
Several other 5-star swords perform very well on Skirk:
Primordial Jade Cutter
Haran Geppaku Futsu
Mistsplitter Reforged
Absolution
Light of Foliar Incision
These weapons have CRIT stats that any DPS wants, and some even have useful passives that can improve Skirk’s damage output. While slightly weaker than Azurelight, they are more than usable if you already own them.
Best 4-Star and Free-to-Play Options
Skirk also has several competitive 4-star weapons.
Calamity of Eshu is one of the best free options, especially when Skirk is protected by a shield. Its passive increases Normal Attack damage and CRIT Rate, which synergizes well with her Skill-based playstyle.
Another excellent option is Finale of the Deep, a craftable weapon that provides ATK bonuses after using an Elemental Skill. This weapon performs particularly well in quickswap builds.
Other workable alternatives include:
The Black Sword
Kagotsurube Isshin
Moonweaver’s Dawn
Overall, Skirk is relatively friendly to free-to-play players since several 4-star weapons perform close to premium options.
Best Artifacts for Skirk
Image Credit: HoYoverse
Skirk primarily scales with ATK and CRIT stats, making her artifact builds fairly straightforward.
Recommended Main Stats
For most builds, the optimal main stats are:
Sands:
ATK%
Goblet:
Cryo DMG Bonus (or ATK% depending on buffs)
Circlet:
CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG
Substats should prioritize:
CRIT Rate
CRIT DMG
ATK%
Since Skirk does not rely on Energy for her Elemental Burst, Energy Recharge is unnecessary, which simplifies her stat requirements.
Best Artifact Set: Finale of the Deep Galleries
The 4-piece Finale of the Deep Galleries set is widely considered Skirk’s best overall artifact set.
Its passive increases both Normal Attack and Elemental Burst damage when the character has no Elemental Energy, which perfectly matches Skirk’s mechanics. This synergy makes it extremely consistent across multiple team compositions.
Other Strong Artifact Options
Other viable sets include:
Marechaussee Hunter (4-piece)
This set provides CRIT Rate and boosts Normal Attack damage when HP fluctuates. It performs particularly well in teams with Furina.
Blizzard Strayer (4-piece)
A classic Cryo DPS set that increases CRIT Rate against Frozen enemies. This set depends heavily on Freeze uptime and will struggle against enemies that cannot be frozen.
Best Teams for Skirk
Image Credit: HoYoverse
Skirk’s best teams are usually Freeze teams, as many of her passive effects rely on Hydro and Cryo teammates.
When built correctly, Freeze teams are where she does the most damage and they also have excellent crowd control.
Premium Freeze Team
The best version of her Freeze team includes:
Skirk – Furina – Escoffier – Shenhe
This team gives Skirk powerful buffs and consistent Hydro application.
Furina
supplies massive damage bonuses and off-field Hydro damage.
Escoffier
provides healing, Cryo/Hydro resistance shred, and additional damage.
Shenhe
boosts Cryo damage through her Icy Quill buff.
Alternative Freeze Teams
If you don’t have some of the premium supports, there are many alternatives and options to pick and choose from.
These include:
Skirk – Furina – Yelan – Escoffier
Skirk – Yelan – Xingqiu – Citlali
Skirk – Furina – Mika – Charlotte
Free-to-Play Team Options
If you’re a new player with very few options, then these characters can also do the job.
Skirk – Xingqiu – Rosaria – Dahlia
Skirk – Xingqiu – Kaeya – Barbara
While these teams deal less damage than the premium options, they still allow Skirk to function effectively.
Quickswap and Dual DPS Teams
Image Credit: HoYoverse
Skirk can also be used as a quickswap Burst DPS, pairing with another on-field damage dealer.
In this setup, Skirk briefly enters the field to unleash her Burst before swapping out.
Strong dual-carry partners include:
Neuvillette
– Excellent synergy with Furina and strong personal damage.
Ayaka
– High Cryo burst damage that fits well into rotations.
Wriothesley
– Flexible rotations and strong Cryo attacks.
Ganyu
– Consistent ranged damage and Cryo support.
These teams are more complex to execute but can deliver extremely high overall DPS.
Skirk is easily one of the strongest Cryo DPS characters in the game, particularly when paired with the right teammates. However, she also has some limitations. Her kit strongly favors Hydro and Cryo teammates, meaning she is less flexible compared to other DPS characters.
Even so, with the right team and build, you’ll easily clear endgame content like the Spiral Abyss in Genshin Impact.

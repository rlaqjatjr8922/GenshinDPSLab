1.
Yun Jin Support Role
Yun Jin is an off-field support with very little benefit outside of buffing
the Normal Attacks of whoever is on-field while her buff is active. This may
seem quite limiting, but it actually opens her up as an excellent option for
certain builds and characters. This buff is based on her DEF stat and
is amplified through her 4th Ascension so we do not have a high investment
cost to setting her up as a support.
The buff comes from her Elemental Burst
Cliffbreaker's Banner
and is the primary reason to add her to any team composition. The
buff lasts for 30 strikes so some characters are going to finish it
quickly, where as a Claymore user might finish slower.

Yun Jin's weapon selection was straightforward with her best 5-star weapon being
Engulfing Lightning
simply due to the bonus Energy Recharge 
available on the weapon, but Update 5.0 has added a new 4-star option with
Footprint of the Rainbow
. This new weapon offers a substantial increase to DEF 
with its weapon perk and a strong boost for DEF stacking with Yun Jin. Still equally 
good is the
Favonius Lance
due
to Energy Recharge and bonus particle generation. Lastly, we have included
the
Kitain Cross Spear
for its particle generation that can occur
off-field. With each weapon we pay little attention to its damage stats and
use this gear slot to add as much Energy Recharge as we can. Yun Jin's Energy
Recharge should be as high as possible when not paired with other
Geo
characters aiming for 220% - 250%. When paired with
another Geo character, 150 - 200% Energy Recharge is required.
As for her Artifacts, we can focus on a single set to get everything we want.
Husk of Opulent Dreams
is that set. Not only does it stack DEF as a
bonus, but its 4-piece bonus will increase that DEF bonus further while
off-field. This allows us to swap her onto the field, and use her burst with
the maximum benefit already to go. An argument could be made for using 2-piece
set bonuses rather than a 4-piece. We have given some of those options as
well.
2.
Combos and How to Play Yun Jin
There is very little to know about using Yun Jin as a pure support
character. We simply line up our buffs for our main DPS and then bring Yun Jin
on the field for her Elemental Burst
Cliffbreaker's Banner
.
Afterwards, swap back to your main DPS to use their buffed Normal Attacks.
There is not much more to know about her Elemental Burst.
Yun Jin's Elemental Skill,
Opening Flourish
, has a
couple of variations depending on how you use it.
A simple press unleashes a
Geo
attack.
Hold and charge the skill to Level 1, then release. This method generates
Energy for your team after being held for just over one second then
released.
Hold and charge to Level 2, then release. This is method will maximize your
particle generation and also provide a limited shield for her. However, it has
a longer casting time of 2.5 seconds. This can be used to block a strong
incoming attack you cannot get away from, but the high cast time takes away
from it being used as an emergency shield. You have to plan for it, which makes
it somewhat useless in intense fighting.
Parring an attack. Yun Jin's 1st Ascension passive grants her the ability
to instantly release a level 2 charge if she uses her Elemental Skill at the
exact moment she gets hit. Very much like you would parry an attack in an
action game. This is by far the best variation of her skill, but it takes some
skill and timing to perfect it, with some players saying it is impossible unless
you have a good connection.
Overall, if you simply focus on using her Elemental Burst and keep her
Energy Recharge as high as you can she can be a fantastic buff for characters
who focus on Normal attacks as their primary source of damage.

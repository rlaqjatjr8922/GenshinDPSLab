Wriothesley's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Constant Cryo use allowing frequent Freeze and Melt.
Most damage comes from enhanced Normal attack, so Wriothesley is less dependent on any cooldowns or energy.
Has low Energy requirements since he does not need to Burst every rotation.
Self-Heal ability.
Can cross water during exploration better than Kaeya or Chongyun.
Weaknesses
Needs a healer and a shielder, even when you have constellations.
Maintaining high HP values can be tricky.
Struggles in multi-target combat.
Needs his C1 to be able to use efficiently his Charged Attack and personal healing.

For Wriothesley's build, his best-in-slot weapon is his signature
Cashflow Supervision
.
It is perfectly fitting for Wriothesley as it plays with his HP fluctuations and increases his
Normal and Charged ATK DMG, which is what Wriothesley uses the most.
Tulaytullah's Remembrance
is also very good.
Flowing Purity
is the best F2P weapon as you can craft it and
it provides some good bonuses as well.
Wriothesley's build works best with the
Marechaussee Hunter
Artifact set fitting perfectly into his toolkit. It is also a set that a lot of
characters can use, so it is always worth farming it.
Wriothesley’s Energy Recharge requirements are in theory very low, unless you really want to
be able to use his Burst every rotation. Know that it is not necessary to Burst every rotation
with Wriothesley. However, if you still wish to do so, you will need around 200 %+ Energy
Recharge. If you are ok with Bursting only every few rotations, then 100 % energy regeneration
will be enough.
For Wriothesley’s build, focus on his upgrading his Normal Attack in priority,
as it is your primary source of damage.

3.
Wriothesley Combos and How to Play
Wriothesley's damage comes mostly from his
Forceful Fists of Frost
:
This attack allows him to deal up to 5
Cryo
DMG. Using your
Elemental skill in between will not reset the combo, however, if you wait too long to hit again it will
reset.
Forceful Fists of Frost
can be enhanced through
Wriothesley’s Elemental Skill
Icefang Rush
. The Elemental skill
also increases your resistance to interruption, and if Wriothesley’s HP is above 50%
it will:
Increase the Normal Attack DMG
When you hit with the enhanced attacks, Wriothesley will loose HP
To remedy the loss of HP, Wriothesley’s Charged Attack will be used. The Charged Attack
unleashes a small AoE attack, and thanks to the
There Shall Be a Plea for Justice
passive,
if you do the Charged attack when Wriothesley HP is under 60%, it will be enhanced as well:
Enhanced Charged Attacks do not consume stamina
Enhanced Charged Attacks deal more damage
Enhanced Charged Attacks heal you
You should use your Charged Attack when you are under
Icefang Rush
and under 60% HP in order to launch an extremely powerful charged attack that costs no stamina and
heals you back up.
His Elemental Burst on the other side is rather straight forward.
Darkgold Wolfbite
will activate Wriothesley’s Gauntlet and launch a
Cryo
attack in
front of him. This attack hits in AoE and will also deal Ousia DMG.
It is important to note that Wriothesley's C1 makes his rotation a lot smoother, as is will allow
Wriothesley to trigger his
There Shall Be a Plea for Justice
through normal attacks,
regarding of the amount of HP left.
The proper rotation for C0 Wriothesley is:
Icefang Rush
x3
Forceful Fists of Frost
1
Charged Attack
You can use his Burst when it's available. If you have C1+ Wriothesley you can use 5
Forceful Fists of Frost
each time instead of 3.
Be careful in Mono-Cryo teams as his Charged Attack Shatters Frozen enemies.

Wriothesley's Constellations
Wriothesley is perfectly viable at C0, however, his C1 adds real comfort to his gameplay,
as well as much higher damage. The rest of his Constellations are very good as well,
but you can do just fine at C1 max.

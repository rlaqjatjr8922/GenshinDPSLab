Building Gorou: It's All About the Team, Not Him
Here's something that trips up a lot of players—don't build Gorou for personal damage. Seriously.
Your main stats should be Energy Recharge Sands, DEF% Goblet, and here's the kicker—CRIT Rate Circlet. Yeah, you read that right. CRIT Rate often beats DEF% because Favonius particle generation provides way more team value than the minimal damage boost you'd get otherwise.
Substat priority goes like this: Energy Recharge to 220% first (non-negotiable), then CRIT Rate 30-50%, followed by DEF% and Flat DEF. His C4 healing scales with DEF, but let's be real—it can't carry your team as primary sustain, so energy utility wins every time.
Favonius Warbow: Your Best Friend
This weapon is Gorou's bread and butter. That 61.3% Energy Recharge at Level 90 is nice, but the particle generation? That's where the real value lies.
You need 30-50% CRIT Rate to make the passive reliable—60% activation chance at R1, scaling up to 100% at R5. I've tested this extensively, and Favonius consistently outperforms even 5-star alternatives like Elegy for the End. Why? Because EM and ATK buffs are basically useless for DEF-scaling teams.
Sure, Sacrificial Bow has that skill reset potential, and End of the Line works as an F2P option. But Favonius remains Best-in-Slot because that team-wide energy generation lets your teammates drop their Energy Recharge requirements by 20-30 percentage points. More room for damage stats? Yes, please.

Talents and Constellations: Where to Invest
Talent priority is straightforward: Elemental Skill > Elemental Burst > Normal Attack (just leave this at Level 1). Your skill determines that flat DEF bonus scaling—that's your bread and butter.
Now, constellations. C6 is the big one—that 40% Geo CRIT DMG bonus is comparable to Faruzan's C6 impact. Absolutely transformative.
C4 adds healing equal to 50% DEF every 1.5 seconds. It's valuable for Furina synergy but won't solo-sustain your team. C2 extends Burst duration by 3 seconds for better uptime. C1? Honestly, the cooldown reduction is unnecessary given natural 100% uptime.
Team Building: The 3-Geo Rule
Here's the golden template: DEF-scaling Geo DPS + Gorou + Geo sub-DPS + Geo shielder.
My go-to recommendation? Itto/Gorou/Albedo/Zhongli. The rotation flows beautifully: Zhongli Hold Skill for shield and resistance shred, Gorou Skill/Burst for buffs, Albedo Skill to snapshot those buffs, then Itto's Skill/Burst combo for the fireworks.
Don't have Albedo? Chiori works great as a replacement. Want to experiment? Furina with C4+ Gorou for healing support opens up interesting possibilities. F2P players can absolutely make this work with Noelle and Geo Traveler—just maintain that 3-Geo core.
Mastering the Rotation
The sequence matters more than you'd think. Elemental Burst when available, then Elemental Skill—this ensures your A1 passive (25% DEF buff) activates during your main damage window.
That 9-second General's Glory field is smart—it follows your active character and auto-collects Crystallize shards every 1.5 seconds. Free Geo Resonance benefits? Don't mind if I do.
With 220% Energy Recharge and Favonius, you should have Burst available every rotation. Pro tip: throw in quick Normal Attacks after character swaps for optimal buff timing, especially when snapshotting abilities like Albedo's Skill.
Common Mistakes (I See These All the Time)
Stop building for personal damage with CRIT DMG and Geo DMG Bonus over support stats. His A4 passive scaling is too low for meaningful impact—you're just handicapping your team.
Don't rely on C4 healing as primary sustain. It can't handle heavy damage or Corrosion mechanics. I've seen too many teams wipe because players overestimated this.
And please, never force Gorou into non-Geo teams. Without that 3-Geo requirement, his effectiveness drops off a cliff. He's not a flexible support—he's a specialist, and that's perfectly fine.

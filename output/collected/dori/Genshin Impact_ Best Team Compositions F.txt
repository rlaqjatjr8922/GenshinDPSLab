Dori
is one of the first few characters released in the game that hails from Sumeru in
Genshin Impact
. She lives in Port Ormos and when it comes to lore, she
wouldn't need anyone on her team except Mora
. Ironically, her skill set is the exact opposite, since she is a heavy support character.

She is a four-star Electro-wielding character who uses a Claymore. Her
kit is centered around supportive healing
, but it is possible to use her in other roles based on her passive talents and constellations. This allows for diverse choices when it comes to the best teams.
Damage Dealer Dori
Dori
Dendro Traveler
Kaedehara Kazuha
Bennett
Let's just get this out of the way — yes,
it is possible to deal big damage with Dori
, and this is the team you'll need for that.
Aggravate
is one of the
Dendro reactions
released with Sumeru in Version 3.0, and it is arguably one of the strongest reactions in the game.
This entire team composition and the build might require you to pull some money out of your wallet,
because Dori's sixth constellation is mandatory
.
The constellation
infuses your weapon with Electro for three seconds
after using an Elemental Skill. This allows you to deal extra Electro damage, making this build work.
Furthermore, you can use the
Thundering Fury artifact set's four-piece bonus
to bring your
Elemental Skill back online faster
. When Electro first reacts with Dendro, it creates Quicken, which can then lead to Aggravate.
When this reaction triggers, your damage for the next Electro attack is increased
.
The rotation for this team can be quite tricky because it's really important to
Swirl the correct element with Kazuha
. This is assuming that you'll be using a
four-piece Viridescent Venerer set
on him. Here's what your rotation should look like:
Start off your rotation with
Dori's Elemental Burst
, if you have it. This is so that you can apply Electro to the opponents. This isn't necessarily required if you have an Electro-infused enemy already.
Follow this up with
Kazuha's Elemental Skill and Burst
, making sure that it Swirls Electro. This will already start increasing the damage your Dori deals.
Finally, use Bennett's Elemental Skill and Burst, followed up by Dendro Traveler's. After everything is done,
switch back to Dori and use your Elemental Skill
before continuing to basic attack with her.
You will easily see Electro
damage of 50-60,000 per hit
if you execute this correctly. While you can quickly deplete the enemy's health, this build does require a lot more resource investment towards Dori, because
you'll have to level up her first talent as well
.

satisfying reactions
that it can execute. Electro-Charged happens when the element meets Hydro. This

, and it has
an added effect
if there are any
enemies nearby with Hydro
applied
to them as well.
Remember those times when you used an
Electro skill in water and then got zapped multiple times
, rendering your character in a stun-lock? That's the reaction we're looking for.
Xingqiu and Yelan are the Hydro characters with the best elemental application. Their Elemental Bursts allow you to
deal a lot of damage as it applies Hydro to the enemy
every couple of seconds. Moreover, having two Hydro characters gives you
Hydro resonance that increases every party member's HP by 25 percent
.
This benefits Dori a lot since her
healing from the Burst scales from her HP
. Furthermore, the rotation for this team also needs quite a bit of attention. Here's what it should look like:
Start off with Dori's Elemental Burst or Skill. It's
recommended to use your Skill here instead
of the Burst, since you will need that later for the Electro application.
Follow this up with
Sucrose's Elemental Burst
and Skill
to make sure you Swirl Electro. The rotation from this point will need to be very quick because her Burst doesn't last for too long.
Use both
Xingqiu's and Yelan's Elemental Bursts
and Skills in any order, before finally switching over to Dori again and using her
Elemental Burst
.
Once you have all the skills on the field, there's no particular character that you need to basic attack with. If you have Dori's sixth constellation
, you can use your Skill and basic attack with her to apply more Electro
. If not, you can try doing it with Sucrose to keep Swirling the elements.
The reason for Sucrose to be a part of this team is that she's a Catalyst user, and
she can Swirl elements even if she doesn't have her skills ready
. That is an important part of this composition.

History can confirm that whenever Pyro comes into the picture, destruction is inevitable.
Overload happens when Electro meets Pyro
, and you can imagine what effect that can have on your enemies. Apart from crowd-controlling them completely, this also deals more damage in a certain area.
If you're using this build against smaller enemies,
they will get thrown far away from you
. This is where Venti comes in, since
he can easily pull these enemies close together
once you have him upgraded to a decent level.
The rotation for this team follows the same process as the ones before, with the most important part being to remember to
Swirl Electro for more applications
. You can use Bennett's Burst before using Yoimiya's Skill to give her the
much-needed ATK buff
.
When you execute the entire combination, you'll see the enemies taking a ton of damage with no way for them to escape. If you're going to fight a larger enemy with this build, you can
switch Venti with Kazuha or Heizou
for more damage as well.
One last specialty with this build is the Pyro Resonance. Upon using two or more Pyro characters, you get an
ATK boost of 25 percent
, which can easily go a long way.
More Heal, More Damage
Dori
Xiao
Bennett
Sucrose
Dori's second constellation
is called
Special Franchise
, and it has a special effect when you spawn a Jinni with your Elemental Burst. When the Jinni heals a character, a shot is fired from the character's location that deals
50 percent of Dori's ATK as damage
.
This requires the character to get healed constantly, and
that is not possible most of the time
since characters easily reach full health. That's where Xiao comes in, since he constantly loses health when he's using his Elemental Burst.
This team uses the
Anemo Resonance
because it decreases cooldowns by five percent. This can help you get the
entire team's Burst back online faster,
which is very important in order to make this team work.
The rotation for this team is pretty simple:
Start with
Bennett's Elemental Skill and Burst, followed up by Dori's Elemental Skill and buff
. It doesn't matter which element you Swirl with this build, so this is okay.
Next, use both of
Sucrose's abilities, before switching to Xiao
and using his abilities. Since he will be fully healed, you won't drop below 70 percent health, which makes it so you will always get healed from Dori's Jinni — allowing the build to work.
While switching to XIao to use his abilities,
make sure you use his Elemental Skill twice before quickly triggering your Elemental Burst
. This is because you don't get elemental particles from his Skill if you use it during the Burst.

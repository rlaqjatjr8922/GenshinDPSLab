Candace is a 4-star Hydro character that wields a Polearm in Genshin Impact. She is an off-field Hydro damage dealer who can provide Hydro infusion for melee characters. Lore-wise, Candace serves as Aaru Village's protector and guardian against the dangers of the Great Red Sand.

Roles & Playstyle of Candace and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Sub-DPS and Support Candace
At lower constellations, Candace's role in a team can be very limited, since other characters can compete or are straight up better. She will typically take on a supportive Off-Field role that has a bit of damage on the side. At C6, her niche is more solidified thanks to her AoE Hydro application, which has a place in several different teams.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field DPS
Pros
- Very easy to play.
- Hydro infusion can be good with several team archetypes.
- When paired with another Hydro character, Candace activates Hydro Resonance that increases Max HP%. This is good for both Candace and other HP-scaling DPSes.
Cons
- Candace at lower cons is easily outclassed by other Hydro Off-Field characters due to her less powerful buffs and personal DPS.
- Candace's Off-Field Hydro application is non-existent until C6.
- C6 increases her usability and can give her a real place in teams, but getting C6 on a 4-Star can be an unrealistic goal due to the lack of a 4-star pity on banners.
Best Build for Candace
Candace is designed to mainly be an Off-Field Support with Sub-DPS capabilities. But she can also be used as an On-Field Driver or DPS depending on team comp. See below for the recommendations.

Team Rotation of Candace and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Candace
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Candace
1. Start with other supports to deploy their abilities.
2. Swap to Candace then use Elemental Skill.
3. Use Elemental Burst.
4. Swap to your On-Field DPS to do their full damage combo.
5. Return to step 1
Candace's durations are relatively short, so activating the support abilities with longer durations helps maximize Candace's.
This rotation can be adapted for her various off-field teams since she's fairly easy to play.
Basic Team Rotation for On-Field Candace
Basic Rotation For On-Field Candace in Bloom Team
1. Start with
Nilou
and then use her Elemental Skill x3.
2. Swap to the Dendro supports to apply Dendro to start triggering Blooms.
3. Swap to Candace then use her Elemental Skill.
4. Use her Elemental Burst then start Normal Attacking. You can also use her Elemental Skill whenever it comes off cooldown.
5. Return to step 1 after Nilou's ring or other support abilities expire.
This team has Candace take the field with her Elemental Burst infusing her Normal Attacks with Hydro. She builds full Elemental Mastery in this comp to ensure the Blooms she creates deal the most damage. She also takes advantage of
Nahida's
Elemental Mastery buff while standing inside her Burst field.
Basics Of Using Candace
Candace is an Off-Field Hydro Sub-DPS and Support who is mainly known to provide your On-Field Sword, Polearm and Claymore characters with a Hydro Infusion on their Normal and Charged Attacks with her Elemental Burst.
This Hydro Infusion can be overridden if the character already has a way of gaining one, like
Ayaka
with her Cryo Infusion after dashing or
Arlecchino's
Pyro Infusion after absorbing Blood-Debts. This Infusion can also be used by Candace herself if you don't swap into other characters after activating her Burst.
Best At Constellation 6
Candace's C6 enables her Elemental Burst to deal and apply constant Off-Field Hydro DMG in an AoE (area-of-effect). This by far her best Constellation and opens her up to even more team options. Notably Candace at C6 allows
Arlecchino
to trigger the Vape reaction on multiple (well-grouped) targets, making her one of the better options for Vape Arlecchino with this constellation.
Increases Elemental DMG From Normal Attacks
Apart from giving a Hydro Infusion, Candace's Elemental Burst also buffs the Elemental DMG dealt by the On-Field character's Normal Attacks. This compliments her synergies with
Arlecchino
and
Nilou's
Bloom team when you have Candace at C6.
Damage Buff Based on Her Max HP
Candace's Passive, Celestial Dome of Sand, boosts your On-Field character's damage by 0.5% for every 1,000 HP she has. This a pretty decent buff if you build her correctly, so having more HP% on her is good to maximize the buff.

How To Play Candace

Provides Hydro Infusions and Normal Attack Buffs
Basic Rotation Of Candace
1. Start with other supports with longer ability durations.
2. Swap to Candace then use her Elemental Skill to   generate Energy Particles
3. Use her Elemental Burst.
4. Swap your main On-Field character to do their usual combos.
5 .Repeat from number 1.
Candace's main gameplay is very straightforward. The duration of her Elemental Burst is noticeably short, and   might expire if you do her first, so you'll want to deploy Candace's abilities towards the end of your rotation before the On-Field DPS to get the most out of the Hydro Infusion and the Normal Attack buff.
Elemental Skill Has a Parry Mechanic
Candace's Elemental Skill functions similarly to
Beidou's
, where holding it causes Candace to go into a guarding stance with her shield. This stance allows her release a counterattack in response to an enemy's attack.
The tap version of her skill simply does damage to the enemies in front of her, which is often more ideal to do in a rotation due to the shorter animation.
Elemental Burst Is Her Most Important Ability
The main highlights of Candace's kit is found in her Elemental Burst.
Activating this infuses the On-Field character's Normal and Charged Attacks with Hydro, provided the character is a Sword, Polearm and Claymore user. This is also where her Normal Attack buff comes from. Her passive, Celestial Dome of Sand, that also boosts the On-Field character's DMG based on her HP, also triggers when her Burst is used.
Candace's Elemental Burst has a slightly high Energy Cost.
Because of this, you'll want to supply her enough Energy Recharge% to get it up consistently. Note that being able to use her Burst on demand as the only Hydro character in the team may prove difficult, so team composition can significantly raise her Energy Recharge% needs.
Can Benefit From Her Own Elemental Burst
Candace can take the field after using her Elemental Burst to make use of the Hydro Infusion.
This allows her to be the driver in a
Nilou
Bloom team, which is one of the better On-Field Candace teams. She can still be played as a traditional On-Field DPS with CRIT this way, though her performance in this setup is likely not going to meet the recent endgame standard.
C6 Enables Her to Have Off-Field Hydro Application
Candace's C6 is arguably her best one, and can almost be mandatory for her to find more use-cases as an Off-Field Hydro applicator.
With C6, her Elemental Burst will periodically radiate a wave of Hydro around the active character,
and can be very synergistic with very mobile characters like
Arlecchino
.

Constellation Priority
C6 > C1 > C2-C5
C6 is Ideal for More Hydro Application
Candace as a Hydro character starts to really shine at C6, since it increases her personal damage and allows her to inflict Hydro from an off-field position. This, in combination with the rest of her constellations, makes her fairly decent as either a support or a main damage dealer with decent Hydro application and a modest collection of teammate buffs on top of that.
If you want to get the most out of Candace, aim to get her to C6 if you have the means to do so.
Candace will still be usable prior to activating C6, however, and will function in her specific use cases regardless, just not at her best.

Effect
Raising her weapon on high, Candace calls upon a divine blessing that deals AoE Hydro DMG based on her Max HP and continuously confers the Prayer of the Crimson Crown on your active character.
Prayer of the Crimson Crown
Effect
This effect has the following properties:
• Characters deal increased Elemental DMG with their Normal Attacks.
• Whenever a character takes the field, they will unleash a rippling wave of water that deals Hydro DMG to nearby opponents. There is a limited number of waves that can be triggered in the duration of this skill.
• Sword, Claymore, and Polearm-wielding characters under this effect will obtain a Hydro Infusion.

How Collei Actually Works as Support
Her Dendro Application Game
Here’s what you need to know about Collei’s kit – her Elemental Skill hits twice (outgoing and return) with 1U Dendro each time, and there’s no Internal Cooldown to worry about. Pretty clean, right?
Her Burst is where things get interesting. It follows that standard 3-second ICD rule, giving you 3-4 solid Dendro applications over 6-9 seconds. Then there’s her A1 passive – the Sprout effect that deals 40% ATK as Dendro damage over 3 seconds. (Honestly, it’s not game-changing damage, but every bit helps.)
C2 changes the game entirely. Guarantees Sprout activation and extends it by 3 seconds when reactions happen. Suddenly, she’s much more consistent.
But let’s be real here – Collei isn’t your main DPS. Her Normal Attack multipliers are frankly terrible, so don’t even think about keeping her on-field longer than necessary. Your rotation should be simple: E > Q > swap out to catch those energy particles during the Burst animation.

Deepwood Memories: The Universal Choice
How It Actually Works
The 4-piece gives you 15% Dendro DMG Bonus (decent) plus 30% Dendro RES reduction for 8 seconds when your Skills or Bursts connect. Works completely off-field, which is perfect for Collei’s playstyle.
Here’s the kicker – it doesn’t stack between team members, so don’t put it on multiple characters. That’s just wasteful.
The resistance shred affects
everything
Dendro-related. Bloom damage, Hyperbloom, Burgeon base calculations – the whole package. Most enemies have 10% base Dendro resistance, so you’re actually pushing them into negative territory. That means more than the apparent 30% damage boost.
When Deepwood Shines
This set is absolutely essential when you’re running dedicated Dendro DPS like Tighnari or Alhaitham. Let Collei hold the support set while your main DPS goes full offense with their artifacts.
Hyperbloom teams? Deepwood amplifies that base Bloom damage before the Electro transformation even happens. And if Collei’s your only Dendro character? This isn’t even a choice – it’s mandatory.
Instructor Set: The Budget Alternative
EM Sharing Mechanics
4-piece Instructor gives you 80 EM from the 2-piece, then 120 team-wide EM for 8 seconds from the 4-piece effect. Catch? The equipped character needs to be on-field when triggering reactions.
The 8-second duration actually works well with most rotation cycles, but timing matters. Collei needs to apply Dendro to existing auras or have her Dendro consumed while she’s active.

Optimization Guidelines
Stat Priorities That Actually Matter
Energy Recharge comes first: 170-200% depending on your setup. 170% if you’re using Favonius, 180% in Double Dendro teams, 200% in other scenarios.
CRIT Rate: 30% minimum for Favonius users (you need those particles), 50%+ if you’re optimizing for damage.
Everything else: CRIT DMG > ATK% > EM
Main stats are straightforward – ER Sands until you hit requirements, then ATK% or EM based on team needs. Dendro DMG Goblet is standard, CRIT Rate or DMG Circlet for typical support builds.

Common Mistakes (Don’t Be That Player)
Neglecting Energy Recharge for offensive stats. Your Burst uptime becomes inconsistent, and suddenly Collei feels terrible to play.
Using multiple Deepwood holders. The resistance shred doesn’t stack – you’re literally wasting artifact potential.
Attempting on-field DPS builds. Collei’s Normal Attack multipliers are weak. Accept her role as an enabler.
Overvaluing personal damage while ignoring team synergy. This reduces overall effectiveness significantly.
Remember – build for team enablement when Collei won’t be triggering reactions herself.
FAQ
Which set provides better team damage?
Deepwood Memories wins most of the time through that 30% Dendro resistance shred benefiting all Dendro damage. Instructor only excels when another character already holds Deepwood and your damage comes from non-Dendro EM-scaling triggers.
What ER percentage should I target?
170-200% depending on team and weapon. 170% with Favonius, 180% Double Dendro, 200% other scenarios. Your artifact choice doesn’t significantly impact ER requirements.
ATK% vs EM Sands choice?
After meeting ER needs, both provide similar personal damage. Choose ATK% for consistent damage, EM when you’re frequently triggering reactions. The difference is minimal in pure support roles anyway.
Is Deepwood farming worth it over existing Instructor pieces?
Yes, absolutely. Universal applicability plus the shared domain with Gilded Dreams makes it a no-brainer. The Spire of Solitary Enlightenment provides excellent resin efficiency for multiple Dendro characters.
Best weapons for each set?
Identical priorities: Elegy for the End if you’ve got it, Favonius Warbow if you don’t. Both builds prioritize energy management and team support over personal damage.
Can Instructor work effectively at C0?
Yes, though C4 creates some overlap with its 60 EM team buff. Instructor’s 120 EM still provides significant value – you’re looking at 180 EM team-wide bonus when combined with C4.

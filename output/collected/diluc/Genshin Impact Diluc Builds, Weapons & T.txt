Diluc Gameplay
Diluc’s gameplay revolves around dealing sustained Pyro damage through his Normal Attacks, Elemental Skill, and Elemental Burst. His
Normal Attack
chain consists of four hits with solid multipliers, and his
Elemental Skill
allows him to perform up to three consecutive forward slashes. By using this Skill immediately after performing two Normal attacks, you are able to cancel the normal attack animation, leading to more damage over time.
Diluc’s
Elemental Burst
summons a phoenix that travels forward, dealing large AoE Pyro damage. It can launch smaller enemies and hit them multiple times during its travel, though bosses and heavier enemies will only be struck about two to three times due to their resistance to knockback. More importantly, casting his Burst grants Diluc a Pyro infusion, allowing his Normal Attacks to deal Pyro damage for a duration—one of the core aspects of maximizing his overall DPS.

Normal Attack - Tempered Sword
Level up his Normal Attack first, as it’s by far the most important talents in both his Plunge playstyle as well as his non-plunge teams. Since you will always be using his normal attacks during his field time, leveling this talent has the most consistent value.
Elemental Skill - Searing Onslaught
Level his Elemental skill next, as it plays a huge part in non-plunge teams. By timing your skill properly, you can cancel the animation of your normal attacks, resulting in higher damage
Elemental Burst – Dawn
Level his Elemental Burst last, even though it does contribute towards Diluc's total Damage, the initial burst hit can be a bit inconsistent with bigger enemies. What matters is that upon casting his elemental burst, Diluc’s normal attacks gain Pyro infusion, so level his normal attack talent first.

Diluc Best Team Compositions
Diluc's damage revolves around triggering Vape or Melt reactions, rather than focusing on his raw Pyro damage. Thus, you will always see him with some Cryo or Hydro character.
1. Diluc Vape Teams
In a regular Vape team, Diluc is able to use the applied hydro aura to increase his damage by a decent amount. You will often see him paired with other support characters, which buff him and the whole team.
Notes:
Your Vape teams will consist of Diluc, who is your main damage dealer
Hydro Sub dps/support (Xingqiu, Furina, Yelan), who applies Hydro for Diluc to vape and additionally buffs Diluc or deals DMG themself
Support who can shred resistance (Xilonen, Kazuha, Sucrose) to increase Diluc’s damage even more
ATK support for Diluc (almost always Bennett)

Diluc Constellations
As a standard banner character, Diluc’s Constellations are not really something you can wish for, but while his Constellations do increase his damage, the increase is quite small overall.
Constellation 1
His C1 simply increases the Damage dealt by Diluc to enemies with below half health by a small amount. This is something nice to have, but the effect also only applies to around 50% of the fight.
Constellation 2
It basically is another decent buff to Diluc’s Damage, as you gain up to 30% Attack just from taking Damage. Honestly, it isn’t worth intentionally getting hit to trigger its effect, but it makes it less punishing when the enemy boss pushes you around.
Constellation 4
While it sounds good on paper, trying to trigger its effect can have a negative impact on your total damage, as you don’t want to directly use your skill two times in a row, since its main use is to cancel your normal attack animation.
Constellation 6
Actually, a surprisingly good constellation, as it buffs your normal attacks by a very decent amount after casting your skill. Since you usually want to perform a skill between two normal attacks anyway, this constellation simply results in more damage without any consequences.
Conclusion

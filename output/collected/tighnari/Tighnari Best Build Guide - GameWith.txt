Tighnari is a 5-star Dendro character that wields a Bow in Genshin Impact. His gameplay focuses on being an off-field Dendro support and reaction enabler, using his Elemental Skill and Burst to consistently apply Dendro and enhance team damage through elemental reactions like Bloom and Burning. His Elemental Skill, "Vijnana-Phala Mine," places a mine that deals AoE Dendro damage when triggered and increases the party's Elemental Mastery for a short duration. His Elemental Burst, "Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft," fires a series of homing arrows that deal AoE Dendro damage, synergizing well with Hydro or Pyro characters for powerful reactions. Lore-wise, Tighnari is a knowledgeable Forest Watcher in Sumeru, known for his calm demeanor and deep care for the rainforest ecosystem.

What Kind of Character is Tighnari?
Tighnari is an on-field Dendro DPS whose main damage comes from fully charging his Charged Attack to release Wreathe Arrows,
which, along with the Clusterbloom Arrows that follow and his Elemental Burst, make up most of his personal DPS. He appreciates having teammates that will allow him to consistently trigger the Spread reaction to increase his damage even more.
Tighnari’s baseline performance is average at best,
though still usable in end-game content like the
Spiral Abyss
. His constellations improve his damage, with C6 making him competitive with C0
Alhaitham
. However, because the recent meta has not favored traditional Dendro reactions, Tighnari may still struggle into newer endgame content.

Roles & Playstyle of Tighnari and His Strengths and Weakness
On-Field DPS Tighnari
Tighnari is designed to be a On-Field Dendro DPS who excels at single-target content. He's best paired with supports that enable him to trigger the Spread or Hyperbloom reactions.
Pros and Cons of On-Field DPS Tighnari
Pros
- Tighnari's kit and gameplay at a baseline are easy to understand.
- Decent to strong single-target damage.
- Usable even without constellations, but gets better with them, which is possibly easier to get as a Standard Banner character.
Cons
- Aiming with a Bow can be tricky, especially on mobile.
- Getting interrupted while charging Wreathe Arrows leads to lower DPS.
- Will typically struggle into multiple targets.
- Has a bit of downtime after doing his usual Charged Attack combos.

Meet Tighnari's Energy Needs First
Tighnari's Elemental Burst is a decent amount of damage, and being able to use it every rotation leads to faster clears or better overall DPS.
Try to have Tighnari reach his required Energy Recharge stat (accounting for other Dendro teammates) before moving to Elemental Mastery and CRIT. Tighnari can still make use of any ATK% subs, but it shouldn't be a priority stat.
Additionally, Tighnari's Signature Weapon,
Hunter's Path
, scales well with more Elemental Mastery (EM), so getting more EM with this weapon equipped can lead to better results.

Team Rotation of Tighnari and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for On-Field DPS Tighnari
Basic Rotation For On-Field DPS Tighnari
1. Start with supports with longer ability durations, then swap to the supports with the shorter durations.
2. Switch to Tighnari then perform the first half of his combo.
3. Swap back into a support who can use the downtime.
4 Swap to Tighnari to repeat his combo.
5. Return to Step 1.
Tighnari's rotation will usually involve downtime between his skill usages, so having a support who can On-Field momentarily can help compensate.
This downtime is around 6 seconds, so you can use Normal Attacks or refresh key abilities before switching back to Tighnari.
Optimal Tighnari Combos
Easy Tighnari Combo
Tighnari Combo - Easy Version
Use Elemental Skill > Charge a Level 2 Charged Attack x3 > Use Elemental Burst
This combo is the easiest to execute that allows his Burst to get the EM bonus from his Keen Sight passive. Using the above combo can result in wasted Energy Particles from his Elemental Skill if his Burst is already full charged however. But overall, this should be a minor issue and can be overlooked in favor of ease of play.
Slightly More Difficult Tighnari Combo
Tighnari Combo - Skilled Version
Use Elemental Skill > Charge a Level 2 Charged Attack x1, then dash to cancel the animation> Use Elemental Burst > Charge a Level 2 Charged Attack x2
This combo requires a little bit more skilled involved since the timing to insert 2 Charged Attacks after using his Burst may be tight given the duration of his Skill. Still, it should allow you to catch the particles generated by his Skill and still have his Burst buffed by the EM bonus received from the Keen Sight passive.
Basics Of Using Tighnari
Tighnari is an On-Field Dendro DPS who primarily uses his enhanced Bow Charged Attacks, or Wreath Arrows, to deal damage.
His core gameplay involves aiming at the enemy, holding down his Charged Attack, then releasing at the precise moment so that it doesn't miss the target.
Wreathe Arrows Need to be Charged
Tighnari's main rotation requires you to spend a few seconds charging his Charged Attack (or holding down the Normal Attack button) to access his Wreathe Arrow, which will also release four Clusterbloom Arrows at the same time, thereby being the bulk of his damage.
Charging his Wreathe Arrow takes a bit of time by default because, like most Bow DPS characters that rely on Charged Attacks, Tighnari's Charged Attack first needs to go from Level 1 to 2, with his Wreathe Arrow being locked to the Level 2 Charge.
This will typically need you to hold the button down longer, but this is compensated by the sizeable DPS output in comparison to Level 1.
Elemental Skill Reduces Wreath Arrow Charging Time
Tighnari's Elemental Skill releases a cloud of Dendro smoke in a small area, which can confuse and taunt enemies inside the radius.
Aside from his Charged Attacks, Tighnari's Skill is his second most important ability since it reduces the next three Wreathe Arrows' charging time by 2.4 seconds.
You will typically always want to use this first before charging his Wreathe Arrows.
Elemental Burst Is Strong Single-Target Damage
Tighnari's Elemental Burst releases 6 Tanglevine Shafts unto an enemy.
These come out as Dendro arrows or projectiles, which is then followed by a second wave of Tanglevine Shafts that deal more damage than the first wave. Because the waves of projectiles have tracking properties, all twelve Tanglevine Shafts can focus a single enemy, which results into sizeable single-target DPS.
Better With a Shield
Tighnari can get interrupted by attacks when he enters aiming mode, which can get frustrating since it can stop you from dealing damage.
His Elemental Skill does add a bit of protection because of the taunt effect that stops enemies from attacking him directly, but the best way to mitigate this issue is to use Tighnari with an appropriate shield support if his team is able to slot them in.
It's also possible to play Tighnari without a shield due to the taunt from his Elemental Skill, but it will still take some skill to be able to use him this way.

Fully-Charge His Wreathe Arrows for the Best DPS
Most of Tighnari's damage comes from his Charged Attacks.
These Charged Attacks have two levels depending on how long you charge it. Level 1 fires a projectile that does Dendro damage. Level 2 turns the projectile into a Wreathe Arrow that has better damage, which is further supplemented by four Clusterbloom Arrows that will home in nearby targets.
Use Elemental Skill to Decrease Charging Time
Elemental Skill Summary
Vijnana-Phala Mine
Throws a Vijnana Stormheart forward, dealing AoE Dendro DMG.
- Creates a Vijnana-Khanda Field that generates illusions that taunt opponents and draw their attacks.
- Grants Vijnana Suffusion, which decreases the Wreathe Arrow charging time by 2.4 seconds.
└ This effect ends when Skill duration expires, or after Tighnari fires 3 Wreathe Arrows total.
Tighnari's Elemental Skill is an important part of his combo due to the utility, rather than the damage.
You always want to use his Skill before firing a Level 2 Charged Attack to lessen the time needed and to provide another layer of defense through the taunts, but it's important to note that the taunt from this Skill doesn't work on Bosses.
The charge time decrease having a limit of 3 Wreathe Arrows is where the issue with downtime comes from, since the cooldown of the Skill is 12 seconds and this is usually more than the total time required to release the 3 Wreathe Arrows. Using supports that can make use of this downtime to deal DPS by going on-field is a common teambuilding choice because of this.
Elemental Burst Has Strong Single-Target DPS
Tighnari's Elemental Burst shoots 6 projectiles called Tanglevine Shafts, with another wave of six following after.
If all 12 of these projectiles hit, the total can possibly contribute a generous amount of damage to a single target.
. It's essential for his personal damage to be able to use it consistently every rotation.
Elemental Mastery Increases His Damage
Passives Descriptions
Keen Sight
After Tighnari fires a Wreath Arrow, his Elemental Mastery is increased by 50 for 4s.
Scholarly Blade
For every point of Elemental Mastery Tighnari possesses, his Charged Attack and Fashioner's Tanglevine Shaft DMG are increased by 0.06%. The maximum DMG Bonus obtainable this way is 60%.
Based on his Ascension Passives, its easy to see that Tighnari is designed to benefit from Elemental Mastery (EM).
He gains more EM every time he fires a Wreathe Arrow, and his Scholarly Blade Passive increases his damage based on his EM. However, it's not necessary to forfeit a CRIT Rate or DMG Circlet for an Elemental Mastery one to get 1000 EM. Tighnari can get by just fine by having around 300-400 EM from his Artifacts, with more coming from Dendro Resonance or support buffs.
His EM scaling naturally makes the Spread reaction a strong teambuilding focus, since it increases the Dendro DMG of an attack that hits.
Shields Make His Gameplay More Comfortable
Tighnari doesn't get increased resistance to interruption while charging his Wreathe Arrows.
So like other Charged Attack Bow users, it'll feel more comfortable to play Tighnari with a shield to ensure he doesn't get knocked around while charging, which leads to a drop in DPS. It's possible to use Tighnari without a shield, especially in favor of more DPS, but this requires a bit of dodging skill and awareness of upcoming enemy attacks.
Should You Pull for Tighnari's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Prioritize Hunter's Path if You're Able To
Signature VS Constellations
Hunter's Path
> C6 > C4
Hunter's Path is a Decent Generalist Bow
Since Tighnari is now a standard character, getting constellation levels on him over obtaining the rateup character is a realistic scenario. Conversely, Hunter's Path may only appear during specific
Chronicled Wish
banners since the weapon was never added to the standard pool.
If you would like to invest in him directly, pulling for Hunter's Path may net you the more noticeable increase in damage overall, at least prior to getting Tighnari's C6 or even C4.
His Signature Weapon is also usable by other Bow characters that might appreciate the Elemental Mastery and Crit it provides.
Tighnari is still capable of dealing competitive damage in his best teams at C0 even without Hunter's Path, so while it'll be nice to have, neither his constellations nor his Signature Weapon are really necessary.

Other 5-star Crit Bows are Better Generalist Options
For Tighnari, a lot of the 5-star bows with Crit as the substat are very close in terms of performance to Hunter's Path.
This is also true for other damage-dealing bow characters. If you have any of these bows, such as Aqua Simulacra,
Polar Star
,
The First Great Magic
or even
Skyward Harp
, then the pull value of Hunter's Path decreases.
Tighnari's constellations start becoming a noticeable upgrade around C4, which peaks at C6.
In this case, whether or not you choose to go for Tighnari's constellations if he gets featured in a
Chronicled Wish
banner will mostly come down to preference and funds.

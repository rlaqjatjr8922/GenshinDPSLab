Clorinde's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Flexible character, that does not hoard on-field time
Centered around her Elemental skill instead of Burst for her Damage
Loads of free CRIT Rate in her kit, making her easier to build.
Weaknesses
Forced to do Electro-based Reactions to unlock a large portion of her damage.
Bond of Life mechanics can be punishing if you get hit by mechanics at the wrong time.
Large amount of damage locked behind her first and second constellations.
1.
Clorinde DPS Build
Clorinde plays around her Elemental skill converting her Normal Attacks for 7.5 seconds,
then most likely swapping out to setup another rotation. Her skill has a 16s cooldown,
so you would have ~7-8 seconds to setup Bursts and effects from other characters.

Clorinde's best weapon is her signature weapon
Absolution
. Not only is it a CRIT DMG weapon, it also tied for the
highest base Attack on Swords, on top of its effect increasing CRIT DMG even
further. Its effect is easy to maintain, as Clorinde has no issue generating
and clearing Bond of Life. Other options are also swords that
complements Clorinde's kit really well. Just be aware of
Haran Geppaku Futsu
, as this weapon gives CRIT Rate instead
of CRIT DMG. At lv90, it gives 33.08%, which means you will baseline have 75.08%
CRIT Rate. This drops the value of CRIT Rate drastically.
Mistsplitter Reforged
as a base, is the exact same as
Absolution
, and Mistsplitter's Effect is easy to maintain as long as you
use your Burst as it comes up.
The Black Sword
is an easy weapon to
pick up, as it is on the Battle Pass, which buffs your Normal Attacks and gives
you a small heal now and then, which will be converted to Bond of Life. This
weapon also gives CRIT Rate, although slightly less. Nevertheless, it
drops your need for CRIT Rate significantly. Between the 4 star weapons,
Calamity of Eshu
comes out on top if you can maintain a shield
on Clorinde. Otherwise Finale of the Deep takes over.
Fragment of Harmonic Whimsy
is her best artifact set,
however
Gladiator's Finale
is the easiest to passively farm as you
can get this from any world boss.
A note on
Thundering Fury
, on paper it seems to perform pretty well
especially in
Aggravate
comps, however
it extends your rotation by a significant
amount, as your skill comes off cooldown faster, which may reduce your
overall DPS.
With regards to stat priorities, Clorinde is a
Main DPS or an
Electro
Driver, with a heavy focus on ATK % and CRIT DMG,
as her kit gives her a lot of free CRIT Rate. Energy Recharge is not a bad
stat either, as her Burst does lean into her Skill by generating a lot of
Bond of Life instantly.
2.
Clorinde's Mechanics and Bond of Life
Clorinde's kit revolves around her Elemental Skill, where she will enter a new stance
called
Night Vigil
. This stance works akin to
Tartaglia
's, where her Normal
Attack changes and is converted to
Electro
DMG. However unlike Childe, the Cooldown of her skill is static.
During this period, her skill changes into a lunge attack.
While
Night Vigil
is up, Her Normal Attacks will generate 35% Bond of Life if the
Bond of Life equals to less than 100% of your health. Any healing received during this
will generate Bond of Life instead of healing as well. The Lunge attack has 3 stages
based on how much Bond of Life you have generated, where its damage increases with each stage.
The 1st stage only happens if you have no Bond of Life and only deals damage,
while the 2 other stages will convert the Bond of Life cleared, into healing.
All the DMG from her Skill is considered Normal Attack DMG, thus any %Skill DMG is avoided.
Her skill multipliers seems low on paper, however her 1st Ascension talent is the reason for this.
Whenever an
Electro-based
Reaction happens, Clorinde gains a buff that stacks up to 3 times.
This buff makes her
Electro
DMG from Normal Attacks and Elemental Burst deal additional damage
equal to 20% of her ATK (up to 1800). This is a base damage increase, meaning its
added onto the damage before any other % modifier.

4.3.
Clorinde's Constellations
Clorinde's Constellations are not essential to her playstyle, however they do add
a lot of damage, especially her first constellation. Generally speaking, if you are going
to roll for multiple copies or her weapon, roll for C1 first. It is more often a larger
upgrade over replacing
Finale of the Deep
. Her C2 is also close to the same,
so if you don't want to risk it, then rolling for C2 is also advisable. C3-5 are unfortunately
lukewarm at best in terms of damage.

Kazuha Gameplay
Kazuha’s gameplay centers around his unparalleled
Swirl
capabilities, which transform his fluid, plunging combat style into a massive elemental amplifier through his unique skill rotations. His kit primarily scales on his
Elemental Mastery
, and he enables high-impact elemental reaction compositions by providing constant crowd control and essential Elemental DMG bonuses for his team's strikes.
His
Elemental Skill
, Chihayaburu, pulls nearby enemies and objects in before launching him gracefully into the air. When Kazuha utilizes the "Hold" version of the skill, he unleashes a larger Anemo vacuum that deals increased damage and covers a wider area; otherwise, the "Press" version serves as his primary tool for quick grouping and energy generation. Following this skill, his next plunging attack is converted into
Midare Ranzan
, a special Anemo plunge that creates a miniature wind tunnel upon landing, perfectly setting the stage for his team's elemental onslaught.
When he drops his
Elemental Burst
, Kazuha Slash, he unleashes a single, powerful strike that creates a massive area-of-effect field called the Autumn Whirlwind. It creates a localized elemental zone that keeps the pressure on enemies as the field pulses with periodic Anemo damage. The best part is that this whirlwind can absorb Hydro, Pyro, Cryo, or Electro; when it does, it deals
additional
elemental damage of that type alongside the Anemo damage, allowing him to continuously trigger Swirl reactions and keep enemies vulnerable until the storm finally dissipates.
His
Passives
are all about making his rotation feel rewarding while scaling his support buffs off his high Elemental Mastery. One of them triggers an elemental absorption during his skill, causing his Midare Ranzan plunge to deal an additional 200% of his ATK as damage of the absorbed element. Meanwhile, his other passive directly grants his entire party an Elemental DMG bonus for the
specific element that was Swirled
, scaling off his total Elemental Mastery, to ensure your elemental carries hit as hard as possible. It makes him super satisfying to play once you find your rhythm because you’re essentially turning him into a wind-wielding maestro who empowers his allies the more he controls the battlefield.
Kazuha Talent Priority
Kazuha
’s abilities mainly affect his personal damage. However, as a support character, his overall damage contribution in the team is usually relatively low, so you can
choose not to level up his talents at all
.
That said, if you want to maximize Kazuha’s personal damage, you can prioritize upgrading his talents in the following order:

This is Kazuha's "bread and butter" for off-field presence. It summons the
Autumn Whirlwind
that deals continuous Anemo DMG and absorbs a secondary element to deal additional damage. Maximizing this talent is essential to ensure his damage over time remains impactful while your main DPS takes the field.
Elemental Skill - Chihayaburu
As a support who frequently rotates in, you will constantly use his Anemo lift. This ability provides excellent immediate burst damage, vital crowd control, and generates the necessary energy to keep your team's rotation smooth. Prioritizing this talent makes sure his brief time on the field hits hard.

This is the biggest trap when building him! While you will rarely use his standard sword strikes on the ground, his special plunging attack (

not
his Elemental Skill. To ensure his signature Skill-into-Plunge combo deals maximum damage, you must level this talent alongside his Skill.

Kazuha Best Team Compositions
Kazuha is a premier Elemental Support and Sub-DPS, serving as the backbone for almost every high-end elemental team in Teyvat. His presence is most valuable when paired with allies who rely on
Pyro, Hydro, Electro, or Cryo
damage, as his ability to double-swirl and provide massive Elemental DMG bonuses allows him to grant the entire team staggering damage increases.
Kazuha International & Vaporize Teams
The "International" team remains the gold standard for Kazuha, utilizing his grouping and double-swirl mechanics to amplify both Hydro and Pyro damage simultaneously. By double-swirling the Hydro from Tartaglia and the Pyro from Bennett, Kazuha provides a massive Elemental DMG bonus and Resistance Shred for both elements. This ensures that the team’s reactions deal maximum damage while significantly boosting the personal damage output of every member. In these rotations, Kazuha acts as the glue that keeps enemies tightly grouped while maximizing the reaction multipliers of his teammates.

Kazuha Freeze & Mono-Element Teams
In Freeze compositions, Kazuha is indispensable for keeping frozen enemies clustered together, allowing your Main DPS to land their entire burst window effortlessly. In these teams, Kazuha ensures that enemies stay "locked" in place while maximizing the blizzard's damage.

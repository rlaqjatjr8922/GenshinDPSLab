Yaoyao is a 4-star Dendro character that wields a Polearm in Genshin Impact. Her gameplay focuses on being a reliable healer and off-field Dendro support, offering both survivability and consistent elemental application. Her Elemental Skill, "Raphanus Sky Cluster," summons "Yuegui," a mechanical bunny that throws "White Jade Radishes" to heal allies or deal Dendro damage to nearby enemies. Her Elemental Burst, "Moonjade Descent," summons a larger "Yuegui" that follows her around, rapidly throwing Radishes that heal and apply AoE Dendro damage while increasing her movement speed and Dendro resistance. Lore-wise, Yaoyao is a kind-hearted disciple of Madame Ping and a junior under Ganyu and Xiangling. Cherished by many in Liyue for her sweet nature and responsible demeanor despite her young age.

What Kind of Character is Yaoyao?
Yaoyao is a typically played as an
Off-Field
Dendro Support or Healer who summons Yuegui, a stuffed rabbit that throws radishes, using her Elemental Skill.
The radishes explode on contact and can heal allies or deal damage to nearby enemies within range.
Yaoyao performs decently in her role as a Dendro Healer for certain Dendro teams, but her slot in teams beyond that can be questionable. There are better options for non-Dendro based teams, and the meta has increasingly become less friendly to these Dendro teams overall. Because of this,
Yaoyao has seen a dip in usage for endgame content, but she is still a good option for Free-to-Play Travelers since she doesn't need constellations to be good at what she does.

Roles & Playstyle of Yaoyao and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Support Yaoyao
Yaoyao's kit is very much oriented towards healing, but she can also be good into teams that appreciate her AoE Dendro application. She will typically be played in Dendro teams as the primary healer and as the holder of the
Deepwood Memories
set for the Dendro RES shred.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field Support Yaoyao
Pros
- Easy to play.
- Decent to strong healing.
- Applies Dendro in an area, making her good into multiple targets.
- Can hold the
Deepwood Memories
in Dendro teams.
Cons
- Low self-damage.
- Doesn't provide other utility beyond her healing and her Artifact set buff.
- Her teamwide healing needs her to be On-Field, which may be inconvenient since those seconds could be used by your main On-Field DPS instead.
- Her On-Field mode after activating her Burst can be frustrating to play since she doesn't get increased resistance to being Interrupted, allowing Yaoyao to get knocked around by strong enemy attacks.

Yaoyao is mainly an Off-Field Healing Support. But she can also be used as an On-Field Driver depending on team comp. See below for the recommendations.

Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Yaoyao
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Yaoyao
1. Start with supports with longer ability durations.
2. Swap to Yaoyao then use Elemental Skill.
3. Swap to other supports with shorter ability durations than Yaoyao if there are any.
4. Swap to your On-Field DPS to do their full damage combo.
5. Use Yaoyao's Elemental Burst if you need stronger or teamwide healing.
6. Return to step 1
Yaoyao wants to be deployed towards the end of the rotation since her Elemental Skill only has a 10 second duration.
You also want to do this to maximize the Dendro RES shred from
Deepwood Memories
by having the On-Field Dendro DPS act after her.

Basics Of Using Yaoyao
Yaoyao is an Off-Field Dendro Support that summons a stuffed rabbit named Yuegui using her Elemental Skill and Burst.
Yuegei tosses out radishes that explode to heal the active character and deal Dendro DMG to enemies within range.
Yuegui Alternates Between Damage-Focus and Heal-Focus
Yaoyao's rabbit heals and does damage depending on the HP of your team. If the nearby character has more than 70%, Yuegui will prioritize attacking enemies. If nearby characters have less than 70% HP remaining, Yuegei will focus on healing.
Elemental Burst Requires Yaoyao to be On-Field
Most of Yaoyao's abilities are centered around healing. Her Elemental Skill can quickly deploy Yuegui to heal the On-Field character. Here Elemental Burst is unique, however, since it forces you to briefly use Yaoyao as the active character to perform teamwide healing. She summons three Yueguis through her Burst, and the combined healing from all of them is significant.
Healing Based on Her Max HP
Yaoyao wants to have a lot of HP to ensure her heals are substantial. Having high Max HP also raises her survivability when you need to use her Elemental Burst to heal the whole team.

Decent Healer That Can Apply Dendro in an Area
Basic Rotation Of Yaoyao
1. Start with other supports with longer ability durations.
2. Swap to Yaoyao then use her Elemental Skill to   summon Yuegui for healing and Energy Particles
3. Swap to supports with shorter durations than Yaoyao.
4. Swap your main On-Field character to do their usual combos.
5. Use Yaoyao's Elemental Burst whenever you need teamwide healing.
5 .Repeat from number 1.
You typically don't need to perform a lot of actions on Yaoyao to achieve what she wants to do in a team.
Most of the time, using her Elemental Skill will suffice for healing and applying Dendro.
Her Elemental Burst is best reserved when you need a stronger heal or if multiple members of your party have low HP.
Elemental Skill Summons Yuegui
Yaoyao's Elemental Skill deploys a stuffed rabbit named Yuegui, who throws White Jade Radishes at enemies or allies. These radishes explode and will heal characters and deal Dendro DMG to enemies.
Yaoyao will focus on healing when active characters are less than 70% HP, but will focus on aiming at enemies when they are above 70% HP. If neither is met, then Yuegui will throw radishes into random areas.
Elemental Burst Gives Teamwide Healing
▲ In general, jumping with Yaoyao in this state is considered the best since it uses less stamina.
Yaoyao's Elemental Burst causes her to enter the Adeptal Legacy state where she has increased movement speed and resistance to Dendro DMG.
Her Burst summons three Yueguis that throw radishes that have increased damage and can heal all party members.
Because of the forced On-Fielding, its an option to only use Yaoyao's Burst when you need it for teamwide heals (unless you're using her with
Nilou
) instead of every rotation.
In addition, her passive, Starscatter, is also activated after using her Elemental Burst, which causes her throw radishes whenever she runs, dashes or sprints while in her Burst state. This amounts to a generous amount of Dendro application in tandem with the Yueguis.
Can be played On-Field with Nilou
Alongside healing, Yaoyao's Elemental Burst can apply a lot of Dendro.
Her increased resistance to Dendro DMG makes her the ideal On-Field driver for
Nilou
, whose Bountiful Blooms deal Dendro DMG that can damage nearby characters.
Her heals also further increase her survivability in this case.

Constellation Priority
C6 > C4 > C2
C6 is Ideal But Not Necessary
Yaoyao starts off at C0 with an already strong baseline in terms of healing, making all her constellations desirable, but not important for her to function well as a supportive character. C6 is her best constellation since it further boosts the healing in her Burst mode, which her specific teams are likely to appreciate.
C2 and C4 are good stopping points if you want to pull for Yaoyao specifically since it makes getting her Burst up a little easier, but going all the way to C6 can be considered if you have the means to do so.

Effect
Calls upon Yuegui: Throwing Mode, a special device created by a certain adeptus to help Yaoyao solve her problems. This skill will be used differently in Holding Mode.
Hold
Effect

Yuegui: Throwing Mode
Effect
Throws out White Jade Radishes that will explode upon hitting characters or opponents, dealing Dendro DMG to opponents within a certain AoE, and healing characters within that same AoE based on Yaoyao's Max HP. If a radish does not hit either an opponent or a character, the radish will remain where it is and explode on contact with a character or opponent, or will explode after its duration expires. Yuegui: Throwing Mode will choose its radish-throw targets.
・If all nearby characters have more than 70% HP remaining, then it will throw the radish at a nearby opponent.
・If nearby characters have 70% or less HP remaining, it will throw a radish at the character with the lowest HP percentage remaining. If no opponents exist nearby, Yuegui will throw White Jade Radishes at characters even if they all have more than 70% HP remaining.
A maximum of 2 instances Yuegui: Throwing Mode can exist at any one time.

Moonjade Descent
Effect
At the enjoinment of a certain adeptus, Yuegui's full potential can be unleashed in an emergency, dealing Dendro DMG to nearby opponents and entering an (in some sense) unsurpassed Adeptal Legacy state.
Adeptal Legacy
Effect
・White Jade Radishes generated will be changed to heal and deal DMG according to this skill. Explosions will heal all nearby party members, and the Dendro DMG that they deal will be viewed as Elemental Burst DMG instead.
・Summons Yuegui: Jumping Mode at intervals until their limit has been reached. The behavior of this version of Yuegui is the same as that of Yuegui: Throwing Mode in the Elemental Skill, Raphanus Sky Cluster. A maximum of 3 Yuegui: Jumping Mode can exist at any one time.
・Yaoyao's Movement SPD is increased by 15%.
・Yaoyao's Dendro RES will be increased.
The Adeptal Legacy state will end once Yaoyao is off-field, and all remaining Yuegui: Jumping Mode will be cleared once this state ends.

Who Is Yaoyao?
Madame Ping's Youngest Disciple
Yaoyao is the youngest of Madame Ping's (Streetward Rambler) disciples. She's well-loved by her master and is often doted on by her seniors. She's been training with her master since before she could read or write.
Born & Raised In The Mountains
Her parents set up their home in the mountains near the Qince Village. It was only after Madame Ping had taken her in did she finally live in Liyue Harbor.

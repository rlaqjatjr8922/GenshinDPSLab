Lynette is a 4-star Anemo character that wields a Sword in Genshin Impact. She is an agile character whose Elemental Skill allows for fast movement, and her Elemental Burst summons a cat that taunts enemies and deals Anemo damage, which can also absorb another element to deal additional corresponding damage. Lore-wise, Lynette is a somewhat reserved yet observant magician's assistant, who works closely with her flamboyant brother,
Lyney
.

What Kind of Character is Lynette?
Lynette is usually played as an
Off-Field
Anemo Support or Sub-DPS focused on triggering Swirl for damage and buffing.
Her Burst summons a Bogglecat Box that taunts, deals Anemo damage, and fires extra attacks based on the first element it swirls. Her Skill can be used for travel and applies her Arkhe energy, which Ousia.
Lynette’s place in the meta isn't very prevalent, offering limited buffs beyond her Artifact set and the small Attack boost from her Sophisticated Synergy passive. She’s often replaceable and outclassed by stronger Anemo supports.

Roles & Playstyle of Lynette and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Support Lynette
Lynette wants to be in a team where she can trigger the Swirl reaction on enemies reliably through her Elemental Burst.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field Support Lynette
Pros
- Easy to play.
- Good with characters that can benefit from the
Viridescent Venerer
set buff.
- Hold Skill is useful for exploration.
Cons
- Not enough damage overall to justify her place in a team.
- No grouping unlike other Anemo supports.
- No teams have her as the best option for her role.

Team Rotation of Lynette and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Lynette
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Lynette
1. Start with supports with longer ability durations. If the DPS requires you to start the rotation with their Skill (like
Arlecchino
), then feel free to do so.
2. Swap to Lynette then use Elemental Skill.
3.  Use Elemental Burst
4. Swap to other supports with shorter ability durations than Lynette if there are any.
5. Swap to your On-Field DPS to do their full damage combo.
6. Return to step 1.
Lynette has a slightly short duration, making her ideal position in the rotation to be before the On-Field DPS but after other supports due to his longer durations and cooldowns
This is to maximize the total duration of her Elemental Burst.
For On-Field C6 Lynette, you simply deploy the support abilities first before swapping into Lynette, where she should perform an Elemental Skill into Elemental Burst. Since her Normal Attacks are infused with Anemo, you can either use her Normal Attacks or Plunge Attacks in a team with
Xianyun
.
Basics Of Using Lynette
Lynette is an Off-Field Anemo Support or Sub-DPS whose primary niche is Swirling elements.
Her Elemental Burst summons a Bogglecat Box which taunts enemies, deals Anemo damage, and can also  launch Hydro, Pyro, Cryo and Electro Vivid Shots depending on the Element she swirled.
Elemental Skill Has Two Versions
Lynette's Elemental Skill can be tapped or held.
The tap version causes her to perform a Enigma Thrust that deals Anemo damage while healing her. The hold version causes her to turn invisible while rapidly dashing forward in a direction, marking nearby enemies with a Shadowsign. After she finishes dashing, Lynette immediately does an Enigma Thrust on marked enemies.
Lynette's Arkhe Is Ousia
As a Fontaine character, Lynette has an Arkhe.
An Arkhe is a unique mechanic found in Fontaine and Fontaine characters where certain attacks produce either a Spiritbreath Thorn that deals Pneuma-aligned damage, or a Surging Blade that does Ousia-aligned damage. An Annihilation reaction is triggered when a Pneuma or Ousia Arkhe interact with each other, which is notably used to temporarily disable specific enemies in Fontaine.
Elemental Burst Triggers Swirl
As mentioned,
the Bogglecat Box produced by Lynette's Elemental Burst shoots out projectiles that deal Anemo damage to enemies within its range.
On top of this, the Swirl reaction is triggered on enemies with Hydro, Pyro, Cryo or Electro applied to them after getting hit, which causes the Bogglecat Box to release Vivid Shots of the same element. The Vivid Shots will take on the first element Swirled until its duration ends.
Lynette's Burst is her main source of damage as well as her primary utility,
since it allows her to apply the
Viridescent Venerer
set buff that reduces an enemy's resistance to the Swirled element.

1. Start other supports with shorter ability durations and use their Elemental Skills or Bursts. Apply an element if possible.
2. Swap to Lynette then use Elemental Skill.
3. Use her Elemental Burst
4 .Swap to your On-Field DPS to execute their damage combos
.5. Repeat from Step 1.
Lynette ideally wants to start swirling with her Elemental Burst as soon as possible, so having other party members apply elements for her beforehand is recommended.
If there are party members who have shorter durations, you can opt to use Lynette first.
Elemental Skill Applies Ousia and Can Be Used for Travel
Lynette's Elemental Skill deals Anemo damage and propels her forward to an enemy.
But you can use her hold Skill as an exploration tool, since it functions similarly to
Yelan's
skill.
Some Clockwork Meka can be disabled after being hit by a Pneuma or Ousia-aligned attack. Lynette's Elemental Skill applies Ousia, which is effective against specific Meka like the
Icewind Suite:
Nemesis of Coppelius.
Elemental Burst Is Her Main Source of Damage
Lynette's Elemental Burst is her most important damage ability.
If you want to get the most out of her, building around her Burst is required.
This means she needs to have enough Energy Recharge% to use it every rotation.
C6 Allows Her to Become an On-Field DPS
Lynette's C6 infuses her Normal Attacks with Anemo, allowing her to be a convincing On-Field DPS with some investment. She can be played as a traditional DPS with more offensive stats like CRIT Rate or DMG, or as a Hyperbloom driver who triggers the reaction by Swirling Electro with her infused Normal Attacks and Elemental Burst. She will build full Elemental Mastery in that case.
Should You Pull for Lynette's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
C6 Opens a Different Playstyle
Constellation Priority
C6 > C4 > C1
Lynette can be played as an on-field DPS character at C6, but she is still a viable Anemo support character at other constellation levels prior to C6. C4 unlocks another stack for her Skill, which helps in generating energy for her burst and in overworld exploration.
These constellations aren't required, but any Lynette constellation level will be a welcome increase to her damage and supportive capabilities.

Who Is Lynette?
A Magician's Assistant
Lynette is the quiet and reserved assistant to the magician Lyney. Unlike her twin brother who prefers the spotlight, Lynette would rather let him shine instead.
A Member Of The House Of Hearth
Lynette is part of the House of Hearth along with her siblings Lyney and Freminet, an orphanage run by the Fatui. Although friendly to the Traveler, her loyalty lies with her father, Arlecchino, a Fatui Harbinger.
Works Secretly For The Fatui
Lynette works in the shadows as part of the Fatui. She collects any information that may be needed by her father and relays it back to her.

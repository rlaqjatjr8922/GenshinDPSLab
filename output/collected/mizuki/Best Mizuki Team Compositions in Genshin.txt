Known as one of the owners of the Aisa Bathhouse, Yumemizuki Mizuki (Mizuki for short) is an interesting addition to the
Genshin Impact
roster. As an Inazuma character arriving after the introduction of many Natlan characters in prior patches, her release is particularly notable. While she is making her debut in a limited banner, similar to Dehya or Tighnari, she will later be added to the permanent banner after her limited run ends. This allows players more opportunities to obtain her over time. Given her accessibility, many players will likely want to experiment with her in different team compositions and test new synergies—especially in
content like Imaginarium Theater
, which requires diverse team-building across different elements.

Mizuki is primarily an on-field Swirl driver who scales with Elemental Mastery. She also has the potential to provide some healing in certain conditions; however, unless players have her fourth constellation, her healing is only triggered when characters have low HP. This makes her unreliable as a solo healer in some team compositions. Nevertheless, as an excellent candidate for the
Viridescent Venerer artifact set
, Mizuki can significantly boost the effectiveness of Electro, Hydro, Cryo, or Pyro characters and their related reactions in
Genshin Impact
.
Since her kit requires her to stay on-field to maximize her utility, she works best when paired with characters who have strong off-field abilities and synergies. Many of the characters mentioned below can be interchanged between different teams (particularly Hydro and Electro units), but here are some of her best team compositions, showcasing a variety of characters rather than repeating the same ones across different setups.
Mizuki + Furina + Ororon + Sigewinne
One of the best team compositions for Swirl-focused
setups is Tazer comps
, as Mizuki can Swirl multiple elements simultaneously, making consistent Electro and Hydro application highly effective. Ororon stands out as one of the strongest candidates for this team, particularly at higher constellations, as he is designed for Electro-Charged teams. Another staple of this composition is Furina, who serves as an exceptional Hydro off-field sub-DPS while also buffing the team’s overall damage output.
Since Furina drains the team's HP, some players might find Mizuki’s snacks to be unreliable for dealing damage as they will be used for healing instead when the character is on low HP. However, if healing is properly managed, these snacks can be prioritized for Swirl damage instead of support. This is where Sigewinne becomes the optimal fourth member. Not only does she provide consistent healing to counter Furina’s HP drain, but she also offers Hydro application and Elemental Skill DMG bonuses for characters like Furina or even Ororon. With a dedicated healer in the team, Mizuki’s full damage potential can be unleashed, as her snacks can be used offensively rather than defensively.
For those who don’t have Sigewinne, other characters like Kokomi or Kuki Shinobu can provide similar healing utility, while still maintaining strong Hydro or Electro application. Ultimately, any off-field Electro, Hydro, or even Anemo unit with good elemental uptime can work in this slot, making this team somewhat flexible in terms of final spot for a character.

Since Nahida can maintain Dendro application on her own, it’s recommended to pair Mizuki with two Electro sub-DPS units, namely Yae Miko and Fischl. This duo ensures consistent Electro uptime, with both characters capable of applying Electro simultaneously and dealing significant damage.
It’s important to note that this team lacks a dedicated healer, especially if Mizuki is not C4, meaning players must be cautious to avoid unnecessary damage. Luckily, unlike Bloom-related reactions, Aggravate won't be dealing DMG to the nearby characters. If additional sustain is required, Kuki Shinobu can replace either Yae Miko or Fischl, providing healing while still maintaining Electro application. However, in this case, Mizuki will need to stay closer to enemies to fully benefit from Kuki’s Electro application range.

Mizuki + Mavuika + Citlali + Bennett
Mavuika and Citlali have quickly become one of the
most synergistic duos in recent updates
, offering a fresh take on Melt reactions with massive damage potential, even with relatively low investment. When paired with Bennett, Mavuika's damage can easily reach millions, making this trio one of the most meta-relevant choices in current challenging content.
Despite being a solid core, the team has a flexible fourth slot, which can be filled by buffers like Xilonen or Furina for additional damage amplification, or Anemo characters that can effectively utilize Viridescent Venerer to shred resistances.
While Mizuki may not be the most optimal choice for this trio, this meta-defining team remains one of the best options for Mizuki’s team compositions. Although Mavuika generally performs better as an on-field unit, her off-field capabilities allow for some rotation flexibility. In this setup, Mavuika can primarily use her Elemental Burst whenever it's available (which may take longer due to fewer Natlan characters in the team) and focus on off-field Elemental Skill usage, rather than relying on her on-field charged attacks.

Mizuki + Xingqiu + Lisa + Xiangling
For players looking for a fully F2P-friendly team that doesn't rely on premium characters, Mizuki can be paired with a strong set of 4-star options. This setup focuses on applying and swirling multiple elements, making Mizuki a "rainbow Swirl" driver, somewhat similar to Chasca, though Mizuki cannot convert her attacks into a specific element.
For Hydro application, Xingqiu is an excellent choice, providing coordinated attacks and damage mitigation. Lisa (or alternatively Ororon/Fischl) can serve as the Electro off-field sub-DPS, while Xiangling (or Pyro Traveler/Dehya) covers the Pyro slot.
An added bonus is that all of these characters, except Mizuki, have obtainable alternate skins through past flagship events, making this team a great way to showcase multiple event-exclusive outfits in a single composition.

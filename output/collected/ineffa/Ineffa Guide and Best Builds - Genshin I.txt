Ineffa Guide & Best Builds
Welcome to our Ineffa build guide for Genshin Impact.
Ineffa

Best Artifacts for Ineffa
Aubade of Morningstar and Moon
Aubade of Morningstar and Moon
2-Piece Set
: Increases Elemental Mastery by 80.
4-Piece Set
: When the equipping character is off-field, Lunar Reaction DMG is increased by 20%. When the party's Moonsign Level is at least Ascendant Gleam, Lunar Reaction DMG will be further increased by 40%. This effect will disappear after the equipping character is active for 3s.
This is the set to use if you want to maximize 
Ineffa's personal damage from off-field. However, in teams where she is a 
lower portion of the overall team damage, it can be better to use her on more 
supportive sets instead. Most Ineffa teams will have Ascendant Gleam, but if 
yours does not, then this set is not worth using.

Ineffa's Stat Priority
:
ATK % > EM
:
ATK % > EM
:
CRIT Rate / DMG > ATK %
Substats:
ER to requirements > CRIT Rate / DMG > ATK % > EM
Ineffa scales much better from ATK % than EM, so ATK % should be used over EM 
on the Sands and Goblet, unless she is also triggering a reaction such as 
Hyperbloom. Building ATK % on Ineffa is not only more damage than EM, but it also 
increases the strength of her Shield, and the size of her Elemental Mastery buff.
Ineffa does not benefit from Elemental DMG %, so a DMG % Goblet should never be 
used. On the substats, depending on how much value your on-fielder gains from 
Elemental Mastery, it can be worth getting ER substats to use Ineffa's Burst 
every rotation. However, ER can also be ignored entirely and just user her Burst 
every other rotation instead.

Ineffa's Talent Priority
Talent Priority:
Cleaning Mode: Carrier Frequency
>
Supreme Instruction: Cyclonic Exterminator
Although most of Ineffa's damage comes from her Ascension 1 passive, 
it is still important to level her Skill, as it increases the strength of her
Shield. Ineffa's Burst is not a large part of her damage, so it doesn't need to
be fully leveled, but it is still worth getting to at least Level 6. 
Ineffa's Normal Attack talent doesn't need to be leveled at all, as it is not
an actively used part of her kit.
How to Play Ineffa
Ineffa's playstyle is casting her Skill and/or Burst, and then swapping to
the next character in the rotation. With her Skill, she will create her Shield, 
and summon Brigitta to deal AoE Electro damage every 2 seconds on a long 20
second duration. With Hydro on the team for the Lunar-Charged Reaction, Brigitta
will also deal direct extremely high direct Lunar-Charged Damage, which is
where the vast majority of her damage comes from. 
Casting Ineffa's Burst won't create the Shield, but will summon Brigitta
If Brigitta was already present, Ineffa's Burst will refresh its duration, 
allowing Brigitta to last even longer than 20 seconds over a full rotation.

With the exception of her C1, Ineffa's Constellations are all focused on 
improving her personal damage rather than giving her more supportive 
capabilities. As such, in teams where she's a smaller portion of the total
team damage, such as in a Flins team, Ineffa's Constellations are of lower
value. But the 50 Lunar-Charged DMG% from Ineffa's C1 is a team-wide buff, 
so on teams where Lunar-Charged is the majority of the teams damage, her C1
is strong and a great investment. After C1, C2 is the next best stopping point
unless you plan to go all the way to C6.
Investment Priority:
Weapon > Constellation 1 > Constellation 2

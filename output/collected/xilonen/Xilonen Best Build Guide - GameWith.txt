Xilonen is a 5-star Geo Character that wields a Sword in Genshin Impact. She is a support character that decreases the Elemental Resistance of Enemies, she need to use Normal attack to gain Nightsoul Points to match with her team's Elements to reduce the enemy's resistance based on her team's element. She can also be a Sub-DPS or a healer depending on the Team's element. Lore-wise, Xilonen is a Blacksmith of the Nanatzcayan, specially skilled at finding good balance between the heavy responsibilities of her job and living well.

What Kind of Character is Xilonen?
In battle, Xilonen is mainly played as an
Off-Field
Geo Support that provides both healing and Pyro, Hydro, Electro or Cryo Elemental Resistance (RES) shred for the party. In order to shred these resistances, Xilonen needs to completely fill her
Nightsoul
bar with Nightsoul points, which she can do by using her Skill before Normal Attacking twice.
Xilonen's slot in some meta teams have started to become contested following the release of specialized supports.
This can be seen in a Mavuika Melt team were Xilonen can be replaced by C6 (or lower)
Iansan
and produce better results, or in Mualani's team where her place is contested by
Mona
after finishing her
Witch's Homework
task. Regardless, she remains a flexible support due to her generalist abilities.

Elemental Damage Support and Healer Xilonen
Xilonen's best role is a Elemental Damage RES shredder and healer. Her access to Nightsoul allows her to hold Scroll set to buff Elemental Damage while her Skill and Passive shreds it. Her Burst is also great since it has powerful Single Target healing that can offset Furina's HP drain.
Pros and Cons of Support Xilonen
Pros
- Can shred up to 3 PECH Elements, allowing the whole team to deal greater damage.
- Access to Nightsoul allows her to hold Scroll set and use its effect to buff both Geo and PECH Element hit when she triggers Crystallize.
- Can be used to buff even Geo DPS since her Elemental Skill creates an initial Geo sampler.
- Not reliant on Elemental Burst for her buff.
Cons
- Elemental Burst can accidentally be made into the damage form if her samplers weren't activated, causing potential Element cleanse.
- Without Furina, her Elemental Burst's Single Target healing may be lacking against teamwide HP drain enemies.
Geo On-Field DPS Xilonen
Xilonen is able to be the On-Field DPS dealing Geo damage if you use equal or less than 1 PECH character in the team. Xilonen has noticeably lesser damage dealt compared to Navia and Itto thus making her DPS role less preferred compared to her support role.
Pros
- Can utilize her own Signature Weapon for DPS.
- Deals a lot of Geo damage since her Elemental Burst now deals damage instead of healing.
Cons
- Weaker damage compared to the dedicated Geo DPS like Navia and Itto.
- Without healing, she needs a Sustain support if unable to dodge or facing a HP draining enemy.

Team Rotation of Xilonen and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Xilonen
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Xilonen
1. Start with the support with the longest ability durations, if there is any. Deploy their Skill and/or Burst accordingly. If there is an On-Field DPS that needs to use an ability at the start of the rotation, like
Mavuika
, then ensure you do so.
2. Switch to Xilonen. Use her Skill, then Normal Attack (x2) to fill your Nightsoul Bar for the Elemental RES shred.
3. If you need to use Xilonen's Burst for healing or to activate an artifact set, do it here.
4. Swap to any support with shorter ability duration if there is any, use their preferred ability combo here.
5. On-Field your main DPS to deal damage with their primary combo.
6. Repeat from step 1 after all support abilities expire.
Xilonen needs to do the Normal Attack (x2) part of her combo to activate her RES shred, but using her Burst is entirely optional, especially if the team you're using doesn't need the healing from it anyway.
Basics Of Using Xilonen
Xillonen's role in a team is to be the
Off-Field
Geo Support that shreds the Elemental resistance of enemies to specific elements.
Since she's a character from
Natlan
, Xilonen also generates and consumes
Nightsoul
points, which is tied to her Elemental RES shred.
Nightsoul Point Mechanics
All characters from
Natlan
integrate a special mechanic called Nightsoul points into their kit. After activating a certain ability or attack, a certain number of points are generated, indicated by a special bar beside the character.
Once the bar is filled, they enter a special state called Nightsoul Blessing and receive buffs that're unique to each character.
For Xilonen, her Nightsoul Blessing state activates her Elemental RES shred for Pyro, Hydro, Electro and Cryo (PHEC) depending on the corresponding elements of her party members.
RES Shred Is Dependent on Teammates
Xilonen makes use of a resource called Source Samples to determine which element's resistance she'll shred.
She will always start with a Geo sample active after her using her Skill. These samples will be converted into a Pyro, Hydro, Electro or Cryo (PHEC) sample depending on the elements of her teammates.
Xilonen can only shred the specific elemental RES that match the samples she activates, which happens after she fills her Nightsoul bar completely.
Use Skill and Normal Attack to Activate Shred
After Xilonen uses her Skill, she goes into Blade Roller mode and gains 45 Nightsoul points. One of her passives makes it so that, as long as she has at least 2 PHEC Source Samples, she can gain 35 points either through a Normal or Plunging Attack every 0.1 seconds.
Her RES shred is triggered after restoring at least 90 Nightsoul points (full bar), which can be easily achieved by using her Skill and then doing two Normal Attacks in quick succession.
If she doesn't have 2 PHEC Source samples, the damage dealt by her Normal and Plunging Attacks are buffed instead.
Note!
Two teammates of the same element will result in two identical samples, e.g. if there are two Hydro characters in the team, Xilonen will have two Hydro source samples.
Skill Can Be Used for Travel
Like most Natlan characters, Xilonen is also equipped with exploration mechanics that match her tribe's Saurian, the Tepetlisaur.
She activates her Blade Roller mode or state after pressing her Skill, enhancing her movement and climbing speed,
as well as enabling her to dash across walls or steep structures.
Her travel speed in this form is significantly faster than the base dashing in game. She's also able to perform long leaps while climbing, briefly transforming into an Ocelot as she does so.
Elemental Burst Is For Sustain or Sub-DPS
When Xilonen has at least 2 PHEC source samples, her Burst will take on a healing effect on the active character, which scales with her defense (DEF) stat.
Conversely, if she has less than 2 PHEC source samples, the Burst will instead deal AoE Geo DMG based on her DEF. In teams where she is the primary source sustain, Xilonen will need to be built with enough Energy Recharge to use her Burst on demand.

Scroll Of The Hero Of Cinder City

Kazuha
in some teams.
Nightsoul Blessing State and Nightsoul Burst
▲ The amount of Nightsoul Points is indicated by the special bar beside the character.
Xilonen is from
Natlan
, and applies a unique nation-specific mechanic like other similar characters called
Nightsoul
in her kit. All Natlan characters enter a special state called the Nightsoul Blessing state after generating Nightsoul Points.
Whatever effects they receive in this state is defined for each character in their respective Skill, Burst or passives.
The amount of Nightsoul Points they have can be tracked in-game via the special 'Nightsoul Bar' beside the character.

Elemental Skill Summary
Yohual's Scratch
Xilonen rushes forward and deals Nightsoul-aligned Geo DMG based on DEF, gaining 45 Nightsoul points and entering Nightsoul’s Blessing, switching to Blade Roller mode.
Blade Roller (Nightsoul’s Blessing: Xilonen):
-  Continuously consumes Nightsoul points (with a 9s time limit).
- Increases Movement SPD and climbing speed, allowing high-speed leaps in Ocelot Form while climbing.
- Ends when Nightsoul points are depleted or the skill is used again.
Source Samples:
- Xilonen carries 3 Samplers that generate Soundscapes based on party members’ elements, reducing nearby enemies’ corresponding Elemental RES.
- Initially, all Samples are Geo. For each Pyro/Hydro/Cryo/Electro party member, 1 Geo Sample converts to that element.
- While in Nightsoul’s Blessing, her Geo Sample is always active.
- After unlocking Netotiliztli’s Echoes (passive), Xilonen can regenerate Nightsoul points; upon reaching max, she consumes all points to activate all 3 Source Samples for 15s.
- Same-element RES shred does not stack.
- Source Sample effects can trigger off-field.

Xilonen's Skill makes use of a special resource called Source Samples, that will initially be Geo.
These samples will change element depending on her teammates' elemental types, which should either be Pyro, Hydro, Electro or Cryo (PHEC). Once she actives her Skill, she generates Nightsoul points and enters her Nightsoul Blessing State.
In order to activate the RES Shred, she needs to fill her Nightsoul Bar completely, which she can achieve after you unlock her passives that lets her gain points via Normal or Plunging Attack.
Ideally, you will want to Normal Attack (x2) after using her Skill. This activates her Source Samples and applies the RES Shred depending on the element of those samples.

When you activate Xilonen's Elemental Burst when there are 2 or more Pyro/Hydro/Electro/Cryo in the team, the current active character will receive continuous healing over time.
This healing scales with Xilonen's DEF stat and can be enhanced with Healing Bonus. Unless you need the healing or her current artifact set requires you to use Burst, it's generally fine to skip this ability in the rotation.

So long as Xilonen's Blade Roller mode is active, Xilonen can manifest exploration abilities similar to her tribe's Saurian, the Tepetlisaurus.

Effect
Let the rhythms of passion resound throughout the land! Xilonen switches to high-speed combat blading gear and rushes forward a certain distance before dealing Nightsoul-aligned Geo DMG based on her DEF.After using this, Xilonen will gain 45 Nightsoul points and enter the Nightsoul's Blessing state. Source Samples
Effect
Xilonen has 3 Samplers with her that can generate different Soundscapes based on her other party members' Elemental Types, decreasing nearby opponents' corresponding Elemental RES while active.
The initial recorded Source Sample within each Sampler will be Geo, and for each party member who is Pyro/Hydro/Cryo/Electro, 1 Geo Sampler will change to that corresponding Element. While Xilonen is in the Nightsoul's Blessing state, the Geo Source Sample she carries will always be active. After unlocking her Passive Talent, "Netotiliztli's Echoes," Xilonen can trigger said Passive Talent's effects to regenerate Nightsoul points for herself. When her Nightsoul points hit the maximum, she will consume all her Nightsoul points and activate the 3 Source Samples she has on hand for 15s. When the Source Samples are active, nearby opponents' corresponding Elemental RES will decrease. Source Sample effects of the same Elemental Type cannot stack. Xilonen can trigger these effects even when off-field.

• Xilonen's Nightsoul's Blessing has the following restrictions: When in this state, Xilonen's Nightsoul points have a 9s time limit. After this limit passes, her Nightsoul points will immediately expire.

Who Is Xilonen?
Xilonen Is A Renowned Smith In Natlan
Xilonen's skill with the forge is so well-known that even the Pyro Archon purchases her sunglasses from her. She's so meticulous in her work that she has her customers answer a form to detail exactly what they want.
Created Kachina's Turbo Twirly
Kachina shares in her voice line about Xilonen that it was the renowned smithy that created her Turbo Twirly! Other things Xilonen have forged includes Mavuika's sunglasses and Xilonen's own unique earphones.
Known Bearer Of An Ancient Name
Xilonen is one of the many warriors that holds an Ancient Name in Natlan. When the tribes and their representatives were mentioned during the Archon quest, Xilonen was mentioned as the representative of the Children of the Echoes.

- For the last slot on the team, while there are 5-star supports that are clearly better options, the difference between each choice of Hydro or Cryo support is minimal. Use whichever you think can help you clear the content more efficiently.
▼ Neuvillette-Skirk Burst DPS

- A team that relies on a powerful Hydro DPS compatible with Freeze, like Neuvillette, to take up most of the On-Field time.

- Furina and Escoffier can increase the damage greatly and provide good damage Off-Field.

★★・・・

- Without access to
Escoffier

Skirk has Passives that require her teams to be limited to Cryo and Hydro characters only, making a Freeze-oriented comp her preferred team overall. She can be played either as the On-Field DPS or as a Quickswap DPS alongside another Cryo or Hydro DPS.

Skirk's Freeze team should only have Cryo and Hydro teammates. Any non-Hydro/Cryo teammate in the party will cause her Death's Crossing stacks to be reduced, leading to her losing access to the buffs provided by her Return to Oblivion (Normal Attack DMG) and her Mutual Weapons Mentorship (Skill level increased by 1) passives.
Does Skirk Need Escoffier and Furina?
Skirk needs Escoffier to be considered in the top meta teams at C0.
She is Skirk's designated best-in-slot Cryo support due to how much value Escoffier provides with her Cryo RES shred in a full Hydro and Cryo team.
Skirk is still playable without Escoffier, but expect her performance to be below top of the meta standards,
especially at base investment with no Constellations or Signature Weapon. Escoffier ensures her some longevity against increasing endgame HP values.
Furina is more replaceable than Escoffier,
since DMG% buffs are more accessible and abundant (through weapons or artifacts) than RES Shred.
But while you can substitute Furina,
she's still a key component in Skirk's highest DPS team and a requirement if you want Skirk to reach her maximum potential.
Is Skirk Worth Pulling Without Her Best Support(s)?
Assuming Free-to-Play standards, Skirk without Escoffier likely results in a performance similar to or worse than some
S tier
characters. Whether or not she'll be worth pulling for when your account is missing one or both of her best supports will depend on the state of your account and personal in-game goals.

✘ Don't pull if:

- You're missing a Cryo DPS and/or already have at least two or three other strong,

teams for specific endgame content.
- You have either Escoffier OR Furina, and not missing both. Having at least one of them in a Skirk team can provide decent enough results to clear certain endgame modes with the right teams and setup, barring some rotations of
Stygian Onslaught
at Difficulty 5 and 6.
- You have her
Signature Weapon
or other synergistic 5-star CRIT swords.
- You really like Skirk as a character and enjoy her playstyle.
- You have more than 3 meta teams at your disposal that can easily clear endgame content.
- You want to clear
Stygian Onslaught
Difficulty 5 (at the very least) consistently and as soon as possible. It's better to invest in other
meta

Escoffier
or don't plan to get her.
- You're more interested in other teased characters or reruns.
- You're mostly Free-to-Play and can't see yourself vertically investing into Skirk's Signature Weapon or her current best supports.
- You don't have enough funds to guarantee both Escoffier and Skirk.
- You don't find her gameplay fun.

This is a Neuvillette on-field team with Skirk as a quickswap DPS who uses her Burst for damage. Escoffier and Furina can buff both Skirk and Neuvillette, while all three can provide Skirk with stacks for her passives.
Rotation Of Neuvillette-Skirk Burst DPS Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start the rotation with Neuvillette's Skill.
2
Swap to Furina. Use her Skill, followed by a dash to cancel the long animation.
3
Switch to Escoffier. Use her Burst.
4
Switch to Skirk and use her Skill (Hold) then Burst. If her Burst is still on cooldown, you can do a few Normal Attacks on Escoffier.
5
Swap back to Escoffier. Deploy her Skill.
6
Use Furina's Burst.
7
Swap to Neuvillette. Perform a [ Charged Attack → Skill → Charged Attack → Burst ] combo once.
8
Switch to Skirk, then use her Skill (hold) into her Burst.
9
Finish the rotation by on-fielding Neuvillette again to do Charged Attack (x2).
10
Repeat from Step 1.

How to Play Skirk
Skirk is a on-field Main DPS with a very unique mechanic compared to most characters in the game.
She does not use Elemental Energy, and instead relies on her Serpent's Subtlety resource to power
her damage and determine whether she leans into sustained Normal Attack uptime or quicker Burst-focused
windows. As a Freeze-focused DPS, Elemental Mastery is not a priority stat for her, and her damage
mainly scales through her core kit and
Cryo
/
Hydro
team synergies.
Skirk has two main playstyles. The first is her on-field Normal Attack focused mode. You enter this
playstyle by using her Elemental Skill,
Havoc: Warp
, to switch into Seven-Phase Flash mode
and gain Serpent's Subtlety. During this mode, you stay on-field and deal sustained damage while managing
your resource and absorbing Void Rifts for additional Serpent's Subtlety. Void Rifts are generated when
nearby party members trigger reactions such as Freeze, Superconduct, Cryo Swirl, or Cryo Crystallize
against opponents.
A simple on-field sequence typically looks like chaining Normal Attacks, weaving in Burst usage when
available, and using a Charged Attack to absorb nearby Void Rifts during your Seven-Phase Flash window
to extend or strengthen your damage cycle. The key is to remain in her mode long enough to benefit from
multiple Rift absorptions and avoid swapping out too early and losing value from your uptime.
Her second playstyle is a quicker Burst-focused approach. Instead of relying on standard Elemental Energy,
Skirk consumes Serpent's Subtlety to unleash her Elemental Burst,
Havoc: Ruin
, once she has
enough resource available. In this playstyle, you aim to build Serpent's Subtlety quickly, then spend it
to deal a large Burst window of damage before transitioning back into the next setup.

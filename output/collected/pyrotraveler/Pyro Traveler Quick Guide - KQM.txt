The Pyro Traveler or Pyro Main Character (PMC) is a 5★ Pyro Sword character in
Genshin Impact
who applies off-field Pyro and provides buffs through their C1 and 4pc Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City. Learn about PMC’s best builds, best weapons, best artifacts, and best teams in this quick guide.
Note that the information given may change if new discoveries are made.

Playstyles
Off-field Enabler
This is Pyro Traveler’s usual role. They use their Skill and Burst to gain Nightsoul points and apply Pyro from off-field, thus enabling reactions for their teammates.
Burgeon DPS
With their Tap Skill that hits once every second, Pyro Traveler can deal decent AoE damage through Burgeon.
On-field DPS
At C6, Pyro Traveler can stay on-field to deal Pyro-converted attacks. This playstyle requires heavy investment into Pyro Traveler and their supports to make it viable.
Talents
Level and Talent Priority
(Character Level) > Burst >= Skill >>> Normal Attack
As none of Pyro Traveler’s team utility scales with their Talent levels and their damage is lackluster in most teams, players can get away with little to no investment into them.
For a more invested build, both their Burst and Skill should be equally levelled, with the Burst having an edge thanks to its high multiplier, making it a potential Vape/Melt nuke. Level 90 is the top priority when Pyro Traveler triggers Overloaded or Burgeon frequently.
Normal Attack is not recommended to be levelled, unless Pyro Traveler is played as an on-field damage dealer.

Nightsoul’s Blessing: Traveler
Continuously consumes Nightsoul points. The Traveler’s Nightsoul’s Blessing state lasts 12s at maximum, and when their Nightsoul points are exhausted, this state will end. If this Elemental Skill is used again while active, the Traveler’s Nightsoul points will be reset to 42 points.
Blazing and Scorching Thresholds will disappear once the Traveler’s Nightsoul’s Blessing state ends, and only 1 Blazing or Scorching Threshold summoned by the Traveler themselves can exist at any one time.
Using Skill will make Pyro Traveler enter the Nightsoul’s Blessing state, giving them 42 Nightsoul points and activating one of two Skill effects. Tap Skill forms a ring that follows the active character and deals Nightsoul-aligned Pyro damage to nearby enemies every second; Hold Skill summons a sphere that deals coordinated attacks, inflicting Nightsoul-aligned Pyro damage to the enemy whenever a party member (including Pyro Traveler) deals damage to that target, with a 3s cooldown. Note that despite the in-game description, Pyro Traveler’s Hold Skill can still be triggered by off-field teammates.

In most teams, you should prioritize getting a 4pc Scroll set with enough ER to Burst every rotation, as the set effect and Pyro Traveler’s off-field application are more important to their team than their damage.
EM gains value if Pyro Traveler can trigger frequent reactions, especially Overloaded and Burgeon. If using them as a deliberate Transformative Reaction trigger, EM becomes their top priority.
Artifact Sets
4pc Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
General Best-in-Slot for Pyro Traveler due to its immense 40% DMG buff that they can easily trigger and maintain. Its 2pc effect also alleviates Pyro Traveler’s ER needs in team with Natlan characters.
4pc Tenacity of the Millelith
Viable alternative if 4pc Scroll is already used by another character. Pyro Traveler’s Skill allows them to keep this artifact set at full uptime.
4pc Noblesse Oblige
Another option for Pyro Traveler when another character holds Scroll. This set gives Pyro Traveler more personal damage compared to 4pc TotM, but TotM’s buff has better uptime and it is less likely to be held by another character already.
4pc Instructor
Instructor can provide a strong EM buff that may be more useful than the ATK% buffs from 4pc TotM or 4pc NO. Be aware that a 4-star set will limit PMC’s stats; use an off-set ER sands to meet ER requirements
4pc Deepwood Memories
Deepwood’s buff is more important to Kinich and Emilie teams than Scroll, so Pyro Traveler should equip it if it is not already held by another teammate.
4pc Archaic Petra
Niche option in teams with a Geo unit where another character already holds Scroll, usually Xilonen. Pyro Traveler can pick up a Crystallize shard to provide a good additional DMG Bonus buff.
4pc Obsidian Codex
Only recommended for on-field damage builds
4pc Marechaussee Hunter
A strictly worse on-field DPS set than 4pc Obsidian Codex but is not that much behind. Only usable with Furina in the team.
4pc Flower of Paradise Lost, 4pc Gilded Dreams
Artifact sets that increase Pyro Traveler’s Burgeon and Overloaded damage. 4pc Scroll can still be optimal for team damage if teammates contribute significant personal damage and there is no other holder.
Weapons
4★ Favonius Sword
Pyro Traveler’s best general-use weapon, providing them with a high ER secondary stat and a useful passive to help with meeting team ER requirements
4★ Sacrificial Sword
At R5, Sacrificial Sword allows for a shorter rotation (around 16s) with its Skill cooldown refresh. Pyro Traveler is still able to maintain their Skill uptime with just the Nightsoul points received from the Skill and remain from previous rotation where both Skill and Burst are used. This benefits characters with shorter rotation length such as Clorinde or Arlecchino.
It is not worth using this Sword before R5 or in a 18s+ rotation otherwise.
4★ Festering Desire
A limited event weapon that provides ER and Skill DMG Bonus/CRIT Rate buffs. Only worth using if Favonius’ passive is not needed for team energy.
4★ Fleuve Cendre Ferryman
Alternative to Festering Desire. It has less powerful damage buffs in exchange for a slight increase to ER for 5s after casting Skill.
4★ Xiphos’ Moonlight
This weapon increases Pyro Traveler’s reaction damage while also providing ER to the team.
4★ Iron Sting, 4★ Toukabou Shigure
Accessible EM options for an Overloaded / Burgeon build.
5★ Freedom-Sworn
Premium weapon choice for Pyro Traveler. The EM stat is good for their reaction damage while the teamwide buff benefits their team’s main damage dealers.
5★ CRIT Swords
Used to improve Pyro Traveler’s damage output and only recommended for a high-investment on-field build.

Off-Field Enabler
Pyro Traveler — DPS — Flex — Flex
Pyro Traveler is mainly used for their off-field Pyro application and their ability to activate the full DMG Bonus from 4pc Scroll as a freely available unit. However, their application is rather slow and thus cannot enable reactions for many demanding units such as Neuvillette or Wriothesley without help.
Pyro Traveler competes with Natlan 4-star units, who can also provide 4pc Scroll with their innate Nightsoul mechanics. What makes Pyro Traveler competitive is their modest but easy-to-maintain C1 DMG Bonus. However, they face stiffer competition when compared to 5-star Natlan supports like Xilonen and Citlali, who are generally leagues above them.

Burgeon Trigger
Pyro Traveler — Dendro Applier — Hydro Applier — Flex
Pyro Traveler can be played as a Burgeon trigger in a team with consistent Dendro and Hydro application. With their Tap Skill, they can trigger Burgeon on nearby Dendro Cores every 1s, but they will only apply Pyro to the enemy target(s) every 3s due to ICD. This is a good thing as it limits Burning uptime while still frequently triggering Burgeon.
Pyro Traveler builds for their Burgeon damage by investing into Character Level (Lv. 90 is highly recommended) and EM stats. They usually use 4pc FoPL or 4pc GD for personal damage, but 4pc Scroll can be considered as a buffing set for their teammates.

On-Field DPS
With their Pyro conversion at C6, Pyro Traveler can perform as the team’s on-fielder and deal respectable damage with good investment into them and their teammates. However, as Pyro Traveler’s statline is rather underwhelming compared to the other popular damage dealers, this playstyle is only recommendable to people who really want to build a team around Pyro Traveler staying on-field and dealing damage through their Normal, Charged, or Plunging Attacks.
Pyro Traveler’s best on-field playstyle is Vape / Melt teams where their damage is significantly enhanced by the Amplifying Reactions and strong buffers such as Furina and Citlali.
Notable Teammates
Bennett
Bennett is one of the best buffers with his massive Flat ATK buff. He also enables Pyro Resonance and fulfills the healer role for Pyro Traveler. At C6, Bennett also gives 15% Pyro DMG Bonus and Pyro infusion, letting Pyro Traveler to continue doing Pyro Normal, Charged, and Plunging Attacks even without their Skill active. This is most notable in Melt Plunge teams with Xianyun where the added application from Pyro Traveler’s Skill is unwanted.
Iansan
Iansan is another ATK buffer with the ability to carry 4pc Scroll for an additional buff. Unlike Bennett, her ATK buff can only be maintained by characters who have mobile playstyles or Nightsoul point consumption, the latter of which includes Pyro Traveler. To preserve Iansan’s highest ATK buff, Pyro Traveler can dash around between combos for more movement.
Furina
Furina applies Hydro to enable Vaporize for Pyro Traveler, and buffs all their hits with her stacking DMG Bonus buff. She requires a healer in the team to stack her Fanfare, preferably one that can also contribute buffs.
Yelan
,
Xingqiu
Other good off-field Hydro appliers for Pyro Traveler in their Vape teams. Yelan provides a ramping DMG Bonus buff while Xingqiu gives interruption resistance to make the on-field experience smoother.
Citlali
Citlali enables Melt with her off-field Cryo application and provides very good buffs through her A1 Passive, 4pc Scroll, and supportive weapons such as TTDS or Starcaller’s Watch. As a Natlanese, she also enables Nightsoul Burst, thus helping Pyro Traveler activate their A4 for more Energy regain.
Xilonen
Xilonen heals, provides RES Shred, and buffs with 4pc Scroll. Her RES Shred and the 4pc Scroll buff  are long-lasting and persist through multiple waves.
Kazuha
,
Sucrose
Anemo buffers who provide different degrees of grouping and RES Shred via 4pc Viridescent Venerer. Kazuha gives a large Elemental DMG% buff that scales with his EM, whereas Sucrose offers potent EM buffs to increase Pyro Traveler’s reaction damage.
Xianyun
Xianyun allows Pyro Traveler to jump and perform Plunging Attacks, dealing considerable damage. She also buffs those Plunging Attacks with her A4, heals the entire team, and carries 4pc VV for RES Shred.
Chevreuse
Chevreuse provides potent buffs to Pyro and Electro characters, with the caveat that the team cannot include any other elements. Off-field Electro units such as Fischl, Yae, and Ororon are preferred for on-field Pyro Traveler.

Correctly building the Pyro Traveler in
Genshin Impact
is a necessary step in optimizing their role as either a sub-DPS or a main DPS in team comps. The Pyro Traveler has finally become available as of Version 5.3, following their arrival in Natlan in Version 5.0.
The Pyro Traveler is, at their core, an off-field sub-DPS capable of helping the party with off-field Pyro DMG against enemies, activated by their Elemental Skill
. The
Pyro Traveler’s kit in
Genshin Impact
revolves mostly around this mechanic. However, it’s possible to transform them into a main DPS, too.
Once you unlock the Pyro Traveler’s C6 (Constellation level 6), their Normal, Charged, and Plunging Attacks will deal Nightsoul-aligned Pyro DMG while in the Nightsoul’s Blessing state, making them a viable NA DPS
. If you unlock their C6, you are not locked out of their sub-DPS role, but can instead opt to use them as your main damage dealer. This, however, affects a large portion of their build, from weapons and Artifacts to the team comps themselves. With this in mind, you should search for the best gear after
unlocking the Pyro Traveler in
Genshin Impact
.
Best Weapons For Pyro Traveler (Best Swords)
Prioritize Swords With Good Offensive Bonuses
Genshin Impact's Pyro Traveler stands in Natlan.
To start working on the Pyro Traveler’s build, you’ll first need to assign a sword for them to work with.
The best sword for the Pyro Traveler’s sub-DPS build is Freedom-Sworn
. This 5-star sword gives them a decent Elemental Mastery sub-stat (198 at level 90) but what really makes it worthwhile is its weapon skill. Freedom-Sworn increases the user’s DMG by 10% and when they trigger reactions, they gain a Sigil of Rebellion. With two Sigils, they are consumed and grant nearby allies 20% ATK and 16% Normal, Charged, and Plunging Attack DMG for 12 seconds.
Sigils can be obtained every 0.5 seconds, an effect that can be triggered even if the character is off-field. Once the Sigils are consumed, no Sigils can be obtained for 20 seconds. The effect of this weapon does not stack if another character is wielding Freedom-Sworn.

If you don’t have Freedom-Sworn, you can use different 4-star swords for the Pyro Traveler.
One of them is Lion’s Roar, which gives them an impressive ATK sub-stat (41.3% at level 90) and increases the DMG against enemies affected by Pyro or Electro by 20% (at R1)
. If you unlock R5, the DMG bonus is increased to 36%. A F2P (free-to-play) alternative is Finale of the Deep. It offers a lower ATK sub-stat (27.6% at level 90) but when using an Elemental Skill, ATK is increased by 12% for 15 seconds (at R1). At R5, the ATK bonus is 24%.
Finale of the Deep grants a bonus effect with Bond of Life in
Genshin Impact
, but considering the Pyro Traveler does not have it, the weapon would only serve to give them the ATK buff.
Sapwood Blade also works for the Pyro Traveler’s sub-DPS build. It grants a considerable Energy Recharge buff via its sub-stat (30.6% at level 90).
After triggering Burning or Burgeon, a Leaf of Consciousness is created near the Pyro Traveler for up to 10 seconds. When picked up, they gain 60 Elemental Mastery for 12 seconds
. This effect can be triggered from off-field. Essentially, this sword helps increase the Traveler’s power during elemental reactions, but only in teams focused on Burning or Burgeon, like one led by
Kinich in
Genshin Impact
.
Wolf-Fang is a great sword for the Pyro Traveler with its useful CRIT Rate sub-stat (27.6% at level 90) and weapon skill.
The 4-star weapon increases the DMG dealt by Elemental Skill and Burst by 16% and, when a Skill hits an opponent, CRIT Rate is boosted by 2% (at R1)
. When the Burst hits an opponent, CRIT Rate is increased by 2% – these effects last 10 seconds separately, up to four stacks, and can be triggered once every 0.1 seconds.

Festering Desire is also a spectacular weapon for the Pyro Traveler’s sub-DPS build

For the Pyro Traveler’s main DPS role, you can equip Mistsplitter Reforged in
Genshin Impact
, which grants them a CRIT DMG sub-stat (44.1% at level 90)
. Additionally, they can gain a 12% Elemental DMG Bonus, up to three stacks. At stacks 1/2/3, Elemental DMG is increased by an extra 8/16/28%. Stacks can be gained by Normal Attacks with Elemental DMG, casting an Elemental Burst, and having less than 100% Energy.
When choosing weapons for the Pyro Traveler’s main DPS build, look for swords that increase offensive stats and that benefit their Normal Attacks or Elemental DMG since these will carry the bulk of their damage output.
Alternatively, you can use Haran Geppaku Futsu. The 5-star sword offers a CRIT Rate sub-stat (33.1% at level 90).
The weapon grants a 12% All Elemental DMG Bonus and when nearby allies use Elemental Skills, the user gains one Wavespike, up to two stacks
. When the user activates their Elemental Skill, all stacks of Wavespike are consumed to gain Rippling Upheaval. Each stack of Wavespike that is consumed increased Normal Attack DMG by 20% for eight seconds in
Genshin Impact

Skyward Blade is a decent 5-star substitute for the Pyro Traveler
. Its Energy Recharge sub-stat (55.1% at level 90) is useful to help fuel their Burst. The sword also increases CRIT Rate by 4% and after using a Burst, the user gains Skypiercing Might, which increases Movement SPD by 10%, increases ATK SPD by 10%, and increases the DMG of Normal and Charged Attack hits by 20% for 12 seconds.

Best Team Comps For Pyro Traveler
The Character Is Versatile
The Pyro Traveler can be a versatile character in both their roles.
As an off-field Pyro sub-DPS, Pyro Traveler can help serve characters like Kinich, Mualani, Wriothesley, and Clorinde, triggering reactions infused with their consistent Pyro application
. It’s worth noting that Pyro Traveler can grant an extra DMG bonus for characters in the Nightsoul’s Blessing state and can restore energy when allies trigger Nightsoul Bursts. While they work well with Natlan characters in
Genshin Impact
, Pyro Traveler can operate very well with units from other regions.

Skill (Talent) Priority For Pyro Traveler
Priorities Change Based On Their Role
Genshin Impact's Pyro Traveler raises his sword in battle.
When leveling up Pyro Traveler’s abilities, you may need to prioritize some over others.
For their sub-DPS build, consider their Elemental Skill the most important part of their kit, followed by their Burst
. Seeing they mainly stay off-field, Normal Attacks are the last priority. If you are working on their main DPS build, however, prioritize Normal Attacks, followed by their Elemental Skill and Burst at the same time.

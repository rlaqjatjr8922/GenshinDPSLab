Klee is a 5-star Pyro character that wields a Catalyst in Genshin Impact. Her gameplay revolves around being an on-field Pyro DPS, using explosive attacks and bombs to deal heavy AoE damage. Klee excels at applying Pyro status quickly, making her ideal for triggering elemental reactions such as Vaporize, Overload, and Melt. Her powerful Pyro attacks and energetic playstyle make her a burst-heavy frontliner.

What Kind of Character is Klee?
Klee is a 5-Star Pyro Catalyst
DPS
who specializes in strong
On-Field
Damage. Her Explosive Sparks allow her perform a Special Charged Attack dealing increased damage with no stamina consumption. She is now able to hold up to 3 Explosive Sparks, gained through using Normal Attacks, Elemental Skill and Burst. Her Elemental Burst is able to deal numerous amounts of damage, good for single and multiple enemy scenarios.
After gaining access to Klee's Hexerei Secret Rite Passive, she can then also gain up to 3 Boom Badges which allow her Special Charged attacks to deal even more damage based on the number of stacks. The stacks are accumulated through the same method of using Normal Attacks, Elemental Skill and Burst.
Klee is strong DPS option if you are able to utilize the Hexerei: Secret Rite resonance and lets her be a viable pulling option if you lack a Pyro DPS. She is a unit who appreciates staying On-Field for a good length so pair her with others who either supports or deals Off-field damage, preferably with another Hexerei character to unlock the Hexerei: Secret Rite Resonance.

Roles & Playstyle Of Klee and Her Strengths and Weaknesses
Pyro On-Field DPS
Klee is a viable On-Field Pyro DPS whose DPS potential is likely a little better than her pre-buff state. Most of her damage comes from her Charged Attacks that can be enhanced using the Explosive Sparks, and because the Hexerei buffs now only require you to use Normal Attacks, her gameplay loop should be more forgiving.
Pros and Cons of DPS Klee
Pros
- Easier to play after the buffs.
- Kit mechanics aren't complicated, so she can synergize well with most generic supports in the game.
- Her Charged Attack is considered a heavy attack like a Claymore, so she can interact with specific combat mechanics that require heavy attacks.
Cons
- No natural interruption resistance in her kit makes her more susceptible to being thrown around by enemy attacks.
- The Pyro DPS niche is very crowded, while also being dominated by
Mavuika
, who makes it hard for free-to-play or low-income spenders to justify pulling for Klee over Mavuika.
- Playing with her model type as an On-Fielder can feel awkward and clunky.

Team Rotation of Klee and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for On-Field DPS Klee (Post-Buff)
Basic Rotation For On-Field DPS Klee (Post-Buff)
1.Start with the supports and deploy their abilities. Order the support usage by doing abilities with longer abilities first before moving to the shorter ones.hr>2. Switch to Klee, use her Skill, into Burst.
3. Use Normal Attack (x3) to trigger the special Charged Attack then use the other Skill charge. Spam Normal Attacks after.
4. Repeat from Step 1.
Klee's basic team rotation follows the On-Field DPS formula that has the supports going first in order to maximize the buff and ability uptime while Klee is the active character,
Ensure that you're aware which support has the Skill or Burst with the longest duration, so they'll be deployed first.
Optimized Klee Combo (Post Buff)
Normal Attack (x2) > Skill, then use Burst immediately to cancel animation > Normal Attack (x3) > Skill > Normal Attack (x3) three times > Normal Attack (x1) into Charged Attack > Normal Attack (x2)
The above combo is little more difficult to do over spamming Normal Attacks, but it should allow you to squeeze out as much damage as possible.
Basics Of Using Klee
Klee is mainly an
On-Field
Pyro
DPS
that uses her special enhanced Charged Attack to deal damage.
Following her inclusion to Hexenzirkel faction, Klee received a buff that allows her to automatically use the enhanced Charged Attack, while her Elemental Burst is active,  after doing three Normal Attacks.
Elemental Skill Throws Out a Dodoco Bomb
Her Skill causes Klee to release Jumpty Dumpty, which is a bomb that bounces before exploding and bursting into smaller Dodoco mines. These mines erupt into tiny explosions when an enemy passes through or steps on them.
Elemental Burst Creates More Explosions
Klee's Burst lets her summon a cluster of four-leaf clovers that will randomly shoot an enemies with range, which shares a visual design with her Charged Attack.
After completing the task given to her via the Witch's Homework system, her Elemental Burst will allow her to automatically trigger the special Charged Attack after doing at least 3 Normal Attacks,
thereby reducing the need to use her normal Charged attack which involves holding down the Normal Attack key.
Explosive Sparks Reduce Stamina Consumption
One of Klee's passives generates an Explosive Spark after her Skill or Normal Attacks deal damage. The Explosive Spark reduces the next Charged Attack's Stamina use and increasing its DMG at the same time.
CRIT Hits Restore Energy for Party
Klee can restore a very small amount of energy for her teammates if her Charged Attacks CRIT, incentivizing building her with a high enough CRIT stat. The impact of this passive is decent, but not impactful enough to be a problem if you can't get a lot of CRIT Rate.
Hexerei Passive Improves Her Enhanced Charged Attack
Klee's Hexerei passive increases the DMG dealt by her enhanced Charged Attacks whenever she deals damage using her Normal Attack, Skill and Burst.
But the buffs also gave her the ability to automatically use an enhanced Charged Attack after using 3 Normal Attacks while her Burst is up, among other quality-of-life changes.
This is only active when she has another Hexerei member with her in the party.

On-Field Charged Attack-Based Pyro DPS
Basic Rotation Of Klee (Post-Buff)
1. Start the rotation with the supports. Deploy the abilities with longer durations first before shorter ones.
2. Switch to Klee. Use her Elemental Skill, into Burst.
3. Use Normal Attacks to trigger her special Charged Attack.
4. Repeat from Step 1.
Klee has always been designed to have most of her DPS condensed into her Charged Attacks.
She can enhance these Charged Attacks through an Explosive Spark that she gains by dealing damage using her Skill or Normal Attacks. Following her inclusion into the Hexerei: Secret Rite system, her gameplay will feel significantly less clunky to play, while also boosting her overall DPS potential.
Skill Deploys a Bomb That Explodes Into Smaller Mines
Icon
Elemental Skill Summary
Jumpy Dumpty
- Klee throws out Jumpty Dumpty, a bomb that bounces three times before exploding into smaller mines.
- These Dodoco mines explode when enemies come into contact with them.
- Her skill has two charges.
Klee's Elemental Skill throws out a Dodoco Bomb she calls Jumpty Dumpty. Damaging enemies with her Skill  is one of ways to create an Explosive Spark, which is further detailed in her 'Pounding Surprise' passive.
Burst Creates Constant Explosions
Icon
Elemental Burst Description
Sparks 'n' Splash
- For the duration of this ability, continuously summons Sparks 'n' Splash to attack nearby opponents, dealing AoE Pyro DMG.
Her Elemental Burst creates a group of Sparks 'n' Splash, which shares an appearance and functionality to her Charged Attack. They attack nearby enemies with similar explosions, but the DMG they deal is often less than what Klee's special Charged Attack can do.
Explosive Sparks Enhance Her Charged Attacks
Icon
Passive Description
Pounding Surprise
When Jumpy Dumpty and Normal Attacks deal DMG, Klee has a 50% chance to obtain an Explosive Spark.
The reason why most of Klee's damage is from her Charged Attacks is thanks to her passive that increases its DMG and lowers the Stamina cost after either her Skill or Normal Attacks hit an enemy.
The Explosive Spark this passive generates is tied to a 50% chance spawn rate, but since her Hexerei: Secret Rite passive makes it so that three Normal Attacks result in an automatic, enhanced Charged Attack, you essentially don't really need to worry about this random chance.
Passive Restores Teammate Energy on a CRIT
Icon
Passive Description
Sparkling Burst
When Klee's Charged Attack results in a CRIT Hit, all party members gain 2 Elemental Energy.
The Energy that Klee gives through this passive is appreciated, especially if some of her teammates have relatively high Energy requirements. This encourages you to build a substantial amount of CRIT Rate on her, but that should already be one of your stat priorities considering her nature as an On-Field DPS.
Better with a Shield
There is no innate increase to Interruption Resistance found in Klee's abilities, which means she's extremely vulnerable to getting interrupted or tossed around by enemy attacks. It's recommended to pair her with a shielder if you're not adept at dodging while On-Fielding Klee.

Should You Pull for Klee's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Constellations are Nice But Not Necessary
Signature VS Constellations
C2 > C4 or
Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds
> C4
C2 Is a Good Stopping Point
Klee has seen an increase in team options with the introduction of new supports that she can work with. And while it's generally better to invest into these supports, getting constellations on Klee is a viable choice if you're willing to go that far to improve her performance directly.
Without going to C6 or even C4,
C2 will be the ideal stopping point due the mines from her skill now decreasing enemy Defense upon hit, leading to a noticeable increase to the team's damage potential.
Interestingly, Klee with C2 can also be played from a supportive angle due to her mines persist after swapping, which is something worth noting if you want to experiment with new teams.
In terms of Signature Weapons, historically, Klee would usually rerun with
Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds
.
It's a usable to decent alternative for other catalyst characters and one of Klee's best options, but can be rivaled or even outperformed by a max refinement
The Widsith
,
Tome of the Eternal Flow
(with
Furina
) or
Cashflow Supervision
(with
Furina
)  on her specifically.
Pulling for Klee's "Signature Weapon" can be worth considering in the event you do not have any of her other weapon options, and if the other weapon is something you see yourself using. But
because Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds is in the Standard Banner pool, it may not be ideal to dedicate pulls for it since there is always the possibility of pulling it there or from the Limited Weapon Banner.
Recommendation if You Have R5
The Widsith
C2 > C4 >
Lost Prayer to the Sacred Winds
> C4
C4 Adds Another Playstyle
If you already have a good catalyst on Klee but are willing to continue vertically investing, then C4 can be an interesting choice. This enables her to become a Burst support with some debuffs on the side (from C2 Def shred) while also making it so that switching off her Burst before it expires can be rewarding as opposed to how it usually is at C0.
If you are capable of getting her C4 and would really like to improve Klee's performance without investing elsewhere, then C2 and C4 are going to yield the best results.

Who Is Klee?
The Knights Of The Favonius' Spark Knight
Klee may be young, but she's known as one of the strongest members of the Knights of Favonius, The Spark Knight! She wields explosive strength with dangerous potential much to the worry of Jean.
Daughter Of Alice
Klee is the daughter of Alice, a famed adventurer and the founder of the Hexenzirkel, an organization of powerful witches. She's well-loved by her mother who often supports Klee's creativity, even if it's a bit too destructive.
Entrusted To The Knights Of Favonius
Alice has entrusted her daughter to the Knights of Favonius as she goes on an adventure. Albedo, who sees her as his little sister, primarily takes care of Klee. However, when it comes to disciplining Klee for her destructive ways, Jean is the one to put her in solitary confinement to reflect on her actions.

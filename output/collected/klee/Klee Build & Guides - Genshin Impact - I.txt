Klee's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
AoE Damage focused which works great with grouping.
Can Stunlock enemies with Charged Attacks, giving you an opening to deal damage safely.
High Pyro application.
Weaknesses
One of the most squishy characters.
Long animations with higher mechanical complexity required in order to cancel them.
Klee's attacks have a short range.

3.
Klee Combos and How to Play
Klee is a character that can feel very clunky to play if you don't know how to 
cancel her long animations. Her Normal attacks combos are very, very slow. Most of her damage comes 
from her Normal Charged Attacks, but is easily staggered. If Klee gets hit mid-animation 
during her Normal or Charged Attack, things will go south extremely fast.
The good part? Almost all her kit has a way of cancelling the long animations! This is 
due to the fact that Klee can move between combos without those being cancelled. The goal will 
be to interrupt your animations by dashing or jumping mid-combo. Once you know how to cancel, 
she is actually rather easy and fun to play.
3.1.
Klee's Animation Cancellations
When playing Klee, you can cancel the following animations:
Normal Attack
: While you will mostly use Klee's Charged Attacks, 
if you're out of Stamina and you need to spam her Normal Attack, you want to hold a 
directional key/your joystick, while you click on the Normal Attack. This basically lets you 
spam the Normal Attack faster, and deal more damage.
Normal Attack - Jump or Dash
: If you don't manage to get the Machine Gun timing right, 
you can instead cancel by dashing or jumping in between attacks. Dashing is easier but depletes 
your stamina. Jumping can make you more vulnerable so keep that in mind.
Charged Attack
: Those animations can be cancelled by using a Normal Attack right after the laser fires. 
This can be done by basically double clicking. You can also Dash in-between.
Machine Gun Into Charged Attack
: If your goal is to get more chances to trigger 
Explosive Sparks, you can Machine Gun before double-clicking for the Charged Attack cancellation.
Double Charge Attack
: You can force two Charged Attacks one after another to benefit from the 
the same Explosive Spark if you cancel the animations. In order to do so you need to use 
Klee's Elemental Skill first, if you got an Explosive Spark you directly use a 
Normal Attack, Charged Attack, Dash, and another Charged Attack. This should give you 
two "free" Charged Attacks that won't consume stamina. You only consume the Dash's stamina.
Elemental Burst
: Klee's burst can be used to cancel any animation. if your Burst is up, you can theoretically 
cast it right before the last attack is fully released. The burst will cancel the animation.
However, be careful if you want to cancel the Skills' animation: Jumpy Dumpty can be completely cancelled 
if you do it too early! You have to use the Burst right after Jumpy Dumpy leaves Klee's hand.
Once you get the hand on Klee's animation cancellations, the rest of her 
gameplay is not very hard. One important thing to know is that Klee's Charged Attack is your 
most powerful attack. If you can, always prioritize the Charged Attack over Normal Attack. Charged 
Attacks from Klee can also help you stunlock enemies very easily, giving you an 
opening to unleash the rest of her power!
Here is Klee's recommended rotation:
Use Klee's Elemental Skill to build energy. Once charge is usually enough.
Spam Normal and Charged Attacks until your Burst is ready
Switch to your supports BEFORE using Klee's Burst
Use your supports' abilities
Switch back to Klee and unleash her Burst
Don't switch her out until her burst is over, as this will cancel it
Spam Normal and Charged attacks
Use your remaining Jumpy Dumpty charges before the burst ends
Repeat
One last thing to note is that you will notice a Floating Golden Flower next to Klee 
when an Explosive Spark is available.

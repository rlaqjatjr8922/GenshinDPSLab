Best Teams For Klee
Team
Rating
Description
▼ Overload
★★★★★
- Strongest team comp for Klee considering her current options support even after the Hexerei buffs
- When in a team with
Chevreuse
, an Overload comp can output decent to high damage, provided you're able to consistently trigger the reaction.
- A pure Overload team falls off without Chevreuse, so it's best to only consider this team once you've secured a copy of her.
▼ Vaporize
★★★★・
- High team damage potential centered around the Vaporize reaction.
- Klee functions more like an On-Field Driver for an Off-Field Hydro Sub-DPS to trigger Vaporize off of due to how much Pyro she applies.
▼ Overload with Vaporize (Overvape)
★★★・・
- Klee can be paired with
Fischl
, and then be complemented by a Hydro character to create a team archetype that focuses on triggering both Overload and Vaporize simultaneously.
▼ Melt-Plunge
★★★・・
- A team that can showcase impressive single-instance damage numbers provided you have the right characters, since Melt has a higher multiplier if the Pyro character triggers the Melt reaction.
- Klee isn't able to consistently trigger Melt however since she applies a lot of Pyro with her normal rotation, so her variation of a Melt team is less appealing than the top meta ones, such as
Mavuika
.
▼ Burning
★★・・・
- This team uses the Burning reaction to deal damage to enemies within a wide AoE due to how the reaction spreads.
- This is best played with
Emilie
who scales well when Burning is present, and is a lackluster comp without her.
▼ Mono-Pyro
★★・・・
- Mono-Pyro typically relies on the brute-force power of the Pyro character's numbers, which is amplified by Elemental RES shredders. It's less powerful than other reaction based teams but is still a viable comp for Klee.
These teams now consider Klee's abilities, gameplay and synergy with other characters following her Hexerei buffs.

Klee has decent synergy with Durin and Fischl in an Overload team since the RES Shred from Chevreuse applies to everyone in the party. Everyone's damage benefits from Chevreuse's substantial Overload-related buffs, resulting in higher DPS potential, but because Chevreuse herself doesn't heal that much, sustain may be a problem in harder content.
Rotation Of Overload Klee Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Durin. Use Elemental Skill twice to enter the Confirmation of Purity state.
2
Switch to Fischl, use her Skill or Burst. Normal Attack if the cooldown isn't ready.
3
Use Durin's Burst.
4
Swap to Chevreuse. Use her Burst then do Skill (hold then shoot) twice.
5
Swap to Klee. Use Normal Attack (x2), Skill, then Burst immediately. Spam Normal Attacks while Burst is active.
6
Repeat step 1 after support abilities expire.
Chevreuse's Pyro and Electro RES shred activates as soon as any party member triggers an Overload reaction, which is why the rotation here starts the way it does so Durin's Burst is immediately buffed by the RES shred. Chevreuse's Skill (Hold) gives a teamwide attack buff, and is greatly appreciated by both Klee and Durin.

This comp variation focuses more on team DPS due to having two strong Sub-DPSes, Furina and Albedo (post-buff) which pairs well with Klee's innate ability to be a very good On-Field driver for both of them. Klee also still contributes a good chunk to the total team DPS following her Hexerei buffs.
Rotation Of Vaporize Klee Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Xilonen. Do her normal rotation of Skill, Normal Attack (x2), then Burst.
2
Switch to Furina. Use Skill into Burst. You can dash after the Skill to cancel the animation.
3
Use Albedo's Skill into Burst.
4
Switch to Xilonen again to reuse her Skill into Normal Attack (x2) combo.
5
Swap to Klee. Use Normal Attack (x2), Skill, then Burst immediately. Spam Normal Attacks while Burst is active.
6
Repeat step 1 after support abilities expire.
Because of how the
Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
set works, the rotation above will have Furina and Albedo receiving the DMG% buff after Xilonen triggers a reaction. Furina's Hydro application rate is slower than Klee's Pyro, so Furina will be triggering most of the Vaporize hits with her Elemental Skill in this team, further justifying the Scroll buff being given to her over Klee.

Klee will usually be unable to trigger Melt on all her attacks due to the sheer amount of Pyro she applies. Because of this, she'll need specific teammates for her to have a proper Melt setup. The easiest one that guarantees a good amount of Melt hits (and not accidentally causing the Cryo character to Melt instead) is by pairing Klee with Xianyun and then using Klee's plunges to trigger the reaction instead, such as in the team sample above.
Rotation Of Melt-Plunge Klee Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Xianyun. Use Skill into Burst.
2
Switch to Bennet. Skill (tap) once, then use his Burst.
3
Next, swap to Citlali to make use of her Skill, then Burst.
4
Swap to Klee. Jump and then plunge until all the buffs run out.
5
Repeat from step 1 after support abilities expire.
This kind of playstyle doesn't utilize Klee's Charged Attacks, so its a much easier rotation, but at the cost of not fully utilizing the character's abilities.

Klee has always had trouble utilizing both Melt and Vaporize reactions due to her Pyro application being too fast, especially with her usual combo. Because of this, often her Melt or Vaporize comps will include a Cryo or Hydro Sub-DPS who can benefit off of the Pyro she leaves behind and trigger those reactions respectively. They would need to have at least average to good personal damage to make the most out of it.
Xianyun makes it so that Klee herself is the one triggering the Melt, but also causes her to be played in a way that goes against her intended design. But if this doesn't bother you, it's a perfectly viable comp otherwise.

Burning functions similar to Mono-Pyro in practice since it relies more on the raw damage of the DPSes involved. Emilie is a key character since she not only does is DPS tied to the Burning reaction persisting, but she  also reduces Burning's self-damage, since the reaction can also negatively affect your own party members.
Durin is also fairly irreplaceable as a core member because he enables Klee's Hexerei passive and provides RES shred for Klee, Emilie and himself.
Rotation Of Burning Klee Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Durin. Use Skill twice to enter Confirmation of Purity state, then Burst.
2
Use Emilie's Skill or Burst.
3
Swap to Bennet. Use Skill (Tap), then Burst.
4
Swap to Klee. Use Normal Attack (x2), Skill, then Burst immediately. Spam Normal Attacks while Burst is active.
5
Briefly use Bennett's Skill (Tap).
6
Repeat from step 1 after the support abilities expire.
Using Bennett's Skill towards the end of the rotation ensures that Burning will persist into the next rotation, since the reaction will keep going provided you keep applying Pyro or Dendro to the Burning enemy.

- Bennett provides healing and a substantial ATK% buff, which no other Pyro support is a significant upgrade to. Mavuika is a close alternative thanks to her decaying DMG% buff after using her Burst, but she lacks sustain capabilities.
- For this reason, you could argue Bennett is also hard to replace in a Burning team, especially if you can't play without a healer. It's strongly recommended to keep Bennett in this team if he isn't used anywhere else.
Teambuilding options for Burning is fairly limited due to the lack of substitutes for each character given their roles in the team. The DPS potential of a Burning team is average at best, but it's still viable Klee team if you don't care about meta or clearing the harder game content available.

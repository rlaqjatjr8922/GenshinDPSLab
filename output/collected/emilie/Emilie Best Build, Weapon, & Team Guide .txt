Emilie is a 5-star Dendro character who wields a Polearm in Genshin Impact. She is an off-field damage dealer who specializes in teams that trigger the Burning reaction. Alternatively, at C6, she can be played as an on-field DPS using her normal attacks. Lore-wise, Emilie is a popular perfume designer residing in Fontaine.

What Kind Of Character Is Emilie?
A Dendro Sub-DPS That Uses Burning to Increase Her Damage
Emilie is an
Off-Field
Dendro Sub-DPS who deploys a Lumidouce Case on to the field through her Elemental Skill.
The Lumidouce Case is her main source of damage, and it can upgrade from Level 1 all the way to Level 3 by collecting Scents from Burning enemies. Increasing the level of the Lumidouce Case increases its damage, range and rate of fire.
Emilie is decent in her niche, but she doesn't really add more to the Burning reaction unlike
Nilou
, or redefined the strength of the reaction like
Escoffier
did with Freeze. She's average at best, and is often replaceable even in her stronger teams due to the lack of buffing capabilities.

Roles & Playstyle of Emilie and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Sub-DPS Emilie
Emilie performs at her best from an Off-Field position as a Sub-DPS. She favors teams where she can prolong the duration of the Burning reaction with Pyro characters and with an On-Field DPS that can take advantage of the consistent Pyro application, such as Melt, Vape, Mono-Pyro or Burning-oriented damage characters.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field DPS Emilie
Pros
- Very easy to play.
- Contributes a decent amount of damage to her teams.
- Does not strictly need Constellations or her Signature Weapon to be good.
Cons
- As a Sub-DPS, she is a lot more replaceable than
Escoffier
who is very strong for Freeze, while also not as universal as
Furina
due to the lack of meaningful buffs for her teammates.
- Because she's replaceable, her pull value tends to be lower over getting a stronger DPS or Buffer.
- Free-to-Play weapon options are limited.

Team Rotation of Emilie and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Sub-DPS Emilie
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Sub-DPS Emilie
1. Start with the Pyro Support or Applicator, using their abilities to apply Pyro to the enemy.
2. Swap to Emilie then use Elemental Skill.
3. Use Elemental Burst.
4. Swap to other supports to use their abilities as well.
5. Swap to your On-Field DPS to do their full damage combo.
6. Return to step 1
Emilie will want to have Pyro already applied beforehand so her Lumidouce Case can start collecting Scents as soon as possible.
This rotation should still apply to most of her team archetypes since she's fairly easy to pilot.
Basics Of Using Emilie
Emilie is an Off-Field Dendro Sub-DPS who gains increased damage when an enemy is affected by the Burning reaction.
This is the reaction that results from combining Dendro and Pyro together. This makes her best teammates be Pyro characters or other Dendro characters that can help maintain the Dendro aura.
Lumidouce Case, the Core of Emilie's Kit
Emilie's kit revolves around her summoned entity, a Lumidouce Case that can deal Dendro damage to enemies. It starts from a Level 1 state and can be upgraded to Level 3 when it collects "Scents." Scents are dropped every 2s by enemies affected by burning. Upon leveling up, the Case gains increased AoE, damage and attack frequency.
Both Elemental Skill and Burst can Summon a Lumidouce Case
Emilie is one of the rare Sub-DPS characters that are able to use both Active Skills to summon their Off-Field damage dealing entity.
Dehya
is another similar character with this unique mechanic.
Elemental Burst summons a Level 3 Lumidouce Case
Only Emilie's Elemental Burst can summon a Lumidouce Case at Level 3 immediately. It has greater range and damage compared to Level 1 and 2 Cases. If there are existing Cases when her Burst is used, it will be stowed away and stored, to be used later when her Burst ends.
Resummons the Case or Returns the Level 2 Case
At the end of Emilie's Elemental Burst, it creates a new Level 1 Case or it returns the Level 2 Case stored earlier. The duration of the Case will be reset as well. As such, her Elemental Burst is more preferred to be used compared to her Skill when it comes to summoning her Lumidouce Case.
Can Lower Burning Damage on Allies
Burning is one of the reactions that can cause damage to your party if you are within range. Emilie has a passive that lowers the damage taken from the reaction, which increases the survivability of her team.

Burning Sub-Dps With Good Damage Potential
Basic Rotation Of Emilie
1. Swap to other supports to ensure Emilie also receives any buffs. Make sure to apply Pyro.
2. Swap to Emilie and use her Elemental Skill to deploy a Lumidouce Case.
3. Use her Elemental Burst to upgrade the Case to Level 3.
4. Swap to other supports to deploy their abilities or to your main On-Field DPS to deal damage.
5 .Repeat from number 1.
Since she also deals damage, Emilie wants to take advantage from any buffs that the team can give beforehand.
Applying Pyro to an enemy first will also ensure her Lumidouce Case collects a Scent immediately when it's placed on the field.
You will always want to use her Elemental Burst whenever it comes up, and its even possible to forego using her Skill and just use her Burst.
Elemental Skill Needs Scents From Burning Enemies
The Lumidouce Case is Emilie's primary source of DPS and it can be initially deployed through her Elemental Skill. It will first be summoned as a Level 1 Lumidouce Case.
When an enemy is Burning, they drop a Scent.
The Case will absorb the Scent and after collecting 2 Scents, the Case is upgraded to Level 2.
At this level, the Lumidouce Case fires an additional attack while also having its range increased.
Elemental Burst Is Important for More Range, Damage and Uptime
Emilie's Elemental Burst is key to improving the damage potential of the Lumidouce Case.
After using it, the Case is upgraded to Level 3, further increasing its range and damage. Instead of collecting Scents, the Level 3 Case will cause Scented Dews (strips of green smoke) to descend on to nearby enemies, which deal Dendro DMG.
This ability is her most important one due to the significant DPS increase from the Level 3 Case.
You cannot use Emilie's Elemental Skill while the Level 3 Case is on the field.
Any existing Lumidouce Case is 'stowed away', which will be redeployed once the duration of her Burst expires.
If there was no Case on the field beforehand, a Level 1 Case is generated instead. The Level 2 Lumidouce Case that is redeployed this way has its duration refreshed, so it's also a viable option to favor using her Elemental Burst always to refresh the Lumidouce Case over her Skill.
Best Paired With Pyro
Emilie's ideal teams include a Pyro character to help her maintain the Burning reaction
, which she needs for her Lumidouce Case to collect Scents. Though she is usable in other non-Burning teams, (such as in
Nilou
Bloom), Emilie was designed to be paired with strong Pyro supports like
Bennett
or an Pyro DPSes like
Xiangling
or
Lyney
.
Majority of Her DPS Is Not Skill Dmg
The Lumidouce Case is summoned using Emilie's Skill, but
the bulk of the damage comes from the Cleardew Cologne released by the Case, which is thanks to her passive, Lingering Fragrance.
The Cleardew Cologne is a long stream of green smoke that deals AoE Dendro DMG, and is not considered Skill DMG. This is why Emilie does not fully benefit from
Golden Troupe
.

Effect

Lumidouce Case
Effect
・Fires Puffs of Puredew at nearby opponents at intervals, dealing Dendro DMG.
・When nearby opponents are affected by Burning, they will give off Scents at intervals, and 1 Scent can be created this way every 2s. The Lumidouce Case collects nearby Scents. The Lumidouce Case will level up after gathering 2 of them, after which it will fire 1 extra Puff of Puredew when firing, while the DMG dealt by and DMG AoE of the above attack will also be increased.
・1 Lumidouce Case created by Emilie herself can exist at any one time. The Case starts at Level 1 and can go up to Level 2. If the Case does not collect any Scents for 8s while it is on the field, it will go back to Level 1.
Arkhe: Pneuma
Effect
At intervals, after Emilie creates a Lumidouce Case this way, a Spiritbreath Thorn will descend in front of her and pierce her opponent, dealing Pneuma-aligned Dendro DMG.

Aromatic Explication
Effect
Guiding the fragrances collected within the Case, Emilie converts them into pure Dendro energy, creating a Level 3 Lumidouce Case and stowing existing Cases away. While it exists, the Level 3 Lumidouce Case will not gather nearby Scents, but it will continuously cause Scented Dew to descend, attacking opponents within range and dealing Dendro DMG. During this time, 1 drop of Scented Dew will descend every 0.3 seconds, and 1 opponent can become the target every 0.7 seconds. When the duration ends, a Level 1 Lumidouce Case will be recreated. If a Lumidouce Case was stowed away when using Aromatic Explication, then the Case that was stowed away will be deployed instead, and its duration will be reset. While Aromatic Explication is active, the Elemental Skill Fragrance Extraction will not create a Lumidouce Case.

Lingering Fragrance
Effect
Each time it collects 2 Scents, the Level 2 Lumidouce Case will consume Scents and release Cleardew Cologne that deals AoE Dendro DMG equal to 600% of Emilie's ATK to opponents. This DMG is not considered Elemental Skill DMG.
Rectification
Effect
Emilie deals increased DMG to Burning opponents based on her ATK, with every 1,000 ATK increasing DMG dealt by 15%. The maximum DMG bonus that can be gained this way is 36%.
Headspace Capture
Effect
When the Lumidouce Case created by Emilie is on the field, all party members gain 85% Pyro RES against Burning DMG.

Freminet
dives into the fray of
Genshin Impact
as an on-field DPS character from Fontaine, joining the ranks alongside his siblings Lyney and Lynette. His combat style is surprising, focusing on Physical damage that belies his Cryo affiliation.

Clad with a claymore, Freminet presents an accessible alternative to Eula, offering players the opportunity to explore the depths of a physical DPS strategy without the steep Primogem investment. This guide peels back the layers of strategy in building Freminet, emphasizing the approaches that capitalize on his physical strengths to unleash his potential.
Freminet Shatterbloom Team
Freminet
:
Main DPS (on-field)
Xingqiu
:
Sub DPS (off-field)
Kuki Shinobu
:
Healer, Reaction support
Nahida
:
Reaction support
This composition
allows Freminet to act as the main DPS and Shatter trigger
, while Xingqiu ensures enemies are consistently frozen via his Elemental Burst. Nahida enhances reaction damage and
can also trigger Bloom Cores with Xingqiu’s abilities,
which are turned into Hyperbloom via Kuki Shinobu's Electro application.
Shatter occurs when a frozen enemy is hit by a
heavy attack, like those from a Claymore weapon or Geo abilities,
dealing extra physical damage. To execute Shatter, you need to first apply
Hydro, then Cryo to freeze the enemy,
and follow up with a heavy attack.
Kuki Shinobu also provides healing, ensuring the team's sustainability in battle
while also adding some Superconduct into the mix.
This
lowers the Physical resistance of enemies,
which maximizes Freminet’s damage.
Character
Action
Nahida
Trigger Nahida's Elemental Burst and Elemental Skill
in sequence, which will apply the Seed of Skandha onto enemies. Her Elemental Burst will create a domain in which
all reactions are boosted,
while her Elemental Skill grants
25 seconds of Dendro application
.
Xingqiu
Next, switch in Xingqiu and
activate his Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst
one after the other. The main goal of this is getting his Elemental Burst active, as it offers
15 seconds of off-field Hydro application and damage.
Kuki Shinobu
Switch in
Kuki Shinobu briefly to activate her Elemental Skill.
This will create a
ring around your active character lasting for 12 seconds,
that will provide
healing and Electro application.
When these interact with Bloom Cores generated by Xingqiu and Nahida, they’ll create
Hyperbloom — one of the best reactions in the game.
Freminet
Once all your characters have set up their off-field abilities, bring out Freminet and
make use of his Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst whilst normal attacking.
Viable Replacements
You don't need every unit on this team for it to work, and
there are other viable options to use that offer certain advantages:
Nahida
can be replaced with other
Dendro applicators like Baizhu, Yaoyao, and Collei.
Baizhu and Yaoyao may offer fewer Dendro applications, but make up for it by
providing sustainability for the team.
Xingqiu
can be replaced with other
Hydro applicators like Yelan and Furina.
All of these characters fulfill the same role of
consistent off-field Hydro application to react with Cryo and Dendro.
Kuki Shinobu
can be replaced with other
Electro applicators like Yae Miko, Raiden Shogun, and Fischl.
These alternatives will offer
more damage (and energy generation in the case of Raiden) at the cost of healing.
Freminet Melt Team
Freminet
:
Main DPS (on-field)
Xiangling
:
Sub DPS (off-field)
Bennett
:
Healer, Buffer
Shenhe
: Buffer
This team composition capitalizes on the
Melt reaction to multiply the damage dealt.
Between the reactions generated by your primary damage dealers Freminet and Xiangling, this is one of the best teams Freminet has if you want to Melt the enemy defenses.
Melt occurs when a Cryo attack is combined with Pyro,
or vice versa, resulting in increased damage. Triggering Melt with a Cryo attack amplifies the damage by 2x, and triggering it with Pyro results in a 1.5x damage increase.
Like many teams in Genshin,
Support units are key to a good team,
and that’s where Bennett and Shenhe come in. Both of these are
able to
buff the stats and damage of your damage dealers,
while also offering other utility, like
healing from Bennett and off-field Cryo application from Shenhe.
Character
Action
Shenhe
Start with Shenhe's Elemental Burst to blanket the area with Cryo whilst
augmenting Cryo damage for the team.
Follow with her
Elemental Skill to grant Icy Quill buffs,
which increase damage output for the active character based on her ATK.
Bennett
Then, switch to Bennett and
deploy his Elemental Burst
to create a field that
boosts the active character's attack and provides healing.
You can utilize
his Elemental Skill for Pyro particle generation,
and switch to Xiangling, allowing her to absorb these particles to replenish her energy.
Xiangling
Switch to Xiangling and
use her Elemental Burst to summon a ring of fire
around the active character. This ring
applies Pyro, enabling Freminet to trigger Melt reactions,
and can also interact with
Shenhe’s Burst for continuous Melt occurrences.
Freminet
Once all your characters have set up their off-field abilities, bring out Freminet and
make use of his Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst whilst normal attacking.
You can
either hold or tap Shenhe’s Elemental Skill.
Doing one or the other will apply
an additional boost to abilities and normal attacks respectively.
Viable Replacements
Apart from Xiangling, you don't need every unit
on this team for it to work, and there are other units you can also employ:
Shenhe
can be replaced with other
Cryo applicators like Diona, Kaeya, and even Ganyu.
Diona is the best replacement as
she can also offer healing,
along with a
substantial Elemental Mastery boost at C6.
Bennett
can be replaced with
Dehya or Thoma for increased off-field Pyro application
along with some manner of
defensive utility.
Freminet Freeze Team
Freminet
:
Main DPS (on-field)
Sangonomiya Kokomi
:
Healer, Reaction support
Kaedehara Kazuha
:
Buffer, Reaction support
Shenhe
: Buffer
This team composition allows for a smooth flow of elemental reactions,
keeping enemies in an extended state of Freeze,
even after delivering shattering blows with Freminet. With Shenhe and Kazuha’s presence, the team benefits from
enhanced damage output and more frequent elemental application,
making Freminet's attacks more potent and
ensuring that the Freeze state is easily maintained.
Kazuha is particularly effective when he wears the Viridescent Venerer artifact set, which
significantly reduces the elemental resistance of enemies upon being Swirled.
Meanwhile, Kokomi's role is twofold; she
applies Hydro consistently to enable the Freeze reaction
with Freminet's Cryo abilities and
provides indispensable healing
, which ensures the team's longevity in battle.
Character
Action
Shenhe
Start with Shenhe's Elemental Burst
to blanket the area with Cryo whilst
augmenting Cryo damage for the team.
Follow with her
Elemental Skill to grant Icy Quill buffs,
which increase damage output for the active character based on her ATK.
Kazuha
Swap to Kazuha to
utilize his Elemental Burst and Elemental Skill,
which swirl and magnify Shenhe's
Cryo application across a larger area continuously,
whilst providing
various buffs and debuffs.
Kokomi
Switch into Kokomi for her
Elemental Skill, providing steady Hydro application
necessary for the Freeze reaction while simultaneously
offering consistent healing.
Freminet
Once all your characters have set up their off-field abilities, bring out Freminet and
make use of his Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst whilst normal attacking.
Kokomi's Elemental Skill
normally has an eight-second downtime.
To circumvent this, switch her in after about ten seconds and
cast her Elemental Burst to reset the skill’s duration.
Viable Replacements
Not every unit is necessary for the team to work, and
you can replace them with the following options:
Shenhe
can be replaced with other
Cryo applicators like Diona, Kaeya, and even Ganyu.
Diona is the best replacement as
she can also offer healing,
along with a
substantial Elemental Mastery boost at C6.
Chongyun
can also make a fantastic substitute for Shenhe as he can create a Cryo field that
turns all of Freminet’s normal attacks from Physical into Cryo.
This is a valid option if you want to
double down on the ‘freeze’ aspect
of this team.
Sangonomiya Kokomi
can be replaced with
other
Hydro applicators like Barbara, Yelan, and Furina.
All of these characters fulfill the same role of
consistent off-field Hydro application to react with Cryo.
Kaedehara Kazuha
can be replaced with other
Anemo characters like Lynette, Sucrose, and Jean.
Essentially any character that can effectively equip the
Viridescent Venerer Artifact Set.

Strengths and Weaknesses
Like other characters, the archer has his pros and cons. It's worth going over them in more detail before you start building Sethos.
Strengths
Weaknesses
Most of his abilities depend on EM, which allows you to focus only on this stat and crits, without worrying about ATK.
He is a 4 ⭐ damage dealer character, so he loses in damage to many legendary characters unless he has a good build.
Has a useful passive that shows Sumeru specialties.
Playing with charged shots can be a bit of a hassle, especially for those using mobile devices.
Has intuitive gameplay without too many complicated rotations.
Sethos cannot move while the skill is fully charged, which can make it difficult to dodge in some cases.
Sethos is an Electro element character, which opens him way into squads through various reactions.
Without C6, Shadowpiercing Shot costs 20 energy to use, which forces you to more carefully monitor energy and place characters in your squad who will help with restoring it. A convenient solution to the problem is to decide on a rotation and play either through charged shots or through elemental burst.
Restores additional energy when triggerring a reaction with his elemental skill.
Plays well only in squads through reactions, since he is built exclusively into EM.

Teams
Sethos is a main DD who deals most of the damage in the party, so allies should be chosen based on his needs. Since his main stat is Elemental Mastery, allies should help put this stat into action. Sethos's best teammates are Dendro and Hydro allies, and any mono-elemental team should be avoided.
For an archer, you need to pick one or two sub-DDs that will apply elemental status, as well as supports that give shields, healing, or buffs. Keep in mind that normal attacks in the ult are considered charged shots, so supports that boost normal shots are not suitable for him.

Skills
Sethos is a primary damage dealer with two charge levels (the second one deals more damage and pierces through enemies). He uses the Electro element and EM as his primary stat, which is where most of his damage comes from. His ult puts the hero into a special stance, during which all of his normal attacks are infused and count as charged attacks. The elemental skill hits and additionally restores some energy if a reaction was triggered with it.

Talent Leveling Order
Elemental Burst (Q) ≥ Normal Attacks > Elemental Skill (E).
Sethos's damage output is based on his charged shots from his ult and normal attack. Elemental Burst should be maxed first. Normal attacks should be levelled up as well.
Elemental skill, unlike other abilities, scales from ATK and is only needed to pump energy. You can not pump it or do it depending on the availability of resources and desire.

How to play Sethos
Sethos' abilities are built around his charged shots, which can come in two different types:
Charged strikes of the second level (Shadowpiercing Shots) dealt with normal attacks.
Dusk Bolt instead of normal attacks during the stance after activating Elemental Burst.
Please note that Shadowpiercing Shots consume 20 points of Sethos' energy. This means that three full shots will consume all the energy that could have been spent on elemental burst. Based on this, if the character does not have enough energy regeneration, he can be played in two ways - through charged normal attacks or through the ult. Keep in mind that for a comfortable game, Sethos should have full energy at the beginning of each rotation. Otherwise, restore it with the help of supports beforehand.
Additionally, Sethos' elemental skill only restores energy if it triggers a reaction. Use support skills before pressing his E to inflict elemental status.

When playing through the ultimate, Sethos uses normal attacks, the damage of which is replaced by damage from the charged ones. In this case, characters that work with normal attacks (for example, Xingqiu or Yelan) are good as supports for reactions. A detailed description of the rotation through Sethos's ultimate:
Activate the skills of supports and secondary damage dealers.
Switch to Sethos and put him into a state of Twilight Meditation using his ultimate.
Hit enemies with normal attacks, which deal damage as charged shots, until the stance ends.
Reset an elemental skill to restore energy. If necessary, you can update status with your supports.
Use one Shadowpiercing Shot. If Sethos has C6, you can do another shot.
Repeat starting from step one as your abilities cool down.
Character Summary
Sethos is a fairly powerful epic damage dealer in Genshin Impact, whose gameplay revolves around using charged attacks. His playstyle can be comfortable even for players who don't like precision shots, as he spams regular attacks while in ult mode. Sethos focuses on Elemental Mastery and Critical Skills, so he has a simplified stat balance, allowing for a successful build even at low ranks. The hero is very friendly to newcomers both due to his simple gameplay and the variety of available gear.
However, he is still a 4⭐ character, inferior to many legendary heroes, if he doesn't have his consts. In addition, the Electro element limits his choice of teams and does not allow him to trigger powerful first-order reactions. Also, without C6, Sethos has energy issues: he doesn't have enough resource to simultaneously spam hold attacks and activate his elemental burst. This problem is solved by choosing one type of action to stick to during one rotation.
History (biography)
Sethos is one of the followers of the Temple of Silence, an ancient organization hidden in the Sumeru Desert that holds the knowledge left over from the civilization of King Deshret. In his early childhood, Sethos and another child, Cyno, were chosen as vessels for the power of the great Hermanubis, a pillar of Deshret and a priest, which resided within the Ba Fragments. The Fragments successfully resonated with both children, so each of them received their own part of the priest's power.
However, Cyrus, a professor at the Academy who was working with Bamoun, the head of the Temple of Silence, decided to stop the experiment and took
General Cyno
with him. Sethos remained in the Temple and was raised according to all their traditions. Later, during a ritual battle, the archer lost his Ba Fragment, but was still able to replace Bamoun and become the new head of the organization.

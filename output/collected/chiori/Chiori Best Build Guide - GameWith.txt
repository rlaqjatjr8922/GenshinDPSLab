Chiori is a 5-star Geo character that wields a Sword in Genshin Impact. She is an off-field Geo damage dealer who summons automaton dolls with her Elemental Skill to attack nearby opponents. Her damage is known to have dual-scaling with DEF and ATK. Lore-wise, Chiori is a renowned fashion designer from Inazuma who now runs the "Chioriya Boutique" in Fontaine.

What Kind of Character is Chiori?
Chiori is an
Off-Field
Geo Sub-DPS who summons a Tamoto doll with her Elemental Skill, and these are her main source of damage.
She usually only summons one doll, but when she is paired with Geo Characters with Geo Constructs like
Zhongli

Her DPS at C0 outside of her ideal Geo teams can be a little subpar when compared to other Off-Field Sub-DPSes.

Roles & Playstyle of Chiori and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field DPS Chiori
Chiori is designed to be an Off-Field Geo DPS where her damage is primarily from her Tamoto Dolls, which are best paired with party members that create Geo constructs. Use her Elemental Skill to summon the Tamoto Dolls before swapping to your On-field DPS.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field DPS Chiori
Pros
- Core gameplay is easy to pickup.
- Damage is competitive as an Off-Field DPS when paired with her ideal teammates.
- Does not strictly need Constellations or her Signature Weapon to be good for pure Geo teams.
Cons
- DPS may be underwhelming without teammates who can make Geo constructs.
- Her kit limitations make her less flexible and only the best choice in pure Geo teams.
- Requires C1 to be usable outside of her ideal Geo teams.

Basics Of Using Chiori
Chiori is a Geo character who deals damage mainly through her Tamoto Doll from her Elemental Skill.
She fits best in teams with Geo Constructs to boost her dolls' damage. Her Skill has unique traits: reactivating it at the right moment lets you instantly switch to the next party member, and holding it allows you to blink to a selected spot, which helps with exploration.
Best with Other Geo Characters
Chiori's ability to deal damage regardless gives her the ability to be used in different teams, but she was designed to perform at her best when paired with other Geo characters,
especially with the ones who can create a Geo Construct, like
Zhongli
with his pillar or
Albedo
with his flower. This is because one of her passives gives her 1 extra Tamoto Doll on the field while also increasing the damage they deal after a Geo party member deploys a Geo Construct.`
Burst is Optional
Chiori's Elemental Burst, "Hiyoku: Twin Blades", is a single instance of burst damage scaling off her ATK and DEF, however
it isn't important to her total DPS and can be used every other rotation.
It does have decent area of coverage but overall, it is recommended to only use her Elemental Burst whenever its available, so she doesn't need to have too many Energy Rechagre% stats on her artifacts.
Decent On-Field Geo DPS
Chiori also has the option to be played as the main DPS in a team, where you proceed to use Normal Attacks after pressing her skill.
She won't be as competitive as other dedicated On-Field Geo DPSes like
Arataki Itto
and
Navia
, but it allows you to explore more playstyles with Chiori if you want to do so.
Elemental Skill, Burst and Abilities Guide for Chiori
How To Use Chiori Skills & Passives
How To Play Chiori
▼Decent Off-Field Geo damage from her dolls.
▼Switches to another character and summon a Geo Construct.
▼Summons 2 Tamoto when there are nearby Geo constructs.
▼Double tap for ES → switch to next character.
▼Provides Geo Infusion with higher Constellations.
▼ Burst deals decent damage but is not necessary.
▼ Hold Skill can be used for exploration.
Decent Off-Field Geo Damage From Her Dolls
Basic Rotation Of Chiori
1. Swap to other supports before Chiori and use their skills, preferably to summon Geo constructs.
2. Swap to Chiori and use her Elemental Skill to summon the Tamoto Dolls
3. With her Ascension Passive, use Elemental Skill again to immediately swap to the next party member on your team, ideally your On-Field DPS, for the Tamato Dolls to perform coordinated Attacks
4. Use her Elemental Burst if available.
5 .Repeat from number 1.
Chiori is able to provide constant and powerful Geo damage while being Off-Field with her Elemental Skill that summons the Tamoto Dolls. Her Ascension Passive, "Tailor-Made", allows the dolls to perform coordinated Geo attacks with your On-Field DPS whenever they attack.
Switches To Another Character Before Summon Her Dolls
The Tamoto dolls periodically deal Geo DMG to enemies. If there is/are any Geo Structures in the area, Chiori will summon 2 dolls instead, effectively doubling her damage.
Elemental Skill Summary
・Her Elemental Skill will make a slash attack and simultaneously summon Tamoto (duration 17s)
・Long press for dash direction.
・Tamoto inflicts DMG to nearby enemies for every 3.6s.
・Deals AoE Geo DMG based on Chiori's ATK and DEF.
・Summons 2 Tamoto whenever there is a Geo construct on-field.
Summons 2 Tamoto When There Is A Geo Constructs Nearby
During Chiori's Elemental Skill, she will summon one (1) Tamoto doll, and if ever there are another geo constructs that is/are on-field, Chiori's Tamoto dolls will increase from 1 → 2 dolls on the field.

This will greatly boost the power of her Elemental Skill, so using this frequently is recommended

The Elemental Skill damage uses buffs it receives in real-time. Because of this, on-field buffs like Gorou's Elemental Skill does not work with it. If you want to buff Chiori's DMG use characters that buff the whole party like Furina.
Burst Deals Decent Damage But Is Not Necessary
Chiori’s Elemental Burst is a simple damage-dealing ability that scales off both her ATK and DEF. It has low Energy cost and can deal decent damage with the right build, but it’s not strong enough to justify stacking Energy Recharge. Use it when it’s up, but prioritize artifact stats that help her hit her main goals.
Hold Skill Can Be Used for Exploration
Holding Chiori's Skill allows her to aim in a direction where she will do the initial slash. This can be used to close distances against enemies or in exploration to move forward or to reach high places.
Should You Pull for Chiori's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Choice Depends on Preference
Recommendation for Navia or Noelle Teams
C1 >
Uraku Misugiri
> C2
C1 is Good for Geo Teams Without Constructs
Notably, Chiori's C1 provides an additional doll provided she's in a team with another Geo party member while also allowing her "The Finishing Touch" passive to trigger just by activating her burst. This matters a lot for teams with Geo carries that do not have Geo constructs, such as Navia and Noelle teams, since the "The Finishing Touch" without C1 only triggers when a Geo construct is on the field.
With the extra doll contributing a fair amount to the team's total damage and the ability to now have all her passives active in these teams makes for a convincing pull option if you want to have Chiori with Navia or Noelle.
Recommendation for Itto or Other Carry Teams
Uraku Misugiri
> C1 > C2
C1 Won't Work with Geo Constructs
Conversely, Chiori's C1 does not give any benefit to Geo teams that do have constructs, such as Itto or Albedo teams, and will also not work in teams where she's the only Geo character.
If you still want to vertically invest in Chiori but do not want to use her with Navia or Noelle, consider getting Uraku Misugiri instead.
Her Signature Weapon will still be a noticeable upgrade to her 4-star alternatives, and is slightly better than other 5-star crit swords.
Uraku Misugiri Is the Best Generalist Sword
In addition to being Chiori's best in slot,
the Uraku Misugiri is considered the best among the universal sword options due to having the highest Crit DMG substat in return for less base Attack and an unconditional damage bonus in its passive.
The other half of its passive is an unconditional Normal Attack and Elemental Skill DMG bonus, and the boost is significantly increased provided you have a Geo character on the team.
The introduction of
Xilonen
who is the ideal support for a noticeable amount of teams makes it so that the full passive of Uraku Misugiri is easily triggered. But even without a Geo character, the unconditional buff it gives is appreciated by the majority of sword characters that want to deal damage.
Consider getting Chiori's Signature Weapon for the versatility of use, especially if you do not own a generalist sword in the first place.

Passive Skill
Tailor-Made
Effect
Gain different effects depending on the next action you take within a short duration after using Fluttering Hasode's upward sweep. If you Press the Elemental Skill, you will trigger the Tapestry effect. If you Press your Normal Attack, the Tailoring effect will be triggered instead.
Tapestry
・Switches to the next character in your roster.
・Grants all your party members "Seize the Moment": When your active party member's Normal Attacks, Charged Attacks, and Plunging Attacks hit a nearby opponent, "Tamoto" will execute a coordinated attack dealing 100% of Fluttering Hasode's upward sweep DMG as AoE Geo DMG at the opponent's location. DMG dealt this way is considered Elemental Skill DMG.
・"Seize the Moment" last 8s, and 1 of "Tamato"'s coordinated attack can be unleashed every 2s. 2 such coordinated attacks can occur per "Seize the Moment" effect duration.
Tailoring
・Chiori gains Geo infusion for 5s.
When on the field, if Chiori does not either Press her Elemental Skill or use a Normal Attack within a short time after using Fluttering Hasode's upward sweep, the Tailoring effect will be triggered by default.
The Finishing Touch
Effect
When a nearby party member creates a Geo Construct, Chiori will gain 20% Geo DMG Bonus for 20s.
Brocaded Collar's Beauteous Silhouette
Effect
When any party member is wearing an outfit apart from their default outfit, or is wearing a wind glider other than the Wings of First Flight, your party members will obtain the Swift Stride effect:
Movement SPD is increased by 10%.
This effect does not take effect in Domains, Trounce Domains, and the Spiral Abyss. Swift Stride does not stack.

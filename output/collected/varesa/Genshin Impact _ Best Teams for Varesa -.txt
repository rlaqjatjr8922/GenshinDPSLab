Ō¢╝ Hypercarry Varesa

- Varesa's high DMG multipliers as a Plunge Attack DPS and rotations that can be longer than the norm can be used as a hypercarry with all supports aimed at buffing her.
- Reactions are not the main focus since Electro as an element has many reactions that can be utilized.
- Furina, Xianyun, & Iansan buffs Varesa's DMG and provide enough HP changes for Furina's fanfare to stack faster.
Ō¢╝ Aggravate Team
ŌśģŌśģŌśģŃā╗Ńā╗
- Aggravate increases the damage dealt by a single Electro attack, provided that you maintain the Quicken aura on enemies.
- While viable, the output is underwhelming in comparison to her other teams since her damage isn't made up of several hits, which Aggravate prefers.
- Nahida & Baizhu can keep a constant Dendro aura and activate Dendro resonance for additional EM.
- Aggravate's DMG increases with EM. This means building Varesa will have the added priority of having EM on her.
Varesa is an Electro On-Field DPS whose primary damage is concentrated in her Plunging Attacks. Her teams will often gravitate towards a setup that buffs her own damage output with dedicated supports, and there are clear teammates she clearly prefers over others.

Chevreuse provides Electro and Pyro RES shred whenever the team triggers the Overload reaction, with the primary condition being the party can only consist of Electro and Pyro characters. Varesa naturally performs well with this team-up, especially since Chevreuse also provides an ATK% buff with her Skill (hold) and more Electro DMG bonus with Chevreuse's C6.

No.
Procedure
1

2
Swap to Durin. Use his Skill (x2) then Burst to enter his Confirmation of Purity mode.
3
Switch to Chevreuse. Use her Skill (hold) then Burst.
4
Swap to Iansan. Start with her Skill, into a Normal Attack (x1), then Burst.
5
Return to Varesa. Start her typical combo with (SkillŌåÆCharged AttackŌåÆplunge) x2. Activate her special Burst when it's up.
6
Repeat the combo in step 5 one more time. Follow it up with (Charged AttackŌåÆplunge) x2 with an optional (SkillŌåÆCharged AttackŌåÆplunge)x1 as an ending.
7
Swap to Durin. Repeat steps 2, 3 and 4. This is the second rotation.
8
Switch to Varesa. Open the combo with Skill, into a Charged Attack, into a plunge. then do the combo stated in step 5. End the combo with a (Charged AttackŌåÆplunge).
9
Repeat from step 1.
Note
While the notation is labeled as Charged Attack in (SkillŌåÆCharged AttackŌåÆplunge), in practice you only need to tap the Normal Attack button since Varesa's Skill automatically changes her Normal Attack into a Charged Attack.
The combo above essentially alternates between two rotations, the first one where Varesa starts it, and the second where Durin starts it. This allows the team to carefully maintain Chevreuse's RES Shred with timed Overload triggers. If this seems overwhelming to you, you can opt to do just the first rotation instead, since it should be enough to clear most content.

- Iansan is essentially Varesa's dedicated support, since she can easily max out Iansan's movement-based ATK% buff while also triggering the full effect of the Cinder City set. Additionally, she also acts as Varesa's Electro battery by generating Electro particles for Varesa.
- Bennett is one of the closest alternatives due to his generous ATK% buff. He can easily replace a low-constellation Iansan, but is less valuable when your Iansan is C6.
- Fischl after completing her Witch's Homework task offers a modest ATK% buff when in an Overload team, which allows her to be a viable but weaker substitute.
- Ororon can be an appealing option for Energy and to ensure you still have access to the full

Xiangling
- While Durin brings RES shred, he can be substituted by any Off-Field Pyro applicator.
- Mavuika is naturally the strongest option since she also benefits from the ATK% buffs and RES shred found in this team, so her personal damage in this team (especially if you do specific combos) are pretty respectable still.
Chevreuse
-
- Chevreuse is the character that can elevate an Overload team into meta status, especially with her C6. Without her, an Overload team's damage can feel underwhelming, and may struggle to clear specific content.
- C6 is ideal, but even at C0 Chevreuse is better than any other option for this team.
Does Varesa Need Iansan or Chevreuse?
Varesa needs Chevreuse to complete her strongest team, which is Overload.
Even at C0, Chevreuse offers a hefty 40% Electro and Pyro RES shred and 40% ATK buff, which are all desirable buffs for an attack-scaling DPS like Varesa. Conversely, while Iansan is designed to be her most synergistic support, you can easily replace her with alternatives and find marginal differences in performance. There is no true alternative for Chevreuse (in an Overload team) in comparison.
Note
No C6 Iansan and Chevreuse?
- You don't need to get either of their C6s for Varesa to perform well. These constellations are good if you want to start reaching Varesa's peak potential outside of investing in 5-star constellations or weapons.
- Additionally, note that getting copies of specific 4-stars on a banner is not guaranteed. So you don't need to go out of your way to pull for their C6s if you don't have the means to.
- You're likely to clear content with whatever Iansan or Chevreuse constellation level you have currently.

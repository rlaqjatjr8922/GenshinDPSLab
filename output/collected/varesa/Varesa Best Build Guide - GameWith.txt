Varesa is a 5-star Electro character that wields a Catalyst in Genshin Impact. She functions as an on-field, main DPS that plays around her "Nightsoul Bar" and Nightsoul-aligned Plunging Attacks. By using a Charged attack and her Elemental Skill, "Riding the Night-Rainbow", Varesa can fill her Nightsoul Bar and enter her enhanced state, "Fiery Passion" to deliver a larger, more powerful Plunging Attack. Lore-wise, Varesa is a ravenous competitive eater that enjoys daily bouts of exercise. During her youth, she aspired to become a hero and is always cautious about using her strength and abilities to do the right thing.

What Kind of Character is Varesa?
Varesa is an
On-Field
Electro DPS whose primary damage comes from her Plunging Attacks. She has a unique Elemental Burst mechanic where she can make use of a no cooldown, low Energy cost version once you max out her
Nightsoul
bar.
Varesa in her highest DPS team, Overload, sits among the top meta teams, but less so without. While she can make use of different teammate combos, whether she can contest with the likes of
Mavuika
or
Nefer
largely depends on having her best-in-slot supports at max constellations.

Roles & Playstyle Of Varka and His Strengths and Weaknesses
On-Field DPS That Wields Two Claymores
Varka is an Anemo DPS whose unique mechanic involves his dual-wielding claymore playstyle.
Once he enters his special state after using his Skill, Varka's claymores will each deal different damage types: one will deal raw Anemo damage, while the other takes on the element of one of his teammates (either Pyro, Hydro, Electro, or Cryo). His main combo seems to involve a mix of Normal, Charged Attacks, and Skill usages.
Varka also has a Hexerei passive, which means his best teams should include other
Hexerei
characters.
Pros and Cons of On-Field DPS Varka
Pros
- Straightforward gameplay with a unique mechanic.
- Some flexible teammate options, which may include future characters that are yet to release.
- Benefits from Hexerei teammates.
Cons
- Mastering his Normal Attack combos and rotations will be necessary to maximize his damage output.
- Meta currently favors Lunar Reaction teams, so he might perform a little worse than most Lunar Reaction DPSes in endgame content.

Team Rotation of Varesa and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for On-Field DPS Varesa
Basic Rotation For On-Field DPS Varesa
1.Start with the supports and deploy their abilities. Order the support usage by doing abilities with longer abilities first before moving to the shorter ones.
2. Switch to Varesa. Use her Skill, then Plunge after being launched into the air. Repeat this one more time after until you see her special Elemental Burst charged up.
3. Use her Special Burst once you see it become available. It shouldn't play a full length animation if you did it right.
4. Do steps 3 to 4 one more time.
5. While remaining on Varesa, you can do Charged Attack into Plunge (x2), followed by another Skill into plunge.
6. Repeat from step 1.
Varesa's combo and rotations may look complex on paper, but in practice the combo translates into a smooth and comfortable gameplay loop that's easy to grasp. The only real problem is the enemy tracking with her Skill dash can be a little frustrating against highly mobile enemies or in multi-target scenarios, but it's nothing you can't work around.
Varesa Combo in Notation
[ Skill > Charged Attack into Plunge > Skill > Charged Attack into Plunge > Special Elemental Burst ] x2 > (Charged Attack into Plunge) x2 > Skill > Charged Attack into Plunge
Executing the combo above correctly will have Varesa entering her Fiery Passion state after her first Charged Attack into Plunge, before exiting it once you use her special Elemental Burst.
Basics Of Using Varesa
Varesa is an Electro
On-Field
DPS whose extremely mobile playstyle involves dashes and Plunging Attacks.
She has several unique kit features that contribute to her unique gameplay: one being how the
Nightsoul
mechanic of all Natlan-based characters is integrated into her abilities, and the fact that her Elemental Burst has special low Energy cost, no cooldown version.
Nightsoul Point Mechanics
Varesa is a character from
Natlan
, and like the rest of the Natlan cast, she also relies on the use of
Nightsoul
points that fill up her Nightsoul bar. All characters from Natlan have unique ways of utilizing this mechanic.
In Varesa's case, she enters the Fiery Passion state once her Nightsoul bar is completely filled.
Charged Attack Causes Her to Plunge
Varesa's Charged Attack causes her to leap upwards instead of being a regular elemental attack. This lets you use her Plunged Attack immediately after, which contributes to her Nightsoul Bar.
Enter Fiery Passion State with Skill
Varesa's Skill causes her to dash forward into an enemy, filling her Nightsoul Bar halfway. Her next Normal Attack becomes an automatic Charged Attack, allowing her to plunge right after, which will fill her Nightsoul Bar all the way.
A full Nightsoul Bar allows Varesa to enter the Fiery Passion state, which enables a second usage of her Skill with increased damage.
Varesa will then exit Fiery Passion after running out of Nightsoul points. Note that you first need to use a Plunging Attack in order to actually activate the state.
Hold Skill Can Be Used for Travel
If not used for her special Elemental Burst,
you can hold Varesa's Skill so she enters her Sudden Onslaught state instead, causing her to dash rapidly in any given direction
, consuming her accumulated Nightsoul points in the process. Varesa will keep dashing so long as she has Nightsoul points or
Phlogiston
when you're in Natlan.
Elemental Burst Has Two Versions
Her Elemental Burst essentially has two versions. The first version is the regular Burst that needs the full Energy Cost to activate, filling her Nightsoul bar and letting her enter the Fiery Passion state.
The second version, her special Elemental Burst, has a lower Energy cost and no cooldown, and is the most common choice due to its fast animation and superior DPS output.
Varesa is able to use the second version while her Fiery Passion state is active for a brief amount of time.
Note!
Varesa's main combo will center around managing her Nightsoul points to maintain the Fiery Passion state. Jump to her combos
here
.
Passive Encourages Other Natlan Teammates
Varesa gains an ATK boost whenever another teammate triggers a Nightsoul Burst, which makes it almost mandatory to pair her with her synergistic Natlan supports like
Iansan
or
Mavuika
.

Plunge Attack Based On-Field Electro DPS
Basic Rotation Of Varesa
1. Start the rotation with the supports. Deploy the abilities with longer durations first before shorter ones.
2. Switch to Varesa. Start with her Elemental Skill, then follow-it up with a plunge. Do this one more time.
3. Use her special Elemental Burst.
4. Repeat steps 2 to 3 one more time before finishing it off with Charged Attack into plunge (x2).
5. Repeat from step 1.
Varesa's combo may seem overwhelming at first, but her abilities seamlessly flow into each other with ease, making it very intuitive once you start learning how to do it.

which usually happens after the first time you use her Skill and then Plunge combo. As stated, she gets 1 free usage of the Tap version of her Skill while the state is active, which increases the damage of the Charged and Plunging Attack that you do after.
Doing this combo right depletes her Nightsoul Bar and ends Fiery Passion. Once you exit the state, she goes into Apex Drive where her special Elemental Burst will become briefly usable.
Because her special Burst is the best in terms of DPS efficiency,
you'll want to ensure to exit Fiery Passion by plunging after you use the free Skill charge.
This is to avoid accidentally triggering her normal Elemental Burst.

Skill Can Be Used for Exploration
Holding Varesa's Skill activates her Sudden Onrush state, where she will continuously use her Nightsoul Points to rapidly dash in any direction you input.
After you use up all her Nightsoul Points, Sudden Onrush will start consuming stored up
Phlogiston
when you're in Natlan.
This dash functions similarly to the Tatankasaur ability when indwelt,
so you can use her to explore and solve puzzles in
Atocpan

Who Is Varesa?
A New Character From Natlan
Varesa is a character from Natlan. She belongs to the Collective of Plenty tribe. Varesa's design and official introduction hints to her being based on Luchadoras.
Student Of Iansan
Iansan introduces Varesa as her most unusual pupil. Stating that she is laid-back and seemingly harmless but once she dons on her mask, her rush tactics are somewhat aggressive.
A Known Food Lover
Bulletin boards of certain Natlan restaurants often feature Varesa's marvelous gastronomic feats. Her official art also features her holding food. Varesa herself shares her love of food by sharing her own harvest with others.

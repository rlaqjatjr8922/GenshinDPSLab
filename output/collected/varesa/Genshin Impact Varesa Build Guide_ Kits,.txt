Genshin Impact Varesa Guide: Kit Explained
Normal Attack: By the Horns
Varesa’s Normal Attack consists of up to three hits, all dealing Electro damage. Since she is a Catalyst user, all of her attacks are infused with Electro by default, making her a reliable source of elemental applications.
Elemental Skill: Riding the Night-Rainbow
Verissa excels in swift, electrifying footwork, dashing forward to deal Electro damage while gaining Night Soul points, enabling a follow-up charge attack without stamina cost. Entering the Fiery Passion state enhances her skills, granting a free skill charge and empowering her plunge attack to consume all Night Soul for a powerful burst.
Her rotation revolves around skill → charge → plunge, repeating with a second skill before finishing with a Burst. However, some attacks miss out on buffs like Codex’s crit rate boost, as it only activates after Night Soul consumption.
Elemental Burst: Guardian Vent!
Verissa’s Burst launches her into the air for a high-impact flying kick, dealing AoE Electro damage and maxing out her Night Soul points, instantly triggering
Fiery Passion
(unless her skill is on cooldown).
While the base burst damage is low, the enhanced version (after generating Night Soul) is stronger. After an empowered plunge in Fiery Passion, she activates
Apex Drive
, granting interruption resistance and enabling a
special burst,
a high-damage, low-energy alternative that bypasses her normal burst cooldown.
This acts like a supercharged plunge attack, but the tight timing makes interruption resistance crucial. Her burst primarily serves as a Night Soul generator rather than a major damage source.
Varesa’s Passives
Genshin Impact Varesa (Image via HoYoverse)
Tag-Team Triple Jump!
After using her skill, Verissa gains
Rainbow Crash
, enhancing her next plunge attack with extra motion value (increased damage).
This only applies to regular plunges triggered after her skill,
not
her special burst. If she skips using her skill, she loses this damage boost, making skill-based plunges more optimal.
The Hero Twice-Returned!
When a nearby party member triggers a
Nightsoul Burst
, Varesa’s ATK increases.
Night Realm’s Gift: A Torch That Incinerates Evil
After Nightsoul points have been fully depleted, Varesa will switch to using Phlogiston to maintain the
Sudden Onrush mode
from her Nightsoul’s Blessing.
Genshin Impact Varesa Build Guide: Constellations
In this section, we will break down the constellations for Varesa in Genshin Impact.
C1
Upgrades her
Rainbow Crash
passive, making all plunge-related damage boosts (including her
Q2 special burst
) use the higher
180 motion value
instead of the weaker 50.
This adds
620 total motion value per rotation
, but the damage increase is still smaller than her signature weapon’s boost, making
her weapon a higher priority than C1
.
C2
Allows Verissa to enter
Apex Drive
(enabling her
special burst/Q2
) after
every plunge
, not just every other one. Also boosts interruption resistance and refunds
23 Energy per rotation
(effectively reducing her burst cost to
7 Energy
).
This makes her
self-sufficient in Energy
, allowing her to
skip skill usage
in teams like Xianyun plunge spam. A
strong constellation
, but still not worth prioritizing over her signature weapon.
C3
Increases her
Elemental Burst level by 3
, improving its damage. While her burst gains more value at
C2+
, it’s still not a major part of her damage, making this a
modest upgrade
.
C4
Makes her
normal burst
worth using by adding
500% motion value
to her next plunge (capped at
4,000 ATK
). Additionally, if she’s in
Fiery Passion or Apex Drive
, her burst deals
100% more damage
(~30% actual increase). A
solid
but not
game-changing
constellation—only relevant if you want to use her normal burst.
C5
Increases her
Normal Attack talent level by 3
, a minor boost since most of her damage comes from
plunges and bursts
. One of her
weakest constellations
.
C6
Grants
unlimited Energy
(removing burst uptime issues) and
120 Crit Value (CV)
to
all plunges and bursts
, drastically increasing her damage. Also improves exploration utility. While strong,
Energy isn’t a major issue at C2+
, making this more of a
luxury upgrade
rather than a necessity.
As far as Constellations go, Varesa’s constellations are pretty average at best.
C2 is the best stopping point
. If you want her to deal more damage,
C6 is a powerful but optional luxury
. In order of best to least best constellation:
C2>C6>C1>C4>C3>C5

Genshin Impact Varesa Build Guide: Artifacts to use
Long Night’s Oath (BiS)
Varesa works exceptionally well with this artifact set. Her primary gameplay loop revolves around skill, then change, then plunge attack, and this set synergizes perfectly by granting stacks upon hitting enemies with a skill, charge attack, or plunge attack.
This results in nearly
100% uptime on the set bonus
, effectively granting a
100% damage increase
to plunges, making it the clear best-in-slot option.
Obsidian Codex
If Veresa is not using a crit-rate weapon or is not overcapped on Crit, then this set is good for her. The correct rotation for maximizing the set involves setting up before initiation, beginning with skill, then dash, plunge, though this can slow down gameplay and may be too annoying for most players.

Best Team Comps for Varesa in Genshin Impact
As a plunge attack character, Vera has strong synergy with Xianyun. While her base motion value is high enough that Xianyun is not her only viable team, she remains a particularly powerful support for plunge-focused compositions. However, the primary strength of Xianyun is her synergy with Furina, allowing for high uptime on strong buffs.
This means
Xianyun teams
that do not include
Furina
are significantly weaker. One of Veresa’s strongest teams consists of
Xianyun, Furina, and Iansan
. If Iansan is unavailable,
Bennett
can serve as a substitute, though the team will perform worse. This composition benefits from high buff uptime and consistent plunge damage.
Image via HoYoverse
In the current meta, however, pulling for new carries is less attractive due to the dominance of characters like
Mavuika
, who has become a top-tier carry with the release of Iansan’s buffs. While Veresa’s best teams are strong, they do not reach the power level of Mavuika’s top compositions. That said, Veresa still performs better, though she lacks the versatility to slot into as many archetypes.
Beyond
Xianyun-Furina teams
, another top-performing composition for Veresa includes
Chevreuse and Mavuika.
Veresa can effectively utilize 18-second rotations, giving her an edge in teams that require extended uptime. Additionally, her synergy with Iansan makes her particularly valuable in certain
Chevreuse-Mavuika setups
. The primary drawback of these teams is their reliance on high off-field damage.

Is Varesa worth pulling in Genshin Impact
Verissa is a strong on-field
Electro DPS
with a unique plunge-focused playstyle, offering solid damage output in her best teams. However, her release in the shadow of powerhouses like
Mualani
makes it difficult to recommend her purely for meta reasons. While she competes with
Clorinde
as the best on-field
Electro carry,
even surpassing her in optimal setups, her performance drops significantly outside of these specialized compositions.
Unlike Clorinde, who remains versatile across multiple team archetypes, Verissa struggles in suboptimal scenarios, making her highly reliant on specific supports. For players who enjoy her plunge-heavy mechanics and already have
Xianyun
and
Furina
,
Verissa
can be a fun and competitive choice. But if meta efficiency is the priority, existing units like
Mualani
or
Neuvillette
still dominate, leaving Verissa in a niche where she’s strong but not essential.
Ultimately, Verissa is a
good but not must-pull
character—worth considering for those who like her playstyle or want a fresh Electro DPS, but hard to justify for players focused solely on the strongest teams.

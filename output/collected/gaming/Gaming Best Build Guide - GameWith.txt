Gaming is a 4-star Pyro Character that wields a Claymore in Genshin Impact. His gameplay focuses on dealing strong damage via enhanced Plunging attacks and his Elemental Burst allows him to continuously deal plunge attack damage. Lore-wise, Gaming is a Gold-Standard Guard of the Sword and Strongbox Secure Transport Agency. He is the head of the "Mighty Mythical Beasts" Wushou Troupe.

Roles & Playstyle Of Gaming and Its Strengths and Weakness
On-Field Pyro DPS Gaming
Gaming is best as an On-Field Pyro DPS since his Plunging Attacks playstyle require on-field time. His ability to empower his Plunging Attacks and natural ability to do Plunges without requiring Xianyun's Elemental Burst makes him a great Plunging Attack DPS.
Pros and Cons of DPS Gaming
Pros
- 4-star Pyro DPS that can deal powerful CRITs due to the high damage multipliers from Plunging Attacks.
- Can do Plunge Attacks without needing external support.
- Great in Melt or Overload teams.
- HP drain mechanic in Elemental Skill allows him to use Marechaussee set for the CRIT Rate buff.
Cons
- Elemental Skill does not deal damage making Viridecent Venerer/Scroll set holders need another Pyro character to trigger their buffs/debuffs.
- Needs to come contact with an enemy to ascend into air for Plunging Attacks.
Best Build for Gaming
As a Burst-reliant DPS, Gaming needs Energy Recharge along with ATK and CRIT stats to ensure consistent damage. If there's an ATK buffer like Bennett, you can use ER sands.

Team Rotation of Gaming and Combos
Basic Gaming Team Rotation
Basic Team Rotation for Gaming
1. Activate support abilities.
2. Switch to Gaming and use his Elemental Burst.
3. Spam his Plunging Attack combo: [Elemental Skill → Plunging Attack] until his buff runs out.
4. Repeat from Step 1.
Gaming's team rotation follows the typical DPS character rotation. While it sounds simple on paper, Gaming's Elemental Skill is known to miss hitting enemies and preventing Gaming from jumping in air. As such, it's best to use dashes or move towards enemies to ensure he can do Plunging Attacks.
Wait for Elemental Application if Vaporize/Melt Team
Gaming's Skill CD reset can cause slow Sub-DPS characters to apply their Element after Gaming's next Plunging Attack, causing loss of DPS. It's recommended to time the Plunging Attacks even though it will cause less hits but more damage in the rotation.
Basics of Using Gaming (Beginner Guide)
Pyro DPS for Melt, Vaporize or Overload Teams
Gaming is a Pyro DPS that deals powerful damage with his Plunging Attacks. His Plunging Attacks have a high ceiling for damage in Melt or Vaporize teams but he can be used in Overload teams as well.
Special Plunging Attacks Reduces His HP
If Gaming uses his Elemental Skill to jump and then deal a Plunging Attack from there, he will use a Special Plunging Attack that deals Pyro DMG. This attack will reduce his HP, allowing him to use Marechaussee set.
Crimson Witch is another very good artifact set for him if you have other sources of CRIT stats such as his weapon and Constellation 6.
Marechaussee VS Crimson Witch
Marechaussee Hunter
- 2-pc set effect doesn't benefit Gaming since he doesn't deal Normal or Charged Attack DMG.
- Only the CRIT Rate buff is good for him but it gives lesser damage amp compared to Crimson Witch's various buffs.
Crimson Witch Of Flames
- Buffs reaction damage, making it great for reaction teams.
- Gaming can easily trigger all 3 stacks and increase the Pyro DMG Bonus from the starting 15% to 37.5% in total.
Elemental Skill Makes Him Jump High
Gaming's Elemental Skill is his main method for using Plunging Attacks. Even if Xianyun or C4 Hexerei Albedo helps Gaming in jumping into air for a Plunging Attack, it won't deal Pyro DMG since his Special Plunging Attack comes from his Elemental Skill effect.
Elemental Burst Allows Gaming to Spam His Skill
Gaming's Elemental Skill has a short cooldown of 6s but it's not enough as a DPS. His Elemental Burst amends it by summoning Man Chai who can link up with Gaming to reset his Skill's CD.
Summary of Gaming's Elemental Burst
- Heals 30% of his HP.
- Summons Man Chai who will reset his Skill CD then leaves the field.
- Man Chai is resummoned every time Gaming does his Special Plunging Attack.
Passive Incentivizes Having Above 50% HP
Gaming's Passive makes his Special Plunging Attack deal 20% more damage if his HP is 50% or more. However, his Special Plunging Attack reduces his HP. Gaming has sources of healing as well but it's best to bring a healer with him.
Gaming's Sources of Self Healing
Passive
- Heals 1.5% of Max HP once every 0.2s for 0.8s after his Special Plunging Attack hits enemies.
Elemental Burst
- Heals 30% of Max HP.
Constellation 1
- Man Chai resetting Gaming's Skill will heal 15% of his HP.
Elemental Skill, Burst and Abilities Guide for Gaming
How To Use Gaming's Skills & Passives?
How To Use?
▼ Plunging Attack Pyro DPS
▼ Elemental Skill To Do Plunging Attacks
▼ Elemental Burst For Spamming Elemental Skill & Healing
▼ Gains More Plunging DMG if >50% HP
Plunging Attack Pyro DPS
Gaming is a 4-Star Plunging Attack Pyro DPS who can be used for powerful Vaporize or Melt attacks or in an Overload team focused on buffing Pyro DMG. He utilizes both Active Skills to do Plunging Attacks and to reduce and heal his own HP.
Elemental Skill To Do Plunging Attacks
Gaming's Elemental Skill is used to collide with an enemy then jumps Gaming high up in the air to do a Special Plunging Attack infused with Pyro. If he misses with his Skill, Gaming can't jump up into air and the Skill goes on cooldown.
Elemental Burst For Spamming Elemental Skill & Healing
▲A Lion Mark will appear once Man Chai goes to Gaming.
Gaming's Elemental Burst summons Man Chai who can link up with Gaming to reset his Skill CD. Man Chai can be resummoned if Gaming does his Special Plunging Attack with >50% HP and have his Skill CD reset again, allowing for a loop of Elemental Skill then Plunging Attack.
Gains More Plunging DMG If >50% HP
Gaming's Passive effect gives him more Plunging Attack DMG if his HP is above 50%. If he's below 50%, he gains more healing. Gaming is able to heal himself but it's best to use him with a dedicated healer.
Should You Pull for Gaming's Constellation Or Best Weapon?
Aim for C6 if You Are Able To
Constellation Priority
C6 > C4 > C2
C6 Makes Him a Capable Carry
Prior to C6, Gaming's performance as a carry will be in-line with other 4-star DPS, which is usually unremarkable. After obtaining C6, he starts outputting enough damage to rival even the more recent 5-star carries, provided you have all the pieces to make his best team.
If you'd like Gaming to become a competitive on-field DPS. getting his C6 is ideal.
Gaming is a Pyro main DPS that focuses on using Plunge attack as a main source of damage. He has a wide AoE and competitive damage making him a good DPS for your teams.

Done Deal (Elemental Burst)
Effect
Gaming enters Wushou Stance, briefly applying Pyro to him, recovering a fixed amount of HP, and summons his companion, the Suanni Man Chai, to smash into his target, dealing AoE Pyro DMG. After bashing its target, Man Chai will roll to a nearby location before moving towards Gaming. When it links up with Gaming, Man Chai will leave the field and reset the CD for Gaming's Elemental Skill, Bestial Ascent. While Wushou Stance is active, his resistance to interruption is increased, and when Gaming lands with Charmed Cloudstrider attack or completes the forward pounce attack from Bestial Ascent with over 50% HP, he will summon Man Chai again.Each Gaming can only have 1 Man Chai on the field simultaneously. This effect will be canceled once Gaming leaves the field.

Who Is Gaming?
Professional Wushou Dancer
Gaming was inspired by his father's Wushou dance (lion dance) and wanted to become a professional Wushou dancer despite his father's disagreement. Gaming's idle animations and character design are also inspired from Wushou dance.
Works Mainly As a Guard of a Transport Agency
While Gaming founded a Wushou dance troupe, it was financially unstable which led to him working as a guard. He thrived in his job and is considered a gold-standard guard.

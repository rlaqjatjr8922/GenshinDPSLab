1.
Geo Traveler Introduction
Geo Traveler is a 5-star
Geo
Sword Character DPS and Support
in Genshin Impact who mainly focuses on providing Geo Constructs, CRIT Rate
buffs and the occasional
Crystallize
shield. Their
Burst and Skill also have high damage multipliers, which can enable them to be
a DPS.
This guide will focus on Geo Traveler's best
weapons, builds, artifacts, teambuilding and more!
Geo Traveler's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Free character and constellations.
Good supportive skills.
Low field time requirement.
Useful for exploration and puzzles.
Weaknesses
Skill and Burst pushes enemies away if used incorrectly.
Skill can be difficult to use.
Base stats are generally low, limiting damage potential.

2.1.
Traveler Geo Support Build
The Geo Traveler's Support build focuses more around staying off-field,
swapping in to cast your Elemental Skill and to keep your Elemental Burst
running at all times. The walls created by your Burst last 15s and
has a 15s cooldown, which means you can keep 100% uptime as long as you
generate the 60 energy required to cast the Burst. Furthermore, the 6th
Constellation extends the duration by another 5 seconds, making the walls
last longer than the cooldown. So for this build, we are building
Energy Recharge to maintain this uptime. With the addition of
Navia
, Geo Traveler has seen some renewed use as a
Support, as Navia greatly benefits from the CRIT Rate buff on the Burst with
the first Constellation,
Invincible Stonewall
,
and the Energy that the Traveler can provide.

Between the two builds, the Traveler tends to work best in the role of a
Support. This is because of the way the Traveler works when it comes to
leveling talents, as every talent needs to be leveled for each of
the Traveler's elements. This makes it very expensive to try and use the
Traveler offensively for multiple Elements, especially because
Geo
is not the strongest element to deal damage with.
However, when you use the Traveler to support, leveling your talents comes
secondary. This means you can still use them while building your team, instead
of depending on the talents to deal damage. You still want to level them as it
is more damage, but less important.
You will not need to build a lot of Energy Recharge for either of the
builds, although it is crucial to keep up 100% uptime for the support build.
The reason you don't need to build that much is due to the 4th Constellation,
Reaction Force
which can return up to 25 Energy per
cast. This means your Burst has an effective cost of 35 instead of 60, on
top of the Skill having a short cooldown. In the DPS setup, you can afford
to wait for the Travelers energy particles, however as Support you will need
about 180-200% Energy Recharge to maintain uptime.
Outside this, the Support build is very flexible and can fit into most teams
that have open Flex Slots, hence why it is mainly recommended to build the
Geo Traveler as a Support character instead of DPS. Focus on getting your
Energy Recharge into a comfortable range, and then slowly
get artifacts that increase your damage while maintaining your Energy
Recharge.
3.
Combos and How to Play Geo Traveler
The Geo Traveler is very simple to play, as with most of their Elements.
Your
Starfell Sword
Skill has 6s cooldown, so you
want to cast that as much as possible. Meanwhile
Wake of Earth
has a 15s CD, meaning that when you play
with the Geo Traveler, you end up quickswapping characters regularly.
Keeping up your Burst and getting as many Meteorites out is important. As a
Support, this is what makes the Geo Traveler great.
If you have built the Geo Traveler for DPS instead, you want to maximize
their on-field time. You would want to start out with casting the
Wake of Earth
, before swapping through your off-field
characters to benefit from the increased CRIT Rate. You
then swap back to the Traveler to use their
Starfell Sword
and then keep swapping around to to other characters to use their skills.
You will use their Normal Attack sometimes, especially if you have off-field dps
who trigger damage on hit like
Xingqiu
. With both the Skill
and the Burst having a very low Cooldown, you will end up swapping into the
Traveler very frequintly.
The hardest part of using the Geo Traveller correctly is placing your
Skill, as it will push displacable targets away from it. This means that you
generally don't want to summon it infront of your target, as it will then make
it harder for yourself to hit the target. Try to aim it behind or on the
side of your enemy. When holding the Skill, you get a small circular aim on
the ground that you can move around before releasing. The circle motion will
ripple, making it look like its formed by 3 circles. Try aim the skill so that
the target is hit between the area created between the 2 outer circles.
Outside of this, you can use the Skill to block missiles aswell as use it to
climb with.

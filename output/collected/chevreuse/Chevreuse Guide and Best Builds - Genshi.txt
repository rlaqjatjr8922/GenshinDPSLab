Chevreuse Guide and Best Builds
Chevreuse is a 4 Star Pyro Polearm Support character, who specializes in
increasing the damage done by
Pyro
and
Electro
characters. She shreds resistance within those elements, gives ATK % buffs to
party members, as well as Elemental DMG Bonus with constellations. She also
provides some healing on top. The caveat is that she only provides these
bonuses to Pyro and Electro characters.

1.
Chevreuse Introduction
Chevreuse is a 4-star Pyro Polearm Support Character in Genshin Impact who
mainly focuses on provides large amounts of DMG buffs to
Pyro
and
Electro
Characters and some healing to the team. She only
really works with those two elements, having it as a hard requirement in some of
her passives.
This guide will focus on Chevreuse's best
weapons, builds, artifacts, teambuilding and more!
Chevreuse's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Amazing support for Pyro and Electro.
Easy and accessible weapons in general.
Able to be built to burst aswell at C4.
Weaknesses
Very limited in team building, due to requiring the team to be only Pyro/Electro.
Building for DMG will partly lower her supportive skills (less healing and ATK % to the team).
Overload can be a drawback due to the reaction's inherent knockback.

As Chevreuse is majorily a support character, building damage on her is not
necessary. This can be seen in her stat priority, as we focus on getting 40,000
HP to cap out her first Ascension Talent. Technically her best weapong is
Symphonist of Scents
,
however it requires you to have HP% as mainstat on all 3 artifact pieces + 31% HP
across all 5 pieces in substats to cap out her second ascension skill. This can
be quite hard to get without sacrificing ER %, so if you are not comfortable with that,
it is recommended to continue to use
Favonius Lance
.
Noblesse Oblige
is
best if you have enough Energy Recharge to be able to use
her Burst with every rotation and if you do not have another character in your
party who already uses it. Otherwise,
Ocean-Hued Clam
is better. An
alternative to Ocean Hued Clam is
Song of Days Past
, use whichever
of the two sets you have better substats with. Running double 2p sets is also
viable for the same reason (Mainly aim for 20% HP, 20% Energy Recharge or 15%
Healing Bonus sets if possible)
If you do not have
Favonius Lance
, then
Black Tassel
is a great replacement and usually very easy to get, as its a 3-star weapon.
Dialogues of the Desert Sages
is also a strong pick, having HP %
and giving a lot of energy. This removes most if not all of her Energy Recharge
needs however, this is an event weapon, so might not be available to everyone.
When leveling her Talents, prioritize her Skill first, then Burst. Do not bother
with the Normal Attack talent, as you will only really have to use Normal/Charged
Attacks if you do not proc Favonius on your Skill/Burst.
Building Chevreuse as a Burst Support is possible as shown if you
like the playstyle. Do note that while this works, it is not always a large
enough damage increase to Chevreuse's personal damage to make the on-field time
worth it. The Selection of weapons is a lot more offensive this time, which also
makes the list of weapons a lot longer. Do remember to consider that there is no
need to give
Engulfing Lightning
to Chevreuse if you are also
using
Raiden Shogun
in that team composition, since Raiden
benefits a lot more from the weapon.
3.
Combos and How to Play Chevreuse
Chevreuse is relatively simple to use. Swap her in after triggering
Overload
once, to trigger her 2nd Ascension Passive
Vanguard's Coordinated Tactics
. You will give your party up
to 40% ATK, while the Overload you triggered before swapping in Chevreuse
shreds the enemies
Pyro
and
Electro
RES by 40%.
This RES Shred is a better equivalent to
Viridescent Venerer
, since
you shred both resistances at once.
If you decided to build Chevreuse as a Burst Support, then you want have her
on-field more. This is due how
The Secret to Rapid-Fire Multishots
(C4) allows her to cast her Elemental Skill,
Short-Range Rapid Interdiction Fire
, thrice, as it will reset
twice within 6s after casting her Burst. It is important that you hold her Skill
and do not tab it, as it will otherwise not reset. Her skill also has a 15s
cooldown normally, so make sure to not cast it before her Burst. Outside of
this, Chevreuse's Skill has an internal 10s CD on generating particles, so you
can't funnel more energy with this.

(C6). However as with other 4-star

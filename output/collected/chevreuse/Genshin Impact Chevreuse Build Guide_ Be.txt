: Chevreuse Abilities and Build Overview
Chevreuse is great in Overload teams – Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chevreuse is primarily a support designed for Pure Overloaded teams. Her biggest strength comes from the buffs she gives when you trigger Overloaded while using only Pyro and Electro teammates.
Once that condition is met, she gives your team:
40% Pyro and Electro RES shred
Up to 40% ATK buff based on her Max HP
Healing through her Elemental Skill
Extra Pyro/Electro DMG Bonus at C6

What that basically means is that Chevreuse is not a generalist support like Kazuha or Bennett. She is much more specialized. But in the teams she belongs in, she is excellent.
Her gameplay and rotation is also very straightforward. You just need to trigger Overload, Use Hold Skill to activate her ATK buff, swap into your damage dealer and go to work. If you’re using 4-piece Noblesse Oblige, you’ll also want to cast her Burst regularly.
Chevreuse Pros and Cons
Chevreuse is easy to build, but she does come with strict team-building rules.
Strengths
Strong support for Pyro and Electro carries
Can provide RES shred, ATK buff, and healing in one slot
Doesn’t absolutely need Burst every rotation
Works well at C0
Scales well with investment and becomes much stronger at C6
Weaknesses
Her best passive is locked to Pyro + Electro only teams
Healing is mostly single-target before C6
Overloaded can still be annoying against lighter enemies due to knockback
She’s much less flexible than top universal supports

There are a lot of weapons to pick from. – Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chevreuse wants one of three things from her weapon:
HP
Energy Recharge
Team buffs
Because of that, some very cheap options are still extremely competitive.
Best-in-Slot Weapons
Symphonist of Scents
This is one of Chevreuse’s best premium options. It gives her strong stats while also adding more offensive support through healing-based buffs. It’s especially good if you can already meet her ER needs through artifacts.
Favonius Lance
This is the most practical weapon for many players. It gives Energy Recharge, helps battery the team, and makes rotations much smoother. If your Raiden, Xiangling, Clorinde, or other teammates are energy-hungry, this often ends up being her most useful weapon overall.
Black Tassel
A surprisingly strong budget option. It gives a huge chunk of HP%, which makes it very easy to hit the 40,000 HP threshold for her full ATK buff. If you don’t need extra ER, this is still one of her best low-investment weapons.
Best 4-Star and F2P Weapons
Dialogues of the Desert Sages
An excellent event weapon if you have it. It gives both HP and Energy, which are exactly the two stats Chevreuse wants most.
Rightful Reward
A craftable Fontaine polearm that works well on her, especially if you need extra HP and occasional Energy. It becomes easier to use at C6 thanks to her teamwide healing.
The Catch
A decent fallback if you’re building her for Burst damage or hybrid use. It’s not her ideal support option, but it works.

Best Chevreuse Artifact Sets in

Chevreuse has a ton of artifact set options. – Image Credit: HoYoverse
Artifacts are where Chevreuse becomes very flexible. If she’s in a support role, most of your job is just to cap HP and meet ER needs.
4-Piece Noblesse Oblige
This is still the most universal and recommended set for Chevreuse. If no one else in your team is holding it, she usually should. It adds another 20% team ATK buff on Burst, stacking beautifully with the rest of her support kit.
4-Piece Song of Days Past
A workable alternative, especially if someone else already has Noblesse. It can provide extra damage to the active character, but it’s a little less consistent in AoE content.
4-Piece Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City
A viable option, especially in teams with Natlan characters who can make better use of the set. Chevreuse can trigger it herself, but she usually isn’t the ideal holder unless the team specifically wants it.
4-Piece Emblem of Severed Fate
The best option if you’re building her for personal damage. It helps with ER and boosts Burst damage significantly.
Other Usable Sets
Other Usable Sets Depending on your build and account, these can also work:
4-piece Tenacity of the Millelith
4-piece Golden Troupe (better at C4+)
4-piece Crimson Witch of Flames
2-piece mixed HP / ER / Healing sets
2-piece mixed ATK / Pyro / Burst / Skill sets for damage builds

Best Chevreuse Teams in
Genshin Impact
6.5
She fits in basically any overload team you can think of. – Image Credit: HoYoverse
Chevreuse is best in Pure Overloaded teams, meaning every character is either Pyro or Electro. She can also work outside that restriction at
C6
, but that’s a separate case.
Best Overloaded Teams
Chevreuse + Raiden Shogun + Xiangling + Bennett
This is still one of her strongest and most consistent teams. It has:
Great damage
Strong buff stacking
Excellent Energy generation
Smooth rotations
Raiden benefits heavily from Chevreuse’s support, while Xiangling and Bennett bring classic Pyro pressure and utility.
Chevreuse + Yoimiya + Fischl + Bennett
One of the easiest and most comfortable Chevreuse teams to play. Yoimiya is especially good here because Overloaded knockback is much less annoying when your carry is ranged.
Chevreuse + Clorinde + Fischl + Thoma / Dehya
A very solid Overloaded core for Clorinde. Fischl keeps Electro flowing, while Thoma or Dehya help with survivability and interruption resistance.
Chevreuse + Arlecchino + Fischl + Bennett
A powerful option if you want to run Arlecchino outside of Vaporize. It’s not her highest ceiling team, but it’s still very strong and much simpler to play.
Chevreuse + Varesa + Mavuika + Iansan
One of the stronger modern Overloaded shells, especially in current team-building discussions around newer units. This team stacks buffs well and gives Varesa a very comfortable environment.
Chevreuse + Mavuika + Ororon + Bennett
A strong option if you’re using Natlan synergy and can take advantage of Scroll of the Hero of Cinder City or other overlapping buffs.
Best Budget / F2P Teams
If your roster is more limited, Chevreuse still has plenty of usable options.
Chevreuse + Yanfei + Fischl + Beidou
A very respectable lower-cost team. Yanfei’s range helps offset Overloaded knockback, and Fischl/Beidou provide steady Electro uptime.
Chevreuse + Xiangling + Fischl + Bennett
Still one of the best low-cost cores in the game. It’s not fancy, but it works.
Chevreuse + Razor + Fischl + Thoma
A more off-meta but functional budget option. Not top-tier, but perfectly playable if you like Razor.
If you’re building Chevreuse anytime soon, these are some of her strongest partners right now.
Is Chevreuse Good Outside Overloaded Teams?
Mostly, not really at C0. At baseline, Chevreuse is designed for Pyro/Electro-only teams, and that’s where she gets most of her value. Once you reach C6, though, she becomes much more flexible because her DMG Bonus and healing fit her in other teams.
That includes:
Overvape
Some Furina-based teams
Select Pyro/Electro hypercarry teams
Still, unless you have C6, don’t force her into random teams.
Should You Build Chevreuse in 2026?
Yes. if you have the right teammates. Chevreuse is not a must-build for every account, but if you play characters like Raiden, Clorinde, Yoimiya, Arlecchino, Varesa, Mavuika, Fischl, Xianglin then she is still very worth building in April 2026.
She’s easy to build, doesn’t need insane artifact stats to work, and gives a lot of value in the exact teams she’s meant for. And unlike some supports that you just use to fill out a slot, Chevreuse is the kind of character that makes an Overloaded team actually click.

Chevreuse Gameplay
Chevreuse’s gameplay centers around her specialized
Overload
niche, which transforms the often-overlooked reaction into a massive offensive engine for her team. Her kit primarily scales on her
Max HP
, allowing her to function as both a potent healer and a versatile buffer who replaces traditional Anemo grouping units by providing raw resistance shred and ATK bonuses.
Her
Elemental Skill
, Short-Range Rapid Interdiction Fire, allows her to quickly fire her musket to deal AoE Pyro DMG. When Chevreuse utilizes the
"Hold"
version of the skill, she enters an Aiming Mode that deals increased damage; however, the true power lies in her
Overcharged Ball
. By triggering an Overload reaction, Chevreuse gains an enhanced shot that deals significantly more damage in a larger area, acting as the primary trigger for her most powerful team-wide buffs. Following this shot, the active character receives a steady stream of healing, perfectly sustaining the team's momentum.
When she drops her
Elemental Burst
, Ring of Bursting Grenades, Chevreuse launches a large explosive projectile that deals AoE Pyro DMG before splitting into several
secondary explosive shells
. These smaller shells burst shortly after, maintaining Pyro pressure on the field and providing a unique form of crowd control through their inward knockback. This ensures that even while enemies are being bounced by Overload explosions, they remain within the reach of your Pyro and Electro carries.
Her
Passives
are what truly define her as a "niche queen," rewarding strict team building with unparalleled utility. One of them triggers a massive
40% Pyro and Electro RES shred
whenever an Overload reaction occurs, provided your team consists exclusively of those two elements. Meanwhile, her other passive directly grants her entire party an
ATK bonus
scaling off her total Max HP (up to 40%), ensuring your elemental carries hit as hard as possible. It makes her gameplay incredibly rewarding for those who embrace the "fire and thunder" playstyle, effectively turning Chevreuse into a field commander who dictates the flow of battle with every shot.
Chevreuse Talent Priority
Chevreuse’s abilities mainly affect her supportive utility and healing. However, as a niche support character, her overall personal damage contribution is relatively low, so you can
choose not to level up her talents at all
if you are only after her passive RES shred and ATK buffs. That said, if you want to maximize Chevreuse’s healing and personal burst damage, you can prioritize upgrading her talents in the following order:

Elemental Skill - Short-Range Rapid Interdiction Fire
This is Chevreuse's "bread and butter" for team utility. It provides the essential healing that scales off her
Max HP
and is the primary trigger for her
Overcharged Ball
mechanic. Firing this enhanced shot after an Overload reaction is what activates her massive team-wide ATK buff. Maximizing this talent is essential to ensure your active character stays healthy while she provides peak offensive support.
Elemental Burst - Ring of Bursting Grenades
As a support who frequently rotates in, you will use her Burst to maintain Pyro pressure. This ability provides respectable AoE damage and generates secondary explosive shells that offer vital crowd control by pulling enemies slightly inward. Prioritizing this talent makes sure her brief time on the field contributes meaningful damage and battlefield manipulation before swapping back to your main carry.
Normal Attack - Line’s Bayonet Thrust (Modified)
This is the biggest trap when building her! While her musket animations look stylish, her standard spear strikes on the ground deal negligible damage compared to her abilities. Chevreuse is designed to be a "Quickswap" support, meaning she should almost never use her Normal Attacks during a proper rotation. To save resources, you should leave this talent at level 1 and focus entirely on her Skill and Burst.

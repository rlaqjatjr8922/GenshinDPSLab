Who is Yelan in Genshin Impact?
If you’ve ever wanted a character who feels like she walked straight out of a spy thriller and into the world of Teyvat, Yelan is your girl. She’s a five-star Hydro bow user who brings a ton of style, mystery, and damage to any team. On paper, she works for Liyue’s Ministry of Civil Affairs, but you’ll rarely find her behind a desk. Instead, she operates in the shadows, pulling strings and gathering intelligence from her base at the Yanshang Teahouse.
Yelan is the person Liyue calls when a problem needs to disappear quietly. She’s a master of espionage with an uncanny ability to get the information she needs, whether through charm, intimidation, or her intricate network of informants. She’s sharp, enigmatic, and always seems to be one step ahead of everyone else. Let’s get to know the woman behind the dice.
Her Story and Role in Liyue
Yelan’s official title at the Ministry of Civil Affairs is a bit of a front. While she is technically on the payroll, her real work is far more clandestine. She is Liyue’s top intelligence agent, tasked with protecting the nation from threats that can’t be handled through official channels. Think of her as the secret weapon of the Liyue Qixing. Her
official lore
reveals that even Lady Ningguang makes exceptions for her frequent absences, a testament to how vital her covert operations are. She’s a ghost, moving through the underworld to dismantle plots before they ever reach the surface, ensuring Liyue’s prosperity from the shadows.
Breaking Down Her Design and Style
From her sleek asymmetrical outfit to the dice she carries, every part of Yelan’s design screams elegance and danger. She’s a five-star Hydro character who wields a bow with deadly precision, and her entire aesthetic reflects her dual nature. The intricate details on her clothing blend modern style with traditional Liyue motifs, while the fur collar on her coat gives her an air of luxury and authority. It’s no surprise the community considers her one of the best-designed characters in the game. She perfectly balances aesthetics with incredible utility and damage, making her a powerhouse who also happens to look amazing while clearing the Spiral Abyss.
How She Fits into the Liyue Storyline
Yelan isn’t a character you’ll see attending public functions with the rest of the Liyue Qixing. Her role keeps her on the fringes, dealing with the messy, under-the-table conflicts that threaten the nation. She was
introduced in the 2.7 update
and played a pivotal role in the Chasm Archon Quest, where her intelligence-gathering skills and calm demeanor in a crisis were on full display. She’s the one who investigates the Fatui, tracks down ancient threats, and manages a vast network of spies. Her work is the unseen foundation of Liyue’s stability, proving that the nation’s strength isn’t just in its commerce and contracts, but in the secrets it keeps.
What Makes Yelan’s Combat Style So Good?
Yelan isn’t just another pretty face in Liyue’s mysterious underworld; she’s a powerhouse on the battlefield. As a 5-star Hydro Bow user, she brings a fast-paced, high-damage playstyle that feels incredibly smooth and satisfying. She primarily shines as a Sub-DPS, meaning she deals a ton of damage even when she’s not the active character on the field. This makes her a fantastic addition to a huge variety of teams, where she can set up powerful elemental reactions without hogging all the screen time.
What really sets her apart is her unique kit. Unlike most characters who rely on their Attack stat, Yelan’s damage is almost entirely based on her maximum HP. This is a game-changer because it allows you to build her for massive damage while also making her surprisingly tanky. You get the best of both worlds: an offensive force who can also take a hit. Her skills are designed for quick swaps and sustained off-field damage, making her a flexible and efficient operator who perfectly complements your main damage dealer. From her speedy Elemental Skill to her relentless Elemental Burst, every part of her kit works together to drown enemies in a flood of Hydro damage.
Why Her Damage Scales with HP
One of the first things you’ll notice about Yelan is that her abilities scale with her Max HP. This is a huge deal. For most characters in Genshin Impact, you’re constantly trying to stack Attack and Crit stats, but Yelan flips the script. By building up her health pool, you directly increase her damage output. This makes her build path incredibly efficient, as artifacts with HP% are generally more common than ATK% ones. It also means she’s naturally durable, so you won’t have to worry about her getting knocked out by a stray hit. This unique
HP scaling
is the foundation of her strength, allowing her to be a top-tier Sub-DPS who contributes massive damage without being a glass cannon.
Using Her Elemental Skill: Lifeline
Yelan’s Elemental Skill, Lingering Lifeline, is where her fluid combat style truly begins. When you activate it, she enters a rapid phantom-like state, allowing you to sprint across the battlefield and through enemies. Any opponent you run through gets marked by the Lifeline. When the skill ends (either by timing out or by activating it again), the Lifeline explodes, dealing a blast of Hydro damage to all marked enemies based on her Max HP. This skill is fantastic for repositioning, grouping enemies, and applying Hydro for reactions. It’s also a great tool for world exploration, letting you zip across terrain with ease. It’s a versatile and powerful part of her
arsenal
.
Mastering the Breakthrough Barb
After being out of combat for five seconds, Yelan enters a “Breakthrough” state, which makes her next Charged Aimed Shot charge almost instantly. This special shot, called a Breakthrough Barb, deals AoE Hydro damage that also scales with her Max HP. You don’t have to wait, though. A much more practical way to trigger this is by using her Elemental Skill. Hitting enemies with Lifeline gives you a high chance to immediately gain the Breakthrough state. Weaving a quick Breakthrough Barb into your rotation after using her skill is a core part of maximizing her personal damage. It’s a simple yet crucial mechanic that adds another layer of damage to her
fast-paced kit
.
Unpacking Her Elemental Burst: Exquisite Throw
This is the crown jewel of Yelan’s kit. Her Elemental Burst, Depth-Clarion Dice, deals an initial wave of AoE Hydro damage and summons an “Exquisite Throw.” This floating die follows your active character around and launches a coordinated Hydro attack every time they use a Normal Attack. It also attacks when Yelan’s Lifeline explodes and hits an opponent. All of this damage scales with her Max HP, making it a relentless source of off-field Hydro application and damage. This
Elemental Burst
is what makes her one of the best Sub-DPS characters in the game, enabling constant Vaporize, Electro-Charged, or Bloom reactions for your team.
How to Build the Perfect Yelan
Building Yelan is all about turning her into a powerhouse Sub-DPS who deals a relentless stream of Hydro damage from off-field. Since her abilities scale with her Max HP, your focus will be on stacking HP, Crit, and enough Energy Recharge to keep her burst active. Getting her build right means she’ll be the engine of your team, setting up powerful elemental reactions and melting through enemies with ease. Let’s get into the specifics of her weapons, artifacts, and talents to make your Yelan an absolute force on the battlefield.
Choosing the Best Weapons
Yelan’s weapon choice is crucial for maximizing her damage. Her signature 5-star bow, Aqua Simulacra, is hands-down her best option, providing a massive Crit DMG boost and an HP increase. If you don’t have her signature weapon, don’t worry—she has plenty of fantastic alternatives. The Elegy for the End is another great 5-star choice that offers Energy Recharge and a team-wide buff. For 4-star options, the Favonius Warbow is a community favorite because it solves her energy needs completely. The Stringless and Sacrificial Bow are also excellent choices that directly increase her skill and burst damage. Each
bow in Genshin Impact
offers different advantages, so pick the one that best balances your stats.
Finding the Right Artifact Sets
When it comes to artifacts, the 4-piece Emblem of Severed Fate set is tailor-made for Yelan. It gives her a much-needed Energy Recharge bonus and converts that ER into a massive damage increase for her Elemental Burst, which is the core of her kit. This is her best-in-slot set, and it’s worth farming for. If you’re still working on a good Emblem set, you can temporarily use a combination of 2-piece Tenacity of the Millelith (for HP%) and 2-piece Heart of Depth (for Hydro DMG Bonus). For main stats, you’ll want an HP% Sands, a Hydro DMG Bonus Goblet, and a Crit Rate or Crit DMG Circlet, depending on what your weapon and substats provide.
Which Talents to Prioritize
Optimizing Yelan’s talents is straightforward because her value is concentrated in specific abilities. Your top priority should be her Elemental Burst,
Depth-Clarion Dice
. This is where the majority of her off-field damage comes from, so leveling it up first will give you the biggest performance increase. Next, focus on her Elemental Skill,
Lingering Lifeline
, as it provides good damage and generates energy particles. You can safely leave her Normal Attack talent at level 1. She is not designed to be an on-field attacker, so investing resources into her Normal Attacks is generally a waste. Prioritizing her
talents correctly
ensures you get the most impact from her kit.
Farming Her Ascension Materials
To get Yelan to her maximum potential, you’ll need to farm her specific ascension materials. You’ll be spending some time on the beaches of Liyue collecting Starconches, so keep an eye out for those bright blue shells. For her boss material, you need to defeat the Ruin Serpent located in The Chasm to get Runic Fangs. Additionally, you’ll need to gather Insignias dropped by Fatui agents scattered across Teyvat. Planning your farming routes ahead of time can save you a lot of hassle. Using an
interactive map
can help you locate all the necessary resources efficiently, letting you ascend Yelan and get her back in the fight faster.
Creating the Best Yelan Team Comps
Yelan is incredibly versatile, slotting into some of the most powerful teams in the game as a top-tier Hydro Sub-DPS. Her strength comes from her consistent off-field Hydro application, which enables a ton of powerful elemental reactions. Whether you want to create massive explosions or lock enemies in place, there’s a Yelan team that’s perfect for the job. Let’s look at some of the best team structures you can build around her.
Building a Vaporize Team
This is one of Yelan’s most popular and powerful team archetypes. A Vaporize team pairs her with a strong Pyro DPS to trigger massive damage numbers. Since Yelan’s Elemental Burst applies Hydro so consistently, it creates the perfect setup for a character like Hu Tao or Yoimiya to come in and
trigger Vaporize
with their Pyro attacks. The synergy is fantastic; Yelan sets them up, and your Pyro carry knocks them down. For this to work, you’ll want to pair them with supports like Zhongli for shielding or an Anemo character like Kazuha to shred elemental resistance and group enemies together.
Setting Up Electro-Charged Teams
If you prefer a storm of continuous damage, an Electro-Charged team, often called a “taser” team, is an excellent choice. In this setup, you pair Yelan with Electro characters like Fischl, Beidou, or Raiden Shogun. Yelan’s Hydro application combines with their Electro attacks to create the Electro-Charged reaction, which zaps enemies with continuous damage. This composition is fantastic for dealing with groups of enemies, as the reaction can jump between them. It also provides a good amount of stagger, helping you control the battlefield while dealing consistent damage from a safe distance. It’s a really fun and active playstyle that keeps the pressure on.
Leveraging Double Hydro Synergy
Why have one amazing Hydro Sub-DPS when you can have two? Pairing Yelan with another Hydro character like Xingqiu is a popular strategy for a reason. This “Double Hydro” core provides a massive amount of off-field Hydro application, ensuring your main DPS can trigger reactions constantly. More importantly, it activates
Hydro Resonance
, which increases the entire party’s Max HP by 25%. Since Yelan’s damage scales directly with her HP, this is a direct and significant damage increase for her. This pairing also helps solve any Energy Recharge issues, allowing both characters to use their powerful bursts more frequently.
Assembling a Freeze Team
For a team focused on crowd control, nothing beats a Freeze composition. Yelan is a fantastic addition to Freeze teams because her off-field Hydro application makes it easy to keep enemies frozen solid. When you pair her with a powerful Cryo DPS like Ayaka or Ganyu, you can lock enemies in place, preventing them from attacking you. This gives your Cryo carry a wide-open window to unleash their strongest attacks without fear of interruption. An Anemo support like Venti or Kazuha is almost essential here to group enemies together, making it even easier to freeze them all at once.
How to Optimize Yelan’s Gameplay
Getting the right build for Yelan is only half the battle; the other half is mastering her in combat. She’s a fast-paced character whose effectiveness depends on smart energy management, good positioning, and seamless team rotations. Once you get the hang of her flow, you’ll see just how much she brings to a team. It’s not just about activating her Burst and swapping out; it’s about understanding the rhythm of her skills to keep her damage consistent and her energy full. Let’s get into the practical side of playing Yelan and turn her from a good character into a great one. These tips will help you get the most out of her kit, whether you’re just starting or looking to refine your strategy.
Managing Her Energy Recharge
Yelan’s power is tied directly to her Elemental Burst, so keeping it ready is your top priority. Without enough Energy Recharge (ER), you’ll have awkward downtime where your main DPS is on the field with nothing to react with. For a Yelan without her first constellation (C1) or a battery like Raiden Shogun, you should aim for around 180% to 200% ER. Using an ER weapon like the Favonius Warbow is a fantastic way to solve her energy needs, as it generates extra particles for the whole team. If you manage to get her C1, which gives her an extra charge of her Elemental Skill, you can relax a bit and focus more on HP and Crit stats.
Positioning Yelan in a Fight
As a bow user, Yelan is best used from a distance. While her HP scaling makes her tougher than many other ranged characters, you still want to avoid taking direct hits. Her Elemental Skill, Lifeline, is your best friend for repositioning. You can use it to dash through smaller enemies, apply Hydro, and get yourself to a safer vantage point. The best part about her kit is that her
Elemental Burst
works entirely off-field. This means you can pop her Burst, use her Skill to generate energy, and immediately swap to your main damage dealer, who can take the spotlight while Yelan provides support from the sidelines.
Are Her Constellations Worth It?
For most players, Yelan is perfectly powerful at C0. However, if you love her playstyle and want to invest more, some of her constellations offer significant upgrades. Her C1 is arguably the best, giving her a second charge on her Elemental Skill. This makes managing her energy a breeze. C2 adds another water arrow to her Burst, which means more damage and more Hydro application for reactions. Her C6 is a game-changer, turning her into a formidable on-field DPS for a short time, but it’s a massive investment. Overall, C1 is a fantastic quality-of-life upgrade, while the others are great but not necessary. You can read the full details on all of
Yelan’s Constellations
to decide for yourself.
Perfecting Your Combat Rotations
A good rotation ensures you’re maximizing your team’s damage output. With Yelan, the goal is to have her Burst active while your main DPS is on the field. A typical sequence looks something like this: use Yelan’s Skill, then immediately use her Burst. This allows her to catch the energy particles generated by her Skill. After that, swap to your support (like Kazuha to swirl Hydro) and then to your main DPS (like Hu Tao or Yoimiya) to start triggering reactions. Mastering these
combat rotations
will make your fights smoother and faster, ensuring Yelan’s Hydro application never goes to waste.
Why These Team Synergies Work
Yelan is a powerhouse on her own, but her true potential shines when you build a team that plays to her strengths. It’s not just about throwing your strongest characters together; it’s about creating a symphony of skills and reactions where Yelan is a key conductor. Her ability to deal damage from off-field while consistently applying Hydro is the secret sauce that makes so many team compositions click. When you understand
why
certain characters work well with her, you can move beyond just copying builds and start creating powerful teams that can clear even the toughest content.
Picking the Right Supports
The key to a great Yelan team is understanding her role as a Sub-DPS. Her main job is to use her Elemental Burst and then swap out, letting her coordinated attacks apply Hydro while your main damage dealer is on the field. This makes her an incredible partner for characters who rely on powerful
elemental reactions
to deal big damage. Think of Pyro characters like Hu Tao or Yoimiya for Vaporize, or Electro characters like Raiden Shogun for Electro-Charged. The best supports for Yelan are those who can either capitalize on her Hydro application or help her get her powerful burst back faster, ensuring she has maximum uptime.
Getting the Most from Elemental Resonance
Elemental Resonance is a fantastic and sometimes overlooked way to get a free stat increase for your team. Since Yelan’s damage scales with her Max HP, the Hydro Resonance is particularly amazing for her. By having two Hydro characters on your team, everyone’s Max HP is increased by 25%. Pairing Yelan with another strong Hydro support like Xingqiu is a popular strategy for this very reason. Not only do you get the HP bonus that benefits them both, but they also help each other generate energy particles, making it much easier to have their crucial bursts ready to go right off cooldown.

There’s a reason Yelan is consistently ranked as one of the best characters in the game. She perfectly blends high damage, incredible utility, and smooth gameplay into one package. In the Genshin “meta,” versatility is king, and Yelan is one of the most flexible characters available. Maximizing Your Team’s Damage
To really see the big numbers, you need to think about how each character’s abilities flow into the next. Yelan’s kit is designed to enable your main DPS to shine. A typical damage sequence might involve using a support to apply an element, swapping to an Anemo character to group enemies and shred resistance, then activating Yelan’s burst before finally switching to your main DPS to unleash a storm of elemental reactions. Her skill set encourages this kind of strategic play, turning a good team into a damage-dealing machine that can melt through enemy health bars with satisfying efficiency and speed.
Advanced Combat Techniques
Once you’ve got Yelan’s build sorted, it’s time to focus on the finer points of her gameplay. Mastering her in combat is less about stats and more about rhythm, timing, and smart movement. These advanced techniques will help you get the most out of every skill and turn your Yelan from a great sub-DPS into an absolute powerhouse on the field. It’s all about making your actions more efficient and your damage output more consistent.
How to Use Animation Canceling
If you want to squeeze every last drop of damage out of Yelan, learning
animation canceling
is a must. This technique lets you interrupt the end of an attack or skill animation to start your next move faster. For Yelan, this is especially useful after firing her charged shots, including her powerful Breakthrough Barb. Instead of waiting for the full recovery animation to finish, you can perform a quick dash or immediately use her Elemental Skill. This not only makes her gameplay feel smoother but also lets you get back to dealing damage or setting up reactions with your team much more quickly. Practice this, and you’ll see a noticeable increase in your overall DPS.
Adapting to Different Enemies
Yelan’s mobility makes her fantastic at controlling the battlefield, but you need to adapt your strategy based on who you’re fighting. Against large, slow enemies like Ruin Guards, use her Elemental Skill to zip around them, dodging their predictable attacks while applying Hydro. For mobs of smaller enemies, her skill is perfect for tagging multiple targets at once, grouping them up for a follow-up attack. A key part of mastering Yelan is learning
enemy patterns
and using her speed to exploit their weaknesses. Don’t just run in a straight line; use her agility to outmaneuver your opponents and stay one step ahead.
Nailing Your Skill Timing
Timing is everything with Yelan. Her Elemental Burst, Exquisite Throw, is her main source of off-field damage, so you need to use it at the right moment. The best practice is to activate her Burst right before swapping to your main DPS character. This ensures they get the full duration of her coordinated Hydro attacks to trigger powerful reactions like Vaporize or Electro-Charged. Similarly, get into the rhythm of using her Breakthrough Barb. While her bracelet glows to signal it’s ready, you’ll eventually learn the internal cooldown and can fire it off seamlessly within your rotation without having to check. Perfecting this combat timing is what separates a good Yelan player from a great one.
Positioning Your Whole Team
Yelan’s abilities make her a master of repositioning, and you should use that to your team’s advantage. Her Elemental Skill isn’t just for damage; it’s a fantastic tool for getting behind shielded enemies or grouping up scattered mobs. Since her Elemental Burst follows your active character, the positioning of your main DPS becomes critical. To maximize her Burst’s effectiveness, keep your active character in the middle of the action. This ensures the waves from Exquisite Throw hit as many enemies as possible. Thinking about your team’s placement as a whole is one of those
advanced techniques
that can dramatically improve your performance in tough fights.
Frequently Asked Questions
Is Yelan a main DPS or a support character?
Yelan shines brightest as a Sub-DPS. Her main role is to activate her Elemental Burst and then switch out, allowing her to deal a massive amount of Hydro damage from off-field. This setup enables your on-field character to trigger powerful elemental reactions, making her the perfect partner for a dedicated main DPS.
Why should I build HP on her instead of Attack?
Unlike most characters, Yelan’s damage for her Skill and Burst scales entirely with her maximum HP. Stacking Attack on her provides almost no benefit to her most important abilities. Focusing on HP not only makes her hit incredibly hard but also gives her a ton of survivability, so she can take a few hits without any trouble.
What’s the best 4-star weapon for Yelan?
The Favonius Warbow is an absolutely fantastic and accessible choice for her. Her Elemental Burst is the core of her kit, and this bow provides the Energy Recharge she needs to use it consistently. Plus, its passive effect generates extra energy particles for your entire team, making your combat rotations feel much smoother.
Do I need her constellations for her to be good?
Absolutely not. Yelan is a top-tier character right out of the box at C0 and can help you clear the game’s toughest content without any constellations. Her first constellation (C1) is a great quality-of-life upgrade that helps with her energy management, but she is incredibly powerful and complete from the moment you get her.
Can I use Yelan in Dendro teams like Hyperbloom?
Yes, she is one of the best characters for Hyperbloom teams. Her consistent, off-field Hydro application is perfect for creating a steady stream of Dendro Cores. When you pair her with Dendro and Electro characters, she enables a constant barrage of Hyperbloom reactions that can melt through enemy health bars with very little effort.

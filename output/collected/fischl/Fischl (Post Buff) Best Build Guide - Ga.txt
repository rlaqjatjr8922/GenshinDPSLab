Fischl is a 4-star Electro character that wields a Bow in Genshin Impact. Her gameplay centers around off-field Electro damage and strong elemental reaction support. Fischl excels as a sub-DPS or support, using her summoned raven to apply consistent Electro damage, enabling powerful reactions like Overload and Electro-Charged.

What Kind of Character is Fischl?
Fischl is best played as an
Off-Field
Electro Sub-DPS with strong personal damage in the right teams.
Her Skill and Burst summon Oz, who delivers most of her DPS via his Electro attacks. This damage is further amplified by her strong Ascension 4 passive, Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex.
Fischl has been strong since launch and remains one of the top 4-Star Sub-DPSes. She's unmatched in Aggravate teams and fits well into various team comps. While she lacks the buffs some Off-Fielders offer, her damage alone keeps her competitive.

Roles & Playstyle of Fischl and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Support Fischl
Fischl prefers teams where she can continuously trigger her Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex passive, so this requires a team that revolves around Electro reactions and an On-Field DPS who can trigger those reactions in quick succession.
Pros and Cons of Off-Field Support Fischl
Pros
- Easy to play and can be used in many teams.
- Reliable DPS even without constellations.
- Good battery or Energy generator for Electro characters with high Elemental Burst costs.
Cons
- Purely single-target.
- Doesn't provide buffs to the team.
- Elemental Burst doesn't have Invincibility Frames throughout the duration, which means Fischl can get damaged in her Burst form.

Team Rotation of Fischl and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Sub-DPS Fischl
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Sub-DPS Fischl
1. Start with supports with longer ability durations. If the DPS requires you to start the rotation with their Skill (like
Arlecchino
), then feel free to do so.
2. Swap to Fischl then use her Elemental Burst
3. Swap to your On-Field DPS to do their full damage combo.
4. If the DPS has long field time, you can use Fischl's Elemental Skill to resummon Oz if you're allowed to.
5. Swap back to your On-Field DPS to finish their rotation.
6. Return to step 1.
Oz's initial duration is relatively short, so swapping to her towards the end of the rotation before your main On-Field DPS is recommended.
If your On-Field character has a longer but flexible rotation, you can swap into Fischl mid-way to use her Elemental Skill to prolong Oz's field time. Otherwise, using only her Elemental Burst (or her Elemental Skill) is usually enough for most teams.
Basics Of Using Fischl
Fischl is an Off-Field Electro Support or Sub-DPS with decent, single-target DPS.
Her Elemental Skill summons Oz, a Night Raven that shoots coordinated Electro attacks. Her passive, Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex, causes a bolt of Lightning to strike an enemy whenever the active character triggers an Electro-related Elemental Reaction. This comprises the majority of her damage, making it ideal to pair her with an On-Field DPS that can rapidly trigger Electro reactions.
Elemental Skill Can Be Recast
Fischl's Elemental Skill initially summons Oz, her Night Raven companion.
This Skill can be recast once while Oz is still on the field, which causes Oz to return to Fischl's position. Recasting the skill doesn't extend the duration, but instead allows you to reposition Oz if needed. You can also hold Fischl's skill to accurately aim at the direction where you want Oz to appear.
Elemental Burst Refreshes Oz's Duration
Fischl's Elemental Burst transforms Fischl into a Night Raven.
She receives increased movement speed in this form and also deals Electro DMG to enemies she collides into. If Oz was on the field beforehand, the Burst will also refresh Oz's duration, allowing him to keep attacking for longer.
Can Generate Energy for Electro Teammates
Oz's quick attacks can generate Electro Energy particles with ease, making Fischl the ideal partner for Electro characters with high Burst Energy costs.
Some examples of this are
Beidou
,
Yae Miko
and
Cyno
.
Buffs Now Give Fischl Utility
Fischl was one among the few Monstadt chracters that are part of the new
Hexerei
faction or system that gave old Monstadt characters direct buffs. Before the buff, Fischl's entire contribution in a team is her personal damage. But if you finished her
Witch's Homework
quest,
her new Hexerei: Secret Rite passive allows her to give an ATK% buff whenever the team triggers an Overload reaction, and an EM increase when the party triggers Electro or Lunar-Charged.

Elemental Skill, Burst and Abilities Guide for Fischl
How To Use Fischl Skills & Passives
How To Play Fischl
▼Single-target Electro Sub-DPS with reliable damage.
▼Elemental Skill summons Oz.
▼Elemental Burst briefly transforms Fischl into Oz.
▼Triggering constant Electro reactions is important for the best dps.
▼Hexerei: Secret Rite gives Fischl buffing capability.
Single-Target Electro Sub-DPS With Reliable Damage
Basic Rotation Of Fischl
1. Start other supports with shorter ability durations and use their Elemental Skills or Bursts.
2. Swap to Fischl then use Elemental Skill or Elemental Burst.
3 .Swap to your On-Field DPS to execute their damage combos
.4. Repeat from Step 1.
Oz has a shorter duration than most Off-Field supports' Skills and Bursts.
Because of this, it's better to deploy Fischl's abilities towards the end of the rotation, just before the On-Field DPS. This is to maximize the duration of Oz and to ensure that Fischl also benefits from the buffs of other supports. Typically you only need to use one of either her Skill or Burst to summon Oz, but if your On-Field DPS has longer field time, then you can use her Elemental Burst to refresh Oz's duration sometime later into the rotation.
Elemental Skill Summons Oz
Icon
Elemental Skill Summary
Nightrider
- Summons Oz, dealing AoE Electro DMG on arrival.
- Oz remains on the field, continuously attacking nearby opponents with Freikugel.
- Hold to choose where Oz is summoned.
- Press again during the Skill’s duration to resummon Oz to Fischl’s side.
Oz is Fischl's main source of damage. The first cast of the skill triggers an AoE Electro attack where Oz is summoned, then Oz shoots out small bullets of Electro at a set interval. These attacks are purely single-target, and cannot reliably trigger the Hyperbloom reaction.
Elemental Burst Briefly Transforms Fischl Into Oz
Icon
Elemental Burst Summary
Midnight Phantasmagoria
- Summons Oz to protect Fischl.
- Fischl takes on Oz’s form, greatly increasing Movement Speed.
- Contact with opponents triggers lightning strikes, dealing Electro DMG (each enemy can only be struck once).
- When the ability ends, Oz stays on the field to continue attacking. If Oz was already present, his duration is refreshed.
Her Elemental Burst allows Fischl to temporarily become a Raven like Oz. In this state, Fischl will glide across the field and cause Lightning to hit enemies that she collides with on the way.
It's important to note that aside from the initial animation, Fischl has no Invulnerability Frames in her Burst form, so she can still take damage.
Triggering Constant Electro Reactions Is Important for the Best DPS
▲ The AoE from Thundering Retribution can trigger Hyperblooms.
Icon
Passive Description
Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex
- If your current active character triggers an Electro-related Elemental Reaction when Oz is on the field, the opponent shall be stricken with Thundering Retribution that deals Electro DMG equal to 80% of Fischl's ATK.
Fischl's A4 passive makes up the majority of her personal damage.
This passive deals a hefty amount of DPS, provided your active character to manages to trigger Electro reactions constantly. DPSes that can attack quickly are the best characters for this, but that doesn't mean slow attackers can't make use of her passive. So long as an Electro reaction happens, the passive should work.
Spread (Dendro applied to Quicken) is not considered an Electro reaction, despite Quicken (Electro and Dendro) needing Electro beforehand.

- After a nearby party member triggers an Electro-Charged or Lunar-Charged reaction, Fischl and other nearby active party members have their Elemental Mastery increased by 90 for 10s.

C6 Is Ideal But Not Necessary
Oz will now perform synchronized attacks with the on-field character in addition to having an increased duration, thereby upgrading Fischl's damage contribution significantly, especially in Quicken-related teams.
If you want Fischl to perform even better than her C0 version, consider getting her C6 if you have the means to do so.
However, do keep in mind that
Fischl is already complete as a character even with no constellation
. She will still output decent damage on a team that can flex an off-field Electro sub-dps or as an on-field Physical DPS. If C6 is not feasible for you, her C3 that adds levels to her Skill is a good stopping point.

Who Is Fischl?
A Mysterious Adventurer From Another World
Fischl is the Prinzessin der Verurteilung of the outer real Immernachtreich. She came to Teyvat with her trusty aide Oz after she was exiled from her kingdom. She has since joined the Adventurer's Guild and made a name for herself as an Investigator there.
Actually A Girl Named Amy From Mondstadt
Fischl is actually the fantasy persona created by Amy, a young girl from Mondstadt. She lives with her parents who have been nothing but supportive of her eccentrics. Some people find it confusing to talk to her due to the way she speaks, but a Fischl dictionary has since helped them understand her.

Fischl is Best Used as an Electro Sub-DPS
Team
Rating
Description
▼ Overload Team
★★★★★
- Fischl greatly benefits from Chevreuse's buffs in a Pyro and Electro only team.
- She can act as the sole Electro in a team without much issue due to her strong Electro application.
- Following her
Hexerei
buffs, Fischl now has an even stronger place in an Overload team due to the ATK% buff she gives.
▼ Lunar-Charged or Electro-Charged Team
★★★★・
- Fischl is a sufficient Electro DMG and Element source for a Lunar-Charged or Electro-Charged team due to her generally decent DPS.
- If you've finished her Witch's Homework quest, Fischl gives an EM boost whenever the party triggers either reaction.
▼ Overload with Vaporize (Overvape) Team
★★★・・
- This comp relies on triggering multiple reactions at once as the main source of damage. While not as good as the dedicated Overload or Vaporize teams, this team still averages good DPS potential that's enough to clear endgame content, even more so with Fischl's
Hexerei
passive that allow her to be an extra buffer for the team.
▼ Quickbloom Team
★★・・・
- As one of the best Electro sub DPS, Fischl is able to trigger Aggravate (Quicken plus Electro) easily for Hyperbloom teams, while also maintaining a Quicken aura.
- Fischl will only occasionally trigger Hyperbloom, but this is compensated by her strong DPS enhanced by Aggravate.
- No other Hexerei teammate really synergizes with this comp, so Fischl won't be able to make use of her Hexerei passive here.
▼ Aggravate Team
★★・・・・・
- As 2nd Electro, Fischl can generate particles and enable Electro resonance for the DPS.
- Keqing's fast attacks will be able to trigger Aggravate more with Fischl's fast off-field Electro application.
- Like Quickbloom, this team can't really make perfect use of Fischl's Hexerei buffs, so no need to go out of your way to enable her Hexerei passive here.
▼ Physical Team with Sub DPS Fischl
★・・・・・・
- Fischl's off-field Electro damage is good for triggering Superconduct
- With Fischl's energy particle generation, Eula will be able to use her Burst often for more damage
▼ Physical Team with DPS Fischl
★・・・・
- Makes use of Pale Flame and Mitternachts Waltz to increase Fischl's damage as Physical DPS
- The lack of a good Cryo sub DPS makes this team weaker compared to others
Fischl is
a good Electro sub DPS with rapid application
and can easily generate energy particles. Since she can also reposition and refresh the duration of Oz, she is one of the most used Electro sub DPS for various team compositions. With her new
Hexerei
passive, she can even double as a partywide buffer provided another Hexerei member is included in the party.
She also has rapid Normal Attacks which give her viability as a Physical DPS, but due to the lack of a Cryo sub DPS that can consistently apply Cryo for Superconduct, Physical DPS Fischl is one of her weaker teams.

Overload is one of Fischl's best team type overall in terms of damage potential, especially after her buffs, due to how synergistic each member is with each other.
Having Chevreuse at C6 increases the DPS of every damage-dealer in this party, and her teamwide ATK buff gets a lot of mileage in a party with 3 ATK scaling teammates.
Durin not only provides further Pyro and Electro RES shred,
he is also a Hexerei member and can enable Fischl's Hexerei passive that provides the party with another ATK% buff
. Additionally, his personal DPS also benefits from Chevreuse's buffs.
Rotation of Overload Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Arlecchino. Use her Skill to apply Blood-Debt on the enemies.
2
Switch to Fischl, use her Skill or Burst.
3
Use Chevreuse's Burst into Skill (Hold) x2 combo.
4
Swap into Durin. Use Skill (x2) to enter the Confirmation of Purity state, then Burst.
5
Switch back to Arlecchino, use her Charged Attack on marked enemies to gain Bond of Life. Start using her Normal Attacks, dodge when necessary.
6
Switch back into Fischl and use her Skill or Burst (whichever you didn't use earlier) to refresh Oz's duration.
7
Switch to Arlecchino to finish her combo until all buffs expire (Durin Burst, Chevreuse RES Shred).
8
Return to Step 1.
Chevreuse's shred automatically activates after the party triggers an Overload reaction, and she doesn't need to be the character that causes the Overload either. Her Skill (Hold) is also what activates her ATK buff, so aim to use that version of her Skill every rotation.

No.

Swap into Flins. Perform his typical combo rotation.

6
Use Sucrose's Skill again.
10
Repeat from Step 1 once Columbina's Elemental Burst expires.
This rotation is generally straightforward, apart from needing to be aware of abilities expiring.

Mona with her C1 increases the damage dealt by all Hydro-related reactions to enemies affected by her Elemental Burst's Omen, making her a decent buffer in a team with multiple Hydro-related reactions like an Overvape Team that has Electro-Charged and Vaporize. She also activates Fischl's Hexerei passive, allowing the team to gain both the ATK and EM buff.
Rotation of "Overvape" Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Arlecchino. Use her Skill to apply Blood-Debt on the enemies.
2
Swap to Xilonen to her Skill into Normal Attack (x2). Burst is optional.
3
Switch to Fischl. Use Skill or Burst. You can use Normal Attack (x4) to extend the Omen's duration.
4
Use Mona's Skill into Burst combo.
5
Switch back to Arlecchino. Use Charged Attack (x1) to absorb the Blood-Debts to gain a Pyro Infusion. Then do the Normal Attacks (x3) into a dash combo.
6
When Oz expires, Use Arlecchino's Skill again.
7
Swap to Xilonen to do another Skill into Normal Attack (x2) combo.
8
Switch to Fischl then use her Burst.
9
Use Mona's Skill a second time.
10
Repeat step 5 on Arlecchino again until all support abilities expire.
11
Repeat from Step 1.
Since Fischl and
Mona
each activate the others' Hexerei passive, Mona has the ability to further increase the damage of Vaporize reactions after using a Normal or Charged Attack, while also extending the Omen (her DMG buff from Burst) duration on enemies. Note that using Normal Attacks on Mona is optional due to her pre-existing issues with animation clunkiness.

Fischl's damage from her passive, Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex, is decently increased by activating the Aggravate reaction.
You activate this reaction by first applying Quicken to an enemy, which is Dendro plus Electro or vice-versa, and then using Electro on the enemy while the Quicken aura is still active on them. By adding a Hydro unit in a team with Dendro and Electro, you're able to trigger a few Hyperblooms on the side as a bonus, thus elevating the team DPS.
Note that Clorinde's self-healing is enough to off-set
Furina's
HP drain mechanic by triggering one of Furina's passives, Endless Waltz. If this healing isn't enough for you, you can instead give Nahida
Prototype Amber
.
Rotation of Quickbloom Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Furina and use her Skill and then Burst
2
Switch to Nahida, activate her Skill and then Burst
3
Switch to Fischl to summon Oz using her Skill
4
Switch to Clorinde, use her Skill, then do Normal Attack x3 into Skill, do this about six times total on her
5
Switch to Fischl to extend Oz's duration through her Burst
6
Switch back to Clorinde and if possible, repeat step 4
7
Repeat from 1
If Oz, Fischl's summon, expires early, you can use her Burst or Skill again to reposition and resummon it, whichever is available for use at the time in the rotation. Her Burst also helps to extend Oz's field time.

Fischl synergizes well with Aggravate thanks to her Ascension 4 Passive, Undone Be Thy Sinful Hex. This passive can be triggered consistently within a short amount of time, and since Aggravate increases Electro DMG, each hit from the passive will have its damage increased in this team.
Keqing is a good teammate for Fischl since she can maintain Fischl's passive by consistently triggering Electro reactions through her fast Normal Attacks and the amount of hits she performs in her Elemental Burst.
Rotation of Aggravate Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Keqing then use her Tap Skill into Charged Attack x1
2
Switch to Xilonen to use her Skill, Burst if needed for healing, then do Normal Attack x2
3
Switch with Nahida to use her Tap Skill into Burst if her Burst field expired
4
Switch back to Fischl and use Skill or Burst, whichever is available
5
Switch to Keqing, use her Burst, then her Skill, then use a combination of Normal and Charged Attacks. Use Keqing's Skill again if it comes off cooldown until support abilities expire
6
Repeat from 1
If Oz, Fischl's summon, expires early, you can use her Burst or Skill again to reposition and resummon it, whichever is available for use at the time in the rotation.
Her Burst also helps to extend Oz's field time or duration. This team is quickswap friendly since Keqing's Elemental Skill is on a low cooldown, especially with the
Thundering Fury
set.
You can occasionally refresh Oz's duration by using Fischl's Burst. reapply Xilonen's RES Shred and other buffs by using her Skill into two Normal Attacks, and even reapply Nahida's Skill mark.

Rotation of Physical Team with Sub DPS Fischl
No.
Procedure

Because Eula's Burst detonates after you swap out of her, you want to deploy every major support ability prior to committing to Eula's Burst. This is to ensure you get the most out of it.

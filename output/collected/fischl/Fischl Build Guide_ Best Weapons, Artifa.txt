Fischl Gameplay
Fischl's gameplay centers around her Elemental Skill which summons Oz to the battlefield for 10 seconds (12 seconds at C6). Oz attacks nearby opponents with Freikugel, dealing single-target Electro damage with excellent tracking. The skill has a 25-second cooldown, creating 15 seconds of downtime, but this can be mitigated by alternating between her Skill and Burst to maintain near-permanent Oz uptime. Her Elemental Burst transforms Fischl into Oz briefly, dealing Electro damage to enemies she contacts before leaving Oz on the field.
Her most powerful mechanic comes from her A4 Passive which triggers whenever an active character causes an Electro-related Elemental Reaction while Oz is on-field. This passive deals additional Electro damage equal to 80% of Fischl's ATK with only a 0.5-second cooldown and no ICD (Internal Cooldown), making her an incredibly consistent Electro applicator. At C6, Oz performs coordinated attacks dealing 30% of Fischl's ATK as Electro damage whenever the active character attacks, further amplifying her off-field damage potential.
Her primary strengths include requiring minimal field time (quick swap in, summon Oz, swap out), functioning as an excellent Electro battery through consistent particle generation, providing continuous Electro application for enabling reactions like Electro-Charged, Aggravate, and Overload, and being easily accessible as a 4-star character who appears in both shop rotations and wish banners. She performs exceptionally well at C0, with C1 and C6 being her most impactful constellations.
However, she has some limitations. Oz targets only single enemies rather than multiple foes, making him less effective in heavy AoE scenarios. Her Elemental Burst lacks invincibility frames, leaving her vulnerable during the transformation animation. Physical DPS builds, while possible, significantly underperform compared to her off-field Electro capabilities. Her damage output, while consistent, can be surpassed by newer 5-star Electro characters in terms of ceiling.
Upcoming Hexerei Buffs (Version 6.2)
In the upcoming Version 6.2 update, Fischl will receive significant buffs through the Hexerei Resonance mechanic after completing Witch's Homework: Of the Unexpected. These buffs activate when paired with at least one other Hexerei character (Durin, Venti, Klee, Albedo, Mona, Sucrose, or Razor).
The core buff introduces powerful team-wide effects tied to Oz's presence on the battlefield. When Oz is on the field and nearby teammates trigger specific Electro reactions, the following buffs activate:
Overload Buff
: For 10 seconds after a nearby team character triggers an Overload reaction, Fischl and all other nearby team characters on the field gain a 22.5% Attack Damage increase. This buff makes Fischl particularly valuable in Overload-focused teams, especially those featuring Chevreuse who further amplifies Overload damage.
Electro-Charged/Lunar-Charged Buff
: For 10 seconds after a nearby team character triggers an Electro-Charged or Lunar-Charged reaction, Fischl and all other nearby team characters on the field gain 90 Elemental Mastery. This substantial EM boost significantly increases the damage of transformative reactions, making Fischl an even stronger enabler for Electro-Charged teams.
Her C6 constellation receives a dramatic enhancement in Version 6.2. In addition to its original coordinated attack effect, C6 now doubles both of these buff effects, granting 45% ATK for Overload teams or 180 Elemental Mastery for Electro-Charged teams. This transforms C6 Fischl from a strong personal damage upgrade into a premier team buffer.

Fischl's talent priority focuses heavily on her Elemental Skill, which is the source of most of her damage and utility.

Elemental Skill - Nightrider
Level Fischl's Elemental Skill first and ideally to maximum level. Oz is responsible for the vast majority of Fischl's damage output, and his damage scales directly with this talent level. Since Fischl's primary role involves keeping Oz on-field as much as possible, maximizing this talent provides the best return on investment.
Elemental Burst - Midnight Phantasmagoria
Level Fischl's Elemental Burst second, though priority depends on how frequently you use it in rotations. The Burst deals respectable Electro damage during Fischl's transformation and leaves Oz on the field afterward, essentially serving as an alternative way to summon Oz with additional burst damage.
Normal Attack - Bolts of Downfall
Normal Attack investment is the lowest priority for off-field Fischl builds. Her Normal Attack is a standard 5-hit bow combo used primarily for Physical DPS builds, which significantly underperform compared to her off-field Electro capabilities. For the vast majority of builds, leave Normal Attacks at level 1. The only exception is Physical DPS builds, but these are considered suboptimal and not recommended.
At C6, Fischl gains coordinated attacks from Oz based on the active character's Normal Attacks, but these scale with her Elemental Skill talent level, not Normal Attack talent level, making Normal Attack investment still unnecessary.

Fischl Constellations
Fischl is fully functional at C0, but her constellations provide meaningful improvements. As a 4-star character appearing regularly on banners and in the shop, most players will naturally acquire constellations over time.
C1 Performance
Even when Oz is not present in combat, he can still watch over Fischl through his raven eyes. When Fischl performs a Normal Attack against an opponent, Oz fires a joint attack, dealing DMG equal to 22% of Fischl's ATK.
C1 is Fischl's first major power spike and highly recommended to obtain. This constellation allows Oz to perform coordinated attacks even when he's not physically on the field, effectively giving Fischl off-field damage potential during his downtime period. The coordinated attacks occur with each of Fischl's Normal Attacks, providing consistent supplemental damage. This constellation is particularly valuable for Physical DPS builds but remains useful for standard off-field builds by reducing Oz downtime impact.
C2 Performance
When Nightrider is used, it deals an additional 200% ATK as DMG, and its AoE is increased by 50%.
C2 provides a straightforward damage increase to Fischl's Elemental Skill initial cast, dealing an additional 200% ATK damage with larger AoE. This translates to approximately 15-20% more total Skill damage. While valuable, the impact is limited compared to C1 and C6 since the boost only affects the initial summon, not Oz's ongoing attacks.
C4 Performance
When Midnight Phantasmagoria is used, it deals 222% of ATK as Electro DMG to surrounding opponents. When the skill duration ends, Fischl regenerates 20% of her HP.
C4 increases Fischl's Elemental Burst damage and adds a healing component when it ends. The damage increase is modest since Fischl's Burst isn't her primary damage source. The 20% HP regeneration provides minor survivability but is generally unnecessary.
C6 Performance
Extends the duration of Oz's summoning by 2s. Additionally, Oz performs coordinated attacks with your active character when present, dealing 30% of Fischl's ATK as Electro DMG.
C6 is Fischl's most transformative constellation and one of the best 4-star C6 effects in the game. The 2-second duration extension increases Oz's uptime from 10 seconds to 12 seconds (effectively 83% uptime instead of 67% when alternating Skill and Burst). The coordinated attacks deal 30% of Fischl's ATK as Electro damage with every Normal Attack from the active character, effectively adding massive off-field DPS to any Normal Attack-focused carry.
This constellation transforms Fischl into a premium off-field Sub-DPS who can rival many 5-star characters in specific team compositions. With fast attacking characters like Tartaglia, Yoimiya, or Ayato, the coordinated attacks trigger extremely frequently, providing immense sustained damage.
With Version 6.2 Hexerei buffs, C6 doubles the team buffs from Oz, granting 45% ATK (instead of 22.5%) for Overload teams or 180 Elemental Mastery (instead of 90) for Electro-Charged teams. This dramatically increases her value as a team buffer, making C6 Fischl one of the most valuable 4-star investments in the game.
Conclusion
This comprehensive guide demonstrates Fischl's position as one of the most reliable, consistent, and valuable 4-star characters in Genshin Impact. Her minimal field time requirements, exceptional off-field Electro application, and strong battery capabilities make her an excellent choice for virtually any team composition requiring Electro support. Her accessibility as a 4-star character who appears in shop rotations ensures that all players can eventually obtain and build her, with C0 performance being fully functional while C1 and especially C6 provide transformative upgrades.
With the upcoming Version 6.2 Hexerei buffs, Fischl is poised to receive significant enhancements that expand her role from pure off-field damage dealer into a hybrid Sub-DPS/Buffer. The ability to provide 22.5% ATK buffs to Overload teams or 90 Elemental Mastery to Electro-Charged teams transforms her into an essential support character for Hexerei compositions, with her C6 doubling these effects to 45% ATK or 180 EM respectively. These updates ensure that the Prinzessin der Verurteilung remains one of the most valuable and versatile characters in the evolving meta, particularly when paired with other Hexerei members like Durin, Klee, Mona, Venti, or Sucrose.

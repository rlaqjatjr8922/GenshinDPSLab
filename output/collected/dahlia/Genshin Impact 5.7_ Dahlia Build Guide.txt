Dahlia’s a solid pick for budget players needing a Hydro shielder or ATK Speed support, especially in Freeze teams or with Normal Attack DPS. Go for him if you:
Run Freeze teams with folks like Skirk or Wriothesley.
Want a shielder who speeds up characters like Ayato or Yoimiya.
Like his exploration perks or want that C6 revive.
Pass if you need off-field Hydro (like Xingqiu or Furina) or don’t use Freeze/Normal Attack teams.
Dahlia’s on banners starting
June 18, 2025
, with Pikulik and Shenhe in Phase 1 of Version 5.7.
Role and Playstyle
Role
: Hydro Support/Shielder, ATK Speed Buffer
Key Mechanics
:
Elemental Burst (Radiant Psalter)
: Hits enemies with AoE Hydro DMG and gives your team a shield that scales with Dahlia’s HP (250% stronger against Hydro DMG). It also boosts ATK Speed based on his Max HP (0.5% per 1,000 HP, max 20%). You can keep the shield going by earning
Benison stacks
through Freeze reactions or Normal Attacks.
Elemental Skill (Mist-Rain Mystery/Sacramental Shower)
: Creates a field that either explodes for Hydro DMG when enemies touch it or launches your character upward for exploration or Plunging Attacks.
Passive (Utility)
: Speeds up your party’s movement by 10% during the day (06:00–18:00) in the overworld, but it doesn’t work in Domains or Spiral Abyss.
C6 (Notable Constellation)
: Lets you revive one teammate to full HP when they’re under Favonian Favor, but only once every 15 minutes.
Dahlia’s great for Freeze teams since his shield refreshes with Frozen enemies, and he helps Normal Attack-focused characters swing faster. He’s not the best for off-field Hydro application, so he doesn’t always click with folks like Furina.
Best Weapons
Dahlia needs weapons that pump up his
HP% and Energy Recharge to make his shields beefy and keep his Burst ready. Here’s what works:
Key of Khaj-Nisut (5-star)
Why
: Gives a ton of HP% (up to 66.2% at R1), making shields and ATK Speed boosts stronger. Also throws in some Elemental Mastery for the team, which is nice in reaction teams.
Source
: Limited Weapon Banner.
Sacrificial Sword (4-star)
Why
: Boosts Energy Recharge (up to 61.3% at R1) and might reset your Elemental Skill cooldown, helping you spam Burst more.
Source
: Gacha.
Favonius Sword (4-star)
Why
: Same Energy Recharge as Sacrificial (up to 61.3% at R1) and makes energy particles for your team, keeping Burst uptime solid.
Source
: Gacha.
Fleuve Cendre Ferryman (4-star, F2P)
Why
: Decent Energy Recharge (up to 45.9% at R1) and a bit of Elemental Skill CRIT Rate, which is okay for free-to-play players.
Source
: Fountain Fishing Association.
Sapwood Blade (4-star, F2P)
Why
: Lowers Energy Recharge (up to 30.6% at R1) but gives a small EM boost for reaction teams. Good enough if you’re on a budget.
Source
: Sumeru Craftable.
Note
: Skip team support weapons like Freedom-Sworn; they don’t really help Dahlia’s kit.
Best Artifacts
You want artifacts that max out
HP%
for beefy shields and ATK Speed buffs, plus
Energy Recharge
to keep his Burst up. Check these out:
4-piece Tenacity of the Millelith
Why
: Ups HP by 20%, which helps shields and ATK Speed, and gives your team a 20% ATK boost plus 30% Shield Strength when you hit enemies with your Skill. The buff only lasts 3 seconds, so it can be tricky to keep it going.
Main Stats
: HP% Sands, HP% Goblet, HP% Circlet
Sub Stats
: Energy Recharge, HP%, Elemental Mastery, DEF%
4-piece Noblesse Oblige
Why
: Makes your Burst hit 20% harder and gives the team a 20% ATK buff for 12 seconds, which is easier to maintain than Tenacity. Great for team support.
Main Stats
: HP% Sands, HP% Goblet, HP% Circlet
Sub Stats
: Energy Recharge, HP%, Elemental Mastery
2-piece HP% + 2-piece HP% (e.g., Vourukasha’s Glow, Tenacity of the Millelith)
Why
: Stacks HP (up to 40% combined) for tougher shields and better ATK Speed buffs. Good while you’re grinding for a full set.
Main Stats
: HP% Sands, HP% Goblet, HP% Circlet
Sub Stats
: Energy Recharge, HP%, Elemental Mastery
Stat Priority
:
HP%
: Shoot for ~40,000 HP to hit that 20% ATK Speed cap.
Energy Recharge
: 150–200%, depending on your weapon and team (less if you’ve got Sacrificial or Favonius).
Elemental Mastery
: Handy for reaction teams like Freeze or Vaporize.
DEF%
: Helps shields a bit but isn’t a must.
Talent Priority
Dahlia’s all about his
Elemental Burst
, with his
Elemental Skill
as a backup for energy and some Hydro application. Normal Attacks? Barely touch ‘em.
Priority
: Elemental Burst (Radiant Psalter) > Elemental Skill (Mist-Rain Mystery/Sacramental Shower) > Normal Attack (Favonia’s Fencing Style)
Leveling
: Get Burst and Skill to Level 8–10; leave Normal Attack at Level 1 unless you’re doing some Plunge attack shenanigans.

Best Team Compositions
Dahlia’s a star in
Freeze teams
since his shield loves Frozen enemies, and he’s solid with Normal Attack DPS characters who dig his speed boost. Here’s who he hangs with:
Freeze Team
(Top Pick)
Dahlia (Hydro Support)
: Brings shields and ATK Speed buffs.
Skirk (Hydro DPS)
: Loves the ATK Speed boost and Freeze setup.
Rosaria/Diona (Cryo Sub-DPS/Support)
: Rosaria adds CRIT Rate, Diona piles on more shields.
Sucrose/Kazuha (Anemo Support)
: Groups enemies and pumps up Elemental DMG with Viridescent Venerer.
Rotation
: Sucrose/Kazuha E > Diona/Rosaria Q > Dahlia Q > Skirk Q/Normal Attacks.
Vaporize Team
Dahlia (Hydro Support)
: Shields and speeds up attacks.
Marjorie (Pyro DPS)
: Sets off Vaporize with Dahlia’s Hydro.
Xingqiu (Hydro Sub-DPS)
: Keeps Hydro flowing for Vaporize.
Bennett (Pyro Support)
: Heals and boosts ATK.
Rotation
: Bennett Q > Xingqiu Q > Dahlia Q > Marjorie NA/CA.
Plunge Attack Team
(Niche)
Dahlia (Hydro Support)
: Sets up Plunging Attacks with his Skill.
Xiao/Diluc (Anemo/Pyro DPS)
: Smashes with Plunge DMG.
Xianyun (Anemo Support)
: Buffs Plunge DMG and heals.
Furina (Hydro Sub-DPS)
: Ups team DMG with Fanfare stacks.
Rotation
: Xianyun E/Q > Dahlia E > Furina Q > Xiao/Diluc Plunge.
Note
: Dahlia’s not great at off-field Hydro, so he’s not Furina’s best buddy. Stick to Freeze teams or pair with someone like Xingqiu for Hydro application.
Constellations
Dahlia’s constellations make him an even better support:
C1 (Infallible Procession)
: Gives back 2.5 Energy per Benison stack during Burst, helping you use it more often.
C2 (Revelation of Mercy)
: Boosts Shield Strength by 25% for 12s after using Benison stacks.
C3
: Ups Burst level by 3 (max 15).
C6
: Revives one teammate to full HP under Favonian Favor, but only once every 15 minutes. Super handy for tough fights.
Recommendation
: C1 and C2 help with energy and shields; C6 is awesome for the revive but not a must-have.
Playstyle Tips
Rotation
: Kick off with your support skills (like Anemo or Cryo), use Dahlia’s Skill to build energy, then pop his Burst for Favonian Favor. Swap to your DPS and Freeze enemies or spam Normal Attacks to keep the shield up.
Exploration
: His Skill lets you jump high for Plunging Attacks or to reach tricky spots, and the daytime Movement Speed boost makes running around easier.
Energy Management
: Aim for 150–200% Energy Recharge for consistent Bursts, especially without Sacrificial Sword.
Team Synergy
: He’s great with Cryo characters (Skirk, Wriothesley) for Freeze or Normal Attack DPS (Ayato, Yoimiya) for ATK Speed. Don’t rely on him for Hydro in Vaporize teams.
Pros and Cons
Pros
:
First 4-star Hydro shielder, fills a cool niche.
ATK Speed buff helps Normal Attack DPS shine.
Rocks Freeze teams with his shield-refresh trick.
Easy to build with F2P weapons and materials.
Handy for exploration with speed and jumps.
Cons
:
Weak off-field Hydro application, so he’s not great with Furina.
Shield uptime needs Freeze or Normal Attacks, limiting team options.
Tenacity’s buff is short (3–6s) and hard to keep up.
Doesn’t deal much damage since he builds HP but scales with ATK.

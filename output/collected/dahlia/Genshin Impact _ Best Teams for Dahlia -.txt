Dahlia works best with a Freeze DPS like Skirk who deals Normal Attacks and can trigger Freeze. In a Freeze team with Escoffier, he'll be able to provide Shields and buffs without affecting the required Elements.
Despite his Attack Speed buff at C0 being underwhelming,
Skirk gets a lot of benefit out of more Attack Speed (ATK SPD) due to being a Normal Attack-based DPS. The ATK SPD buff he provides is further enhanced with his C6, making Dahlia a competitive option for the last spot in Skirk's teams as a result.
Rotation Of Premium Freeze (Skirk) Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Furina and use her Skill, then Burst. You can dash after her Skill to cancel the long animation.
2
Switch to Escoffier. Deploy her Skill, followed by her Burst.
3
Use Dahlia's Skill, followed by his Burst.
4
Deploy Skirk as the on-field character. Do a few Normal Attacks before using her Skill (tap).
5
Use Skirk's Elemental Burst to buff her Normal Attacks. This will also absorb nearby Void Rifts.
6
Deal damage with Normal Attacks, and after a few Void Rifts spawn, use her Charged Attack to absorb them.
└ Optimal combo demo can be found
here
.
7
Repeat from Step 1.
If you need healing, use Escoffier's Burst if it's available.

Escoffier and Skirk Freeze teams perform at their best when the lineup consists of only Hydro or Cryo characters. Other Hydro or Cryo DPSes have some room for flexibility, but Escoffier is typically the preferred Cryo support, and to maximize her RES Shred, the team must comply with the Cryo or Hydro only rule.

Dahlia's ATK SPD buff has some uses outside of Freeze teams thanks to his Benison stacks only being tied to Normal Attacks. However, since he does not have any Off-Field damage, a secondary Hydro character is needed if you want Off-Field Hydro damage and application.
Rotation Of Normal Attack DPS Team
No.
Procedure
1
Start with Yelan and use her Skill, then Burst.
2
Switch to Dahlia and use his Skill, then Burst.
3
Switch to Faruzan and use her Skill, then Charged Attack to create Energy Particles.
4
Use Faruzan's Burst before switching her out.
5
Switch to Wanderer and use his Skill.
6
Deal damage with Normal/Charged Attacks.
7
Use his Burst before ending his rotation.
8
Repeat from Step 1 once buffs expire.
Due to Faruzan's high Energy costs, it's best to extend her field time a bit by using her Skill and Charged Attack to generate Particles for her, especially if Faruzan doesn't have her C6.

No.

Lauma is a 5-Star Dendro Catalyst user in Genshin Impact, primarily serving as an Off-Field Sub-DPS with supportive utility. She is the only character capable of enabling the Lunar-Bloom reaction, which replaces the standard Bloom reaction. Lunar-Bloom generates Verdant Dew, a resource Lauma consumes to activate the Hold version of her Elemental Skill. This version of her Skill deals powerful Lunar-Bloom damage and currently stands as the sole source of it in the game. Her Elemental Burst further boosts the damage of Hyperbloom, Bloom, Burgeon, and Lunar-Bloom.

What Kind of Character is Lauma?
In battle,
Lauma is a 5-Star
Off-Field
Dendro
Support
and Sub-DPS who enables the Lunar-Bloom reaction when she's in a party.
Her Hold Skill is the only source of Lunar-Bloom damage, and it consumes a resource called Verdant Dew. You can only generate Verdant Dew by triggering a Lunar-Bloom reaction, which is essentially the same as the regular Bloom reaction. Lauma also buffs Hyperbloom, Bloom, Burgeon and Lunar-Bloom damage via her Burst, which makes her a decent support for teams that focus around those reactions.

Roles & Playstyle Of Lauma and Her Strengths and Weakness
Off-Field Dendro Support and Lunar-Bloom Enabler
Lauma converts the Bloom reactions triggered by the party into Lunar-Bloom, which creates Verdant Dew stacks for her to be able to use her Hold Skill.
Lauma's Elemental Skill applies Dendro around the active character, while the Hold version deals a burst of Lunar-Bloom damage to nearby enemies in an AoE.
Pros and Cons of Support Lauma
Pros
- Easy to use.
- Shreds Dendro and Hydro RES.
- Contributes a good amount of her own personal DPS.
- Allows Bloom, Hyperbloom and Burgeon DMG to CRIT when she's the only Nod-Krai character in the team.
Cons
- Semi-high Energy Burst cost requires her to build some Energy Recharge to Burst consistently.
- Despite converting Blooms into Lunar-Bloom, these converted Blooms don't really do enhanced damage but are instead only a way to get Verdant Dew stacks for her Hold Skill to consume.
- Elemental Burst buffs Bloom, Hyperbloom, Burgeon and Lunar-Bloom damage but has limited instances, similar to
Shenhe
's Quills, making it easy for the buff to run out in AoE situations.

Team Rotation of Lauma and Combos
Basic Team Rotation for Off-Field Support Lauma
Basic Rotation For Off-Field Support Lauma
1.Start with the supports with longer ability durations if there are any. If any Sub-DPS or DPS needs to deploy their abilities first as part of their mechanic, such as
Nilou
, then ensure they do so beforehand.
2. Swap to Lauma, use her Elemental Burst first, then use her Elemental Skill (Hold).
3. Swap to the main On-Field DPS if there is any.
4. Switch back to Lauma again when you see the Verdant Dew indicator next to her icon light up with three stacks. Use her Hold Skill to do Lunar-Bloom damage.
5. Repeat from Step 1.
If Lauma is mainly going to be Off-Field on your team, you can start her combo with her Elemental Burst so her Hold Skill also benefits from the buff.
You can end the whole rotation by using the Hold Skill again once you have three Verdant Dew stacks, since three stacks equates to significant damage potential thanks to the higher scaling.
Basic Team Rotation for On-Field Hyperbloom Driver Lauma
Basic Rotation For On-Field Hyperbloom Driver Lauma
1.Start with the supports and deploy their abilities.
2. Swap to Lauma, use her Elemental Burst first, then use her Elemental Skill (Hold).
3. Use Lauma's Normal Attacks to continuously apply Dendro as well as drive key abilities like
Xingqiu's
Rainswords.
4. Use Lauma's Hold Elemental Skill whenever you get three Verdant Dew stacks.
5. Repeat from Step 1.
As the On-Field character, Lauma will be using her Normal Attacks to apply Dendro to an enemy and to activate her supports abilities, if any of them require Normal Attacks to trigger.
This team rotation is usually best suited for Hyperbloom or Burgeon teams.
Basics Of Using Lauma
Lauma is designed to be an Off-Field Dendro Sub-DPS that also provides supportive buffs for specific Dendro reactions and teams.
She is designed to be
Nefer's
best teammate in a Lunar-Bloom team, where her DMG% buffs and RES Shred are fully maximized.

Like all Nod-Krai characters, Lauma's kit interacts with a special region-limited mechanic called a Moonsign.
When Lauma is in the party, a specific Moonsign effect is activated depending on the number of Nod-Krai characters present.
Her passive, Light for the Frosty Night, has different effects depending on the Moonsign Benediction active.
Lunar-Blooms Give a Stack of Verdant Dew
Bloom reactions triggered by a party with Lauma in it are automatically converted to Lunar-bloom, which is similar to how
Ineffa
enables Lunar-charged. When a Lunar-bloom reaction is triggered, Lauma gains a stack of Verdant Dew, which is an important resource that can be used by her Hold Skill or by other Lunar-Bloom DPSes. Lunar
Lunar-Bloom stacks can be tracked via the crescent moon shapes beside the active character, as well as underneath the party lineup on the right side of the screen.
Note!
As of the moment, only
Nefer
,
Columbina
and
Lauma
through her Hold Skill can deal direct Lunar-Bloom damage.
Shred RES or Deal Lunar-Bloom DMG with Skill
Lauma's Skill summons a Frostgrave Sanctuary, and has a Tap and Hold version.
The Tap version deals regular Dendro damage in an AoE, but her Hold Skill consumes all Verdant Dew stacks to deal two waves of AoE Dendro damage, with the second wave considered to be Lunar-bloom damage.
Furthermore, all attacks made by the Sanctuary reduces the an enemy's Dendro and Hydro
Resistance
for a short period of time, provided the attack hits.
Note!
To be able to use the Hold version, at least one Lunar-bloom reaction needs to be triggered beforehand.
This version also converts a Verdant Dew stack (maximum of three) to a
Moon Song
stack, which is a different resource required by her Elemental Burst.
Elemental Burst Buffs Dendro-related Reactions
After using Elemental Burst, Lauma generates 18 stacks of Pale Hymn. Additionally, her Burst also consumes Moon Songs to create 6 stacks of Pale Hymn per stack.
This amounts to a total of 36 Pale Hymns, and each Hymn increases the damage of any Bloom, Hyperbloom, Burgeon or Lunar-Bloom reaction.
When the aforementioned reactions are triggered, it consumes 1 Pale Hymn. If the damage damages multiple enemies, then the reactions consumes multiple Pale Hymn stacks.

Off-Field Dendro Support That Can Deal Lunar-Bloom Damage
Basic Rotation Of Lauma
1.Start with the supports with longer ability durations if there are any.
2. Swap to Lauma, use her Elemental Burst first, then use her Elemental Skil (tap).
3. Swap to the main On-Field DPS to perform their ideal damage combo.
4. Switch back to Lauma again when you see the Verdant Dew indicator next to her icon light up with three stacks. Use her Hold Skill to do Lunar-Bloom damage.
5. Repeat from Step 1.
Starting the rotation with other supports allows you to trigger Lunar-Bloom first to stack Verdant Dews early.
You can thus start Lauma's first combo with her Burst and a Hold Skill to deal the Lunar-Bloom damage quickly.
Converts Triggered Bloom Reactions to Lunar-Bloom
Moonsign Benediction Passive Summary
Icon
Moonsign Benediction Passive Summary
Moonsign Benediction: Nature's Chorus
When a Bloom reaction is triggered by a party member, it will be converted into the Lunar-Bloom reaction, with every point of Elemental Mastery that Lauma has increasing Lunar-Bloom's Base DMG by 0.0175%, up to a maximum of 14%.
Additionally, when Lauma is in the party, the party's Moonsign will increase by 1 level.

Runo: All Hearts Become the Beating Moon
Lauma incants a sacred song of the far north, gaining 18 stacks of Pale Hymn.
- If Lauma uses Elemental Burst with Moon Song, or gains Moon Song within 15s of using it, she consumes all Moon Song stacks and gains 6 Pale Hymn stacks per Moon Song stack.

Pale Hymn:
- Bloom, Hyperbloom, Burgeon, or Lunar-Bloom DMG consumes 1 Pale Hymn stack to increase DMG based on Lauma's Elemental Mastery.
- Hitting multiple opponents consumes multiple stacks.
- Each stack duration is counted independently.
Lauma’s Elemental Burst empowers Dendro-related reactions through the Pale Hymn mechanic.
Pale Hymns are gained immediately at the initial Elemental Burst cast and from the Moon Songs generated from the Verdant Dews consumed by the Hold version of her Elemental Skill, which equals to 36 Pale Hymns.
Characters that can deal direct Lunar-Bloom DMG like
Nefer
and
Columbina
are the largest benefactors of Lauma's Pale Hymn stacks.
Note!
If a single instance of a Dendro-reaction hits multiple enemies, it will deplete the same amount of Pale Hymn based on how many enemies are damaged.

Can be unleashed when you have at least 1 Verdant Dew. Effect

Effect
When nearby party members deal Bloom, Hyperbloom, Burgeon, or Lunar-Bloom DMG, 1 stack of Pale Hymn will be consumed and the DMG dealt will be increased based on Lauma's Elemental Mastery.

Lauma can obtain a Stack of Pale Hymn Through the methods below:
- Elemental Burst activation
-  Consuming Moonsong from Elemental Burst.
- Constellation 6: Attacking opponents with Frostgrove Sanctuary

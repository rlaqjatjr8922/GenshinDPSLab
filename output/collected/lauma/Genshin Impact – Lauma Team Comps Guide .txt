Genshin Impact – Lauma Team Comps Guide
Posted by:
BlueStacks Content Team
Editor’s Rating:
Feb 02, 2026
Play Genshin Impact on PC
Lauma is a 5-star Dendro Catalyst in
Genshin Impact
who acts as one of the strongest Dendro supports in the current meta, especially for Bloom and Lunar-Bloom teams. She boosts Bloom-type reactions, enables Blooms to crit through her Moonsign mechanics, and provides powerful party-wide buffs that make her a near-staple in many Dendro–Hydro lineups.
This guide explains Lauma’s role in teams, then walks through her best Bloom, Hyperbloom, Lunar-Bloom, and off-meta lineups so you can slot her effectively into your account.
Lauma’s Role in Teams
Lauma is designed as a Dendro facilitator and reaction buffer, not as a pure on-field
DPS
. Her kit applies Dendro, ramps Moonsign levels, and directly increases both the damage and crit potential of Bloom-related reactions (Bloom, Hyperbloom, Burgeon, Lunar-Bloom).
In most teams she provides:
Consistent Dendro application from Skill/Burst.
Buffs to Bloom/Lunar-Bloom damage and often crit stats.
Dendro and sometimes Hydro RES shred when built with the right artifact sets.
A central Moonsign anchor for Nod-Krai-era mechanics.
This makes the standard team-building template around Lauma:Dendro (Lauma) + Hydro + Reaction trigger (Hydro/Electro/Pyro or Nefer) + Flex support.
Best Bloom Teams for Lauma
Bloom teams focus on Dendro + Hydro to generate Dendro cores that then explode for large AoE damage. Lauma’s Bloom buffs and Moonsign passives make her ideal here.
1. Premium Bloom: Lauma + Nahida + Nilou + Kokomi
GameWith and other guides highlight a Lauma–Nilou–Nahida–Kokomi core as one of the strongest Bloom teams in the game.
Lauma – Off-field Dendro and Bloom damage buffer; can also hold Silken Moon’s Serenade or Deepwood depending on the build.
Nahida – Additional Dendro application, huge EM buff, and commonly the holder of Deepwood Memories for Dendro RES shred.
Nilou – Converts Blooms into powerful Bountiful Cores in a Dendro–Hydro-only team, massively increasing Bloom damage and frequency.
Kokomi – On-field Hydro driver and healer; applies Hydro constantly with Skill and Burst while sustaining the team through Bloom self-damage.
This team excels against packs of enemies, deleting entire waves with overlapping Bloom explosions while staying safe through Kokomi’s healing.
2. F2P-Friendly Bloom: Lauma + Traveler (Dendro) + Barbara + Xingqiu
For players without many 5-stars, Lauma can still drive strong Bloom teams.
Lauma – Core Dendro support and Bloom buffer.
Dendro Traveler – Extra Dendro application and Burst control.
Barbara – Accessible Hydro healer.
Xingqiu – Off-field Hydro with strong single-target damage and defensive utility.
This team offers constant cores, solid sustain, and good single-target damage while using mostly free or 4-star characters.
Best Hyperbloom Teams for Lauma
Hyperbloom teams add Electro to convert generated Blooms into homing projectiles. Lauma powers up the underlying Bloom damage and keeps Dendro uptime high.
3. Premium Hyperbloom: Lauma + Nahida + Furina + Kuki Shinobu
GameWith and LDShop showcase a Lauma-centric Hyperbloom shell with flexible options; this version leans premium.
Lauma
– Dendro facilitator and Bloom buff engine.
Nahida
– Strongest Dendro applier and EM buffer; often holds Deepwood.
Furina
– Massive teamwide damage buff and Hydro application that also sustains through her Burst when paired with healers.
Kuki Shinobu
– Off-field Electro applicator who reliably triggers Hyperbloom thanks to her ring.
Lauma’s buffs increase Bloom damage, and Hyperbloom projectiles inherit that value, leading to extremely high and consistent DPS in both single-target and AoE scenarios.
4. Flexible Hyperbloom: Lauma + Hydro + Electro + Flex
GameWith outlines a more general template: Lauma with any Hydro and Electro that scale well with EM or provide strong off-field application.
Examples:
Core:
Lauma + Yelan/Xingqiu + Kuki Shinobu/Raiden/Yae Miko
, plus one of:
EM buffer (Sucrose, Nahida).
Extra Hydro or Dendro.
Defensive support (Zhongli, Baizhu).
As long as Lauma is generating Blooms and an Electro ally is consistently triggering them, the team will perform well, giving you freedom to adapt to your account.
Lunar-Bloom and Nefer-Centered Teams
With the Luna updates, Lunar-Bloom became a more advanced, crittable version of Bloom, and Lauma is effectively the definitive Dendro support for these teams.
5. Lunar-Bloom with Nefer: Lauma + Nefer + Nahida + Aino
Multiple Nefer and Lauma team guides describe this core as a “synergy of powerhouses,” with Lauma as the central enabler.
Lauma
– Triggers Ascendant Gleam Moonsign, increases Lunar-Bloom base damage, and buffs its Crit Rate and Crit DMG, making Nefer’s Lunar-Bloom hits rival top DPS units.
Nefer
– On-field Dendro DPS and primary Lunar-Bloom trigger, converting Blooms into Lunar-Blooms via her kit.
Nahida
– EM and Dendro application backbone, holding Deepwood and buffing the entire team’s reaction damage.
Aino
– Highly compatible Hydro who maintains Hydro aura and can build EM to further raise Bloom/Lunar-Bloom damage.
Lauma is non-negotiable in this archetype: she is explicitly identified as Nefer’s best teammate because her Lunar-Bloom buffs are unique and stack directly with Nefer’s scaling.
6. Columbina + Lauma + Aino + Nefer (Dual-Core Bloom & Petal-Burst)
LDShop’s Lauma build guide highlights a dual-core example built around Lauma and Columbina.
Columbina
– Acts as a main or sub-DPS (depending on build) and provides strong off-field Hydro or utility.
Lauma
– Central Dendro facilitator for Bloom/Lunar-Bloom.
Aino
– Additional Hydro and EM support.
Nefer
– On-field detonator for seeds, dealing large Lunar-Bloom (often called petal-burst) damage.
The rotation has Columbina and Lauma applying elements off-field, Nahida/Aino supporting with EM and Hydro, and Nefer entering to trigger and detonate cores with her Charged Attacks.
Non-Bloom Lauma Teams (Burgeon, Aggravate, and Fridge)
While Bloom and its variants are Lauma’s specialty, she can still function in other Dendro reaction shells.
7. Burgeon Shell: Lauma + Hydro + Pyro + Flex
Burgeon uses Dendro + Hydro + Pyro, with Pyro detonating Blooms for explosive AoE. Lauma strengthens the underlying Bloom damage before Burgeon converts it.
Example teams (from GameWith/LDShop):
Lauma + Xingqiu/Yelan + Thoma + flex Dendro/defensive support.
Lauma + Kokomi + Thoma + secondary Hydro or Anemo grouper.
These teams are more niche right now, but still playable if you enjoy Burgeon-style gameplay.
8. Fridge / Off-Meta Teams
Content creators have also showcased Lauma in “fridge” teams (Cryo + Hydro + Dendro) and other creative lineups.
Example concept: Lauma + Mona + Kuki Shinobu + Cryo DPS, where Lauma fuels Bloom for extra damage while the rest of the team plays around Freeze and Electro-Charged.
These are more for fun than meta optimization, but they underline how flexible Lauma is as long as Bloom-style reactions are present somewhere in the rotation.
Lauma has quickly become one of Genshin Impact’s most important Dendro supports, especially for Bloom, Hyperbloom, and Lunar-Bloom teams. Whether you are pairing her with Nilou and Kokomi for massive AoE Blooms, slotting her into Hyperbloom shells with Electro triggers, or building top-tier Lunar-Bloom cores around Nefer and Aino, Lauma consistently amplifies your reaction damage and smooths out team rotations. If you enjoy reaction-focused gameplay in Nod-Krai and beyond, building teams around Lauma is one of the most rewarding choices you can make. For the best gaming experience,

Genshin Impact - FAQs
What is the simplest team template to build around Lauma?
A reliable formula is Lauma + at least one Hydro + one reaction trigger (Electro or Pyro for Hyperbloom/Burgeon, or Nefer for Lunar-Bloom) + a flex slot for extra Dendro, Hydro, or defensive support.
Is Lauma mandatory for Nefer’s best Lunar-Bloom teams?
Guides consistently state that Nefer reaches her highest DPS with Lauma, since she boosts Lunar-Bloom base damage and crit stats while enabling Ascendant Gleam; without Lauma, Nefer’s output drops noticeably.
Can Lauma work in teams that do not use Bloom reactions?
She can still function as a generic Dendro support, but most of her unique strengths are tied to Bloom-type reactions, so non-Bloom teams usually gain less value compared to Bloom, Hyperbloom, or Lunar-Bloom setups.
Which Lauma teams are best for beginners with few 5-star characters?
F2P-friendly options like Lauma + Dendro Traveler + Barbara + Xingqiu or Lauma + Yaoyao + Xingqiu + Kuki Shinobu provide strong Bloom or Hyperbloom performance using mostly 4-star and free units.
How do I decide between Bloom and Hyperbloom teams for Lauma?
Bloom with Nilou and Kokomi excels in large AoE scenarios but requires careful healing, while Hyperbloom with Electro triggers like Kuki is safer and more consistent for single-target and general Abyss content; both benefit heavily from Lauma’s Bloom buffs.

Priestess takes the buffering role under Dendro Reaction, taking the power of such compositions to a new level, making her virtually indispensable in her groups. Players uninterested in this style of gameplay will struggle to realize her potential, and her usefulness will be limited to comfortable exploration.

Best Lauma Builds in Genshin Impact
The build for Lauma is based on the scaling of her abilities and her role in the group. In the team, the priestess takes the place of a buffer-applicator, distributing buffs from her own Elemental mastery. In the build, you should give preference to this stat and in every possible way gain at least the threshold for revealing a full buff of the heroine's cores and skills.
Recommended Stats
The priority stat is Elemental Mastery, since all key skills deal damage and give buffs based on this stat. The secondary stat is Energy Regeneration, which affects the replenishment of the ultimate with elemental particles. The Priestess does not have an acute need for Crit DMG and Crit Rate, so she can focus on levelling other stats.

Teams
The best teams for Lauma are groups with Hydro and Dendro characters, which not only allow the heroine to reveal herself, but also receive the greatest boost. All interaction within the team is based on triggering Bloom with the creation of nuclei that can trigger critical damage when exploding. The priestess's team must necessarily have a Hydro applicator or damage dealer capable of maintaining the status, and the heroine herself can act as a "bud" generator.
It is more difficult to create a strong team with Hyperbloom and Bloom, since in addition to the interaction of key elements, Pyro or Electro are included in the group. Damage dealers on these reactions may have a dependence on the ATK stat, not Elemental mastery, which is why the damage from the core rupture will be calculated incorrectly. It is important to create Dendro cores in time for the correct operation of the priestess's buffs, which will not always coincide with a comfortable rotation.

Skills
The Priestess is played as a buffer for the Dendro reactions in the party, relying on interaction with the Hydro element to create Lunar-Bloom. Based on the cores created, the heroine receives stacks of Verdant Dew, which she spends on a bonus for the entire party. After using the elemental burst, all inflorescences created during Bloom, Burgeon, Hyperbloom and Lunar-Bloom are strengthened and spend stacks of Gray Hymn.

How to play Lauma
Lauma's abilities are aimed at levelling up damage in teams with reactions based on Elemental mastery. To create Verdant Dew, on which all received buff levels are based, the interaction of Hydro and Dendro elements is necessary, thanks to which Lunar-Bloom is formed if there is a priestess in the team. To realize the full potential of the buffer character, it is necessary to consistently use her skills to convert stacks of buffs into charges of Gray Hymn.
Let's take a closer look at the existing statuses in the skill set:
Verdant Dew
— a general mechanic for Nod-Krai. Activates when cores are generated from Lunar-Bloom, up to three stacks.
Song of the Moon
- appears for the cost of one dew when using an elemental skill.
Blue Hymn
- the priestess gets 18 stacks for activation and another 18 if there are 3 levels of Song of the Moon, for a total of 36. This status is a damage bonus for Bloom, Burgeon, Hyperbloom and Lunar-Bloom and is consumed when dealing damage to an enemy from the core rupture.
The Shrine of the Frost Thicket
— an effect that follows the active character. Created by the priestess's E, it can cut 25% of resistance to Dendro and Hydro.

The rotation for the heroine is built like this:

Who is it for and do you need to spend spins?
Lauma in Genshin Impact "updates" the Bloom reaction to its lunar counterpart and increases outgoing damage due to the ability of the cores to crit when exploding. The heroine has a beautiful and smooth gameplay, a simple rotation, which will not trigger big problems for beginners. However, she is a rather narrowly focused buffer and many teams with her require event supports or damage dealers.
She is especially useful for those who want to strengthen the group of
dancer Nilou
with Bloom, and is also useful for some compositions with Hyperbloom or Burgeon.
When implementing the priestess, you must have at least one Hydro character who can constantly maintain the elemental status to create cores. The girl is unpretentious to equipment, but is unlikely to suit beginners or players without a pre-created team with all the conditions that require a "bud" buffer.
What to Choose: C1 or Signature?
The signature and the first const bring many benefits to the heroine, and the choice between them depends on the needs of the group:
The first constellation allows you to replace the healer slot with an additional buffer, applicator or reactor. Recommended for compositions where there is no healer or you want to remove one.
Signature directly increases damage and increases an important stat for reactions. The best investment if the support will definitely stay in the squad and another heal is not needed.

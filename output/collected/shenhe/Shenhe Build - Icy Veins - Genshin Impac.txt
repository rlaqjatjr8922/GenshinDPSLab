Shenhe Build
Shenhe Guide and Best Builds
Shenhe is a 5 Star Cryo Polearm character, whose kit is mainly meant to
purely support Cryo teams. She provides a very strong Flat DMG buff to Cryo
Damage instances and otherwise buffs either Elemental Skill/Burst Damage or
Normal/Charged/Plunging Attacks. She is a very strong pick to use with Cryo
Main DPS characters, as the DMG bonus is quite substantial while also providing
a good amount of energy to your DPS.

Shenhe's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Amazing Cryo Support and Battery.
Relative easy to build.
Low field time requirement.
Fixes some of the Single Target issues many Freeze comps have.
Weaknesses
Personal damage takes a lot of investment to scale high.
Requires high amounts of Energy Recharge.
Her supportive skills are less valuable at high target counts.
Only works effectively in Cryo/Anemo teams for most part.
1.
Shenhe Support Build
Shenhe's Support build is super straight forward. You want to keep uptime of
your Burst high, so Energy Recharge is important, but other
than that Shenhe solely focuses on building ATK %, which many characters
usually tend to avoid. This means that picking up artifacts for Shenhe is
easier, as she does not compete with other characters.

Shenhe is a very simple character to gear. She wants ATK % as her Elemental Skill scales
based on that. Energy Recharge should be prioritized to be
able to burst and then she wants CRIT Rate /
CRIT DMG, more so CRIT Rate if you use
Favonius Lance
on her. Since Shenhe is a Support, we utilize
Noblesse Oblige
to further boost her supportive side, especially
since you want to get her Burst out to support your Cryo Main DPS.
Her best weapon is
Calamity Queller
, which is also her signature
weapon. It perfectly fits her playstyle and sports the highest base attack in
the game together with
Song of Broken Pines
. The problem with this
weapon is that it does not really fit any other character. Unless
you want to really invest in Shenhe, it is better to go for a few of her other
readily available weapons like
Favonius Lance
. The particles from
this weapon are great, especially if they are used to funnel particles to other
characters. It also helps with the your Energy Recharge requirements.
On the topic of Energy Recharge, you want close to 190%-200% if your
team consists of 2
Cryo
. A 3rd Cryo character will reduce
the needs to about 170%. As with any Favonius weapons, they tend to reduce the
requirement somewhere between 10% and 20%. Ultimately, these are just
recommendations with team composition and the content you generally run
changing this. Try different levels of Energy Recharge and see what you find
comfortable to play with.
2.
Combos and How to Play Shenhe
As a Support, the field time for Shenhe is rather short, as you would want
to spend as much time as possible on your Main DPS. You swap in Shenhe for her
Skill and Burst essentially, and potentially a melee attack or two in the case
that you use
Favonius Lance
and have not triggered the effect after
using your Skill/Burst.
While Shenhe's Elemental Burst is a click and forget type of Burst, her
Elemental Skill requires some consideration.
Spring Spirit Summoning
has both a Press and a Hold
function. This changes the damage values of the second part of her
Skill and also changes how the skill does damage. If you press the Skill, you
dash forward, dealing damage in a line. If you Hold, then you trigger an AoE
around you. Both methods deal Cryo DMG. Holding her Skill
causes it to have a 5s longer duration and CD (10s vs 15s for both duration
and CD).
The second part is called
Icy Quill
which is a flat Cryo
DMG buff based on Shenhe's Attack. This DMG buff can trigger 5 times if you
pressed the Skill or 7 times if you held. Furthermore, her 2nd Ascension Talent
Spirit Communion Seal
increases your Skill and Burst DMG by
15% for 10s when you press her Skill and increases the DMG of
Normal/Charged/Plunging Attacks by 15% for 15s if you held her skill. These
increases are party wide.
The way the Flat Cryo DMG increase from
Icy Quill
works
is that it applies the damage onto the base damage the Talent would do, before
CRIT/other DMG bonuses are applied. So the order of operations would be
Character's Total ATK times the Skill DMG % on the talent. Then the Icy Quill
DMG bonus applies to this, which is based on Shenhe's
total Attack times the Coefficient listed in her Elemental Skill Talent. Then
CRIT is multiplied onto that if it iss a CRIT and finally other DMG Bonuses like
Cryo DMG increases. Only Shenhe's Attack is used in this calculation, the
CRIT/DMG bonus calculations are from the active character. The DMG bonus
triggers on any source of Cryo DMG except Cryo Swirls.

5.
Shenhe's Constellations
Shenhe's constellations are pretty straight forward. They are not needed
but they do make her supportive skills stronger.
Centered Spirit
(C2) is the most notable one, which makes her
burst last much longer and also give additional Cryo CRIT DMG. This is very
beneficial as most Cryo based teams gain a lot of free CRIT Rate.
Mystical Abandon
(C6) is notable too, although expensive,
especially when used with
Ayaka
, as she want to use her
Normal/Charged Attacks after her burst.

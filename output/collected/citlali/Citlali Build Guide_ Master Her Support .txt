Introduction to Citlali and Her Build
Who is Citlali?
In the ever-expanding roster of characters available to players, Citlali stands out as a unique support unit often mischaracterized as merely a “glorified Mowika buffer.” This article aims to dispel this misconception by thoroughly examining her capabilities, synergies, and potential as both a support and damage dealer. Commonly referred to as “Seedlali” or “granny” by the community, this Constellation Zero character brings significant utility that deserves proper recognition and understanding.
Optimal Build Configuration
After extensive testing, we have determined that Citlali performs optimally with specific equipment and artifact combinations. The Cinder City Force set emerges as her unquestionably superior artifact option, outperforming all alternatives by a considerable margin. For her weapon, the three-star catalyst Thrilling Tales of Dragonslayers proves surprisingly effective, often surpassing her signature weapon when supporting characters like Mowika.
Artifact Stat Priorities
For maximum effectiveness, Citlali benefits from a strategic distribution of artifact main stats. We recommend:
Sands: Energy Recharge or Elemental Mastery (depending on burst uptime requirements)
Goblet: Elemental Mastery
Circlet: Elemental Mastery
This triple Elemental Mastery configuration works exceptionally well when energy requirements are met, allowing her burst to be used consistently. Energy Recharge sands become necessary only when facing energy regeneration challenges.
Talent Priority
Our testing confirms that investment priorities for Citlali should focus on her Elemental Skill and Elemental Burst, both ideally raised to level 10. Her Normal Attack talent can remain at level 1, as it contributes negligible damage to her overall performance. This talent prioritization reflects her role as a support character rather than a primary attacker.
Constellation Considerations
While this article primarily examines Citlali at Constellation Zero (C0), we will address her constellation upgrades and their impact on her performance in later sections. Understanding her baseline capabilities provides essential context for evaluating her potential at higher constellation levels and in various team compositions.
In the following chapters, we will delve deeper into Citlali’s kit mechanics, damage potential, and specific synergies with Pyro DPS characters to provide a comprehensive understanding of this often underestimated support character.
Understanding Citlali’s Kit and Gameplay Mechanics
Core Mechanics and Abilities
Citlali’s kit, while initially appearing complex, follows a straightforward gameplay pattern that revolves around her Elemental Skill and Burst. At its essence, her optimal rotation involves using her Skill, followed by her Burst, and then switching to another character. This simplicity belies the nuanced mechanics that make her an exceptional support unit.
The Elemental Skill serves multiple functions: it creates a protective shield, deals modest Cryo damage, and summons Itzpapa, a companion entity crucial to Citlali’s utility. While Itzpapa initially appears passive, it becomes significantly more valuable once Citlali accumulates at least 50 Night Soul points. At this threshold, Itzpapa generates a medium-sized Cryo field around the active character, applying Cryo every 2 seconds—a mechanic that proves invaluable for reaction-based team compositions.
Night Soul Points System
The Night Soul points system represents the core resource management aspect of Citlali’s kit. These points can be accumulated through various means, though the most efficient method involves the sequential use of her Skill and Burst. This combination consistently generates sufficient Night Soul points to activate Itzpapa’s Cryo application field, ensuring seamless uptime on this valuable effect.
We find that understanding this point system is essential for maximizing Citlali’s support capabilities, as maintaining the 50-point threshold optimizes her Cryo application frequency and consistency.
Damage Potential Through Elemental Mastery
While Citlali’s Elemental Skill damage remains relatively modest even with investment, her Elemental Burst offers surprising damage potential. The scaling on her Burst reaches an impressive 1000%, explaining our earlier recommendation for triple Elemental Mastery artifacts. This scaling allows Citlali to deliver significant damage despite being built primarily as a support character.
In practical terms, this means Citlali can contribute meaningful damage even while equipped with Thrilling Tales of Dragonslayers. Under optimal conditions, her Burst can achieve melt reactions exceeding 100,000 damage—an impressive figure for a support unit not primarily designed for dealing damage.
Rotation Considerations and Cryo Application
One technical consideration when utilizing Citlali involves her substantial Cryo application at the beginning of rotations. This can potentially remove Pyro auras from enemies, which might interfere with planned reaction sequences. To mitigate this issue, we recommend a specific rotation when pairing Citlali with Bennett: activate Bennett’s Burst first, then switch to Citlali to use her Skill followed by her Burst. This sequence preserves enough Pyro aura to ensure Citlali’s Burst successfully triggers a melt reaction.
Additionally, her Burst can generate up to three Spirit Vessel skulls per enemy. While these deal minimal damage individually, they provide supplementary Cryo application that can facilitate reactions for other team members.
Practical Application in Combat
In practical combat scenarios, Citlali’s straightforward rotation belies her strategic depth. The consistent Cryo application from Itzpapa creates numerous opportunities for reaction-based damage, particularly for Melt and Freeze compositions. Her shield provides defensive utility, while her potential for substantial Burst damage adds offensive value to her support package.
This combination of reliable Cryo application, defensive shielding, and surprising burst damage potential makes Citlali significantly more versatile than the “glorified Mowika buffer” label suggests. In the next chapter, we will explore her damage-dealing capabilities in greater detail, examining optimal scenarios and team compositions that maximize her offensive contribution.
Citlali’s Potential as a Damage Dealer
Beyond Support: Examining Citlali’s Damage Ceiling
While our previous chapters established Citlali’s fundamental role as a support character, this section explores her often overlooked potential as a damage contributor. It’s important to acknowledge that Citlali’s damage profile is highly specialized—concentrated almost entirely in her Elemental Burst. Her normal attacks and Elemental Skill damage remain negligible even with significant investment, making her damage pattern distinctly burst-oriented rather than sustained.
Burst Damage Potential
The centerpiece of Citlali’s damage capability lies in her Elemental Burst, which can achieve impressive numbers under the right circumstances. With proper setup, her Burst can trigger powerful Melt reactions, resulting in damage that far exceeds what might be expected from a support character. Our testing reveals that a well-built Citlali can achieve Melt reactions exceeding 100,000 damage—a substantial contribution to team damage output.
This damage potential increases significantly when Citlali wields her signature catalyst. While we previously established that Thrilling Tales of Dragonslayers often provides better overall team damage when supporting characters like Mowika, her signature weapon unlocks considerably higher personal damage ceilings through enhanced Melt reactions.
Rotation-Dependent Performance
It must be emphasized that Citlali’s damage contribution is heavily rotation-dependent. Her damage potential exists almost exclusively in scenarios where her Burst can trigger Melt reactions. In rotations where this cannot be achieved, her personal damage contribution becomes essentially non-existent, reverting her to a pure support role.
This characteristic creates an interesting decision point for players: optimize rotations to capitalize on Citlali’s Burst damage potential, or focus entirely on her support capabilities. The optimal choice varies based on team composition and investment levels.
Support Capabilities Enhancing Team Damage
Even when not directly dealing damage, Citlali significantly amplifies team damage through multiple support mechanisms:
Her first passive talent reduces enemy Hydro and Pyro resistance by 20% when Frozen or Melt reactions are triggered—a substantial debuff that enhances overall team damage
The recommended Cinder City Force artifact set provides a 40% elemental damage bonus to the corresponding element when reactions are triggered
Her weapon options either provide 48% ATK (Thrilling Tales) or 28% bonus damage (signature catalyst) to the next character
Her shield, while primarily defensive, allows more aggressive play from damage dealers by reducing interruption and damage taken
Elemental Mastery Scaling Considerations
Citlali’s damage potential scales exceptionally well with Elemental Mastery, explaining our earlier recommendation for triple EM artifacts. This scaling affects not only her Burst damage but also enhances her shield strength. With approximately 710 EM, her shield can absorb around 10,400 damage against most attacks, and up to 26,000 damage against Cryo attacks due to its 250% efficiency against that element.
This dual scaling makes Elemental Mastery particularly valuable for Citlali, as it simultaneously improves both her offensive burst potential and defensive utility.
Cryo Application as Indirect Damage Contribution
While not directly contributing to her personal damage numbers, Citlali’s consistent Cryo application through Itzpapa enables reaction chains that significantly increase team damage output. This application occurs every 2 seconds in a medium-sized field around the active character, providing ample opportunities for Melt and Freeze reactions.
The value of this application should not be underestimated when evaluating her overall contribution to team damage potential, as it enables damage-dealing teammates to consistently trigger powerful elemental reactions.
Citlali’s Synergy with Pyro DPS Characters
Analyzing Reaction Dynamics with Pyro Characters
Having established Citlali’s core mechanics and damage potential in previous chapters, we now turn our attention to her specific interactions with Pyro DPS characters. This synergy represents one of Citlali’s most promising team compositions, though it comes with important technical considerations. Our testing reveals that Citlali’s Cryo application pattern creates both opportunities and limitations when paired with Pyro damage dealers.
Front-Loaded Cryo Application Pattern
A critical aspect of Citlali’s kit that affects her synergy with Pyro characters is her front-loaded Cryo application pattern. At the beginning of rotations, she applies substantial Cryo through her initial abilities. However, once this initial application phase ends, her ongoing Cryo application becomes limited to Itzpapa’s field effect, which applies Cryo only once every two seconds. This application frequency proves insufficient to maintain consistent Melt reactions with many Pyro DPS characters who apply Pyro at a faster rate.
Synergy with Diluc
In our testing with Diluc, we observed that the initial Cryo application from Citlali enables his Elemental Burst to trigger a powerful Melt reaction, achieving impressive damage exceeding 200,000 in optimal scenarios. Additionally, his first two normal attacks can also benefit from Melt reactions. However, after these initial hits, Diluc’s Pyro application rate overwhelms Citlali’s Cryo application, preventing further Melts during his attack sequence.
Despite attempts to modify rotation timing and attack patterns, we found that Citlali simply cannot maintain sufficient Cryo aura to enable consistent Melts throughout Diluc’s entire damage window. Nevertheless, the combination of powerful initial Melts and Citlali’s substantial buff package still makes this pairing viable and effective.
Interactions with Yoimiya, Klee, and Yanfei
Our analysis extends to other prominent Pyro damage dealers, including Yoimiya, Klee, and Yanfei. The pattern observed with these characters mirrors our findings with Diluc—initial Melt reactions are possible, but sustained Melting throughout their attack sequences remains unachievable due to Citlali’s limited ongoing Cryo application rate.
With Yoimiya specifically, we found that her rapid normal attack sequence quickly depletes any Cryo aura provided by Citlali. Similarly, both Klee and Yanfei apply Pyro too frequently for Citlali’s two-second Cryo application interval to maintain consistent Melt reactions beyond the initial hits.
Dual Cryo Solutions
To address the limitation of Citlali’s Cryo application rate, we tested compositions incorporating a second Cryo character alongside Citlali. Characters like Kaeya can supplement Citlali’s Cryo application, enabling more consistent Melt reactions throughout longer damage windows. This approach mirrors established strategies previously employed with Rosaria in similar team compositions.
This dual Cryo approach represents a viable solution for players seeking to maximize Melt reaction uptime with Pyro DPS characters, though it comes at the cost of a team slot that might otherwise be used for additional utility or buffing.
Vape-Melt Complications
Our investigation into Vape-Melt compositions (combining Hydro, Cryo, and Pyro) with Citlali revealed several technical complications. When fighting bosses, freezing can disrupt elemental auras, interfering with planned reaction sequences. Against freezable enemies, characters like Diluc can trigger Shatter reactions due to claymore weapons dealing blunt damage, further complicating reaction chains.
Hu Tao Compositions
Among the Pyro characters tested, Hu Tao stands out as having particularly interesting synergy with Citlali in specific team compositions. When paired with Xingqiu, Yelan, and a shielder like Zhongli, this team can leverage Citlali’s ability to simultaneously reduce both Hydro and Pyro resistance by 20% through her passive talent.
While consistent Vape-Melt reactions remain challenging to achieve in this composition, the dual resistance shred provided by Citlali significantly enhances Hu Tao’s damage output. This demonstrates that even when perfect reaction chains cannot be maintained, Citlali’s utility extends beyond merely enabling Melt reactions.
Optimizing Rotation Timing
To maximize Citlali’s synergy with Pyro DPS characters, rotation timing becomes crucial. We recommend applying Pyro first (typically through Bennett’s Burst), followed by Citlali’s abilities, to ensure her Burst can trigger a Melt reaction. This sequence optimizes damage output while maintaining sufficient Pyro aura for subsequent reactions.
For sustained damage phases, players should recognize that after the initial Melt reactions, Pyro characters will likely transition to raw Pyro damage without reaction bonuses. Team rotations should be planned with this limitation in mind, potentially cycling back to reapply Citlali’s abilities once her cooldowns reset.
Overall Assessment and Conclusion
Citlali’s True Value Beyond the Misconceptions
Throughout this article, we have conducted a comprehensive analysis of Citlali’s capabilities, mechanics, and team synergies. Our investigation reveals that the common characterization of Citlali as merely a “glorified Mowika buffer” significantly undervalues her versatility and utility. While she indeed excels in supporting Mowika, her kit offers substantial benefits to various team compositions beyond this specific pairing.
Balanced Support Package
Citlali’s strength lies in her well-rounded support package that combines multiple valuable elements:
Consistent Cryo application through Itzpapa’s field effect
Defensive utility via her shield
Resistance shred for both Hydro and Pyro elements
Substantial buffing potential through artifact sets and weapon options
Surprising burst damage potential with proper setup
This combination of offensive and defensive support capabilities makes her an asset to many teams, even when unable to maintain perfect reaction chains.
Limitations in Context
Our testing confirms that Citlali cannot sustain Melt reactions for most Pyro characters due to her limited Cryo application frequency. This limitation is most apparent when pairing her with rapid Pyro applicators like Diluc, Yoimiya, Klee, and Yanfei. However, this should be viewed as a balanced design choice rather than a fundamental flaw.
The front-loaded nature of her Cryo application still enables powerful initial Melt reactions, providing significant damage spikes at the beginning of combat rotations. Furthermore, her additional utility ensures she remains valuable even after these initial Melts, contributing to team performance through her buffs, debuffs, and defensive capabilities.
Optimal Use Cases
Based on our analysis, Citlali performs optimally in several specific scenarios:
As a dedicated support for Mowika and Gamin, where her Cryo application pattern aligns well with their attack sequences
In burst-oriented teams that capitalize on powerful initial reactions rather than sustained reaction chains
As part of dual-Cryo compositions that supplement her Cryo application rate
In teams that benefit from her resistance shred, particularly those utilizing both Hydro and Pyro elements
Investment Considerations
For players considering investing in Citlali, our findings suggest that she performs admirably even at Constellation Zero with modest investment. Her core utility functions effectively with the accessible three-star Thrilling Tales of Dragonslayers catalyst and the farmable Cinder City Force artifact set. This accessibility makes her a valuable addition to most accounts without requiring significant resource expenditure.
Higher investment—particularly in her Elemental Skill and Burst talents—enhances both her support capabilities and personal damage contribution. While her constellations provide additional benefits, they represent optimizations rather than fundamental changes to her gameplay pattern or team role.
Final Verdict
In conclusion, Citlali emerges as a versatile, accessible, and effective support character whose value extends well beyond her synergy with specific characters. Her well-designed kit offers meaningful contributions to various team compositions, providing both offensive amplification and defensive utility. While she has clear limitations regarding sustained Cryo application, these are balanced by her substantial buffing potential and surprising burst damage capabilities.
This article has demonstrated that Citlali deserves recognition as a valuable support unit in her own right, capable of enhancing team performance across numerous compositions. Her design represents a thoughtful balance of power and limitations, creating a character that remains relevant and useful across various content without becoming mandatory or overpowered. Players who invest in understanding and optimizing her unique mechanics will find her to be a rewarding addition to their character roster.

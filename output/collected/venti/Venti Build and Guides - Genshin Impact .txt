Venti's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Strong Small Target Crown Control.
Has a large variety of team options.
Easy to play.
Facilitates Exploration with his Wind current.
Weaknesses
Large enemies are not affected by Venti's Elemental Burst.
Elemental Burst can be difficult to place exactly where it is suppose to be.
Burst sometimes lifts the enemies too high and they can not be reached by the Main DPS anymore.
1.
Venti Build Guide, Best Artifacts, Weapons and Talent Priority
Venti's role is mainly as a reaction off-field DPS, where you will have to focus on 
EM and ER to be able to use his Burst off-cooldown and increase his Swirl damage.
Reaction DPS Venti is the recommended build. However, we will give you some ideas for 
a more
Anemo
DPS focused build, in case you want to use Venti as a Main DPS. But keep in mind 
that the second option will require way more investment to make it work.

3.
Venti Combos and How to Play
Whether you Build Venti as a Reaction DPS or
Anemo
DPS, the gameplay mostly
remains the same.
Venti's Elemental Skill
Skyward Sonnet
has two modes, a tap one and a hold:
Tap the skill to launch a single tunnel of wind that will lift
small enemies into the air and grant you 3 particles of energy.
Holding the skill however, will create a large wind current, deal more damage, 
and produces 4 particles of energy.
In most cases, you will use the Hold version only outside of combat during exploration, 
in order to make use of the Wind Current to launch yourself in the air. During a fight, 
using tap only will be faster and more efficient, and still crowd control enemies.
Venti's Elemental Burst skill
Wind's Grand Ode
is his most important ability,
whether you build him as a Reaction DPS or
Anemo
DPS. This skill creates a massive
vortex that will suck in all surrounding enemies and control them in the air. It also absorbs
and swirls any element which will deal even more damage to the enemies trapped within. 
This is one of the main reason Venti will shine more if you build him as a Reaction DPS 
with high Elemental Mastery.
This Burst offers 20 ticks of
Anemo
damage and 16 ticks if you
absorb another element, with a Swirl effect occurring 7 times regardless of
which element is absorbed. This can create some powerful elemental reactions
when combined with the right teammates. However, this burst can also lift some
enemies out of reach for certain melee fighters and skills, so use it wisely.
Venti will use both of his abilities when you bring him out onto the field,
leading with his Elemental Skill first and then his Elemental
Burst. This combo is the most common combination for Venti due to the fact
that you can cut the animation time of his Skill by combining the two
abilities. Afterwards, you simply switch back to another support to stack a
different Element to create reactions or move to a DPS character to fight.
4.
Venti's Best Team Comps
Venti is best placed teams who focus on
Elemental Reactions
and teams who can take advantage of his Crowd Control capabilities. Both Reaction and
Anemo
DPS Venti
can take advantage of the same teams. Be careful with ground melee character such as
Wriothesley
as Venti tends to send enemies too high for them, putting them out of reach.

Venti's Rainbow Teams consist in creating a Rainbow Vortex that will melt down enemies in a few seconds using a 
range of elemental reactions. 
The goal is to accumulating multiple elements in one team in order to create massive AoE elemental Damage.
Bennett
and
Fischl
are two of the best teammates for this. Bennett with his usual buffs, 
and Fischl for the amount of
Electro
damage and energy recharge she provides, allowing 
you to lower the energy requirement on Venti.
Iansan
can also be a good pick 
instead of Bennett.
The Main DPS slot is rather flexible, but teammates such as
Kamisato Ayato
or
Ganyu
are good options for massive AoE damage boosts. As mentioned before, just don't use characters
who deal damage close to the ground (Wriothesley, Heizou, Claymore users as they tend to have a 
hard time reaching up when the enemies are flying too high).
Lyney
is another great option. Venti can allow Lyney to deal AoE
damage, since Lyney will be able to shoot his Fire into Venti's vortex with ease, while benefiting 
from Bennett's Buffs and
Citlali
's Shield.

Anemo-focused teams with Venti will only benefit from him if you play Venti as an Anemo DPS and 
not a Reaction DPS, as he will not have many reactions to do in those teams.
Bennett
and
Faruzan
are two characters you will want to use in those teams, as they provide important buffs to

Anemo

The main options are
Wanderer
or
Xiao

have a massive burst window to attack while Venti packs and controls enemies in his Vortex. 
Xiao works, but be aware that he tends to have a hard time hitting Venti's enemies inside the Vortex 
depending on where you put it.

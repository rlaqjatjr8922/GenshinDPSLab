Combat Talents
Normal Attack – The Classics of Acupuncture
Baizhu can perform up to 4 consecutive attacks that deal Dendro DMG. His Charged and Plunging Attack function in similar ways, both dealing AoE Dendro DMG to opponents in front of him.
Elemental Skill – Universal Diagnosis
Upon activation, Baizhu releases a Gossamer Sprite that attacks nearby opponents, dealing Dendro DMG. After it performs 3 attacks or if there are no opponents nearby, the Sprite will return and heal all nearby party members based on Baizhu’s Max HP.
Elemental Burst – Holistic Revivification
Baizhu enters the Pulsing Clarity state and creates a Seamless Shield that absorbs Dendro DMG with 250% effectiveness. He will generate a new Seamless Shield every 2.5 seconds while in the Pulsing Clarity state.
The Seamless Shield will also heal your active character based on Baizhu’s Max HP and attack opponents with Spiritveins. Spiritveins deal Dendro DMG whenever a new Seamless Shield generates, or when the Shield expires or is shattered.
Passive Talents
1st Ascension – Five Fortunes Forever
Baizhu gains different buffs depending on the current HP of your active character. When your character’s HP is less than 50%, Baizhu gains a 20% Healing Bonus. When their HP is more than or equal to 50%, Baizhu gains a 25% Dendro DMG Bonus.
4th Ascension – All Things Are of the Earth
Characters healed by Seamless Shields will gain the Year of Verdant Favor effect. For each 1,000 Max HP that Baizhu possesses that does not exceed 50,000 will increase the Burning, Bloom, Hyperbloom, and Burgeon reaction DMG dealt by these characters by 2%. Aggravate and Spread reaction DMG will be increased by 0.8%. In total, Burning, Bloom, Hyperbloom, and Burgeon reaction DMG can be increased by up to 100%. Aggravate and Spread reaction DMG can be increased by up to 40%. This effect lasts for 6 seconds.
Utility Passive – Herbal Nourishment
When Baizhu is in your party, interacting with certain harvestable items will heal your active character for 2.5% of Baizhu’s Max HP. This typically applies to plants, such as Sweet Flowers, Mint, and Qingxin.

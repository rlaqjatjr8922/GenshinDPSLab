Understanding Neuvillette’s Kit and Team Role
Here’s the thing about Neuvillette – he’s not your typical DPS. This 5-star Hydro Catalyst user scales entirely off Max HP, which fundamentally changes how you approach team building. At Level 90, he sits at 14,695 base HP with 88.4% base CRIT DMG (pretty generous, honestly).
His bread and butter comes from ‘Equitable Judgment’, but you’ll need to master Sourcewater Droplet management first. Think of it this way: his Skill drops 3 droplets when hitting enemies, his Burst gives you 6. Absorb 3 droplets? That painful 4-second charge time shrinks to roughly 1 second. That’s your damage window right there.
Each droplet heals him too, which counteracts the HP drain when he’s above 50% Max HP. The Charged Attack hits individual targets once per 0.4 seconds – maximum 8 hits per use – with 50% Poise DMG reduction during execution. Not bad for staying alive while you’re channeling.

The Pneuma Alignment Game-Changer
Now here’s where it gets interesting. Neuvillette’s A1 Passive grants ‘Past Draconic Glories’ stacks when your party triggers Vaporize, Frozen, Electro-Charged, Bloom, Hydro Swirl, and Hydro Crystallize. Each stack lasts 30 seconds independently – and trust me, you want all three.
The damage scaling jumps to 110% (1 stack), 125% (2 stacks), or a whopping 160% (3 stacks). This is why diverse elemental teammates aren’t just nice to have – they’re essential.
His A4 Passive? 0.6% Hydro DMG Bonus per 1% current HP exceeding 30% Max HP, capped at 30% when HP reaches 80%. Just remember this bonus decreases during Charged Attack HP drain without external healing.
Core Team Building Philosophy
After testing dozens of combinations, Neuvillette’s team building boils down to two priorities: maximizing A1 passive stacks and ensuring consistent energy generation. 4-piece Marechaussee Hunter isn’t just Best-in-Slot – it’s in a league of its own, providing +15% Charged Attack DMG and up to 36% CRIT Rate.

FAQ
What are minimum ER requirements for different team compositions?
Double Hydro with Furina needs 100-115% with Tome/Prototype Amber, Double Electro requires 100-110%, Solo Hydro needs 100-130%. These jump by 10-25% with other weapons.
How important is C1 for team building flexibility?
C1 provides 0-24% DPS increase with automatic A1 stack generation and interruption resistance. It enables Double Hydro teams and eliminates shielder requirements – pretty significant quality-of-life improvement.
Which Anemo support provides best value?
Kazuha offers highest value with 30-40% Elemental DMG Bonus and essential grouping. Sucrose serves as an excellent budget alternative with EM sharing, but the gap is noticeable.
How do you optimize rotations for maximum buff uptime?
Split Neuvillette’s Charged Attacks into two phases (2+2 or 3+1) with buff refreshing between phases. Use VV supports immediately before damage windows – timing is everything here.
What’s the performance difference between Tome and alternatives?
R1 Tome achieves 137% performance relative to R5 Prototype Amber, while R5 Sacrificial Jade reaches 130%. The signature weapon provides optimal damage scaling, but F2P options are definitely viable.
How do reaction teams compare to Hypercarry compositions?
Hyperbloom teams achieve highest DPS at 83,849, followed by Electro-Charged at 79,509 and Hypercarry at 71,500. Hypercarry offers more consistent performance across enemy types, though – sometimes raw numbers don’t tell the whole story.

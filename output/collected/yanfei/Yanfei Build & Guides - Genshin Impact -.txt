Yanfei's Strengths & Weaknesses
Strengths
Overload knockback isn't an issue for Yanfei due to being a ranged character.
Does not require Constellations to deal decent damage while C4 instead turns Yanfei into a shielder.
Great Pyro application due to being an on-field catalyst character.
Weaknesses
While fun to play, damage output will be noticeably lower than the many other powerful Pyro DPS.
Primarily uses Charged Attacks which consumes a lot of stamina leaving Yanfei vulnerable.

Yanfei as a shielder is built with
Prototype Amber
, because it
increases the strength of Yanfei's shield, generates Energy, and also provides
a small amount of Healing for the team.
Thrilling Tales of Dragon Slayers
can also be a good build choice
due to the powerful ATK buffs it provides but Prototype Amber is easy to get with no downsides
to using it.
The exact artifacts for Yanfei is less important because you are building 
around the 2-Piece effects. Choose what sets have the correct 2-Piece bonuses 
you need that have good substats.
Build Yanfei with a lot of Energy Recharge to hit her requirements to regularly Burst.
The only other stat you need to build Yanfei with as a shielder is HP. This
then increases Yanfei's shield strength.
4.1.
Energy Recharge Requirements
When playing Yanfei as an on-field DPS, she generally wants around
120-130% Energy Recharge. This is because Yanfei normally
bursts every other rotation so that artifacts can focus on CRIT instead.
If you did want Yanfei to burst every rotation, or when played as a
Shield Support, you instead need to build 200-240% depending on your team composition.
When building Yanfei as a shielder, it is better to build around 240%. This
provides flexibility and does not take away from other stats that Yanfei needs
to build because she only needs HP.
5.
Combos and How to Play Yanfei
Yanfei's Normal Attacks, Skills and Bursts help to generate Scarlet Seals
up to a maximum of 3 seals (or 4 at C6). These seals decrease Stamina
consumption and are consumed with
Charged Attack
to deal
increased DMG as well as a wider AoE to defeat enemies.
Both
Signed Edict
and
Done Deal
generate
the maximum number of Scarlet Seals. This means that you want to use a
Charged Attack between activations of your Skill and Burst to ensure you
do not waste Seals. An example would be:
Yanfei
Done Deal
Yanfei
(If C6)
Charged Attack
Yanfei
Signed Edict
Charged Attack
From there you can continue to build up Seals and Deal Charged Attacks.
Depending on how fast you can apply
Hydro
, you may only
want to do 2/3 Normal Attacks so that you Charge more often to
deal bigger
Vaporize
damage hits.

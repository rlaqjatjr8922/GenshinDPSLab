Genshin Impact Flins
Guide: Kit Explained
Flins is an
Electro damage dealer
who mainly revolves around his
skill and special burst
. When you use his skill, he enters a stance where his normal attacks become
Electro-infused
and he gains
interruption resistance
. These infused attacks also generate energy particles, which are important for keeping his bursts active.
Pressing his skill again unleashes his
special skill
, a fast
AoE Electro attack
that also unlocks his
special burst
for a short time. This is the real core of his gameplay since his special burst only costs
30 energy
, deals strong
Lunar Charge damage
, and can be used multiple times in a rotation if you manage his energy properly. His
normal burst
exists too, costing a hefty
80 energy
, but it’s less efficient.
Genshin Impact Flins (Image via HoYoverse)
You generally prioritize the cheaper,
faster special burst
and only use the normal burst if you have excess energy. In practice, Flin’s gameplay loop is simple: you swap him in, use his skill to enter stance, land normal attacks for energy, then follow with his
special skill
and
special burst
. You repeat this while weaving in normal attacks until his cooldowns reset.
His passives further boost his
Lunar Charge damage
and give him extra
Elemental Mastery
, making his reactions stronger when paired with
Hydro or Electro
teammates. Overall, Flins thrives on quick rotations, frequent bursts, and steady energy generation, making him a high-output DPS when played in the right setups.
Genshin Impact Flins Build Guide: Constellations
C1
Reduces Flins’
special skill cooldown
from 6 seconds to
4 seconds
, which means he can fit in three uses of his
special skill and special burst
per rotation instead of just two.
On top of that, it helps manage his energy needs by letting him
recover 8 energy every 5.5 seconds
whenever a party member triggers
Lunar Charge
.
While you may still need to adjust his
Energy Recharge
depending on your team, this constellation is already a
big damage increase
and makes his rotation smoother.
C2
Another strong upgrade because it boosts Flins’
normal attack damage
while in his special stance. More importantly, when the team is in
Ascendant Gleam
, his Electro attacks also reduce enemy
Electro resistance by 25%
.
This is a huge debuff that not only improves Flins’ personal output but also makes your
Lunar Charge damage
significantly stronger, benefiting both him and his team.
C3
C3 boosts his Burst talent level
C4
Gives Flins more
stats
, specifically increasing his
Attack stat
and improving his
Attack-to-Elemental Mastery conversion
.
This means higher personal damage and stronger scaling for his
Lunar Charge reactions
, making him even more effective in reaction-based teams.
C5
C5 is a skill talent level boost.
C6
Biggest power spike. It introduces a new multiplier called
Elevated
, which further buffs his
Lunar Charge damage
with an entirely separate scaling factor.
On top of that, when the team is in
Ascendant Gleam
, this same effect also applies to his
teammates’ Lunar Charge damage
, though at a lower value. This makes him not only a massive personal DPS at C6 but also a
team-wide Lunar Charge enabler
, transforming his role into a top-tier carry and support hybrid.
Flins becomes really good at
C2,
which is the best investment; the rest are more luxury or whale upgrades.
Genshin Impact Flins Guide: Best in-slot weapon
Bloodsoaked Ruins (5*)
His
signature weapon
, which is theoretically the strongest choice since it gives him more
crit rate, attack, and elemental mastery
that directly boost his
Lunar Charge damage
. It’s an
8% damage increase
compared to his free option.
Prospector’s Shovel (4*)
For a F2P
option
, the
Prospector’s Shovel
performs surprisingly well. Because Flins’ damage comes from multiple hits that only need to
crit a few times
, you can reliably “crit fish” with it, making the weapon very practical and efficient without needing to spend resources.
Favonius Lance (4*)
If you suffer from energy issues, then Favonius lance is highly recommended.

When building a team for
Flins
, there is always a clear archetype to follow: you need a
Hydro unit
to enable his Lunar-Charged attacks, an
Electro unit
to provide energy and maintain consistent rotations, and finally a
support slot
that usually belongs to
Sucrose
, since she provides the strongest buffs and resistance shred. With this core structure in mind, there are several variations depending on what characters you own.
Flins Premium Team
The strongest possible team for
Flins
right now is
Flins, C6 Aino,
Ineffa
, and
Sucrose
.
Aino
at C6 ensures constant
Hydro application
, while
Ineffa
not only contributes around thirty percent of the team’s total damage but also provides crucial shielding and
improves overall team DPS
by about twenty percent compared to other options.
Image via HoYoverse
Meanwhile,
Sucrose
with the
Viridescent Venerer set
and
Thrilling Tales
massively buffs Flins’ attack and shreds enemy Hydro and Electro resistance, making her rotation as simple as pressing skill, swapping out, and empowering Flins to do the rest. This composition represents Flins at his absolute peak, both in damage and survivability.
Flins F2P Team
If you don’t have
Ineffa
, the best alternative is to run
Flins, Aino,
an Electro support such as
Shinobu
,
Dori
, or Ororon, and Sucrose
. Losing Ineffa lowers overall team damage by roughly twenty percent, but the setup still performs extremely well.
Shinobu
or
Dori
can provide valuable healing, while
Ororon
, especially at C6, increases team DPS by around seven to nine percent.
Even in this budget variation,
Sucrose
remains the most important support thanks to her massive elemental mastery buffs, attack boost from Thrilling Tales, and universal resistance shred. If survival becomes an issue,
Lan Yan
can replace her for shielding, or
Xilonen
can be used for healing, but in terms of damage potential,
Sucrose
is always the best choice.
Is Flins worth pulling in Genshin Impact
Honestly, I think
Flins is 100% worth pulling
if you’re even a little bit interested in him. His kit feels super fun and balanced, whether you want to spam mini-bursts or go all-in with triple burst rotations once you manage his energy.
And yeah, he does need more ER than usual, but once you cover that, he just goes wild. What really sells me is how
F2P-friendly
he is, which is kind of amazing compared to most other 5-stars. If you’re lucky enough to grab his
C1
, it basically gives you the same benefits as having
Ineffa
, which is huge for his team’s flexibility.
Sure, the only real drawbacks are that his best artifact set is locked to one option right now and he doesn’t have the flashy exploration utility that some
Natlan units
bring, but in actual combat, his damage is ridiculous. Even with budget builds, he keeps up with the strongest units, and with his premium setup, he’s an absolute monster.
